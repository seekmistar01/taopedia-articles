# Start Call

A start call is the activation step that turns on a newly created subnet's emissions. Official
documentation describes activating a subnet to begin its first emission period, after which the
subnet starts distributing alpha to its participants.

## What It Represents

Subnet registration and the start call are separate milestones. Registration establishes the
subnet's identity and owner on chain; the start call switches on the first emission period and
begins alpha issuance for that netuid
([Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Once emissions run, rewards accumulate each tempo and are distributed through Yuma Consensus at the
interval boundary ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's activation path. They should not be read as
representing production emission timing on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Register

A start call is the subnet-owner step that turns on a newly created subnet's emissions. Register is
the participant process that purchases a UID slot to join a subnet
([Subnets: Create a subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Start call** — the subnet-owner step that activates a subnet's emissions.
- **Register** — the participant process that buys a UID slot to join a subnet.

## Distinction from Subnet Is Active

Start call is the action that begins a subnet's emissions. Subnet is active is the resulting state
that reports whether those emissions have started
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

- **Start call** — the operation that switches a subnet's emissions on.
- **Subnet is active** — the flag that reports emissions have started.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

The start call turns on a subnet's first emission period. The immunity period is the grace window
during which a newly registered neuron is shielded from deregistration
([Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

- **Start call** — the activation step that begins a subnet's emissions.
- **Immunity period** — the grace window protecting a new neuron from early deregistration.

## Distinction from Recycle

The start call begins a subnet's emissions once it is registered. Recycle describes TAO removed from
a holder's balance and returned to the supply, such as the TAO consumed by registration
([Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Glossary: Recycle](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycle)).

- **Start call** — the step that switches a registered subnet's emissions on.
- **Recycle** — TAO returned to the supply for re-issuance, as with the registration cost.

## Distinction from Participation

Start call is the activation step that turns on a subnet's first emission period. Participation
names miner and validator activity on that subnet once emissions are running
([Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Start call** — the activation that begins subnet emissions.
- **Participation** — miner and validator activity that becomes economically live once emissions
  run.

## Distinction from Subnet Creator

A subnet creator is the participant who registers and owns a subnet. The start call is the
activation step that creator uses to begin the subnet's first emission period
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

- **Subnet creator** — the role that owns and activates a subnet.
- **Start call** — the activation event that turns on emissions.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Start call is the subnet-creator activation step that turns on a newly created subnet's first
emission period. It names a one-time launch event in the subnet lifecycle, not the recurring
settlement pass that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares
([Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Emission: Injection and Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary once the subnet's emission
path is active. It reads the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding,
and converts the result into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate start call from subnet creator ownership and from participation
vocabulary. The start call begins injection and distribution on the netuid; Yuma is the recurring
consensus settlement that allocates rewards after validators submit weights—not the activation
transaction itself
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Readers should treat start call as the launch trigger for a subnet's first emission period, and Yuma
as the epoch-boundary mechanism that settles weights into payouts while emissions run.

- **Start call** — creator activation that begins a subnet's first emission period.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level. It does not report whether a particular subnet has
been activated, when its start call happened, or its current emissions. Those are live chain state
and should be checked for the relevant netuid at the time of observation.
