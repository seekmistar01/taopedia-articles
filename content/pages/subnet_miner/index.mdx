# Subnet Miner

A subnet miner is the task-performing role inside a Bittensor subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) defines a subnet miner
as the entity that performs tasks given by subnet validators, while
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) describes
subnets as markets where miners produce a specific digital commodity and validators measure the
work.

## Role in a Subnet

The miner role is tied to the subnet's work target. A miner in one subnet may produce a different
kind of output than a miner in another subnet because each subnet can define its own goals and
standards. [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)
explains that subnets specialize around different digital commodities, and the
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task) connects subnet tasks to
the work miners perform.

## Relationship to Validators

Subnet miners and validators form the basic work-and-evaluation relationship inside a subnet. Miners
provide work for the subnet, and validators measure that work against the subnet's standards. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) frames subnet miners
around tasks from validators, and
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) describes
validators as the role that measures miner work.

## Relationship to the Task

A subnet miner is the role performing the work; the subnet task is the work target itself. Keeping
those ideas separate makes the term easier to read: the miner is the role, while the task describes
what that role is expected to produce.
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)
places the task, protocol, and evaluation method together as parts of an incentive mechanism, and
the [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task) defines the task as
the work miners perform.

## Incentive Mechanism Context

Miner work becomes meaningful inside the subnet's incentive mechanism. The task says what work is
requested, the protocol shapes miner-validator interaction, and the evaluation method gives
validators a way to measure the result.
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)
describes these pieces as part of subnet design, while
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) explains why
subnet goals and standards differ.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from local testing to testchain and then mainchain. For a
subnet miner, that sequence changes how readers should interpret evidence about miner behavior and
performance.

In local testing, a subnet miner can show whether the work-producing role fits the subnet's task and
evaluation loop. That is useful for checking the design shape, but it remains isolated feedback from
a local environment.

On testchain, miner behavior can be observed in a shared testing network. This gives stronger
evidence about how miners respond to validator evaluation than a private local run, while still
keeping the result separate from production mainchain incentives.

On mainchain, subnet miners participate in the live subnet market. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes miners as producing a digital commodity and validators as measuring that work, so
production miner context is tied to live validator evaluation and incentive outcomes.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A miner result observed in one environment should not be
read as identical evidence about miner performance in another environment.

That distinction also keeps evaluation language scoped. A local miner response, a testchain result,
and a mainchain outcome are all miner evidence, but they answer different questions.

For readers, this keeps miner examples from sounding stronger than their environment supports.
Local, testchain, and mainchain contexts can all describe subnet miner activity, but they do not
carry the same interpretive weight.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

A subnet miner is usually discussed for one incentive mechanism at a time, but subnets can also run
multiple mechanisms in parallel. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that miner performance in one mechanism does not affect their rating in another, and the
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)
docs explain how multiple mechanisms can operate inside one subnet.

For readers, a subnet miner still performs work inside a subnet market, but the mechanism context
matters. Work valued in one mechanism does not automatically describe miner performance in another
when a subnet uses more than one evaluation system.

## Relationship to Netuid

A subnet miner and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor subnet vocabulary. A netuid
selects which subnet context is in scope, while a subnet miner names the task-performing role inside
that selected subnet. The
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level, and the
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) describes
the entity that performs tasks given by subnet validators.

For readers, netuid answers which subnet market is being discussed, while a subnet miner answers
which role performs work inside that subnet context.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner)

## Relationship to UID Slot

A subnet miner and a UID slot are related but different parts of subnet participation vocabulary. A
UID slot describes a participant position inside a subnet, while a subnet miner names the role a
registered participant performs inside that position. The
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) describes a
participant position, and the
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) describes
the task-performing role for that subnet.

For readers, a UID slot names the position identifier, while a subnet miner names the work-producing
role associated with a miner occupying that slot.

References: [Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner)

## Relationship to Miner Registration

A subnet miner and miner registration are related but different parts of Bittensor subnet
vocabulary. Miner registration describes the entry process that gives a participant a miner UID
slot, while a subnet miner names the task-performing role after that entry succeeds. The
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)
documentation describes miner entry into a subnet, and the
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) describes
the role that performs subnet tasks.

For readers, miner registration names how a miner enters a subnet, while a subnet miner names the
work role that entry makes possible.

References: [Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner)

## Relationship to Neuron

A subnet miner and a neuron are related but different parts of subnet participant vocabulary. A
neuron names a registered participant in subnet state, while a subnet miner names the
task-performing role that participant plays inside a subnet. The
[Glossary: Neuron](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#neuron) describes a registered
subnet participant, and the
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) describes
the entity that performs tasks given by subnet validators.

For readers, a neuron is the participant record term, while a subnet miner is the role term for work
production inside a subnet.

References: [Glossary: Neuron](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#neuron),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner)

## Relationship to Autostaking

Subnet miner and autostaking address related but different parts of Bittensor mining vocabulary. A
subnet miner is the task-performing role inside a subnet — the participant that responds to
validator requests, produces work for the subnet's task, and earns emission proceeds when validators
rank the miner favorably through Yuma Consensus — while autostaking is the feature that
automatically stakes a miner's emission proceeds to a chosen validator rather than requiring a
separate manual staking action each time emissions arrive. The
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) describes
the work-producing role that earns emissions through subnet evaluation, and the
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) documentation describes the
feature that routes those emission proceeds to a staking position automatically. Subnet miner names
the work role and the evaluation context that produces emission proceeds; autostaking names the
optional per-miner routing of those proceeds once earned, separate from the work-production role
itself.

References:
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)

## Relationship to Child Hotkeys

A subnet miner and a child hotkey sit on different sides of subnet participation. A subnet miner is
the task-performing role that produces the work validators measure, as the
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) defines it. A child
hotkey, by the [Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)
documentation, is a separate key that a parent hotkey authorizes to validate on its behalf by
re-delegating a portion of the parent's stake to it, without transferring ownership of that stake.

For readers, a subnet miner answers who produces the work in a subnet, while a child hotkey answers
which key carries out validation for a parent on the validator side. The two are not
interchangeable: a miner generates the output that gets scored, whereas a child hotkey is a
delegated validation key used by a validator's parent hotkey. A miner produces work to be evaluated;
a child hotkey helps perform the evaluation on a parent validator's behalf.

References:
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)

## Reader Boundary

Subnet miner should be read as a role concept, not as a procedure or claim about any particular
miner. The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) keeps the
term scoped to task-performing behavior, and
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) provides the
broader subnet context without requiring procedural detail in this article.

## Relationship to Coinbase

A subnet miner and coinbase are related but different parts of how subnet work turns into rewards. A
subnet miner is the role that performs the tasks a subnet asks validators to evaluate. Coinbase is
the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission, injects TAO into emitting subnets' pool
reserves, accumulates pending emissions, and checks epoch boundaries to trigger Yuma Consensus
rounds. The
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) defines
the task-performing role, and the
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes the per-block emission operation.

A subnet miner supplies the work, and coinbase distributes the rewards for that work. A miner's
share of emissions is not paid directly for each response; it is settled when coinbase triggers the
subnet's Yuma Consensus round at the epoch boundary and allocates miner incentives based on
validator evaluations. The miner produces the output that earns a score; coinbase is the mechanism
that converts the consensus built from those scores into the miner's emission outcome. One names who
does the work, while the other names the process that pays for it.
