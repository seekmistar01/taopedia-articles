# Senator

A senator is a delegate who participates in Bittensor Senate voting. Senator names the individual
voting role inside the Senate, while Senate names the collective governance body
([Glossary: Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#senate),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

## Role in the Senate

The Senate reviews proposals created by the Triumvirate. A senator is the delegate role on the
Senate side of that review flow ([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

This keeps three terms distinct: proposal names the governance item, Senate names the review body,
and senator names a delegate who can cast Senate votes.

## Delegate and Stake Context

Senators are delegates in Bittensor governance vocabulary. That makes senator a governance role
connected to the Senate, not a subnet miner role, validator-permit label, wallet-storage term, or
general validator recommendation ([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

The delegate context is important, but the article-level term stays narrow. Senator names the role
that votes in Senate review; detailed eligibility and membership records belong with governance
interfaces and Senate references.

## Voting Context

Senate voting is how senators act on proposals. Senators cast approval or disapproval votes on
Triumvirate proposals, and those votes contribute to the Senate review outcome
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

That does not make senator the same as proposal, proposal hash, or vote result. A proposal hash
identifies the governance item under review; senator names a voting delegate role
([Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal),
[Glossary: Proposal Hash](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal-hash)).

## Representation Context

Senator vocabulary can appear near delegation because senators are delegates. That representation
context should not be turned into a delegation recommendation, validator ranking, or voting
instruction ([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

The useful boundary is role-based. Senator names a governance voting delegate; it does not name a
subnet scoring mechanism or a wallet operation.

## Relationship to Senate

Senator and Senate are related but different parts of Bittensor governance vocabulary. The Senate is
the governance body that reviews Triumvirate proposals and votes to approve or reject them, while a
senator is the individual delegate role within that voting process
([Glossary: Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#senate),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

Every senator belongs to the Senate, but the Senate is the collective structure rather than a single
delegate. The same distinction keeps senator separate from Triumvirate, which names the
proposal-origin body.

## Reader Boundary

Senator should be read as governance-role vocabulary. It does not list seated senators, proposal
outcomes, delegation advice, voting instructions, or operational steps.

When the focus is the collective review body, Senate is more precise. When the focus is the
proposal-origin body, Triumvirate is more precise. When the focus is the governance item, proposal
is more precise.

## Development Stage Context

Bittensor documentation separates localnet, testnet, and mainnet development contexts
([Introduction to Bittensor: subnet development](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development),
[Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)). For senator
examples, that sequence changes how governance voting observations should be interpreted.

In localnet, senator and Senate governance mechanics can be tested in an isolated environment.
Localnet governance votes and proposal outcomes do not represent production governance decisions.

On testnet, senator activity can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet Senate
votes are separate from mainnet governance state and proposal outcomes.

On mainnet, senator references describe governance voting delegate behavior on the production
Bittensor network. A senator example from one environment should not be read as representing
governance authority or vote outcomes in another environment.
