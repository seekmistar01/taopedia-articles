# Recycling and Burning

Recycling and burning are Bittensor token-accounting terms for different kinds of removal from
ordinary circulation
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

The distinction matters because both terms describe removal, but they do not describe the same
supply outcome. Recycling keeps the amount available for later protocol distribution; burning leaves
the amount unavailable for future emission.

## Recycling Context

Recycled tokens are subtracted from issuance so they can be emitted again
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

Transaction fees are one official example. Fee documentation treats fees as recycled rather than
permanently removed from future emission
([Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

For readers, recycling is therefore a reuse concept. A recycled amount leaves ordinary circulation
for the moment, but the accounting treatment lets that amount return through protocol emission
later.

## Burning Context

Burned tokens remain in issuance while unavailable for future emission
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

Subnet stake burn is one context where the distinction is useful. The stake-burn documentation
describes TAO moving into a subnet reserve while corresponding alpha is removed from circulation and
burned ([Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)).

For readers, burning is therefore a removal concept. It is not the same as a temporary deduction,
ordinary transfer, or recycled fee flow.

## Issuance Boundary

Issuance gives recycling and burning their accounting meaning. The issuance glossary entry describes
TAO circulating in the network, including TAO held in subnet liquidity pools
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

Recycling affects emission accounting by making removed tokens re-emittable. Burning removes tokens
without making them available for later emission.

That makes issuance the shared accounting backdrop. Recycling changes how a deducted amount can
return to emission; burning explains why a removed amount no longer effectively circulates even
though issuance accounting still records it
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

## Mechanism Context

Removed-token statements should stay attached to the mechanism being described. Transaction fees,
subnet stake burn, and emission accounting can all involve removed tokens, but they do not imply the
same later-emission result
([Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

The useful question is not only whether tokens left ordinary circulation. It is whether the relevant
mechanism treats the removed amount as re-emittable or unavailable for future emission.

This keeps fee language, stake-burn language, and emission language from collapsing into one token
sink label. Each mechanism needs its own article when the reader needs the details.

## Network Context

Network labels tell readers where an observation belongs. Bittensor documentation separates mainnet,
testnet, and localnet contexts
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

A recycling or burning example should keep the selected network attached when it depends on observed
supply accounting. A figure from one environment should not be generalized across all Bittensor
environments.

## Reader Boundary

Recycling and burning should be read as conceptual supply-accounting terms
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

It does not by itself prove an exact amount, diagnose a specific transaction, or explain every
mechanism that can move TAO or alpha. Mechanism-specific statements need the relevant mechanism
article.

The stable article-level point is the accounting contrast: recycled amounts can return through
future emission, while burned amounts are unavailable for that future emission route
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).
