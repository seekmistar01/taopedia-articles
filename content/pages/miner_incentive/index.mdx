# Miner Incentive

A miner incentive is the emission share received by Bittensor miners for their work in a subnet. It
is the miner-facing side of subnet emission distribution
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The term is reward vocabulary. Mining is the process of performing subnet tasks; miner incentive is
the emission-side term for what miners receive when validators evaluate their work positively.

## Emission Context

Miner incentives are part of subnet emission distribution. Emission covers the full
creation-and-allocation process, while miner incentive names the specific role-facing share that
miners receive through that process ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

The incentive is distinct from validator dividends. Validators receive dividends based on their
consensus role and staked support position; miners receive incentives based on the quality of their
work as evaluated by validators.

## Subnet Context

Miner incentives are subnet-specific. Each subnet defines the task miners perform, and validators in
that subnet evaluate whether the work meets the subnet's quality standard
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

This keeps miner incentive from being a generic mining reward. The term is tied to a specific
subnet's evaluation context and its portion of the emission distribution process.

## Miner Rewards, Emission Distribution, and Yuma Scoring Pipeline

Miner incentives connect to the
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) process. Validators submit
weights that assess miner performance, and consensus aggregates those evaluations into incentive
outcomes for miners. An incentive follows from positive validator evaluation through consensus; it
does not replace the validator weight, miner rank, or trust vocabulary used inside the consensus
process.

[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)
documentation derives each miner's incentive share from an aggregate rank built inside the subnet.
That rank is the stake-weighted sum of consensus-clipped validator weights aimed at the miner, not
one validator's raw score copied forward unchanged. The
[Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank) states that ranks are
calculated from consensus-clipped weights and directly determine emissions to miners.

Miner-side allocation is a proportional split. Each miner's incentive portion equals that miner's
share of the total aggregate rank across miners in the subnet. Stronger relative rank therefore
means a larger slice of the miner-side emission pool for that epoch, while weak or filtered support
yields a smaller slice.
[Implementation of the Yuma Consensus Epoch](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/epoch)
documentation adds that final normalized ranks become the miner-side incentive shares before
emission amounts are applied across the subnet's miners.

[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) documentation
describes tempo-end distribution inside each subnet, where alpha accumulated during the tempo is
allocated to subnet participants after Yuma Consensus runs. Miner incentive belongs to that
subnet-side payout path rather than to a standalone TAO transfer label.
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation places subnet rewards
inside Bittensor's two-level emission model: network-level TAO flow supports reserve injection,
while subnets emit
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-tokens) for
role-facing rewards to miners, validators, and stakers. Miner incentive names the miner-facing slice
of that subnet alpha flow. Alpha distribution and TAO reserve injection are related parts of the
broader emissions process, but miner incentive language stays with the subnet alpha share assigned
to miners after Yuma Consensus determines miner-side outcomes.

[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)
documentation states that Yuma Consensus calculates emissions within each incentive mechanism and
that those results are finalized and credited to participants' hotkeys at the end of each
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo). The glossary defines
tempo as the subnet-specific interval over which Yuma Consensus calculates emissions from the latest
available ranking weight matrix. Miner incentive therefore arrives on that boundary schedule rather
than on a separate reward clock outside subnet epoch timing.
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the per-block process that advances emission processing and
triggers epoch-boundary activity. Validators may submit weights throughout the tempo, but the
miner-facing share that incentive vocabulary names is credited when epoch processing completes, not
at every intermediate submission.

For readers on a subnet such as netuid 1, the pipeline order is: validator weight submission and
consensus clipping, aggregate rank calculation, proportional miner-side share within the subnet
alpha pool, tempo-bound distribution after Yuma Consensus, and alpha credited to miner hotkeys.
Miner incentive vocabulary names the miner-facing outcome; Yuma vocabulary names the consensus
aggregation that produces it.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For miner
incentive on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret
emission-share and ranking examples.

In localnet, miner incentive flows can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet incentive
shares on netuid 1 reflect local chain configuration rather than production subnet rewards.

On testnet, miner ranking and emission distribution can be exercised in a shared non-production
network. Testnet incentive outcomes on a selected netuid are separate from mainnet subnet state
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

On mainnet, miner incentive describes live production reward shares on subnets such as netuid 1.
Observed amounts depend on the selected subnet's consensus and emission context
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A miner-incentive example from one environment should not
be read as representing production reward shares in another environment.

## Reader Boundary

Miner incentive is concept vocabulary for the miner's share of subnet emission. The term should not
be read as a mining profitability estimate, a subnet ranking, or an endorsement of a specific subnet
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

Actual incentive amounts depend on subnet context, emission configuration, validator evaluations,
and the selected environment. Mainnet, testnet, and localnet are separate contexts
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Miner incentive names the miner-facing emission outcome after Yuma Consensus resolves ranks. It does
not replace rank, weight, or trust vocabulary used inside the consensus process
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Relationship to Dividends

Miner incentive is the emission share miners receive for evaluated work in a subnet. Dividends are
the validator-side emission share tied to consensus role and bonded support
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Subnet emission distribution uses both role-facing outcomes after consensus runs. Miner incentive
names the miner reward side, while dividends name the validator reward side produced in the same
flow ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

Use miner incentive when covering miner emission outcomes on a netuid; use dividends when covering
validator emission outcomes rather than miner rewards
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

Miner-side and validator-side emission shares answer different reader questions in the same subnet
pass
([Glossary: Validator Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)).

## Relationship to Immunity Period

Miner incentive and the immunity period describe a new miner's early life as reward earned and
protection given. Miner incentive is the emission share a miner receives in proportion to its
evaluated work, while the immunity period is the block window that shields a newly registered miner
from deregistration regardless of how little incentive it earns at first, so the window buys time to
start earning before low incentive can cost the slot
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period)).

- **Miner incentive** — the emission share a miner earns for evaluated work.
- **Immunity period** — the grace window protecting a new miner before that earning begins.
