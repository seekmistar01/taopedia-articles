# Conviction and Locked Stake

Conviction and locked stake describe a Bittensor staking feature where subnet alpha stake can be
locked so that it builds a time-based commitment signal
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## What It Represents

Locked stake is subnet alpha constrained by a lock that limits how far the staked alpha balance on
that subnet can fall while the lock is active
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

Conviction is the score that grows from locked stake over time. In perpetual mode it rises quickly
at first and then levels off, approaching but never quite reaching the full locked mass
([Conviction score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#the-conviction-score)).

Decaying mode reduces the locked amount over time; perpetual mode keeps the locked amount from
decaying until the mode changes. Switching from perpetual back to decaying is recorded on chain and
starts the unwind path
([Decaying and perpetual modes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#decaying-and-perpetual-modes)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's conviction path. They should not be read as
representing production staking behavior on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Staking

Staking attaches alpha support inside a subnet. Locked stake applies a commitment constraint to
alpha that is already staked
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

- **Staking** — attaching alpha support inside a subnet.
- **Locked stake** — a commitment constraint on already-staked alpha.

## Distinction from Unstaking

Unstaking is the ordinary path that withdraws staked alpha. Locked stake is the floor that blocks
reductions below the locked amount until the lock unwinds
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

- **Unstaking** — the ordinary withdrawal of staked alpha.
- **Locked stake** — the floor that blocks withdrawal below the locked amount.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Stake weight names the influence measure derived from staked value. Conviction names the separate
time-based commitment signal produced by locking stake
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

- **Stake weight** — influence measure derived from staked value.
- **Conviction** — time-based commitment signal built from locked stake.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Validator take is the share of delegated-stake emissions a validator keeps. Conviction and locked
stake describe a staker locking alpha to build a public commitment signal
([Glossary: Validator Take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take),
[Conviction Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

- **Conviction and locked stake** — time-locked alpha with a commitment signal.
- **Validator take** — validator commission on delegated-stake emissions.

## Distinction from Conviction Score

Locked stake is the floor on subnet alpha that cannot be reduced below the locked amount. Conviction
score is the time-based signal built while that locked mass remains in place
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[The conviction score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#the-conviction-score)).

- **Locked stake** — minimum alpha mass that cannot be reduced below the locked floor.
- **Conviction score** — time-based signal built from locked commitment over time.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Conviction and locked stake name time-locked alpha staking that builds a public commitment signal.
Yuma Consensus converts validator weight submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Conviction and locked stake** — time-locked alpha with a commitment signal.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Conviction and locked stake are commitment vocabulary for subnet alpha, not a separate token, a
validator recommendation, or a reward forecast
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

## Distinction from Moving Stake

Conviction (locked) stake and moving stake pull a position in opposite directions. Conviction and
locked stake commits alpha for a fixed duration to earn extra voting weight, while moving stake
relocates a position between hotkeys or subnets — an action a locked position cannot take until its
lock expires
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Conviction and locked stake** — committing alpha for a duration for extra weight.
- **Moving stake** — relocating a position between hotkeys or subnets.
