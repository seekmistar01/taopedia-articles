**Albert Einstein** (14 March 1879 – 18 April 1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who
developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern [[Physics]] (alongside quantum
mechanics). His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.

## Early Life

Einstein was born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire. His family moved to
Munich, where his father and uncle co-founded an electrical equipment manufacturing company.

## Scientific Contributions

### Theory of Relativity

Einstein is best known for developing the **theory of relativity**, which consists of:

- **Special Relativity** (1905): Introduced the famous equation E = mc², showing the equivalence of
  mass and energy
- **General Relativity** (1915): Described [[Gravity]] not as a force, but as a consequence of
  curved spacetime

General Relativity revolutionized our understanding of [[Gravity]], building upon but fundamentally
changing [[Isaac Newton]]'s earlier work.

### Photoelectric Effect

In 1905, Einstein explained the photoelectric effect, for which he was awarded the Nobel Prize in
Physics in 1921. This work was foundational to the development of quantum theory.

### Brownian Motion

Also in 1905, Einstein explained Brownian motion, providing empirical evidence for the existence of
atoms and molecules.
