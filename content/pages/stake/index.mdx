# Stake

Stake is the amount of currency tokens associated with a validator position in a Bittensor subnet
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).
It is amount vocabulary, not the action vocabulary of staking and not the downstream weighting
vocabulary of stake weight.

The term should stay attached to its validator and subnet context. A stake amount by itself is not a
wallet balance, validator ranking, or network-wide performance signal.

## Validator Position Amount

Stake is read in relation to validator support. A TAO holder can stake to a validator, and a
validator's total stake can include both the validator's own stake and stake delegated by others
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

That combined view matters when reading Bittensor consensus and emission material. Stake names the
support amount associated with the validator position; it does not say who supplied every portion
unless the surrounding context identifies self-stake or delegated support.

This makes stake broader than delegate stake but narrower than every economic fact about an account.
Delegate stake identifies the validator's own supplied portion, while nominated or delegated support
can add to the validator-associated stake context
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

The amount should therefore be read with the relationship attached. If the question is who supplied
the support, nominator and delegate-stake vocabulary are more precise. If the question is the
validator-associated amount, stake is the broader term.

## Custody Boundary

Stake describes token support attached to a validator relationship, not a separate key-custody
system. Wallet documentation explains where coldkey and hotkey material is managed, while staking
documentation explains how token support is attached to validators
([Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

That boundary keeps stake from being mistaken for spending authority. A statement that tokens are
staked identifies their validator-support context; it does not by itself identify where the wallet
keys are stored or who can sign unrelated wallet actions.

## Subnet Context

Stake is local to subnet state. Staking into subnets places stake in that subnet's alpha-token
context, and subnet references use validator stake weight when explaining validator influence inside
a subnet
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets),
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

This locality keeps stake from becoming one universal account number. A precise statement about
stake should identify the subnet, validator, and asset context being discussed.

The asset context matters because subnet staking can be discussed through alpha-token vocabulary
while Root-side staking is discussed through TAO staked through Root. A static article should not
collapse those contexts into one undifferentiated amount
([Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)).

For source reading, the relevant question is which staking path is being discussed. A subnet-local
stake statement, a Root Subnet staking statement, and a wallet-balance statement are related but not
interchangeable.

## Stake Weight and Delegation

Stake and stake weight are related but not identical. Stake names the validator-associated amount,
while stake weight is the interpreted measure used for validator consensus power and emissions
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

Delegation is the process that can attach holder support to a validator. Stake is the resulting
amount that can include both self-supplied and delegated support
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

Effective stake is another nearby amount term. It summarizes total staked TAO associated with a
delegate after the delegate's own stake and nominator-delegated stake are counted
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

Those distinctions keep a useful order: delegation describes how support is attached, stake
describes the validator-associated amount, effective stake summarizes the delegate-associated total,
and stake weight describes the consensus-power interpretation.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. Stake examples on
a subnet such as netuid 1 from one environment should not be treated as evidence for another because
validator sets, balances, and subnet staking state are environment-specific
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

In localnet, stake examples can test staking mechanics in isolation. A validator stake total on
netuid 1 in localnet reflects local configuration rather than production delegated support.

On testnet, stake can be observed in a shared non-production network. Testnet stake readings on a
selected netuid are separate from mainnet validator relationships.

On mainnet, stake interpretation concerns production validator relationships on the active network.
The stake recorded behind a validator on netuid 1 on mainnet is the live delegated-support figure
that feeds stake weight in later consensus passes.

## Staking Attaches Support Before Stake Is Counted

[Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking) names the process of attaching
TAO support to a validator, while stake names the amount associated with that supported validator
position after support is attached
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

For readers, staking is the action vocabulary and stake is the resulting amount vocabulary. A TAO
holder performs staking to support a validator; the validator's stake is the measured token amount
that includes both own and delegated support. The terms belong to the same domain but name process
versus amount.

On a subnet such as netuid 1, subnet staking routes TAO through the paired reserve pool before alpha
stake is recorded behind the validator hotkey
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Delegation Feeds Validator Stake Totals

Delegation names the support relationship by which nominated TAO becomes attached to a validator
hotkey, while stake names the measured token amount associated with that position after support
exists ([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

A validator's stake can include delegated support, but delegation names how that support is attached
rather than the resulting quantity alone. A delegation action does not by itself report the full
stake total behind a hotkey, because stake counts the combined support figure after delegation and
self-stake are recorded together
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

Relationship vocabulary and amount vocabulary stay separate so readers can tell whether prose is
describing support attachment or the measured stake behind a validator
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

## Stake Weight Feeds Yuma Validator Signals

[Stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) interprets the
validator-associated stake amount for consensus power inside a subnet. At tempo boundaries,
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) uses those stake-weighted
validator signals when turning weight submissions into miner
[incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) and validator
[dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission).

Stake therefore sits upstream of Yuma's allocation step. Delegation and staking change the measured
stake behind a hotkey; stake weight translates that amount into validator influence; Yuma converts
the resulting weight signals into role-facing outcomes when distribution runs
([Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

## Reader Boundary

Stake is amount vocabulary for validator-associated support. It is not wallet custody, a validator
ranking, or stake weight itself
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

A stake amount should stay attached to its validator and subnet context.

## Relationship to Validator Permit

Stake and the validator permit sit on the same validation-rights path but name different things.
Stake, interpreted as stake weight, is the validator-associated amount that ranks a neuron's
consensus influence, while the validator permit is the binary flag awarded to the top neurons by
stake weight that records whether they may submit counting validator weights
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Stake / stake weight** — the validator-associated amount and its consensus weighting.
- **Validator permit** — the binary right to submit counting weights that top-stake neurons earn.
