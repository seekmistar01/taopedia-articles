# Recycling and Burning

Recycling and burning are Bittensor tokenomics terms for tokens that leave ordinary spendable
circulation. The
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
entry defines both terms for TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens, and the distinction depends on
how the chain accounts for issuance after the tokens are removed.

## Recycling

When tokens are recycled, they are subtracted from the chain's issuance record so the same quantity
can be emitted again later. In reader terms, recycling is not just hiding tokens in an unreachable
wallet; it changes the supply accounting used by Bittensor's emission system.

Transaction fees are a common context for recycling. The official
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees) page states that
fee-bearing actions charge weight and length components from the sender's TAO free balance and
recycle those fees by deducting them from `TotalIssuance`.

References:
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning),
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)

## Burning

Burning also removes tokens from wallets and pools, but the accounting effect is different. The
official glossary describes burned tokens as no longer transactable while still remaining in the
issuance record. Because they are not returned for future emission, burning creates a gap between
the issuance record and the effective amount that can circulate.

Subnet stake burn is one Bittensor example. The official
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn) page describes a mechanism
where a subnet owner sends TAO into a subnet reserve while corresponding alpha is removed from
circulation and burned.

References:
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)

## Issuance Context

The distinction matters because issuance is the supply record that tokenomics claims often depend
on. The [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) entry
describes issuance as TAO circulating in the network, including TAO held in wallets and subnet
liquidity pools. Recycling affects that issuance record directly, while burning removes usable
tokens without making that quantity available for future emission.

This keeps a recycling claim separate from a burning claim. Both can reduce tokens available for
immediate use, but they do not imply the same future-emission behavior.

References: [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Reading Supply Claims

A Bittensor supply statement should say whether it is discussing TAO, subnet alpha, recycled
amounts, burned amounts, or circulating issuance. Exact effects depend on the mechanism being
described: transaction fees, subnet stake burn, and emission accounting use related words, but they
are not interchangeable.

References: [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Burning

Within recycling and burning, burning removes tokens from transactable circulation without returning
them for future emission. The
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning) describes removal
from spendable circulation, while the
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
groups the two supply-side accounting outcomes together.

For readers, burning leaves tokens in the issuance record but outside wallets and pools, while
recycling subtracts tokens from that record so the same quantity can be emitted again.

References: [Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Recycling and burning describe how removed TAO and alpha tokens are accounted for in issuance. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, recycling and burning still name network tokenomics sinks. Subnet incentive mechanisms
distribute emissions among participants, but these supply terms describe issuance accounting rather
than per-mechanism miner evaluation.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)
