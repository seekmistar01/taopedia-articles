# Unstaking

Unstaking describes removing staked TAO from a staking position. Bittensor staking and delegation
material uses the term when explaining how TAO can be withdrawn from validator support and returned
to the coldkey ([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Staking and Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

It is not the same as moving stake to a different hotkey or subnet, and it is not reducing a
conviction lock while keeping the position active
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

## What It Represents

Unstaking ends the staking relationship and frees TAO for other use. On alpha subnets, withdrawal
passes through subnet pool reserves, converting subnet-specific alpha back toward TAO rather than a
fixed one-for-one exchange
([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

On netuid 1, examples illustrate one subnet's withdrawal path. They should not be read as
representing production pool conversion behavior on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Moving Stake

Unstaking removes support from delegation and returns TAO to the coldkey. Moving stake repoints that
position between hotkeys and/or subnets in one atomic step without first withdrawing to the coldkey
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

- **Unstaking** — exit delegated support and return TAO to the coldkey.
- **Moving stake** — keep support delegated under a new hotkey or subnet target.

## Distinction from Slippage

Unstaking withdraws from a validator hotkey and converts subnet-specific alpha back to TAO through
the subnet automated market maker. Slippage names the gap between expected and received amounts when
that reserve-based conversion runs
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Glossary: Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#slippage),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Unstaking** — withdrawal action that enters the conversion path.
- **Slippage** — gap between expected and received conversion amounts.

## Distinction from Price Protection

Price protection is the tolerance mechanism that can govern how an unstaking conversion proceeds
once pool movement is measured. Unstaking is the withdrawal action that enters that conversion path
([Price protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

- **Unstaking** — withdrawal step on the selected subnet.
- **Price protection** — tolerance mechanism after pool movement is measured.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking attaches TAO support to a validator hotkey. Unstaking removes that support from the
delegated position on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

- **Staking** — add support behind a validator.
- **Unstaking** — remove support through the documented withdrawal path.

## Distinction from Transfer

Unstaking removes support from a validator and returns TAO to the owner's own balance. Transfer
sends TAO from one wallet address to another
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Glossary: Transfer](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#transfer)).

- **Unstaking** — withdraw staked support back to the owner's balance.
- **Transfer** — send TAO from one wallet address to another.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Autostaking routes miner emission proceeds into a staking position over time. Unstaking is the exit
path that removes from an accumulated delegated position through the ordinary withdrawal process
([Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

- **Autostaking** — accumulation mechanism that adds to a staked position.
- **Unstaking** — exit mechanism that removes from that position.

## Distinction from Nominate

Nominate is the action that attaches TAO support to a validator hotkey. Unstaking is the withdrawal
action that removes that support from the delegated relationship on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

- **Nominate** — attach TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Unstaking** — remove that support from the delegated relationship.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Unstaking is the operation that removes staked TAO from a validator and returns it to the coldkey.
Effective stake is that validator's combined self-and-delegated total, so unstaking lowers the
effective stake of the validator the TAO leaves
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Unstaking** — withdrawal operation that reduces delegated support.
- **Effective stake** — combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Unstaking names the action of withdrawing TAO from a validator delegation position back toward a
coldkey. It describes a staking-market movement, not the on-chain mechanism that converts validator
weight submissions into emission shares
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on a subnet. It reads the
weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result into
miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Unstaking changes how much stake backs a validator over time; Yuma settles weight signals from
validators who remain eligible at the epoch boundary. Withdrawal timing and dividend settlement
answer different questions on the same subnet
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

Readers should treat unstaking as a delegator action that moves TAO out of a staking position, and
Yuma as the mechanism that allocates rewards from consensus while stake remains committed.

- **Unstaking** — withdrawing TAO from a validator delegation position.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the term. It does not report balances, timing, fees, validator choices, or a
specific staking action
([Staking and Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

## Distinction from Transferring Stake

Unstaking and transferring stake both touch an existing stake position but with different outcomes.
Unstaking withdraws a position, converting subnet alpha back into free TAO balance, while
transferring stake reassigns an existing staked position to another coldkey without unwinding it
into liquid TAO
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

- **Unstaking** — withdrawing a position back into free TAO balance.
- **Transferring stake** — reassigning a staked position to another coldkey intact.
