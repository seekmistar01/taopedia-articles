# Halving

Halving is a tokenomics term for a reduction in token emission rate. In Bittensor, it belongs to
emission mechanics because it changes how quickly new tokens enter the reward-distribution path
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

## What It Represents

Bittensor halving is tied to issuance thresholds rather than fixed block heights. The halving
mechanism reduces emission rate when the relevant supply threshold is reached
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

TAO halving belongs to the network-token side. Alpha halving belongs to subnet alpha-token emission
on a named netuid. The shared word does not erase the asset distinction
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Recycled TAO can shift when the next supply milestone is reached without triggering a halving by
itself ([Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling)).

Halving examples depend on which network you connect to. Supply schedules from one environment
should not be read as production behavior on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Emission

Emission names the ongoing creation-and-allocation process. Halving identifies the supply-triggered
rate reduction in that schedule ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)).

- **Halving** — supply-triggered cut to the emission rate.
- **Emission** — ongoing injection and distribution flow.

## Distinction from Issuance

Issuance is the circulating TAO total at a moment. Halving is the threshold event that slows future
minting as issuance climbs
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

- **Issuance** — circulating TAO supply measure.
- **Halving** — rate cut triggered at supply thresholds.

## Distinction from Block Reward

Block reward names new TAO minted each block for the whole network. Halving steps that per-block
mint rate down at supply milestones ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

- **Block reward** — new TAO minted each block network-wide.
- **Halving** — supply event that lowers the per-block mint rate.

## Distinction from Coinbase

Coinbase is the per-block process that advances emission accounting. Halving changes how much new
TAO that step mints after a threshold is reached
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

- **Coinbase** — per-block emission processing step.
- **Halving** — supply threshold that lowers the mint rate.

## Distinction from Maximum Supply

Maximum supply names the 21 million TAO lifetime ceiling. Halving slows how quickly new TAO
approaches that ceiling without changing the cap itself
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

- **Maximum supply** — protocol lifetime ceiling on total TAO issuance.
- **Halving** — scheduled rate cut as issuance crosses supply thresholds.

## Distinction from Recycling

Halving cuts the per-block mint rate at supply thresholds. Recycling returns deducted TAO toward
future emission, which can delay when the next threshold is reached
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling)).

- **Halving** — supply-threshold event that reduces the per-block mint rate.
- **Recycling** — protocol reuse of deducted TAO toward future emission.

## Distinction from Alpha Halving

TAO halving responds to network issuance milestones. Alpha halving reduces a subnet alpha token's
emission rate after that subnet's alpha issuance threshold
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Alpha Halvings](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving#alpha-halvings)).

- **Halving** — network TAO or general halving vocabulary at supply thresholds.
- **Alpha halving** — subnet-scoped cut to that subnet's alpha emission rate.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Halving names a supply-triggered emission-rate cut. Yuma Consensus converts validator weight
submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Halving** — scheduled reduction in emission rate at supply thresholds.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Halving is supply-schedule vocabulary. It is not a date forecast, price forecast, staking strategy,
or complete reward model ([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).
