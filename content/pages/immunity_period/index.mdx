# Immunity Period

An immunity period is a temporary protection window after a miner or validator enters a Bittensor
subnet. The glossary defines it as the period during which new subnet entrants are protected from
deregistration for poor performance
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

In plain language, immunity period answers: for how long after registration is a new subnet entrant
protected from being replaced by a stronger candidate? During that window, low performance does not
trigger automatic deregistration
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

For a reader, immunity period is not a grace period for reward collection. It protects against
removal, not against low emission outcomes. A new entrant with poor performance during immunity
still earns according to their subnet results; the protection is specifically against the
replacement mechanism that removes low performers to free up UID slots
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term is a timing concept, not a performance score. It says that ordinary low-performance removal
is temporarily limited for a new subnet entrant; it does not say the entrant has already produced
strong results.

## Entry Grace Window

The miner documentation describes the immunity period as a number of blocks that begins when a miner
or validator enters a subnet
([Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period)).

That makes the subnet-entry event part of the meaning. The window exists because a new entrant needs
time to begin contributing before ordinary removal rules based on poor performance can apply.

## Block Measurement

Immunity period is measured in blocks. The glossary gives the default as 4096 blocks, and the subnet
hyperparameter reference lists `ImmunityPeriod` as the subnet setting for the protection window
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

Readers should treat the block count as timing context. A block-based window advances with chain
progression, so wall-clock interpretation depends on the surrounding network and block context.

## Deregistration Boundary

Immunity period is closely tied to ordinary entrant deregistration. Miner documentation explains
that, when a subnet is full and a new subnet entry arrives, the lowest-emission eligible entrant can
be replaced; an entrant still inside the immunity period is protected from that ordinary removal
path ([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

## Eligibility for Removal Does Not Depend on Role

The deregistration check behind immunity period does not treat miners and validators as separate
pools. Official mining documentation states that the subnet does not distinguish between miners and
validators for the purpose of deregistration; the chain looks only at emissions, represented as
pruning score, when deciding which non-immune entrant to replace
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

That means immunity period protects an entrant from the same emissions-based replacement check
regardless of which role it occupies. A newly registered validator and a newly registered miner on
the same subnet are shielded by the identical immunity rule, not by separate role-specific
protections.

## What It Does Not Prove

Immunity period does not prove quality, emission rank, or long-term subnet participation. It is a
temporary block-count boundary. Performance, weights, and emissions are still interpreted through
the subnet's evaluation and reward mechanisms after subnet entry
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

That boundary keeps the term from being overread. An entrant can be protected from ordinary
low-performance removal while still needing to earn its later standing under the subnet's normal
rules.

## Evidence Boundary

The immunity-period references support claims about temporary post-entry protection. They do not by
themselves prove an entrant's current status, diagnose why an entrant was removed, or state the
active window for every subnet without the selected subnet context.

For readers, the safe use of immunity period is as a reference label for a block-measured grace
window after subnet entry
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

## Registration Timing Boundary

The immunity period should be read as a timing context after subnet registration, not as permanent
protection. Registration documentation explains how a participant obtains a UID slot, while subnet
hyperparameters describe activity and timing controls that shape subnet participation
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

For readers, immunity-period claims should keep the relevant subnet and block context attached. The
concept describes an early participation window, not a guarantee that the participant remains active
or protected indefinitely.

## Immunity Delays Miner Deregistration

Miner deregistration documentation explains that a participant can be removed when a subnet is full
and a new registration displaces the lowest-scoring incumbent, but immunity period can delay that
displacement for a newly registered UID
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period)).

For readers, immunity period is a timing shield on the registration block axis, not a performance
waiver. Once the configured block window ends, normal deregistration rules can apply again.

## Each Subnet Sets Its Own Immunity Length

The
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)
entry lists immunity period as a subnet-configurable value measured in blocks after registration.
That makes the window subnet-specific rather than a single network-wide constant.

A precise immunity-period claim should name the subnet whose hyperparameters are being cited. The
same term can describe different block counts on different subnets.

## Block Window Scope

Immunity period should be read as a block-counted grace window measured from the registration block,
not as an uptime guarantee, a performance window, or a stake-quality threshold. The mining
documentation and the subnet hyperparameters entry both describe immunity period as a number of
blocks after registration, with the formula comparing the current block against the registration
block ([Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

For readers, the relevant boundary is the unit and the starting point. The window is measured in
blocks, not in time, and it begins at the registration block of a UID rather than at any quality,
stake, or activity event. A precise claim should keep the block-counted grace window separate from
the surrounding performance, deregistration, and subnet configuration vocabulary.

## Registration, Immunity Blocks, and Deregistration on a Subnet

On a subnet such as netuid 1, a miner or validator obtains a UID through
[registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration), then enters an
[immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period) measured in
blocks from that registration block
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

While the window is active, ordinary low-performance
[deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration) displacement is
delayed even if emissions remain low. Once the configured block count expires on netuid 1, the same
replacement rules can apply to eligible incumbents when the subnet is full and a new entrant
arrives.

Immunity period therefore names timing protection after entry, not reward quality or validator
consensus outcomes.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For immunity
period on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how registration and deregistration
examples should be read.

In localnet, immunity period on netuid 1 can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet block
windows reflect that test chain's hyperparameter state rather than production subnet lifecycle
behavior.

On testnet, immunity period on netuid 1 applies in a shared non-production network where
registration blocks and configured windows are separate from mainnet.

On mainnet, immunity period on netuid 1 concerns live production subnet entry protection and later
deregistration timing on the selected subnet
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Immunity-period examples from one environment should not
be read as subnet lifecycle behavior on another network.

## Reader Boundary

Immunity period should not be read as permanent protection, validator immunity, or proof that a UID
remains active. It names a block-counted grace window after subnet registration
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period)).

## Relationship to Subnet Creator

The immunity period and the subnet creator meet at configuration. The immunity period is an
owner-settable hyperparameter that fixes how long a new UID is protected from deregistration, while
the subnet creator is the role that defines a subnet's task and evaluation and sets its
hyperparameters
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

Because the immunity period is one of those owner-settable hyperparameters, the subnet creator
chooses the immunity window its subnet enforces, so the protection length is a per-subnet setting at
the creator's discretion rather than a fixed network constant
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

## Relationship to Active UID

Immunity period and active UID both describe a UID's standing on a subnet but answer different
questions. The immunity period is the block window that exempts a newly registered UID from
deregistration regardless of its score, while an active UID is the participant slot that currently
counts as usable for validating or mining
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Glossary: Active UID](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#active-uid)).

The two also differ in duration. The immunity period is a fixed, block-counted window that expires
after a set number of blocks, after which ordinary deregistration rules resume; active-UID status is
not bounded that way, since it is the current usable standing that can change as participation and
scores change
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

## Relationship to Register

Immunity period is the block-measured protection window after a hotkey enters a subnet. Register is
the subnet-entry action that grants the UID slot where that protection begins
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

Readers tracing post-entry timing need both terms on the same netuid. Register explains how the
entrant obtained a slot, while immunity period explains how long ordinary deregistration protection
lasts after that entry
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

Use register when covering subnet admission; use immunity period when covering the protection window
that starts after admission rather than the entry step itself
([Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Entry action and post-entry protection window answer different reader questions on the same netuid
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

## Relationship to Pruning Score

Immunity period and pruning score act on the same deregistration decision from two sides. Pruning
score is the per-neuron value that ranks which UID is removed first when a subnet is full and a new
registration arrives, while immunity period is the block-measured window that exempts a newly
registered UID from that removal
([Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

During the immunity window, a low pruning score does not trigger displacement. A new entrant can
hold the lowest score on the subnet and still be protected until the configured block window ends,
after which the pruning-score ranking applies again
([Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

Use pruning score when covering which neuron is selected for removal; use immunity period when
covering the grace window that temporarily suspends that selection for a new entrant
([Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Removal ranking and removal-protection window answer different reader questions on the same
deregistration path
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).
