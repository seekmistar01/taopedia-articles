# Hotkey Swap

A hotkey swap is a Bittensor key-rotation operation that moves subnet participation from an old
hotkey to a new hotkey owned by the same coldkey
([Coldkey and Hotkey Workstation Security](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-hotkey-security),
[Glossary: Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey)).

## What It Represents

Hotkey swap is the response when an operational hotkey may have leaked or should be retired. The
coldkey owner creates a replacement hotkey under the same coldkey and moves subnet registration,
including delegated stake, to that fresh key
([Coldkey and Hotkey Workstation Security](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-hotkey-security)).

Subtensor supports all-subnet and per-subnet swap paths. The chosen scope determines which subnet
roles rehome to the replacement hotkey
([Rate Limits](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/chain-rate-limits)).

Hotkey swap examples from one network environment should not be read as representing production
registration state on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Hotkeys

A hotkey is the operational key that holds subnet participation. Hotkey swap is the on-chain
rotation that moves that participation to a replacement hotkey
([Glossary: Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey)).

- **Hotkey** — operational key that signs routine subnet work.
- **Hotkey swap** — rotation that rehomes subnet participation to a new hotkey.

## Distinction from Coldkey Swap

Coldkey swap migrates the full on-chain identity to a new coldkey while preserving hotkey
registrations. Hotkey swap keeps the same coldkey and replaces only the operational hotkey
([Coldkey Swap](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap)).

- **Hotkey swap** — operational-key rotation under the unchanged coldkey.
- **Coldkey swap** — ownership migration to a new coldkey.

## Distinction from Coldkeys

The coldkey authorizes hotkey swap but is not replaced by it. Coldkeys control balances, stake
authority, and hotkey management
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey)).

- **Coldkey** — custody anchor that authorizes the swap.
- **Hotkey swap** — operation that changes which hotkey holds the subnet role.

## Distinction from Register

Register is subnet entry that purchases a UID slot for a hotkey. Hotkey swap moves an existing
registration without treating the participant as a new entrant
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Register** — subnet entry that assigns a UID slot.
- **Hotkey swap** — rotation of an already registered operational key.

## Distinction from Encrypting the Hotkey

Encrypting the hotkey protects the keyfile at rest with a password. Hotkey swap replaces the
operational key on chain when the current hotkey should no longer be used
([Glossary: Encrypting the Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#encrypting-the-hotkey)).

- **Encrypting the hotkey** — at-rest file protection.
- **Hotkey swap** — on-chain rotation to a replacement hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation stakes TAO toward a validator hotkey. Hotkey swap rotates the operational key that may
receive that delegated stake
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

- **Hotkey swap** — coldkey-authorized rotation of the operational key.
- **Delegation** — staker support attached to a validator hotkey.

## Distinction from Rate Limits

Hotkey swaps are subject to transaction rate limits and a per-subnet swap interval documented in
rate limit guidance, plus a recycling fee
([Rate Limits](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/chain-rate-limits)).

- **Hotkey swap** — deliberate recovery or rotation action.
- **Rate limits** — documented limits on how often swaps can be submitted.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Hotkey swap replaces the operational signing key. Yuma Consensus converts submitted validator
weights into emission shares after evaluation completes
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Hotkey swap** — rotation of subnet participation to a new hotkey under the same coldkey.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Hotkey swap should not be read as coldkey swap, a transfer of ownership, or a reset of subnet
performance history. It names operational hotkey rotation under the same coldkey
([Coldkey and Hotkey Workstation Security](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-hotkey-security)).
