# Root Claims

Root claims are the Bittensor mechanism for handling alpha dividends earned through stake placed on
validators in the Root Subnet, also called Subnet Zero. The official Root Claim overview describes
two outcomes: dividends can be converted to TAO and restaked on root, or they can remain as the
subnet alpha that generated them.

References:
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Why Root Claims Exist

The Root Subnet is different from ordinary subnets. The official subnet overview describes it as a
special subnet without its own alpha currency, where validators can register and TAO holders can
stake in a subnet-agnostic way. Root claims provide the dividend-handling layer for that structure:
they decide how alpha dividends from active subnets are treated when the stake is on root.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Swap and Keep Modes

The Root Claim overview names two modes. In swap mode, alpha dividends are converted to TAO and
restaked on the Root Subnet. In keep mode, the earned alpha remains on the subnet that produced it.
The initial TAO stake remains on root in both cases; the distinction is about the dividend output,
not about moving the original stake away from root.

Reference: [Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Automatic and Manual Claims

Root claims can be processed automatically by the chain or triggered manually. The same selected
mode applies in either path, so the core concept is not the trigger method; it is how the resulting
alpha dividends are handled after the claim is processed. This keeps root-claim behavior separate
from ordinary subnet staking decisions.

Reference: [Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Relationship to Flow-Based Emissions

The official Root Claim overview notes that claim swaps in swap mode do not count as TAO outflows
for subnet flow-based emission calculations. This matters because flow-based emissions evaluate
subnet inflows and outflows, while root-claim swaps are a dividend-handling process. Readers should
avoid treating every alpha-to-TAO conversion as the same kind of flow event.

References:
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to Root Subnet

Root claims and the Root Subnet address related but different parts of Bittensor staking context.
The [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) overview
describes the Root Subnet as a special subnet where validators register and TAO holders can stake in
a subnet-agnostic way, while the
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
how alpha dividends from that stake are handled.

For readers, the Root Subnet is the staking environment, while root claims name the
dividend-handling mechanism for stake placed there.

References:
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Root Claim Type

Root claims and root claim type are related but distinct parts of Bittensor staking vocabulary. Root
claims name the mechanism that processes accumulated alpha dividends for stake placed on Root Subnet
validators, while root claim type names the per-staker dividend-handling preference that is applied
inside each claim. The
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) names two
claim-type options — Keep, where dividends remain as the alpha of the originating subnet, and Swap,
where dividends are converted to TAO and restaked on Root — and notes that this setting applies to
every claim, whether automatic or manual.

For readers, root claims name the act of processing accumulated dividends, while root claim type
names which of the two dividend outcomes (Keep or Swap) the staker has chosen for that processing.
The claim is the event; the claim type is the setting that determines what the event produces.

References:
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Relationship to Halving

Root claims and halving are related but distinct parts of Bittensor tokenomics. Root claims are the
per-staker dividend-handling mechanism that processes accumulated alpha dividends for stake placed
on Root Subnet validators, while halving is the supply-threshold mechanism that progressively
reduces new token emission rates. The
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) names the
two claim modes (Swap and Keep) that determine what happens to each dividend, and the
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) entry describes
halvings as supply-threshold events that cut emission rates at both the network scope (TAO halving)
and the subnet scope (alpha halving).

For readers, root claims name the dividend-treatment layer applied to alpha dividends as they
arrive, while halving names the long-run supply rule that shapes how many alpha dividends the
network produces over time. The two are not interchangeable: a halving changes the rate at which new
alpha dividends are produced, while root claims change how the produced dividends are routed between
Keep and Swap. A halving can affect how much dividend flows through root claims; root claims do not
affect when halvings happen.

References:
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)

## Careful Interpretation

Root claims are not a general staking tutorial or a separate subnet identity. They are a specific
mechanism for dividend treatment when stake is placed through the Root Subnet. Claims about exact
thresholds, current settings, or operational steps should be checked against the official Bittensor
documentation because those details can change.

References:
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Coinbase

Root claims and coinbase are related but different parts of how dividends arise and are then
handled. Root claims are the mechanism that decides how alpha dividends from Root Subnet staking are
treated — kept as alpha or swapped to TAO. The
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission,
accumulates pending emissions, and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch boundaries, while the
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
the dividend-handling mechanism.

The two sit at different stages. Coinbase is part of the upstream emission process that produces the
dividends in the first place, while root claims are a downstream handling layer that routes the
alpha dividends already earned through root stake. Coinbase answers how emissions are generated and
advanced each epoch; root claims answer what happens to the resulting alpha dividends — Keep or Swap
— once they have arrived. One is the emission engine, the other is a per-staker treatment choice
applied after dividends exist.
