# Chain Reads

Chain reads are a Bittensor information-access concept. They describe looking at public state on
Subtensor without asking the chain to record a new action. In plain terms, a chain read is
observation rather than authorization.

The official [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees) page says
that reading chain state is free. That statement is the key boundary for Taopedia readers: looking
at public chain information is different from submitting a state-changing action that the chain must
process.

Reference: [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)

## Why the Boundary Matters

The read-versus-submit boundary helps separate ordinary public inspection from actions that need
wallet authority. A person can look at many public facts about the chain without moving funds,
changing stake, registering anything, or changing a subnet participant's on-chain position.

This does not make chain data private or risk-free to interpret. It only means the read itself is
not the same as giving permission for the chain to do something new.

Reference: [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)

## Chain Inspection Context

The official
[Inspecting the Chain with Polkadot.js](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/inspecting-the-chain)
page frames chain inspection as a way to look at Bittensor chain state, metadata, blocks, and other
public records. It also separates inspection from signing flows.

Chain reads fit inside that inspection side. They are useful when a reader wants to understand what
the chain currently records before deciding whether any separate action is needed.

References:
[Inspecting the Chain with Polkadot.js](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/inspecting-the-chain),
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)

## Fee Context

Because the official fee page treats reading chain state as free, chain reads should not be
described as fee-bearing transactions. Fees belong to the action side of the network when a
submitted operation asks Subtensor to process a change.

This distinction is especially helpful in articles about wallet safety, staking, registration,
transfers, and validator activity. Those topics often involve both observation and action, and the
two should not be collapsed into one category.

Reference: [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)

## Network Context

A chain read belongs to the Bittensor network being inspected. The
[Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) page separates
mainnet, testnet, and localnet contexts. A fact observed on one network should not automatically be
treated as the same fact on another network.

For readers, this means a chain read should be interpreted with its network context in mind. A
mainnet observation, a testnet observation, and a localnet observation can all be read-only, but
they are not interchangeable chain records.

Reference: [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)

## Reading Boundary

Chain reads explain how public observation differs from submitted action. They do not name a
specific wallet app, procedure, or live chain value. Exact values can change as blocks are produced
and as the inspected network changes.

This makes the concept useful as a safety and interpretation boundary. Reading first can help a
person understand the current public record, but any later state-changing step should still be
treated as a separate action with its own authority and fee context.

## Relationship to Subtensor

Chain reads and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while chain reads name observation of
public state on that chain without asking it to record a new action. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees) documentation states
that reading chain state is free.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while chain reads name passive observation of public
state on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)

## Relationship to Block

Chain reads and a block are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. A block names
one unit of chain progression on Subtensor, while chain reads name looking at public chain state
such as blocks and other records without submitting a new action. The
[Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block) describes a block as a
unit of chain data, and the
[Inspecting the Chain with Polkadot.js](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/inspecting-the-chain)
documentation frames chain inspection as a way to look at blocks and other public records.

For readers, a block is one kind of public chain record, while chain reads name the observation
activity that can inspect such records without changing state.

References: [Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block),
[Inspecting the Chain with Polkadot.js](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/inspecting-the-chain)

## Relationship to Batch Transactions

Chain reads and batch transactions are related but different parts of Bittensor chain action
vocabulary. Chain reads name observation of public chain state without changing it, while batch
transactions name grouped submission of multiple state-changing actions inside one on-chain
transaction. The [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)
documentation contrasts free chain reads with state-changing actions, and the
[Batch Transactions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/batch-transactions) documentation
describes grouped submission of multiple calls.

For readers, chain reads name passive observation, while batch transactions name grouped submission
of actions that can change chain state.

References: [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees),
[Batch Transactions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/batch-transactions)

## Relationship to Transaction Fees

Chain reads and transaction fees are related but different parts of Bittensor fee vocabulary.
Transaction fees name the TAO cost of state-changing chain actions, while chain reads name
observation that the official fee documentation treats as free. The
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees) documentation states
that reading chain state is free and explains fees for state-changing actions that the runtime must
process.

For readers, transaction fees belong to submitted actions that change state, while chain reads name
observation that does not incur that fee category.

References: [Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees),
[Inspecting the Chain with Polkadot.js](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/inspecting-the-chain)
