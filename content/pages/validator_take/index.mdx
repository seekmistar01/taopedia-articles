# Validator Take

Validator take is the share of delegated-stake emissions that a validator keeps before the remaining
emissions flow to stakers
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The term is a distribution concept. It describes a split inside delegated-stake emissions after the
validator-side amount already exists, not the consensus step that produced that amount
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## What It Represents

Validator take concerns the portion of emissions tied to delegated stake on a selected subnet. On
netuid 1, the key relationship is between a validator and the stakers who delegated to it: the take
is the validator's kept share, and the remaining delegated-stake emissions flow to those stakers at
tempo distribution ([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's commission split. They should not be read as
representing production settings on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Subnet Owner Take

On a subnet such as netuid 1, validator take and subnet owner take name different emission slices.
Validator take is the percentage a validator keeps from delegated-stake dividends before the
remainder flows to nominators, while subnet owner take is the documented 18 percent share of subnet
alpha emissions routed to the subnet owner as stake
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Validator take** — validator–delegator split on validation dividends at tempo distribution.
- **Subnet owner take** — global owner cut from that subnet's emission bucket.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

Validator take names the commission rate on validation dividends on a subnet such as netuid 1, while
a child hotkey names the authorized key a parent delegate uses to perform validation with
re-delegated stake
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

- **Validator take** — fee applied when dividends split between validator and stakers.
- **Child hotkey** — worker identity that earns dividends under delegation.

## Distinction from Dividends

Validator take is the share of validator dividends kept by the validator hotkey. Dividends are the
validator-side reward stream produced from subnet emissions before that split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

- **Dividends** — validator-side payout category consensus produces.
- **Validator take** — configured share kept from the delegated-stake portion at distribution.

## Distinction from Childkey Take

A child hotkey carries its own take rate, the childkey take, which applies at a different point in
the dividend flow. Validator take is the commission a validator keeps from emissions tied to
delegated stake before the remainder reaches delegators
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

- **Validator take** — split between validator and its delegators.
- **Childkey take** — split between parent hotkey and child hotkey validating on its behalf.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Validator take is the commission on delegated-stake dividends. A validator permit is the on-chain
right to submit counting weights and take part in consensus
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

- **Validator permit** — right to submit counting weights before dividends exist.
- **Validator take** — commission applied once delegated-stake dividends are credited.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake is the combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey. Validator take is the
commission rate applied when delegated-emission returns are split at distribution
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Effective stake** — combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey.
- **Validator take** — commission rate on delegated returns at distribution.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator delegates TAO to a validator hotkey. Validator take names what share of
delegated-emission returns the validator keeps before the remainder flows to those nominators
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Nominator** — role that supplies delegated support.
- **Validator take** — configured share the validator keeps before the remainder flows to stakers.

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Validator take is the commission a validator keeps from delegated-emission returns before nominators
receive the remainder, while delegation rewards are what remains for delegated stake after that
split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Validator take** — cut taken off the top of delegated returns.
- **Delegation rewards** — portion that reaches nominators afterward.

## Distinction from Emission Split

Validator take is not a fourth headline share in the emission split. It is the cut a validator keeps
from delegated stake inside the validator-and-staker bucket after that bucket is credited
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Emission split** — role proportions credited at tempo end.
- **Validator take** — commission applied inside the validator-and-staker bucket.

## Distinction from Incentives

[Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) name miner-side emission
outcomes produced from clipped and aggregated weight signals. Validator take names the configured
share a validator keeps when the delegated-stake portion is later split
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Incentives** — miner-side emission outcomes from consensus.
- **Validator take** — validator commission on delegated-stake dividends at distribution.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Validator take is the configurable commission a validator keeps from delegated-stake emissions at
distribution. It names a post-settlement split rule inside the staking relationship, not the
consensus mechanism that builds validator dividend shares from weight submissions
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on the selected netuid. It
reads the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the
result into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Yuma produces the validator-side dividend pool from consensus; validator take applies afterward when
the delegated-stake portion is split between validator and nominators. Settlement and commission are
separate stages on the same emission path
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Readers should not read a higher take percentage as proof of stronger weight influence—that is stake
weight vocabulary. Take only governs how delegated dividends are shared after Yuma settles.

- **Validator take** — commission on delegated-stake validator emissions at distribution.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Distinction from Staking

Validator take belongs to the staking relationship between a validator and the stakers who support
that validator. Delegation describes the support relationship, while validator take describes the
validator-kept share of the delegated-stake emissions
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Delegate stake / effective stake** — stake context around a validator.
- **Validator take** — how the delegated-stake emission share is split at distribution.

## Reader Boundary

Validator take should be read as reference vocabulary for emission distribution. It is not a
recommendation, validator quality score, or complete explanation of staking outcomes
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).
