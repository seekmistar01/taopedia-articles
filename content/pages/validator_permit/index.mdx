# Validator Permit

A validator permit is a Bittensor permission flag for validation rights inside a subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit) defines it as a
boolean flag for whether a specific subnet member has validation rights. The related
[VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit) entry describes the subnet IDs
where a delegate is authorized to validate.

## Subnet Permission Flag

Validator permit is a scoped permission term. It answers whether validation rights exist for the
relevant subnet, not whether the same validator has rights everywhere else. The
[Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit) glossary
entry ties the flag to one subnet, while the
[VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit) entry summarizes the subnet
IDs associated with a delegate.

This makes validator permit useful as a one-subnet concept. It should not be read as a universal
validator label or as a general statement about every subnet role.

## VPermit Scope

Validator permit and VPermit describe nearby ideas at different scopes. A validator permit is the
one-subnet flag. VPermit is the delegate-level view that lists where validation is authorized, as
described in the [Glossary: VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit).

The distinction keeps the vocabulary readable. Validator permit answers a local permission question
inside a subnet, while VPermit summarizes those permissions across subnet IDs for a delegate.

## Stake-Weight Context

The
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)
connects permit awards to stake-weight position. The
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) defines
stake weight as the computed stake value that determines validator consensus power and emissions in
a subnet.

For readers, this means a validator permit is not a reputation score by itself. It is a validation
permission flag connected to stake-weight eligibility inside the subnet context.

## Stake Weight and Permit Reading

Stake weight and validator permit describe different parts of the validation picture. Stake weight
is a computed value used for consensus power and emissions, while validator permit records whether
validation rights exist for the subnet being discussed
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

That distinction matters because stake weight is continuous context and validator permit is a scoped
flag. A reader can use stake weight to understand why validator influence matters, then use
validator permit vocabulary to describe whether the subnet recognizes validation rights for the
validator
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

The two ideas are related but not interchangeable. Stake weight helps explain the eligibility and
influence setting, while the permit term names the rights state inside a specific subnet.

## Delegate-Level View

VPermit is the wider view of validator authorization across subnet IDs. The glossary defines VPermit
as the subnet IDs where a delegate is authorized to validate, which makes it useful when the reader
needs a delegate-level summary rather than a one-subnet flag
([Glossary: VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit)).

This keeps the language precise. Validator permit fits a single subnet context; VPermit fits the set
of subnet contexts associated with a delegate. Mixing them can make a local validation-rights claim
look broader than it is.

## Validator Capacity

Subnet capacity also matters for permit vocabulary. The
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#maxallowedvalidators)
documentation lists `max_allowed_validators` as the maximum number of validators on a subnet. That
capacity setting helps explain why validation rights are a bounded permission rather than a right
held by every subnet member.

The capacity context keeps validator permit language tied to one subnet. The permit records
validation rights inside that subnet, while `max_allowed_validators` describes the subnet-level
capacity around those rights.

This makes validator permit different from a general validator title. The useful reading combines
the one-subnet flag, stake-weight context, and subnet capacity rather than treating validation
rights as unlimited across Bittensor.

## Reading Boundaries

Validator permit is the right term when the focus is permission to validate inside a subnet. If the
question is stake-derived validator influence,
[stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) is more precise. If
the question is the delegate-level list of authorized subnet IDs,
[VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit) is more precise.

These boundaries keep the article focused on a permission flag, not a full validator guide, a
general ranking, or a description of every subnet role.
