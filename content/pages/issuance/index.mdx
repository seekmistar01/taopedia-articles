# Issuance

Issuance is the circulating amount of TAO in Bittensor. The term includes TAO held directly and TAO
held in subnet liquidity pools
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

That makes issuance a supply term. It tells readers what TAO is counted as circulating, not which
role received a reward or which wallet holds a balance.

## What Issuance Counts

Issuance counts circulating TAO. The glossary's wording includes both directly held TAO and TAO held
in subnet liquidity pools, so the concept is broader than a simple wallet-balance view
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

This matters because Bittensor tokenomics often moves between personal balances, subnet pools, and
network-level supply. Issuance is the network-level supply word in that group.

## Pool-Held TAO

Pool-held TAO remains part of issuance because it is still counted in circulating TAO. A subnet pool
changes where TAO sits inside the system, but it does not make that TAO disappear from issuance
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, this keeps subnet liquidity language separate from supply language. A pool can hold TAO
for subnet mechanics while issuance still names the broader circulating amount.

## Emission Relationship

Emission and issuance are related but distinct. Emission is how new token amounts are distributed
over time; issuance is the circulating TAO amount that supply discussions refer to
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

The difference is useful when reading rewards material. A reward event may affect circulating
supply, but issuance is not the reward event itself.

## Halving Relationship

Halving uses issuance as supply context. TAO halving concerns emission-rate changes tied to supply
milestones, while issuance names the TAO amount being counted for those milestones
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

This keeps the vocabulary precise. Issuance is the measured supply concept; halving is the rule that
changes emission rates at supply thresholds.

## Recycling and Burning Relationship

Recycling and burning describe supply-flow changes around issuance. Recycling can return amounts to
future emission, while burning removes tokens from ordinary circulation
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

Issuance is the surrounding supply count. It should not be used as a substitute for either recycling
or burning.

## Reader Boundary

Use issuance when the topic is circulating TAO supply. Use emission when the topic is distribution
over time, halving when the topic is rate reduction, and recycling or burning when the topic is
supply removal or reuse
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

This page is a reference article for the term. It does not report supply figures or interpret market
conditions.
