# Subnet Weights

Subnet weights describe how Bittensor compares subnets for cross-subnet emission allocation. They
are tied to net TAO flows and the flow-based emission model
([Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights),
[Emission: Distribution Across Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution-across-subnets)).

The term is about relative emission share across subnets. It is not the same as validator weights,
weight vectors, or weights used inside a single subnet's consensus process.

## What It Represents

Subnet weights compare subnets for relative TAO injection share before rewards are resolved inside
any one subnet. The signal is net TAO flow smoothed with an exponential moving average; positive net
inflows increase relative share, while negative net flow can zero out injection after clipping
([Emission: Distribution Across Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution-across-subnets),
[Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema#subnet-flow-emission-smoothing)).

Registration burns from `burned_register` are excluded from net inflows, while stake and unstake
operations count. Subnet weights therefore respond to staking movement rather than UID purchase
burns alone ([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

Cross-subnet allocation examples should not be read as representing production injection shares on
another network ([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Validator Weights

Subnet weights compare subnets for cross-subnet allocation. Validator weights compare miner outputs
inside one subnet's consensus context
([Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights)).

- **Subnet weights** — subnet-level relative emission share across netuids.
- **Validator weights** — miner-evaluation signals inside one subnet.

## Distinction from Tempo

Subnet weights set how much relative emission share a subnet receives in the cross-subnet injection
stage. Tempo sets how often a subnet distributes its accumulated rewards through consensus
([Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission: Injection and Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

- **Subnet weights** — the relative cross-subnet share of TAO injection.
- **Tempo** — the block interval on which a subnet distributes accumulated rewards.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Subnet weights name the relative cross-subnet share of TAO injection assigned to each netuid. Yuma
Consensus runs at the epoch boundary inside one subnet, converting validator weights into incentive
and dividend shares from that subnet's accumulated pool
([Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission: Injection and Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

- **Subnet weights** — relative cross-subnet share of TAO injection per netuid.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights inside one subnet.

## Distinction from Alpha Token

Subnet weights describe relative cross-subnet allocation. Alpha tokens are subnet-specific assets
within Dynamic TAO tokenomics
([Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission: Distribution Across Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution-across-subnets)).

- **Subnet weights** — relative allocation share across subnets.
- **Alpha token** — subnet-specific asset within Dynamic TAO.

## Distinction from Smoothing

Subnet weights use a smoothed net-flow signal rather than raw event totals. Smoothing keeps brief
movement from being treated as a durable allocation signal
([Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema#subnet-flow-emission-smoothing),
[Emission: Distribution Across Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution-across-subnets)).

- **Subnet weights** — allocation shares derived from smoothed net flow.
- **Smoothing** — EMA window that filters short-lived flow spikes.

## Reader Boundary

Use subnet weights when the focus is cross-subnet emission share. Use validator weights, weight
vectors, or weight matrices when the focus is narrower within-subnet consensus signals
([Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights),
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector)).
