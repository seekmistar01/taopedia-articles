# UID Slot

A UID slot is a participant position within a Bittensor subnet. The
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) describes it as a
position occupied by a subnet miner or subnet validator and identified by a unique UID.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For UID slots on a
subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret participant positions,
registration burns, and displacement examples.

In localnet, UID slot examples can be tested in an isolated environment. Local slot occupancy
reflects local chain configuration rather than production subnet participant state.

On testnet, UID slots can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet slot readings on
a selected netuid are separate from mainnet metagraph state
([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

On mainnet, a UID slot is a live participant position inside production subnet markets on the
connected Bittensor network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A UID slot example from one environment should not be read
as representing production participant identity on another network.

## Distinction from Register

Register describes purchasing entry into a subnet through the dynamic registration burn. A UID slot
is the numbered position that process grants once entry succeeds on a subnet such as netuid 1
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

Register is the action. The UID slot is the resulting participant position inside the chosen netuid.

- **Register** — the entry process that purchases a subnet position.
- **UID slot** — the numbered participant position granted after entry succeeds.

## Distinction from Netuid

A UID slot names one participant position inside a subnet. Netuid names which subnet market is in
scope ([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid)).

UID 7 on netuid 3 is not the same position as UID 7 on netuid 19. Metagraph queries should include
netuid when resolving a slot holder.

- **UID slot** — one indexed participant position inside a subnet.
- **Netuid** — the numeric subnet identifier that scopes that position.

## Distinction from Active UID

A UID slot names the occupied participant position. Active UID status reports whether that slot
currently counts as participating under subnet activity rules
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Active UID](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#active-uid),
[Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

A hotkey can hold a UID slot while activity evidence changes with subnet state and timing.

- **UID slot** — the indexed participant position on a netuid.
- **Active UID** — whether that slot currently counts as participating.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

A UID slot names the occupied position on a subnet such as netuid 1. Immunity period is the
post-registration window that can protect a new slot holder from early displacement
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

The slot names the position. Immunity names a timing rule that can shield that position from early
removal when the subnet is full.

- **UID slot** — the indexed participant position on a netuid.
- **Immunity period** — post-registration window that can block early displacement.

## Distinction from Deregistration

A UID slot names the participant position inside a subnet. Deregistration is the process that can
vacate that position when a participant is removed
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration),
[Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration)).

Participant-level deregistration vacates one slot. Subnet deregistration ends the subnet that
contained the slot collection.

- **UID slot** — one indexed participant position on a netuid.
- **Deregistration** — removal process that can vacate that position.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

A UID slot grants participant position inside a subnet such as netuid 1. A validator permit marks
which neurons may set weights and participate in consensus on that netuid
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Holding a slot does not by itself grant validation rights. Permit rules apply after the slot exists.

- **UID slot** — indexed participant position on a netuid.
- **Validator permit** — subnet-level right to submit counting weights.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

A UID slot is the indexed position a parent hotkey holds on a subnet. A child hotkey is a separate
authorized key that can validate on the parent's behalf
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

The slot belongs to the parent position. The child hotkey is the authorized validating key.

- **UID slot** — indexed participant position on a netuid.
- **Child hotkey** — authorized key that validates using routed stake.

## Distinction from Neuron

A UID slot is the indexed participant position inside a subnet. A neuron is the registered
participant record that occupies that position after registration succeeds
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Neuron](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#neuron),
[Understanding Neurons](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/neurons)).

- **UID slot** — one indexed position on a netuid.
- **Neuron** — the registered participant record at that position.

## Distinction from Metagraph

A UID slot is one indexed participant position inside a subnet. The metagraph is the per-netuid
snapshot that lists those positions and their values at a block
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#metagraph),
[The Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

- **UID slot** — one indexed participant position inside that state.
- **Metagraph** — subnet-wide participant state at one block height.

## Distinction from Pruning Score

A UID slot is the participant position a miner or validator occupies in a subnet. The pruning score
ranks which occupied slot is reclaimed first when a full subnet takes a new registration
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **UID slot** — one indexed participant position on a netuid.
- **Pruning score** — ranking that selects which occupied slot is reclaimed first.

## Distinction from Validator Registration

Validator registration names the paid entry process when a participant registers a hotkey to
validate on a subnet. A UID slot names the numbered position inside that subnet that successful
entry grants
([Validator Registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration),
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

Validator registration is the entry step. The UID slot is the numbered position that step grants on
a subnet such as netuid 1.

- **Validator registration** — paid entry process for a validating participant.
- **UID slot** — numbered subnet position granted after entry succeeds.

## Distinction from Max Allowed UIDs

A UID slot is one participant position inside a subnet. Max allowed UIDs is the subnet
hyperparameter that sets how many slots the subnet can hold
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

When the slot ceiling is reached, accepting a new registrant depends on displacement rules for that
subnet.

- **UID slot** — one indexed participant position on a netuid.
- **Max allowed UIDs** — subnet ceiling on how many slots can exist.

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the slot concept. It does not list subnet participants, count occupied slots,
or describe current subnet state. Readers should use subnet references for role-specific details.

A UID slot identifies who can occupy a participant position inside a subnet.
[Coinbase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase) names the
per-block emission operation that advances reward processing; the slot names the position that can
receive subnet outcomes, not the emission mechanism itself.
