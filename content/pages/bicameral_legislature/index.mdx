# Bicameral Legislature

Bicameral legislature is the two-body governance structure used for Bittensor proposal review. The
term names a system made of the Triumvirate and the Senate, with proposal creation separated from
proposal approval
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)).

The structure matters because it keeps governance authority split across two roles. The Triumvirate
originates proposals, while the Senate reviews them and votes on whether they should proceed
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

## Two-Body Structure

The Triumvirate and Senate are separate governance bodies. The Triumvirate is the proposal-origin
body, and the Senate is the approval-side body that reviews Triumvirate proposals
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

For readers, bicameral legislature names the structure that includes both bodies. It is broader than
either body alone, because the term is about the split between proposal origin and approval
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

## Proposal Path Context

In the governance flow, proposals originate with the Triumvirate and move to Senate review. Senate
votes determine whether the proposal is approved or rejected, while the proposal remains part of the
governance path described by the overview
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

Approval is not the same thing as saying the bicameral legislature is a proposal record. The term
describes the review architecture, while individual proposals are the items that move through that
architecture ([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

## Sudo Boundary

Bittensor governance also has a security context. The governance overview frames the bicameral
system as a replacement for direct sudo-style privileged control, so privileged changes require the
documented proposal-review path rather than depending on one administrative key
([Governance Overview: security](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance#security)).

That boundary helps explain why the structure exists. Bicameral legislature is not just a name for
two groups; it is the governance pattern that distributes proposal authority across origin and
approval roles ([Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo)).

## Scope Boundary

Bicameral legislature is governance vocabulary, not subnet scoring vocabulary. Approved governance
changes may affect subnet rules, but the term itself names the proposal-review structure made of the
Triumvirate and Senate
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)).

This keeps the concept separate from subnet incentive mechanisms. Multiple incentive mechanisms
describe how a subnet can distribute emissions across different evaluation systems; bicameral
legislature describes how governance proposals are reviewed
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)).

## Body-Specific Context

The Triumvirate and Senate are best read as body-specific pieces of the bicameral structure. The
Triumvirate supplies the proposal-origin side, and the Senate supplies the approval-side review
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

For readers, this means a page about the Triumvirate or the Senate is narrower than a page about
bicameral legislature. The body pages name roles within the structure; the bicameral term names the
two-body structure itself ([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

## Reader Boundary

Bicameral legislature should be read as a structural governance term. It does not list proposal
statuses, body membership, or governance activity. Those details belong in governance interfaces and
records rather than in a concept article
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

The stable article-level point is the separation of proposal creation and proposal approval. That
separation is what makes the term bicameral in Bittensor governance
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For the bicameral
legislature, that sequence changes how readers should interpret examples of governance proposal
creation and Senate approval behavior.

In localnet, bicameral governance mechanics can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet
proposal and approval outcomes reflect local chain settings and do not represent production
governance state.

On testnet, bicameral governance behavior can be observed in a shared, non-production network.
Testnet proposal and Senate interactions are separate from mainnet governance state.

On mainnet, the bicameral legislature is the live governance structure where the Triumvirate creates
proposals and the Senate votes to approve or reject them in production Bittensor operations. The
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) documentation describes the
governance mechanics that apply on the production network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A governance example from one environment should not be
read as representing proposal or Senate behavior in another environment.
