# Validator Take

Validator take is the share of delegated-stake emissions that a validator keeps before the remaining
emissions flow to stakers
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The term is a distribution concept. It describes a split inside delegated-stake emissions after the
validator-side amount already exists, not the consensus step that produced that amount.

## Emission Distribution Context

Validator take belongs inside Bittensor's emissions distribution flow. Validator emissions are
calculated through consensus first, and validator take applies later when the validator/staker
allocation is split
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

This makes validator take a distribution term rather than a separate reward source. Yuma Consensus
explains the validator-emission calculation; validator take explains how the delegated-stake share
is divided afterward.

## Delegated-Stake Boundary

Validator take concerns the portion of emissions tied to delegated stake. It should not be expanded
into a statement about every emission a validator may receive
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

For readers, the key relationship is between a validator and the stakers who delegated to it. The
take is the validator's kept share; the remaining delegated-stake emissions flow to those stakers
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

## Validator Emissions Boundary

Validator emissions and validator take describe different stages. Validator emissions come from the
Yuma Consensus path; validator take describes how the delegated-stake portion is shared after that
validator-side amount exists
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

This keeps consensus outcomes separate from distribution splitting. Validator take is downstream of
the calculation that produced validator-side emissions, so it should not be used as shorthand for
validator quality or consensus performance.

## Multiple-Mechanism Context

When a subnet uses multiple incentive mechanisms, evaluation and bond context can be mechanism
specific. Validator take still names the later validator/staker fee share on delegated-stake
emissions
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The mechanism rules decide subnet work and scoring. Validator take describes the later
validator/staker split after the relevant emissions context has already been established.

## Staking Context

Validator take belongs to the staking relationship between a validator and the stakers who support
that validator. Delegation describes the support relationship, while validator take describes the
validator-kept share of the delegated-stake emissions
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

Delegate stake and effective stake describe stake context around a validator. Validator take
describes how the delegated-stake emission share is split later
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For validator take
on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret delegated-stake
commission splits and staker distribution examples.

In localnet, validator-take examples can be exercised in an isolated environment. Local commission
settings reflect local chain configuration rather than production validator economics.

On testnet, validator take can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet validator
commission and staker splits are separate from mainnet delegation state
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

On mainnet, validator take is the live documented split between validators and their stakers on the
production Bittensor network
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A validator-take example from one environment should not
be read as representing production commission settings on another network.

## Take Applies in the Distribution Stage After Consensus

[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) places
validator take inside the distribution stage that follows Yuma Consensus. The
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)
defines take as the share a validator keeps from emissions tied to delegated stake before the
remainder flows to stakers.

Validator emissions and validator take therefore answer different questions. Yuma Consensus produces
the validator-side emission total first; take names how that delegated-stake portion is split
between the validator and its delegators afterward
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Dividends Precede the Take Split

[Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission) name the validator-side
emission result produced through Yuma Consensus from bonds and miner incentives. Validator take
names the configured share a validator keeps when the delegated-stake portion is later split.

Dividends describe what consensus produces on the validator side; take describes how part of that
delegated-stake value is divided afterward during distribution
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Readers should not treat a dividend figure as the take a validator keeps, or a take percentage as a
substitute for the consensus-produced dividend it is applied against.

## Delegators Receive the Share After Take

[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
describes delegation as TAO support attached to a validator. Validator take sits on the return path
for that support: the validator keeps its configured share, and the remaining delegated-stake
emissions flow to delegators
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

That split is why delegated support does not automatically mean a staker receives the full
validator-side emission total. Take vocabulary names the validator-kept portion; delegation reward
vocabulary names the delegator's proportional share after that fee is applied.

## The Take Split Runs at Tempo End

[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) describes subnet rewards as being
distributed at the end of each [tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
after Yuma Consensus aggregates validator evaluations. Validator take is part of that distribution
step on emissions tied to delegated stake rather than a separate label on every block.

Delegators and validators therefore read take as part of the tempo-bound reward split that follows
consensus processing for the period
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

## Incentives Stay on the Miner Side

[Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) name miner-side emission
outcomes produced from clipped and aggregated weight signals. Validator take names the configured
share a validator keeps when the delegated-stake portion is later split.

A validator take percentage does not by itself report miner incentive totals, because incentives and
validator take answer different role-facing questions in the same emission pass
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Take Is a Rate on Delegated Stake, Not the Whole Reward

A frequent misreading is that validator take is the validator's entire reward. Documentation places
take only on the delegated-stake portion: it is the configured share a validator keeps before the
remainder flows to delegators, not a cut of every emission the validator touches
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

For readers, this is why a take percentage and a validator's total earnings are different figures. A
validator's own stake and its consensus-produced dividends exist alongside the take, so the take
describes how delegated-stake rewards are split rather than the size of the validator's overall
return
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

That framing also explains why delegators care about take as a rate. Because it applies to the
delegated portion at distribution, a higher take leaves a smaller delegator share of the same
underlying emissions, which is the comparison the term is meant to support
([Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo)).

## Distinction from Subnet Owner Take

On a subnet such as netuid 1, validator take and subnet owner take name different emission slices.
Validator take is the percentage a validator keeps from delegated-stake dividends before the
remainder flows to nominators, while subnet owner take is the documented 18 percent share of subnet
alpha emissions routed to the subnet owner as stake
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

For readers comparing economics on netuid 1, validator take governs the validator–delegator split on
validation rewards at tempo distribution, and subnet owner take governs the global owner cut from
that subnet's emission bucket. The two rates answer different questions and should not be read as
interchangeable commissions
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

Validator take names the commission rate on validation dividends on a subnet such as netuid 1, while
a child hotkey names the authorized key a parent delegate uses to perform validation with
re-delegated stake
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

For readers on netuid 1, take is the fee applied when dividends are split between validator and
stakers, and child hotkey is the worker identity that earns those dividends under delegation.
Setting take does not by itself change which child validates, and assigning a child hotkey does not
by itself change the take percentage configured on the parent
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Reader Boundary

Validator take should be read as reference vocabulary for emission distribution. It is not a
recommendation, validator quality score, or complete explanation of staking outcomes
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

The narrow meaning is the validator-kept share before remaining delegated-stake emissions flow to
stakers.

## Take Applies to Delegated Emissions, Not Self-Stake Fraction First

Delegation documentation describes validators keeping emissions proportional to their self-stake
share of total stake, then applying take to the delegated portion before the remainder flows to
nominators. Validator take vocabulary therefore names a cut on delegated rewards rather than a
relabeling of the validator's own self-staked emissions
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

For readers, that ordering matters when comparing validators. Two validators with the same take rate
can leave different nominator outcomes if their self-stake fractions differ, because take applies
after the self-stake versus delegated split on the validator-side stream
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

Take is also separate from miner-side incentives. Miner emissions follow rank and incentive
mechanism vocabulary; validator take sits on the validator dividend path at tempo distribution
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

## Tempo Boundaries Mark When Take Splits Become Credits

Emission documentation finalizes subnet rewards at tempo boundaries before they appear as balances
participants can stake or transfer. Validator take therefore applies inside that distribution step
rather than as a continuous wallet fee
([Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, comparing take rates across validators still requires reading the same subnet and tempo
context. Take vocabulary names the validator-kept share of delegated emissions at distribution time,
not a separate manual withdrawal action
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

## Relationship to Dividends

Validator take is the share of validator dividends kept by the validator hotkey. Dividends are the
validator-side reward stream produced from subnet emissions on a selected netuid such as netuid 1
([Glossary: Validator Take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

Emission documentation routes subnet rewards into miner incentives and validator dividends. The
validator take rate is capped to prevent excessive extraction from delegators. Validator take names
the validator's retained portion, while dividends name the broader validator-side payout category
before the take split applies at tempo end on each emission-active netuid
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

Use validator take when covering the validator's retained share; use dividends when covering the
validator-side payout stream rather than the take rate alone on netuid 1
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Validator Take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Relationship to Child Hotkeys

Validator take and child hotkeys describe the commission on validation and the worker that performs
it. Validator take is the commission percentage skimmed from the dividends that validation produces
before the remainder reaches stakers, while a child hotkey is the key a parent delegate authorizes
to validate on its behalf using re-delegated stake
([Glossary: Validator Take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

The two answer different questions. Validator take names the commission rate applied to the
dividends, while child hotkey names the authorized key doing the validation that earns those
dividends, so the take is the fee and the child hotkey is the worker it applies to
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## Distinction from Childkey Take

A child hotkey carries its own take rate, the childkey take, which is separate from the validator
take and applies at a different point in the dividend flow. Validator take, also called delegate
take, is the commission a validator keeps from emissions tied to delegated stake before the
remainder is credited to its delegators, with a documented default of 18%. Childkey take is the
fraction a child hotkey retains for performing validation on behalf of a parent hotkey, configurable
between 0% and 18% and subnet-specific, so the same child hotkey can have one childkey take on one
netuid and a different one on another
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

The two takes sit on different relationships. Validator take governs the split between a validator
and the delegators who staked to it, while childkey take governs the split between a parent hotkey
and the child hotkey validating on its behalf. A single delegated position can therefore be touched
by both: the validator take on the delegate side and a childkey take where stake is re-delegated to
a child ([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Validator take** — the validator's commission on delegated-stake dividends (default 18%).
- **Childkey take** — the per-subnet 0%–18% fraction a child hotkey keeps for validating for a
  parent.
