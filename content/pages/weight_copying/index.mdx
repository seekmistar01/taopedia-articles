# Weight Copying

Weight copying is a consensus-quality problem where a validator relies on another validator's weight
information instead of independently evaluating miners. It is framed as free-riding on validator
work because copied weights can imitate evaluation without adding a new assessment of miner
performance
([Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## What It Represents

Weight copying names dependence on another validator's published scores rather than independent
miner evaluation. Copied signals can enter the same validator-weight path as honest signals but
contribute less new information; Commit Reveal and consensus-based weights are documented defenses
that reduce the value of copied or stale weights
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal),
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)).

Similar weight patterns do not automatically show copying. Provenance, not similarity, separates
independent evaluation from reuse of visible scores
([Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's weight-visibility path. They should not be read as
representing production consensus behavior on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Similar Validator Rankings

Honest validators can evaluate the same miner work independently and still converge. Weight copying
is dependence on another validator's published scores rather than independent evaluation
([Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Similar rankings** — independent validators reaching similar conclusions from miner work.
- **Weight copying** — reusing another validator's visible scores without independent evaluation.

## Distinction from Validator Trust

Validator trust measures how consistently a validator's clipped weights align with consensus over
time. Weight copying reuses visible weight signals instead of independently evaluating miners
([Glossary: Validator Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-trust),
[Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor)).

- **Validator trust** — consensus-alignment measure over time.
- **Weight copying** — reusing published scores rather than independent evaluation.

## Distinction from Collusion

Weight copying is dependence on another validator's published scores. Collusion is validators
coordinating inflated weights toward chosen targets
([Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

- **Weight copying** — dependence on another validator's published scores.
- **Collusion** — coordinated inflation toward favored miners.

## Distinction from Self-Weighting

Self-weighting is one validator inflating weights for entities it controls. Weight copying reuses
another validator's visible signals rather than favoring one's own miners directly
([Yuma Consensus: Clipping excess weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#clipping-excess-weights),
[Glossary: Weight Copying](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-copying)).

- **Self-weighting** — one validator inflating weights for entities it controls.
- **Weight copying** — reusing another validator's visible scores without independent evaluation.

## Distinction from Commit Reveal

Commit reveal delays when fresh validator weights become readable. Weight copying is the problem
where a validator reuses another validator's visible weight information instead of evaluating miners
independently ([Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal),
[Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor)).

- **Commit reveal** — mechanism that delays weight visibility to reduce copying.
- **Weight copying** — dependence on copied signals once they are visible.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Weight copying names whether a validator derived its submitted scores from independent miner
evaluation or from another validator's visible signals. That evaluation-integrity question is
separate from the tempo pass that aggregates submitted weights into emission shares
([Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor),
[Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector)).

Yuma Consensus combines many validator submissions into rank and credited outcomes when the epoch
closes on the selected netuid
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Copied weights may still enter aggregation. Weight copying describes how those signals were
produced; Yuma describes how included submissions settle into miner incentives and validator
dividends
([Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)).

- **Weight copying** — reuse of visible scores instead of independent evaluation.
- **Yuma Consensus** — tempo step that turns submitted weights into emission shares.

## Distinction from Consensus Score

Weight copying is when a validator submits another validator's weights instead of evaluating miners
itself. Consensus score is the stake-weighted benchmark those submitted weights are measured against
([Glossary: Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Weight copying** — dependence on another validator's signal.
- **Consensus score** — stake-weighted alignment benchmark used in clipping.

## Distinction from Consensus-Based Weights

Consensus-based weights adjust validator bonds around consensus alignment as a copier deterrent.
Weight copying names dependence on another validator's published scores
([Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights),
[Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor)).

- **Weight copying** — upstream provenance failure in how scores were formed.
- **Consensus-based weights** — bond adjustment that reduces value of copied or stale signals.

## Distinction from Weight Setting

Weight setting is the validator action of assigning scores to miners. Weight copying is dependence
on another validator's published scores instead of performing that evaluation
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor)).

- **Weight setting** — validator action of assigning miner scores.
- **Weight copying** — reusing another validator's scores instead of independent evaluation.

## Distinction from Commit Reveal Period

Commit reveal period is the hyperparameter count of tempos before committed validator weights are
revealed. Weight copying is the problem where validators reuse visible weight signals instead of
evaluating miner work independently
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor)).

- **Commit reveal period** — tempos before committed weights become visible.
- **Weight copying** — reusing visible weights instead of independent evaluation.

## Distinction from Drand Time-Lock Encryption

Weight copying is the problem of reusing public weight signals instead of evaluating independently.
Drand time-lock encryption conceals committed weights until a later round to reduce that copying
window
([The Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor),
[Glossary: Drand/time-lock encryption](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#drandtime-lock-encryption)).

- **Weight copying** — the problem of reusing public weight signals.
- **Drand time-lock encryption** — timing tool that conceals weights to reduce it.

## Reader Boundary

Weight copying should not be read as a synonym for agreement, rank, trust, validator trust, or every
repeated-looking score. It names a consensus-quality risk where submitted weights depend on another
validator's weight information rather than on independent miner evaluation
([Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor),
[Glossary: Weight Copying](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-copying)).

## Distinction from Trust

Weight copying and trust sit on different sides of consensus quality. Weight copying is a
freeloading strategy where a validator replicates other validators' published weights instead of
evaluating miners, while trust is a legitimate consensus metric describing how broadly a miner's
weight is supported across validators
([Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor),
[Glossary: Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Weight copying** — a strategy that imitates others' weights instead of evaluating.
- **Trust** — a consensus metric for how broadly a miner's weight is supported.
