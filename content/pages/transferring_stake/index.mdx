# Transferring Stake

Transferring stake is the operation of changing which coldkey owns a staked position. The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
lists it alongside moving stake and direct unstaking as one of the participant-facing ways to
rearrange an existing staked position.

In Taopedia terms, transferring stake names an ownership-change operation. It belongs alongside
staking (adding stake), unstaking (withdrawing stake), and moving stake (rearranging between
hotkeys) as one of the core stake-management operations.

## Why Transferring Stake Exists

A participant may need to reassign a staked position to a different coldkey — for example, when
migrating to a new coldkey or reorganizing wallet custody. The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
places transferring stake as a participant-facing alternative to unstaking and restaking from the
new coldkey.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For transferring
stake on a delegated position in a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should
interpret evidence about stake transfer mechanics and ownership reassignment outcomes.

In localnet, stake transfer mechanics can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet stake
transfer outcomes reflect local chain configuration and do not represent production staking state on
a subnet such as netuid 1.

On testnet, stake transfers can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet stake
transfer outcomes and coldkey assignment changes are separate from mainnet delegation state.

On mainnet, transferring stake is a live Subtensor action that reassigns ownership of delegated
stake between coldkeys. The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
describes the delegation mechanics that apply on the production network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A stake transfer example from one environment should not
be read as representing ownership reassignment or staking outcomes in another environment.

## Distinction from Toggle Transfer

Transferring stake is the action of moving a staked position between coldkeys, while toggle transfer
is the per-subnet switch that determines whether that action is permitted at all
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

- **Transferring stake** — the operation that moves stake to another coldkey.
- **Toggle transfer** — the subnet setting that allows or blocks that operation.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Transferring stake and effective stake separate ownership from validator influence. Transferring
stake is the operation that changes which coldkey owns a staked position while leaving it on the
same hotkey, whereas effective stake is the total associated with the validator that position backs,
so transferring ownership does not move effective stake between validators the way moving stake does
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

- **Transferring stake** — changing the coldkey that owns a position, same validator.
- **Effective stake** — the validator total, unmoved when only ownership transfers.

## Distinction from Unstaking

Transferring stake reassigns coldkey ownership of an existing delegated position. Unstaking
withdraws stake back to the coldkey, often converting subnet alpha through the pool
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

- **Transferring stake** — change ownership while the position stays delegated.
- **Unstaking** — exit the delegated position and return stake to the coldkey.

Readers choosing custody change without exit use transfer; readers seeking TAO back use unstaking
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey)).

Transferring stake reassigns coldkey ownership over a delegated position that remains staked. The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
lists transferring stake alongside moving stake and direct unstaking as ways to rearrange an
existing position, but transferring stake specifically changes which coldkey owns the stake rather
than withdrawing it to an unstaked balance. The validator hotkey receiving the delegation can stay
the same while ownership moves
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey)).

Readers should not treat transferring stake as an automatic withdrawal.
[Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking) returns stake to the
coldkey as unstaked TAO or converted alpha proceeds; transferring stake moves ownership of the
still-delegated position to another coldkey. Operational fees, conversion paths, and command details
belong in staking documentation rather than in this concept definition.

## Distinction from Moving Stake

Moving stake transfers delegated support between hotkeys and/or subnets in one atomic step.
Transferring stake changes which coldkey owns a delegated position without necessarily changing the
validator hotkey that receives the delegation
([Staking and delegation overview: Moving stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#moving-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview: Transferring stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#transferring-stake)).

Moving stake re-points delegation. Transferring stake reassigns ownership of the same delegated
position.

- **Transferring stake** — changes which coldkey owns a delegated position.
- **Moving stake** — re-points delegated support between hotkeys or subnets.

## Distinction from Transfer

Transferring stake and a transfer move different things. Transferring stake reassigns which coldkey
owns a delegated stake position, while a transfer sends free TAO from one wallet address to another
([Glossary: Transfer](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#transfer),
[Staking and delegation overview: Transferring stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#transferring-stake)).

- **Transferring stake** — reassigns ownership of a delegated stake position.
- **Transfer** — sends free TAO from one wallet address to another.

## Distinction from Coldkey Swap

Transferring stake changes ownership of one staked position between coldkeys. Coldkey swap migrates
the wallet's full on-chain identity to a new coldkey while preserving associated hotkeys and subnet
registrations ([Coldkey Swap](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap),
[Staking and delegation overview: Transferring stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#transferring-stake)).

Use transferring stake when only one delegated position should change owner. Use coldkey swap when
the participant needs the broader identity migration described in coldkey swap documentation.

- **Transferring stake** — reassigns ownership of one staked position between coldkeys.
- **Coldkey swap** — migrates the full on-chain identity to a new coldkey.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Transferring stake is the operation of changing which coldkey owns a staked position without
withdrawing delegation first. It names a stake-ownership reassignment, not the on-chain step that
turns validator weight submissions into emission shares
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that converts submitted validator weights into emission
shares after evaluation completes. It settles scoring outcomes on a subnet; it does not by itself
reassign coldkey ownership over delegated stake
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers should keep ownership reassignment separate from the settlement step. Transferring stake
answers which coldkey owns a delegated position; Yuma Consensus answers how validator weights become
emission shares in that subnet
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Transferring stake** — reassignment of coldkey ownership over delegated stake.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

This article defines transferring stake as a concept. It does not describe transfer steps, recommend
when to transfer, or state current stake amounts, fees, or subnet behavior. Readers should use the
staking documentation for operational detail.
