# Mnemonic

A mnemonic is a recovery phrase associated with wallet key material. In Bittensor wallet context,
documentation discusses mnemonics as part of key management and wallet recovery.

## Wallet Security Context

The practical meaning of a mnemonic is security-sensitive. A mnemonic can be used to recover access
to wallet keys, so it should be treated as secret key material rather than ordinary account
metadata. Protecting it is part of protecting the wallet.

## Recovery Scope

A mnemonic is useful because it can restore wallet access, but that same property makes it risky to
expose. Someone who obtains recovery material may be able to recreate access to the related wallet
keys. That is why wallet recovery phrases belong in the same security category as private wallet
material.

## Recovery Material, Not an Account Identifier

A mnemonic should be read as recovery material, not as the public name of a wallet. Wallet addresses
and other public identifiers help users recognize where funds or chain actions belong, while a
mnemonic is meant to recreate private wallet access. That makes it closer to key material than to
ordinary metadata.

This distinction matters because Bittensor wallets can involve both coldkey and hotkey roles. The
mnemonic sits behind recovery of those keys; it is not itself the coldkey, the hotkey, or the
address shown to other participants. Treating the phrase as a private recovery root helps separate
safe account identification from dangerous key exposure.

## Backup and Storage Context

The seed-phrase guidance places mnemonics in the backup and storage layer of wallet security. A
reader who sees the term in Bittensor documentation should understand it as something that preserves
access after loss, deletion, or device failure, not as something used in normal day-to-day subnet
participation.

That backup role also explains why mnemonics are more sensitive than wallet file paths or public
addresses. A wallet location tells a user where local wallet files live, but a mnemonic can help
regenerate the keys themselves. The phrase therefore belongs with the material a wallet owner keeps
out of screenshots, chat logs, support requests, and routine operational notes.

## Words That Regenerate Wallet Keys

The [Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic) describes a
mnemonic as a sequence of words used to regenerate keys in case of loss and restore coldkeys and
hotkeys in a Bittensor wallet. The phrase is recovery material, not a public account name shown to
other participants.

Readers should treat a mnemonic like other secret wallet material. Anyone who obtains the phrase may
be able to recreate access to the related keys, so it belongs in backup and custody discussions
rather than in ordinary subnet or staking prose.

## Recovery After Loss or Device Failure

Seed-phrase guidance places mnemonics in the backup layer of wallet security. A mnemonic can restore
wallet access after deletion, device failure, or loss of local wallet files, which is why
documentation discusses it beside key-handling practices rather than beside live chain queries.

That recovery role is different from day-to-day subnet participation. Miners, validators, and
stakers still use hotkeys and coldkeys for routine actions; the mnemonic sits behind restoring those
keys when access must be rebuilt.

## Not a Wallet Address or Public Identifier

Wallet addresses and SS58-encoded identifiers help readers recognize where TAO or chain actions
belong. A mnemonic does not serve that public identification role. The
[Glossary: Wallet Address](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#wallet-address)
describes public destination identifiers, while mnemonic vocabulary names private recovery material
for the keys behind a wallet.

Keeping that boundary clear prevents recovery phrases from being quoted or shared like ordinary
metadata. The mnemonic restores access; the wallet address identifies where funds are sent.

Seed-phrase security guidance should be read alongside this term whenever recovery material is
stored, copied, or restored.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. The mnemonic
concept applies across the Bittensor lifecycle: wallet mnemonics can be used for key recovery in
localnet for isolated development, testnet for shared non-production participation, and mainnet for
live operation with real emissions. Production mnemonics should be protected with greatest care on
mainnet, where keys are associated with real TAO.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A mnemonic's security context depends on which network the
associated wallet is used on.

## Regeneration Requires Wallet Tooling

The
[Glossary: Regenerating a Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#regenerating-a-key)
describes recreating a lost or deleted coldkey or hotkey, while mnemonic vocabulary names the
recovery material that can support that action. The phrase alone does not perform regeneration;
wallet tooling and current guidance carry out the recovery step.

Readers should treat a mnemonic as secret input to a recovery workflow, not as a self-executing
repair command ([Working with Keys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/working-with-keys)).

## Seed-Phrase Handling Stays Out of Routine Operations

[Handle Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase) guidance places
mnemonics in backup and custody discussions rather than in day-to-day subnet or staking operations.
Miners, validators, and stakers sign with coldkeys and hotkeys during normal work; the mnemonic
remains offline recovery material.

That separation keeps recovery phrases out of screenshots, chat logs, and support tickets. Exposure
there can recreate wallet keys just as surely as exposing a private key file
([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

## Distinction from Seed Phrase Security

A mnemonic is the recovery phrase itself. Seed phrase security names the handling practices that
keep that phrase secret and recoverable
([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic),
[Handle your Seed Phrase/Mnemonic Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

Recovery-material vocabulary and custody-practice vocabulary answer different wallet-safety
questions.

## Distinction from Regenerating a Key

Regenerating a key names the recovery action that recreates lost coldkey or hotkey material. A
mnemonic is the secret input that can support that action
([Glossary: Regenerating a Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#regenerating-a-key),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

Procedure vocabulary names the recovery step; mnemonic vocabulary names the backup material that may
enable it.

## Distinction from Coldkeys

A coldkey is the custody key that controls TAO and owns hotkeys. A mnemonic can regenerate that
coldkey when local key files are lost
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

Key-role vocabulary names live wallet authority; mnemonic vocabulary names offline recovery material
behind it.

## Distinction from Hotkeys

A hotkey is the operational key used for subnet work. A mnemonic can restore hotkeys as well as
coldkeys when wallet files must be rebuilt
([Glossary: Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

Operational signing vocabulary and recovery-phrase vocabulary sit on different security layers.

## Distinction from Wallet Address

A wallet address is a public destination identifier. A mnemonic is private recovery material that
must not be shared like an address
([Glossary: Wallet Address](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#wallet-address),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

Public destination vocabulary and secret recovery vocabulary require opposite handling.

## Distinction from Seed Phrase

Mnemonic and seed phrase are adjacent recovery terms in Bittensor wallet documentation. Both refer
to the word sequence that can regenerate wallet keys
([Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

Readers may see either label in prose; both belong to secret backup material rather than on-chain
roles.

## Distinction from Private Key

A private key authorizes signing for a specific keypair. A mnemonic can regenerate the private keys
behind a wallet but is not itself a signing key
([Glossary: Private Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#private-key),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

Signing-material vocabulary and backup-root vocabulary must stay separate in wallet-safety reading.

## Distinction from EdDSA Cryptographic Keypairs

Mnemonic names recovery words that can regenerate keys. EdDSA keypairs name the cryptographic pair
structure produced during wallet generation
([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic),
[Glossary: EdDSA Cryptographic Keypairs](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#eddsa-cryptographic-keypairs)).

## Distinction from NaCl Format

NaCl Format names legacy encryption around stored wallet files. A mnemonic is the recovery phrase
used to regenerate wallet keys after loss
([Glossary: NaCl Format](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nacl-format),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic),
[Handle Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

- **NaCl Format** — file-at-rest encryption format for legacy wallet files.
- **Mnemonic** — recovery words that can regenerate keys after loss.

File-format vocabulary and recovery vocabulary answer different wallet-security questions
([Bittensor wallet documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

A mnemonic is the seed phrase used to regenerate coldkey and hotkey pairs from backup. It names
recovery vocabulary, not the on-chain step that turns validator weight submissions into emission
shares ([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic),
[Wallets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that converts submitted validator weights into emission
shares after evaluation completes. Mnemonics restore signing keys offline; Yuma Consensus settles
scoring outcomes on a subnet
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers should keep seed-phrase vocabulary separate from the settlement step. Mnemonic answers how
keys are backed up and restored; Yuma Consensus answers how validator weights become emission shares
in a subnet ([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Mnemonic** — seed phrase that regenerates wallet key pairs.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Distinction from Coldkey and Hotkey Restoration

The [Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic) describes a
mnemonic as a sequence of words used to regenerate keys in case of loss and restore coldkeys and
hotkeys in a Bittensor wallet. For readers, the term names recovery material for local wallet keys
rather than an on-chain account identifier.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the term. It does not ask readers to enter a mnemonic, share one, or follow a
specific recovery workflow. Readers should use current wallet documentation for security-sensitive
wallet actions.
