# MEV Shield

MEV Shield is Bittensor's encrypted mempool-protection feature for sensitive transaction contents
before block inclusion ([MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield),
[MEV](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)).

## What It Represents

Documentation describes sensitive transaction contents as encrypted while pending and revealed when
the chain can process them. A validator-supplied public key anchors encryption in the mempool; only
the producing validator decrypts immediately before finalization
([MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield),
[Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool)).

MEV Shield applies to coldkey-signed transactions. Hotkey-signed submissions through MEV Shield are
unsupported ([MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield),
[Using MEV Shield with the Bittensor SDK](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/sdk/mev-protection)).

MEV Shield examples from one network environment should not be read as representing production
pending-privacy behavior on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from MEV

MEV names value extractable by observing or controlling transaction ordering before block inclusion.
MEV Shield names encrypted mempool protection for sensitive pending contents
([MEV](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)).

- **MEV** — risk category tied to ordering and pending visibility.
- **MEV Shield** — pending encryption for eligible coldkey-signed submissions.

## Distinction from Sandwich Attack

A sandwich attack brackets a victim's pending trade to profit from the price move it causes. MEV
Shield reduces the pending visibility those attacks depend on
([Glossary: MEV](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value),
[Managing Your Stakes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/managing-stake-sdk)).

- **Sandwich attack** — adversarial before-and-after ordering around a visible pending trade.
- **MEV Shield** — pending encryption that hides sensitive contents until inclusion.

## Distinction from Front-Running

Front-running submits a similar transaction ahead of a visible pending trade. MEV Shield limits what
observers can read while the transaction waits in the mempool
([MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield)).

- **Front-running** — higher-priority transaction submitted ahead of the target.
- **MEV Shield** — encrypted pending contents before block inclusion.

## Distinction from Commit Reveal

Commit Reveal conceals validator weight submissions until a later reveal. MEV Shield encrypts
sensitive pending transaction contents such as staking and unstaking until block inclusion
([Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal)).

- **Commit Reveal** — delayed disclosure for validator weight submissions.
- **MEV Shield** — pending encryption for eligible transaction contents.

## Distinction from Price Protection

Price protection caps acceptable execution movement during pool-moving actions. MEV Shield hides
sensitive pending contents before inclusion
([Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection)).

- **Price protection** — tolerance on execution outcomes for the caller's own order.
- **MEV Shield** — limits what observers can read while a transaction is pending.

## Distinction from Transfer

A transfer names a token-movement action once executed. MEV Shield names a pending-privacy step
applied before block inclusion
([Glossary: Transfer](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#transfer)).

- **MEV Shield** — encrypts sensitive pending contents before inclusion.
- **Transfer** — token-movement action independent of pending shielding.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

MEV Shield names pre-inclusion privacy for sensitive extrinsics. Yuma Consensus converts submitted
validator weights into emission shares after evaluation completes
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **MEV Shield** — encrypted mempool protection before block inclusion.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

MEV Shield supports claims about encrypted mempool protection for sensitive transaction contents. It
does not make every transaction risk disappear, prove final inclusion, or replace runtime and block
evidence ([MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield)).
