# Emission Split

Emission split is how a subnet's alpha emission is divided among the participants who share it.
Documentation gives explicit proportions at tempo distribution: 41% to miners, 41% to validators and
their stakers, and 18% to the subnet owner
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## What It Represents

On netuid 1, documentation describes alpha distributed at tempo end as 41% to miners, 41% to
validators and their stakers, and 18% to the subnet owner. Injection adds alpha liquidity each
block; distribution applies these proportions at the tempo boundary. Yuma Consensus subdivides the
miner share by rank and fills validator dividends inside the validators-and-stakers bucket
([Emission: Injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's emission split path. They should not be read as
representing production reward shares on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Coinbase

Coinbase is the per-block process that advances TAO and alpha emission accounting and accumulates
pending emissions. Emission split names the documented 41/41/18 role proportions applied when
distribution runs at the tempo boundary
([Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Coinbase** — per-block emission engine that accumulates pending alpha.
- **Emission split** — role proportions applied at tempo distribution.

## Distinction from Subnet Owner Take

Emission split names all three headline role buckets, including the documented 18 percent owner
share. Subnet owner take names that owner slice specifically as stake routed to the subnet owner
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Emission split** — full 41/41/18 role partition at tempo end.
- **Subnet owner take** — the owner's documented emission share within that partition.

## Distinction from Emission

Emission is the whole reward system: tokens are created, injected into subnet pools, and allocated
to roles. Emission split names only the documented 41/41/18 shares applied at tempo distribution
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Emission** — the wider injection and allocation flow.
- **Emission split** — documented role shares at tempo end.

## Distinction from Tempo

Tempo is the recurring subnet interval. The split is applied when that interval ends, not on every
block ([Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Tempo** — recurring subnet interval for reward accumulation.
- **Emission split** — role partition applied at tempo distribution.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Emission split names the documented 41/41/18 role partition applied when subnet alpha is distributed
at tempo end. Yuma Consensus turns submitted validator weights into miner incentives and validator
dividends inside those role buckets
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Emission split** — documented role proportions at tempo distribution.
- **Yuma Consensus** — weight-to-share settlement inside miner and validator buckets.

## Distinction from Incentives

Emission split is how alpha is divided among roles at tempo end. Incentives name the miner-side
rewards inside the miner share after Yuma aggregates validator weights
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Emission split** — documented role shares at tempo end.
- **Incentives** — miner-side rewards inside the miner share.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Validator take is the cut a validator keeps from delegated stake inside the validator-and-staker
bucket after that bucket is credited. It is not a fourth headline share
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Emission split** — headline 41/41/18 role partition at tempo end.
- **Validator take** — later split inside the validator-and-staker bucket.

## Distinction from Miner Incentive

Emission split is how a subnet's alpha emission is divided among participants. Miner incentive is
the specific portion that flows to miners for their work inside the miner share
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Emission split** — role partition among miners, validators, and owner.
- **Miner incentive** — miner-side payout inside the miner share.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the split at a high level. The 41, 41, and 18 percent figures are the documented
proportions, but how much total alpha a subnet emits varies over time. Live amounts should be
checked from current Bittensor sources rather than inferred from the proportions alone
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).
