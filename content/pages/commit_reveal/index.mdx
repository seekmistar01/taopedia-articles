# Commit Reveal

Commit Reveal is a Bittensor mechanism that delays when validator weights become visible. The
official [Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) reference frames
the mechanism around concealing fresh weights before they can be reused by observers.

The term is about visibility timing, not about changing the meaning of validator weights.
[Validator weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights) remain the
evaluation signals that validators submit for subnet consensus.

## Why It Exists

The [weight copying](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor) problem
comes from visible weight information being useful to validators that did not independently evaluate
subnet work. If new weights are immediately visible, a later submitter can copy or closely follow
the public pattern.

Commit Reveal reduces that timing advantage by making fresh weights unavailable until a reveal
point. The [Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) documentation
describes the mechanism as a protection around the weight-submission path, not as a replacement for
independent evaluation.

## Commit and Reveal Timing

At a high level, the commit side records concealed weight information and the reveal side later
makes the usable weight data available. The
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) reference describes this
two-step timing model as the core of the mechanism.

That timing model keeps the article boundary narrow. Commit Reveal answers when weight information
can be seen; [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) explains how
available validator signals are aggregated into subnet outcomes.

## Drand Time-Lock Context

Commit Reveal uses Drand time-lock encryption to prevent concealed weight data from being decrypted
before the intended reveal time
([Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal)). That source of delayed
decryption is why the mechanism can enforce a visibility gap without relying only on convention.

For readers, Drand is best understood here as timing infrastructure for hidden weights. The article
does not need operational detail to explain the role Drand plays in the Commit Reveal mechanism.

## Subnet Configuration Context

Commit Reveal is controlled through subnet hyperparameters. The
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) reference names the settings
that enable the mechanism and define the reveal period, while the
[subnet hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters) reference
places those settings in the broader subnet-configuration vocabulary.

This makes Commit Reveal a configurable subnet mechanism rather than a universal description of
every validator-weight path. The important reader-facing point is that the visibility delay belongs
to subnet configuration.

## Consensus Context

Commit Reveal and Yuma Consensus sit at different points in the validator-weight path. Commit Reveal
concerns delayed visibility before weights are available, while
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) concerns aggregation after
validator signals are available for consensus processing.

Keeping that order clear prevents the terms from collapsing into one idea. Commit Reveal protects
the timing of weight disclosure; Yuma Consensus describes how disclosed signals affect subnet
incentives.

## Reader Boundary

Commit Reveal should be read as a visibility-control mechanism for validator weights. The
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) page, the
[weight-copying](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor) discussion,
and the [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) reference together
show why timing, copying resistance, and consensus aggregation are related but distinct.

This article does not provide validator strategy, subnet-configuration instructions, or performance
claims. Its purpose is to define the mechanism and its place in the weight-submission and consensus
path.
