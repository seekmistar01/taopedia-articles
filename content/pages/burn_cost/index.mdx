# Burn Cost

Burn cost is the TAO amount required when creating a new Bittensor subnet. The official
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost) defines it as the amount of
TAO recycled when a new subnet is created, and the
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost) guide uses the
same term for the current cost to register a new subnet.

## Subnet Creation Cost

For readers, the important point is that burn cost belongs to subnet creation. It is not a general
transaction fee, a staking amount, or a quality score for an existing subnet. It is the cost
attached to registering a new subnet at the time the creation action is made.

The official [Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost)
page describes the cost as dynamic. It can fall gradually over time and increase when new subnets
are created, so a static article should not state a fixed current value.

## Why "Burn" Is a Careful Term

The name can be confusing because Bittensor documentation also distinguishes burning from recycling.
The official
[local subnet creation guide](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/local-build/create-subnet#subnet-creation-cost)
notes that the subnet creation cost is labeled "burn cost" even though the cost is technically
recycled rather than burned.

That accounting detail matters for interpretation. Burn cost is the familiar label used by the
subnet creation flow, while recycling describes how the TAO is treated in the documentation's
token-accounting language.

## Reader Boundary

Burn cost should be read as a dynamic creation-cost concept, not as advice about when to create a
subnet or as a prediction of future cost. The official
[BTCLI reference](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/btcli/#btcli-subnets-burn-cost) documents how
interfaces can show the current value, but Taopedia's role is to explain what the term means rather
than mirror a live number.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Burn cost applies when registering a new subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator) notes that subnet
creators can configure multiple incentive mechanisms to distribute emissions across different types
of work or evaluation criteria.

For readers, burn cost still names the TAO amount for subnet creation. A newly created subnet may
later run more than one incentive mechanism, but burn cost belongs to the creation step.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)

## Relationship to Subtensor

Burn cost and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while burn cost names the dynamic TAO
amount recycled when a new subnet is created on that chain layer. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost) defines burn
cost as the amount of TAO recycled when a new subnet is created.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while burn cost names a subnet-creation cost recorded
through that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)

## Relationship to Recycling and Burning

Burn cost and recycling and burning are related but different parts of Bittensor tokenomics
vocabulary. Recycling and burning name how removed TAO and alpha tokens are accounted for, while
burn cost names the TAO amount required when creating a new subnet. The
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
distinguishes recycled tokens from burned tokens, and the official
[local subnet creation guide](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/local-build/create-subnet#subnet-creation-cost)
notes that subnet creation cost is labeled burn cost even though the TAO is technically recycled
rather than burned.

For readers, burn cost names the creation-cost label, while recycling and burning name the supply
accounting language that explains how that TAO is treated.

References:
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning),
[Local subnet creation: Subnet creation cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/local-build/create-subnet#subnet-creation-cost)

## Relationship to Netuid

Burn cost and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A netuid identifies
a subnet on the network, while burn cost names the TAO amount recycled when that subnet is first
created and registered. The
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level, and the
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost) guide uses burn
cost for the current cost to register a new subnet.

For readers, burn cost names the creation-step cost, while a netuid identifies the subnet that
creation action brings into scope.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost)

## Relationship to Extrinsics

Burn cost and extrinsics are related but different parts of Bittensor chain action vocabulary.
Extrinsics name signed transactions and calls submitted to Subtensor, while burn cost names the TAO
amount recycled when a subnet-creation extrinsic is processed on chain. The
[Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics) describes
signed transactions submitted to the chain, and the
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost) defines burn
cost as the amount of TAO recycled when a new subnet is created.

For readers, extrinsics name submitted chain actions, while burn cost names the creation-cost amount
associated with the subnet-registration action among those submissions.

References: [Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)

## Relationship to Crowdloans

Burn cost and crowdloans address related but different parts of Bittensor subnet creation
vocabulary. Burn cost names the dynamic TAO amount required to create a new subnet, while crowdloans
are a collective funding mechanism through which community members contribute TAO to help cover that
creation cost. The [Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans) documentation
describes crowdloans as enabling community-level participation in subnet funding, with contributor
shares recorded on chain and the subnet's alpha emissions distributed pro-rata to contributors after
being swapped to TAO. The
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost) defines burn
cost as the TAO recycled when a new subnet is created.

For readers, burn cost names the TAO amount the creation process requires, while crowdloans name a
community path for collectively covering that amount. The burn cost is still paid when using a
crowdloan; crowdloans distribute the registration payment across contributors rather than requiring
one participant to fund the full cost alone.

References: [Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)

## Relationship to Coinbase

Burn cost and coinbase are related but different parts of TAO supply flow. Burn cost is the TAO
amount recycled when a new subnet is created. The
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission,
accumulates pending emissions, and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch boundaries, while the
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost) describes the
subnet-creation cost.

The two sit on different sides of the supply ledger. Burn cost removes TAO from a creator at subnet
registration and recycles it into issuance so it can later be emitted again; coinbase is the
per-block mechanism that performs that emission. Burn cost is a one-time creation charge that
returns TAO to the emittable pool, while coinbase is the recurring engine that pays new emission out
of it. One names what a subnet creator spends to register, the other names the protocol process that
drives ongoing emission.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from local testing to testchain and then mainchain. For burn
cost, that sequence changes how readers should interpret evidence about subnet creation costs.

In local testing, burn cost can show what TAO amount is required to create a subnet inside an
isolated environment. That is useful for checking the subnet registration mechanism, but it remains
isolated feedback from a local environment and does not reflect production cost dynamics.

On testchain, burn cost behavior can be observed in a shared testing network. This gives stronger
evidence about how the dynamic cost changes with creation demand than a private local run, while
still keeping the result separate from production mainchain cost values.

On mainchain, burn cost is the live dynamic TAO amount required when registering a new Bittensor
subnet. The [Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)
defines burn cost as the amount of TAO recycled when a new subnet is created, so production burn
cost context is tied to the current cost on the active network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A burn cost value observed in one environment should not
be read as identical evidence about subnet creation cost in another environment.

For readers, this keeps burn cost examples from sounding stronger than their environment supports.
Local, testchain, and mainchain contexts can all describe subnet creation cost, but they do not
carry the same interpretive weight.
