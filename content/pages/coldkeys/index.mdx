# Coldkeys (Bittensor)

A coldkey is the long-term, security-sensitive key in Bittensor. It controls your TAO and stake and
is used to sign financial operations.

References: [Wallets (learnbittensor.org)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets),
[Working with Keys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/working-with-keys),
[BTCLI Reference](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/btcli)

## Responsibilities

- Hold and secure TAO balances and delegated stake.
- Authorize staking, unstaking, moving stake, and transfers.
- Own one or more hotkeys used for operational roles.

## Security practices

- Keep the coldkey and its seed phrase offline when possible.
- Never place the coldkey on internet-exposed nodes used for mining/validating.
- Verify official binaries and documentation before performing key operations.

## Operational relationship

- Coldkey provides ownership and control; hotkeys derive authority for operational actions.
- If a hotkey is compromised, the coldkey can rotate or revoke without exposing funds (assuming best
  practices).

## Tooling

- `btcli` and the Wallet SDK support creating, importing, and managing coldkeys.
- Use minimal on-chain interactions from environments you fully trust.

## Relationship to Hotkeys

Coldkeys and hotkeys occupy opposite ends of the Bittensor key model. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey) describes a coldkey as the
security-sensitive key that holds funds and stake, while
[Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey) are the operational keys used
for day-to-day network actions such as registering and submitting weights.

For readers, a coldkey should not be confused with a hotkey despite both being part of the same
wallet. The coldkey signs financial operations and owns one or more hotkeys, but it is not present
on live network nodes. A hotkey, by contrast, is designed to run on online infrastructure without
exposing funds.

References: [Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey),
[Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey),
[Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)

## Relationship to Coldkey Swap

A coldkey and coldkey swap address the same on-chain identity through different operations. A
coldkey is the persistent cryptographic key that holds TAO balances and controls the hotkeys and
subnet registrations associated with a wallet. A coldkey swap is the staged migration procedure that
replaces a compromised or aging coldkey with a new one while preserving everything the old coldkey
controlled. The [Rotate or Swap your Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap)
documentation describes the swap as an operation that moves on-chain identity—including TAO balances
and subnet ownership—to a new key pair when the current key may have been leaked or compromised.

For readers, the coldkey is the identity being protected, while coldkey swap is the lifecycle
operation that re-homes that identity when key rotation is necessary. The swap does not change the
hotkeys owned by the old coldkey or the subnet registrations associated with them; the new coldkey
inherits all of that state. This inheritance is what distinguishes a coldkey swap from an ordinary
transfer: a transfer moves funds, while a swap migrates the identity and ownership itself. The
[Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets) documentation
describes how a coldkey owns one or more hotkeys, clarifying why key migration rather than fund
movement is the correct framing when the coldkey itself is the security concern.

References: [Rotate or Swap your Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap),
[Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)
