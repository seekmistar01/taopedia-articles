# Effective Stake

Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake) defines it as the
total staked TAO amount of a delegate, including the delegate's own TAO and TAO delegated by
nominators.

## Delegate Context

A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators. Effective stake is read
in that same staking context: it describes the stake associated with the delegate, not a separate
asset type or a standalone participant role.

## Own and Delegated TAO

The term combines two sources of staked TAO for the same delegate: the delegate's own staked TAO and
TAO supplied by nominators. This makes effective stake a combined staking measure rather than only a
description of self-stake.

## Relationship to Stake Weight

Effective stake is an amount of staked TAO. It is distinct from stake weight, which the
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) defines as the computed
total stake value for a validator that determines their consensus power and emissions in a subnet.

## Delegate Stake Context

The [Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)
defines delegate stake as the amount of TAO staked by the delegate themselves. Effective stake names
the combined total that includes that self-staked amount plus TAO delegated by nominators, so
delegate stake is the self-staked component within effective stake.

References:
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)

## Relationship to Hotkey-Coldkey Pair

Effective stake and the hotkey-coldkey pair refer to related but different parts of delegate
staking. The
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)
describes effective stake as the total staked TAO amount of a delegate, and the
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)
describes the hotkey-coldkey pair as the authentication mechanism for delegates and nominators.

For readers, effective stake names the combined TAO amount associated with a delegate, while the
hotkey-coldkey pair names the key relationship that controls who can operate that stake. The stake
amount and the key pair that authorizes it are separate concepts, so effective stake should not be
read as a key-pair term.

References:
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Effective stake describes staked TAO associated with a delegate inside a subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, effective stake still names the combined staking total for a delegate in one subnet.
That subnet may run more than one incentive mechanism, but effective stake remains part of the same
subnet context.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Relationship to Autostaking

Effective stake and autostaking address related but different parts of Bittensor staking vocabulary.
Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate — the delegate's own TAO plus TAO
delegated by nominators — while autostaking automatically routes a miner's emission proceeds to a
staking position on a chosen validator. The
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)
defines effective stake as the combined staking total of a delegate, and the
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) documentation describes
autostaking as automatically staking a miner's emissions to a chosen validator.

When a miner enables autostaking, emission proceeds are automatically staked to the chosen validator
rather than remaining unallocated. This automatic staking can increase the total stake associated
with that validator over time as emissions arrive. Effective stake names the combined total of
staked TAO on a delegate at any given point; autostaking is one mechanism by which that total can
grow — routing miner emission proceeds to the validator's staking context without the miner manually
submitting each staking action.

References:
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the term at a high level. It does not list live delegate balances, compare
delegates, recommend staking choices, or describe staking steps. Readers should use official staking
references for staking-specific actions.

## Relationship to Coinbase

Effective stake and coinbase are related but different parts of subnet consensus and emissions.
Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate, including its own TAO and TAO
delegated by nominators. The
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission,
accumulates pending emissions, and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch boundaries, while the
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)
describes the delegate's total staked amount.

The connection is indirect. A delegate's effective stake contributes to its stake weight, which in
turn shapes its consensus influence; coinbase separately triggers the epoch and
emission-distribution process. Effective stake is a staked-amount measure on the staking side, while
coinbase is the per-block mechanism that advances emissions and fires the epoch calculations.
Effective stake is not itself a direct input that coinbase reads; it matters to consensus only
through the stake-weight relationship, whereas coinbase is the protocol process that runs the epoch
cycle.
