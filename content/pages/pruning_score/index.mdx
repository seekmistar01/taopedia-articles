# Pruning Score

A pruning score expresses how a subnet decides which participant makes way when it is full and a
newcomer wants in. It captures a simple principle of Bittensor: a place in a subnet is held by
continuing to earn, and the participant that has earned the least is the one asked to leave
([Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)).

## What It Represents

Pruning score ranks emission standing among subnet participants when a full subnet must displace one
incumbent for a new registration. The lowest-scoring participant is the first candidate for removal
under documented deregistration rules
([Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's slot competition path. They should not be read as
representing production displacement outcomes on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Activity Cutoff

Pruning score reflects emission standing when a full subnet must displace one participant. Activity
cutoff is the early-epoch block window within which a validator must submit weights to count in the
current Yuma Consensus pass
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#activitycutoff),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing for single-slot displacement on a full subnet.
- **Activity cutoff** — epoch block window for weight submission to count.

## Distinction from Rank

Pruning score reflects emission standing when a full subnet must displace one participant. Rank is
the consensus standing that divides miner-side rewards after validator weights are aggregated
([Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing for slot displacement on a full subnet.
- **Rank** — consensus standing that drives miner-side emission shares.

## Distinction from Trust

Pruning score reflects emission standing when a full subnet must displace one participant. Trust is
the share of validators that assign a non-zero weight to a miner, measuring how broadly that miner
is supported before consensus
([Glossary: Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing for slot displacement on a full subnet.
- **Trust** — the share of validators assigning non-zero weight to a miner.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

Pruning score reflects how much a participant has been earning on a subnet. Immunity period is the
post-registration window that can protect a newcomer from early displacement
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing among participants on a full subnet.
- **Immunity period** — post-registration window that can block early displacement.

## Distinction from UID Slot

Pruning score ranks which incumbent is weakest when a full subnet must free one position. A UID slot
is the indexed participant position that displacement can vacate
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings standing used to rank displacement candidates.
- **UID slot** — the indexed participant position on a netuid.

## Distinction from Active UID

An active UID records whether a neuron currently meets activity conditions on the metagraph. Pruning
score names earned emission standing when slot displacement is driven by registration pressure on a
full subnet ([Glossary: Active UID](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#active-uid),
[Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings standing for displacement ranking on a full subnet.
- **Active UID** — whether a slot currently satisfies activity rules.

## Distinction from UID Trimming

Pruning score ranks which incumbent gives way when a full subnet admits one new registration. UID
trimming is an owner-initiated bulk resize that lowers the subnet toward a chosen maximum
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[UID Trimming](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/uid-trimming)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings standing for single-slot displacement at registration time.
- **UID trimming** — owner-initiated bulk reduction of low emitters.

## Distinction from Validator Deregistration

Pruning score ranks which UID is most expendable when a full subnet must displace one. Validator
deregistration is the validator-specific removal that frees the selected slot
([Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — role-neutral ranking that selects which UID is removed.
- **Validator deregistration** — validator-side removal that frees a slot.

## Distinction from Subnet Validator

Pruning score ranks which neuron is most expendable when slots are contested. A subnet validator is
a neuron that evaluates miners and submits weights; validators are not exempt from that ranking
([Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings standing that ranks displacement candidates.
- **Subnet validator** — evaluation role that still faces displacement ranking.

## Distinction from Deregistration

Pruning score reflects how much a subnet participant has been earning when slots are contested.
Deregistration is the removal of a participant when capacity and replacement rules apply
([Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing that can put a participant closest to displacement.
- **Deregistration** — the removal step when a slot must be freed.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Pruning score names the earnings-based metric that ranks which neuron may be displaced when a
subnet's UID slots are full. It describes metagraph capacity competition, not the settlement step
that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on the selected netuid. It
reads the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the
result into miner incentives and validator dividend shares—including the earnings that feed pruning
rankings over time ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate pruning score from deregistration and from validator role vocabulary. Yuma
credits emissions that shape earnings standing; pruning score ranks who is most expendable when
`MaxAllowedUids` capacity forces a slot replacement—not how weights were aggregated
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Readers should treat pruning score as a displacement-ranking metric on one netuid, and Yuma as the
recurring settlement pass that allocates rewards from consensus.

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based ranking for displacement when UID slots are contested.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page describes the concept at a high level. Which participant actually stands lowest on a given
subnet at a given time is live state that shifts as earnings change, and is best read from current
chain data at the time of observation
([Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)).

## Distinction from Subnet Miner

A pruning score and a subnet miner relate as a metric and the participant it is computed for. A
subnet miner is the participant that performs the subnet's task at a UID, while the pruning score is
the per-UID value the chain uses to decide which neuron is dropped when a full subnet must make room
for a new registration
([Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Subnet miner** — the participant performing the task at a UID.
- **Pruning score** — the per-UID value that decides which neuron is deregistered.
