# Root Claim Type

Root claim type is the choice a Root Subnet staker makes about how their alpha dividends are handled
when a claim is processed. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) names two
options — Keep, where dividends remain as the alpha of the originating subnet, and Swap, where
dividends are converted to TAO and restaked on Root.

In Taopedia terms, root claim type names a dividend-handling preference, not a separate chain
action. It belongs alongside staking (adding stake), unstaking (withdrawing stake), moving stake
(re-pointing delegation), and transferring stake (reassigning coldkey ownership) as one of the
participant-facing choices that shape how a staked position behaves over time.

## Why Root Claim Type Exists

A participant who stakes on a Root Subnet validator receives alpha dividends generated by the
subnets those validators weight. The participant may want those dividends to stay invested in the
subnets that produced them, or may prefer to convert them to TAO and keep accumulating on Root. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) places
root claim type as a per-staker setting that influences every claim, whether automatic or manual.

## What Keep Mode Does

Keep mode lets accumulated alpha dividends stay on the subnet that generated them. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
Keep as the option that keeps earned alpha on the originating subnet, favoring remaining invested in
the producing subnet while accepting the price movement that can follow.

Reference: [Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## What Swap Mode Does

Swap mode converts accumulated alpha dividends to TAO at the current alpha-to-TAO rate and restakes
the resulting TAO on the Root Subnet. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
Swap as the default option, favoring long-term TAO accumulation and minimum exposure to per-subnet
price movement.

Reference: [Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Automatic Versus Manual Claim Timing

Root claim type applies whenever accumulated dividends are processed, whether that processing
happens on an automatic schedule or through a manual claim action. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) treats
Keep and Swap as dividend outcomes tied to the staker's standing preference, not as separate claim
modes with different eligibility rules. A staker who selects Keep still receives alpha on the
originating subnet when a claim runs; a staker who selects Swap still receives TAO restaked on Root
when a claim runs.

Readers should not read root claim type as a trigger for claiming. It names how dividends are
handled after a claim executes, while claim timing and thresholds belong to the broader root claim
mechanism described in official staking documentation. Operational steps for checking pending
dividends or initiating a manual claim stay outside this concept article.

References:
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Glossary: Root Staker](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-staker)

## Alpha Exposure When Keep Is Selected

Keep mode leaves earned alpha on the subnet that produced the dividend. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
that choice as remaining invested in the producing subnet rather than converting proceeds to TAO on
Root. In Taopedia terms, Keep ties the staker's dividend outcome to subnet alpha rather than to
network-token accumulation on Subnet Zero.

That exposure is intentional at the preference level. Alpha balances on an ordinary subnet can move
with subnet-specific market conditions, while TAO held or restaked through Root follows a different
asset context. Readers comparing Keep and Swap should treat the distinction as a dividend asset
choice, not as a statement about validator quality or subnet ranking. Live alpha-to-TAO rates and
pool conditions belong in operational references rather than in this definition.

References:
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)

## TAO Accumulation When Swap Is Selected

Swap mode converts accumulated alpha dividends to TAO at the prevailing conversion rate and restakes
the resulting TAO on the Root Subnet. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) presents
Swap as the default preference and frames it as favoring long-term TAO accumulation with reduced
exposure to per-subnet alpha price movement. Root claim type therefore names a network-token outcome
for dividends that originated as subnet alpha.

Readers should not equate Swap with unstaking from Root or with moving stake between validators.
Swap only describes what happens to dividend proceeds at claim time. The underlying Root Subnet
delegation remains in place unless the staker performs a separate staking action. Conversion rates,
slippage, and restake destinations at execution time are operational details documented in official
root claim and staking material rather than repeated here.

References:
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)

## Relationship to Root Claims

Root claim type is the preference applied inside each root claim. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
the broader claim mechanism that processes accumulated dividends, and Keep and Swap are the dividend
outcomes that mechanism can produce.

For readers, root claims name the act of processing accumulated dividends, while root claim type
names which of the two dividend outcomes the staker has chosen.

Reference: [Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Relationship to Root Subnet

Root claim type is a setting that only applies to stake placed on Root Subnet validators. The
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)
describes the Root Subnet as the subnet-agnostic staking context where TAO can be delegated, and
root claim type governs what happens to alpha dividends earned through that delegation.

For readers, the Root Subnet names where the staking position lives, while root claim type names
what happens to the dividends earned from that position.

References:
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Relationship to Root Staker

Root claim type is the dividend-handling setting chosen by a root staker. The
[Glossary: Root Staker](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-staker) describes a
root staker as a participant who stakes on Root Subnet validators, and that role is the participant
to whom root claim type applies.

For readers, a root staker names who holds the position, while root claim type names how that
position's dividends are handled.

References: [Glossary: Root Staker](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-staker),
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Reader Boundary

This article defines root claim type as a concept. It does not describe how to set the type, list
current claim thresholds, or quote specific alpha-to-TAO rates. Readers should use the official root
claim documentation and the live chain state for operational detail.

Reference: [Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For root claim
type, that sequence changes how readers should interpret evidence about dividend-handling preference
and claim processing behavior.

In localnet, root claim type mechanics can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet claim type
settings and dividend routing outcomes reflect local chain configuration and do not represent
production Root Subnet state.

On testnet, root claim type behavior can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet
dividend processing outcomes are separate from mainnet claim state.

On mainnet, root claim type is the live per-staker preference that determines whether accumulated
Root Subnet dividends are kept as alpha or swapped to TAO and restaked on root. The
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)
documentation describes the claim type mechanics that apply on the production network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A root claim type example from one environment should not
be read as representing dividend routing behavior in another environment.
