# Delegate

A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators and performs validation
tasks in one or more subnets
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

The term sits where Bittensor's validation role and delegated-stake vocabulary meet. It should not
be confused with the nominator role that supplies TAO support.

## Validation and Staking Role

Delegate is a role term for the receiving side of a staking relationship. The delegate receives
staked TAO and performs validation work for subnet consensus
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

This makes delegate more specific than a generic staking reference. It names a validator role that
can receive staked TAO from others while carrying out validation tasks.

## Nominator Boundary

A delegate is paired with the accounts that supply stake. A nominator is the TAO-holder role that
stakes with validators, while delegate names the validator that receives that stake
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The relationship is directional: nominators supply delegated stake, and delegates receive that stake
while performing validation work. Keeping the two sides separate prevents delegate from being read
as the account role that supplies TAO.

## Delegate Stake and Effective Stake

Delegate stake and effective stake refine the staking side of the delegate role. Delegate stake is
the TAO the delegate stakes themselves. Effective stake is the broader total associated with the
delegate, including the delegate's own TAO and TAO delegated by nominators
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

These terms keep the role and stake amounts separate. Delegate names the receiving validator role;
delegate stake names the self-staked component; effective stake names the combined total behind that
role.

## Delegated Stake Boundary

Delegated stake supports the delegate's validator role; it does not turn the nominator into the
validator for that subnet. The delegation overview describes nominators staking to validators, while
the delegate glossary entry keeps delegate attached to the receiving validator role
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

That boundary keeps stake support separate from role assignment. A nominator can add stake behind a
delegate, but delegate still names the validation side of the relationship.

## Permit Context

Validation rights are separate from the role label. A validator permit is the permission flag for
validation rights in a subnet
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

That boundary keeps delegate from carrying every permission detail by itself. Delegate names the
staking-and-validation role; permit vocabulary describes validation rights for the relevant subnet
context.

## Autostaking and Emission Context

Delegates are a destination for miner autostaking. When a miner uses autostaking, mining emissions
are automatically routed as stake to the configured delegate rather than requiring a separate
staking transaction. The delegate, as the receiving validator, earns dividend outcomes as staked TAO
accumulates behind its hotkey
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

This connection puts delegate in the miner-reward path. A miner's autostaking configuration
determines which delegate receives the emission-derived stake, which keeps delegate selection from
being purely a validator-side decision.

Yuma Consensus shapes how delegate validators receive reward outcomes. Validator dividends depend on
how the consensus process aggregates weight signals, and delegates serve as the recipient side of
those dividend outcomes ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, delegate belongs at the intersection of validation work, received staked TAO, and
dividend outcomes. Autostaking explains one source of incoming stake; Yuma Consensus explains how
validation work translates into the dividend outcomes that make that stake worth holding behind the
delegate hotkey ([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For delegates on a
subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how staking totals and dividend examples should be
read.

In localnet, delegate relationships on netuid 1 can be tested in isolation. Localnet effective stake
and validator take reflect that test chain rather than production delegation outcomes.

On testnet, delegates on netuid 1 apply in a shared non-production network where stake support and
emission returns are separate from mainnet.

On mainnet, delegate interpretation on netuid 1 concerns live production validator relationships,
take splits, and Yuma dividend outcomes on the selected subnet
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Delegate examples from one environment should not be read
as staking outcomes on another network.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation names the support relationship by which nominated TAO attaches to a validator hotkey.
Delegate names the receiving validator role on the other side of that relationship
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation** — support relationship attaching nominated TAO to a hotkey.
- **Delegate** — receiving validator role that performs validation work.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake is the combined total behind a delegate hotkey after self-stake and nomination are
counted. Delegate names the receiving validator role, not that measured total
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

- **Delegate** — receiving validator role in the staking relationship.
- **Effective stake** — combined self-stake plus nominated support behind the hotkey.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Validator take names the share a delegate keeps from delegated-stake emissions before the remainder
returns to nominators. Delegate names the receiving validator role, not that split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Delegate** — receiving validator role in the staking relationship.
- **Validator take** — commission on delegated-stake returns at distribution.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Stake weight names the consensus influence measure derived from stake behind a delegate hotkey.
Delegate names the validator role that receives support and submits weights
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Delegate** — receiving validator role behind the hotkey.
- **Stake weight** — consensus influence derived from stake behind that role.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Autostaking routes a miner's earned emissions into a validator-staking position. Delegate names the
receiving validator role that accumulated stake sits behind
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

- **Autostaking** — automatic routing of miner emissions into validator stake.
- **Delegate** — receiving validator role that holds the resulting support.

## Distinction from Root Validator

A delegate on a subnet such as netuid 1 is a subnet validator that receives nominated stake and
performs validation work inside that subnet's incentive mechanism, while a root validator is
associated with the Root Subnet, also called Subnet Zero, where miners do not operate and stake is
not bound to one task subnet
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator),
[Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)).

For readers on netuid 1, delegate vocabulary names the receiving validator role inside a mining
subnet's staking relationship. Root validator vocabulary names the validator role on Subnet Zero
rather than evaluation inside one task subnet
([Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegate** — receiving subnet validator role on a selected netuid.
- **Root validator** — validator role on Subnet Zero without task-subnet mining.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

Delegate names the parent validator role that holds stake on a subnet such as netuid 1, while a
child hotkey is a separate authorized key that parent uses to validate with re-delegated stake on
that subnet ([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)).

- **Delegate** — parent validator role that holds stake on the selected netuid.
- **Child hotkey** — authorized worker key that signs validation using routed stake.

## Distinction from Nominator

A delegate is the subnet validator role that receives staked TAO from delegators. A nominator is the
TAO-holder role that stakes behind a delegate
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegate** — receiving validator role that performs validation and earns validator take.
- **Nominator** — supplying role that delegates TAO behind a chosen validator.

The two words name opposite ends of the same staking relationship rather than interchangeable role
labels.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators and performs validation
tasks in one or more subnets. Yuma Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts validator
weight submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Delegate** — validator role that receives delegated stake.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Delegate names the receiving validator role in a staking relationship. It is not the nominator role
that supplies TAO support, nor a balance report or dividend quote
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).
