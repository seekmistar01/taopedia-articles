# Staking

Staking is the process of attaching TAO support to a validator in Bittensor
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term is process vocabulary. It should be read separately from stake, which names the amount
associated with validator support after staking has occurred.

## Validator Support

Staking is read through validator support. The staking and delegation overview explains that
validator total stake can include stake delegated by others, and that this support affects consensus
power and emissions
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

This makes staking different from the validator role itself. The validator performs validation;
staking attaches support to that validator's subnet position.

## Relationship to Stake

Staking and stake are closely related but not identical. Staking names the process, while stake
names the associated token amount after support is attached to a validator position
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

For readers, the distinction is action versus amount. Staking is what attaches support; stake is the
amount that becomes relevant for consensus and emissions.

## Relationship to Delegation

Official Bittensor documentation uses staking and delegation as closely related terms. Delegation
describes staking TAO to a validator, while staking is the broader process name
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

In Taopedia prose, staking emphasizes the process. Delegation emphasizes the support relationship
between a TAO holder and the validator receiving that support.

## Consensus Context

Staking matters because validator stake affects consensus power and emissions. The staking overview
connects validator stake to consensus power, while Yuma Consensus describes how validator signals
and stake context feed emission calculation
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

This article does not calculate emissions or compare validators. It only defines staking as the
validator-support process.

## Relationship to Autostaking

Staking and autostaking describe related paths for attaching support. Staking is the general process
of attaching TAO support to a validator, while autostaking routes miner emissions into a staking
position automatically
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

The distinction is trigger rather than destination. Autostaking feeds an ordinary staking position;
it does not create a separate staking category.

## Reader Boundary

Staking should be read as a concept article term. It is not a validator recommendation, balance
report, fee explanation, or operational staking guide
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The stable article-level point is that staking names attaching TAO support to a validator in
Bittensor.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. Staking concepts
apply across the Bittensor lifecycle: stake attached on testnet is separate from mainnet, and
validator support or reward outcomes from one environment do not represent live production staking
behavior in another.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Staking examples or reward outcomes from one environment
should not be read as representing production subnet staking behavior in another environment.
