# Delegate

A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) defines a delegate as a
subnet validator that receives staked TAO tokens from delegators and performs validation tasks in
one or more subnets.

## Staking Context

Delegates sit at the boundary between subnet validation and staking. The glossary definition ties
the role to validation work, while
[Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake) distinguishes
the delegate's own staked TAO from TAO supplied by delegators.

## Relationship to Nominators

A [nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) supplies stake to a
validator, while a delegate is the validator that receives that delegated stake. A delegate may
receive stake from many nominators while performing validation in one or more subnets.

## Relationship to Effective Stake

The [Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)
defines effective stake as the total staked TAO of a delegate — their own TAO plus TAO delegated by
others. Delegate stake is the self-staked portion of that total, so a delegate's effective stake
combines personal stake with stake supplied by nominators.

## Relationship to Child Hotkeys

A delegate and a child hotkey both relate to performing validation, but they name different things.
A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators and performs validation
tasks, as the [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) defines it. A
child hotkey, by the [Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)
documentation, is a separate key that a parent hotkey authorizes to validate on its behalf by
re-delegating a portion of the parent's stake to it, without transferring ownership of that stake.

For readers, a delegate is the validator role that holds and validates with delegated stake, while a
child hotkey is a key a delegate can authorize to carry out that validation in its place. The two
are not interchangeable: a delegate receives stake from delegators and is the accountable validator,
whereas a child hotkey validates for a parent using re-delegated stake-weight it does not own. A
delegate authorizes a child hotkey to validate on its behalf; the child does not take ownership of
the delegators' stake.

References: [Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the term at a high level. It does not list live delegates, rank validators,
report stake balances, or describe staking steps. Readers should use official staking references for
staking-specific details.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

A delegate performs validation work inside Bittensor subnets. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that validators must evaluate miners separately for each mechanism.

For readers, a delegate still names a validator that receives delegated stake. When a subnet runs
more than one mechanism, evaluation duties may span separate mechanism contexts inside the same
subnet.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Relationship to Subtensor

Delegate and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while delegate names a subnet validator
role that receives staked TAO from delegators and performs validation work recorded on that chain
layer. The [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)
describes the blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) defines a delegate
as a subnet validator that receives staked TAO tokens from delegators.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while delegate names a validator role that receives
delegated stake on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)

## Relationship to Netuid

Delegate and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A netuid identifies a
subnet on the network, while delegate names a subnet validator that can perform validation work in
one or more subnets and receive delegated stake tied to that participation. The
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level, and the
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) defines a delegate
as a subnet validator that performs validation tasks in one or more subnets.

For readers, a netuid selects which subnet context is in scope, while delegate names a validator
that can receive delegated stake for work in that subnet context.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)

## Relationship to Extrinsics

Delegate and extrinsics are related but different parts of Bittensor chain action vocabulary.
Extrinsics name signed transactions and calls submitted to Subtensor, while delegate names the
validator role whose staking and validation activity can involve those chain actions. The
[Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics) describes
signed transactions submitted to the chain, and the
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) defines a delegate
as a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators.

For readers, extrinsics name submitted chain actions, while delegate names the validator role that
staking-related and validation-related actions can involve.

References: [Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)

## Relationship to Subnet Validator

Delegate and a subnet validator are related but different scopes in Bittensor subnet role
vocabulary. A subnet validator evaluates miner work and submits weights on chain, while delegate
names that role when it also receives staked TAO from delegators. The
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)
describes the validator role, and the
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) defines a delegate
as a subnet validator that receives staked TAO tokens from delegators.

For readers, subnet validator names the evaluation role, while delegate names the same role in the
specific staking context where others delegate TAO to that validator.

References:
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)

## Relationship to Autostaking

Delegate and autostaking address related but different parts of Bittensor staking vocabulary. A
delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators and performs validation
tasks in one or more subnets, while autostaking is a mechanism that automatically routes a miner's
emission proceeds into a staking position on a chosen validator without requiring a manual staking
step. The [Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) places
the delegate role at the validator level, while the
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) documentation describes
autostaking as automatically staking a miner's emissions to a chosen validator.

When a miner configures autostaking, they select which validator's staking position receives their
emission proceeds. That validator is a delegate in the staking vocabulary sense: a subnet validator
whose effective stake can grow from the emissions that autostaking routes to it, alongside TAO
supplied by other delegators.

References: [Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)

## Relationship to Coinbase

A delegate and coinbase are related but different parts of Bittensor's validation and emission
picture. A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators and performs
validation work. The
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission,
accumulates pending emissions, and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch boundaries, while the
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) describes the
validator role.

The connection is that a delegate participates in the consensus rounds coinbase triggers and can
earn validator dividends when coinbase distributes each epoch's emissions. The delegate's received
stake contributes to its stake weight and consensus influence; coinbase is the per-block mechanism
that runs the epoch rounds and advances the emission distribution from which the delegate and its
delegators are paid. A delegate names the validator role on the staking and evaluation side, while
coinbase names the recurring engine that drives the emission cycle that role takes part in.
