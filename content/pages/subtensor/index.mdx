# Subtensor

Subtensor is Bittensor's blockchain layer. The official
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) defines it as Bittensor's
layer 1 blockchain and describes it as the system of record for transactions and rankings. In
Taopedia, the term is most useful as the chain context behind subnet activity, consensus, and
incentive accounting.

## System of Record

The Bittensor blockchain records balances and transactions for participants in the network. It also
provides the record that subnet-related activity can rely on when work, staking context, and rewards
need a shared chain state. The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction) describes this
blockchain role separately from the subnet markets that produce and evaluate digital commodities.

Subtensor therefore should not be read as a single subnet or a specific digital commodity. Subnets
define the work market; Subtensor supplies the chain layer that records the network activity around
those markets.

## Consensus Context

Subtensor also provides the on-chain context for Yuma Consensus. The
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) documentation describes the
process as running on-chain within Subtensor and computing miner and validator emissions from
validator rankings of miner performance.

For readers, this connects two ideas that can otherwise blur together: subnet validators evaluate
work inside a subnet, while Subtensor is the chain layer where the consensus process and resulting
emission calculations are recorded.

## Incentive Context

Bittensor's incentive flow depends on both subnets and the blockchain layer. The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction) explains that TAO
incentivizes participation in subnet activity, and the
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) places Subtensor beside
transactions, rankings, Yuma Consensus, and liquidity emission.

At an article level, this makes Subtensor the protocol context for recording activity and supporting
incentive flow, not a guide to running infrastructure or a catalog of interface methods.

## Substrate Layer Foundation

Subtensor is Bittensor's layer-one blockchain built on Substrate within the Polkadot SDK stack. The
official [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)
describes that foundation explicitly rather than treating the chain as an unnamed backend service.

For readers, that substrate context explains why Bittensor documentation also discusses extrinsics,
runtime storage, and block-based state updates together with subnet markets. Subtensor names the
chain layer where those records live, while individual subnets name the participant markets
operating on top of that shared system of record.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction)

## SDK Chain Interaction Modules

Official Subtensor documentation points developers to SDK modules for chain interaction. The
glossary notes `bittensor.core.subtensor` and `bittensor.core.async_subtensor` as the modules used
to handle Subtensor blockchain interactions from application code.

That tooling context keeps Subtensor separate from a subnet role or tokenomics label. It names the
chain endpoint developers query when they need balances, registrations, stake state, or other
on-chain records maintained by the runtime.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Subtensor Nodes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subtensor-nodes)

## Liquidity Emission on Chain

The same glossary entry states that Subtensor operates Yuma Consensus and emits liquidity to
participants to incentivize network activity. That emission behavior belongs to the chain layer's
recorded incentive flow rather than to any one subnet's local scoring model.

Readers should not collapse Subtensor into a single subnet market. Subtensor records consensus
outcomes and emission-related chain activity, while subnet articles describe the work, evaluation,
and reward context inside each netuid.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Relationship to Mainchain

Subtensor and Mainchain name related but distinct concepts. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes
Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and the system of record for transactions and rankings, while the
[Glossary: Mainchain](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mainchain) describes the
primary Bittensor network used for production purposes.

For readers, Subtensor is the chain layer that records transactions and rankings, while Mainchain is
the production network context that runs on that layer. A mainnet example belongs to production
context without replacing the chain-layer meaning of Subtensor.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Mainchain](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mainchain)

## Relationship to Public Subtensor

Subtensor and Public Subtensor are related but name different layers of the same picture. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes
Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and the system of record for transactions and rankings, while the
[Glossary: Public Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#public-subtensor)
describes publicly reachable access to that chain layer.

For readers, Subtensor is the chain layer itself, while Public Subtensor names live public access to
it. Public access context does not replace the chain-layer meaning of Subtensor.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Public Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#public-subtensor)

## Relationship to Bittensor Networks

Subtensor and Bittensor Networks name related but different parts of the Bittensor stack. The
[Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference groups
mainnet, testnet, and localnet, while the
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes
Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and the system of record for transactions and rankings.

For readers, Bittensor Networks is the environment label, while Subtensor is the chain layer that
records activity in each environment. The same Subtensor concept applies across network contexts,
but production, test, and local settings are still read separately.

References: [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks),
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)

## Relationship to Local Blockchain

Subtensor and local blockchain refer to related but different parts of Bittensor chain context. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes
Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and the system of record for transactions and rankings. The
[Glossary: Local Blockchain](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#local-blockchain)
describes a private, isolated chain used for developing and testing subnet incentive mechanisms.

For readers, Subtensor is the chain-layer concept, while local blockchain is a private development
environment rather than production Mainchain behavior.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Local Blockchain](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#local-blockchain)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Subtensor records transactions and validator rankings on chain. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix) notes that each
incentive mechanism has its own weight matrix for independent consensus calculations.

For readers, Subtensor still names the blockchain layer. Weight-matrix context should be read per
mechanism when a subnet runs more than one incentive mechanism.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Weight Matrix](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix)

## Relationship to Yuma Consensus

Subtensor and Yuma Consensus are related but different parts of Bittensor protocol vocabulary.
Subtensor names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record for transactions and rankings,
while Yuma Consensus describes how validator weight signals are aggregated into miner incentives and
validator dividends on chain. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)
describes the on-chain consensus process that runs within Subtensor.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while Yuma Consensus names the consensus process
recorded on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Relationship to Block

Subtensor and a block are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor names
Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain, while a block names one ordered unit of chain data recorded inside
that layer. The [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)
describes the blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block) describes a block as a
unit of chain data on Subtensor.

For readers, Subtensor names the blockchain layer, while a block names one unit of data recorded
inside that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block)

## Relationship to Extrinsics

Subtensor and extrinsics are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain, while extrinsics name the signed transactions and calls
submitted for inclusion in blocks on that chain. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics) describes
signed transactions submitted to the chain.

For readers, Subtensor names the blockchain layer, while extrinsics name the signed actions
submitted to that layer for inclusion in blocks.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics)

## Relationship to Netuid

Subtensor and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. Subtensor names
Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while a netuid identifies a subnet on the
network whose activity is recorded through that chain layer. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer that records network activity, while a netuid selects
which subnet is being discussed within that recorded activity.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid)

## Reader Boundary

Subtensor is the Bittensor blockchain layer, not the same thing as a particular subnet, subnet
participant role, or current network status report. Claims about live chain state, endpoint
availability, or exact method behavior should come from current official references rather than from
a general concept article.

## Relationship to Coinbase

Subtensor and coinbase are related but different in scope: one is the chain layer, and the other is
a mechanism that runs inside it. Subtensor is Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record
for transactions, rankings, and incentive flow. Coinbase is the per-block protocol mechanism — part
of the Subtensor runtime — that drives TAO emission, injects TAO into emitting subnets' pool
reserves, accumulates pending emissions, and checks epoch boundaries to trigger Yuma Consensus
rounds. The [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)
defines the blockchain layer, and the
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes the per-block emission operation.

Coinbase is one of the routines the Subtensor runtime executes as it produces blocks. Subtensor is
the broader chain that records balances, stake, weights, and rankings; coinbase is the specific
per-block process within that chain that turns those records into emission outcomes. When a block is
produced, the Subtensor runtime runs coinbase to advance pending emissions and trigger epoch
distribution for subnets at their boundary. Subtensor names the system of record and execution
environment, while coinbase names the emission engine operating inside it.
