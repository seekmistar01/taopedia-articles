# Bittensor Emissions

Bittensor emissions are the process that distributes newly created TAO and subnet-specific alpha
tokens to the roles that support the network.

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation frames emissions
around both network-level TAO and subnet-level alpha tokens, while
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) gives the
subnet market context behind that split.

## Network and subnet assets

Emission is not only a TAO release schedule. In Bittensor, emissions include TAO and the alpha token
associated with each subnet. TAO remains the network asset, while alpha tokens represent
subnet-level exposure inside the [[dynamic_tao|Dynamic TAO]] model. That distinction matters because
an emission claim can be about network issuance, subnet-token distribution, or the relationship
between the two.

Sources: [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Emission stages

Emissions can be read in two stages: injection and distribution. The injection side describes how
newly created assets enter subnet contexts, while the distribution side describes how those assets
reach miners, validators, and stakers. Keeping those stages separate prevents emission statements
from collapsing into a single TAO-only number.

Source: [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Subnet demand

Subnets are specialized markets for useful digital work. Dynamic TAO connects subnet demand to
tokenomics by giving each subnet its own alpha token and liquidity relationship with TAO. Emissions
therefore do not only reward work inside a subnet; they also express how subnet-level value is
represented in the wider network economy.

Sources: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Miners and validators

Inside a subnet, miners produce the work the subnet is meant to measure, while validators evaluate
that work. Emissions make those roles economically meaningful. Miner rewards depend on useful
outputs, and validator rewards depend on evaluation signals that help the network allocate subnet
incentives.

Sources: [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[[mining_and_validating|Mining and Validating]]

## Staking outcomes

Staking connects token holders to validator reward outcomes. The emission process includes rewards
for stakers in TAO and in subnet alpha tokens, depending on the validator and subnet context. This
is why a precise emissions article needs to name whether it is discussing TAO, alpha tokens, or both
assets together.

Sources: [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Staking/Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[[staking_and_delegation|Staking and Delegation]]

## Reading emission claims

An emission claim should state its scope. Network-wide issuance, subnet-token rewards, miner
incentives, validator rewards, and staking outcomes are related, but they are not interchangeable.
Exact formulas and percentages should be read next to the source that defines the relevant process.

Sources: [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Issuance

Bittensor emissions and issuance address related but different parts of tokenomics. The
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) describes TAO
circulating in the network, while [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)
describes how newly created TAO and subnet alpha tokens enter subnet and participant contexts.

For readers, emissions name the distribution process, while issuance names the circulating supply
state that process affects.

References: [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Bittensor emissions distribute rewards inside subnet markets. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, emissions still name how TAO and subnet alpha tokens reach participants. When a subnet
runs more than one incentive mechanism, distribution context should be read with that subnet's
mechanism layout in mind.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)
