# Validator Dividend

A validator dividend is the emission share received by Bittensor validators for their consensus role
in a subnet. It is the validator-facing side of subnet emission distribution
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The term is reward vocabulary. Staking is the process of attaching TAO support to a validator;
validator dividend is the emission-side term for what validators receive as a result of their
consensus activity.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For validator
dividends on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret
validator-side emission outcomes after consensus.

In localnet, validator-dividend examples on netuid 1 can be exercised in an isolated environment.
Local emission distribution reflects local chain configuration rather than production subnet
rewards.

On testnet, validator dividends on netuid 1 can be observed in a shared non-production network.
Testnet validator-emission outcomes are separate from mainnet subnet state
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

On mainnet, validator dividends on netuid 1 are live validator-facing emission outcomes on the
production Bittensor network after Yuma Consensus resolves validator weights
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

A validator-dividend example from one environment should not be read as representing production
validator payouts on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Emission Split

Emission names the full subnet reward flow; emission split names the documented miner and validator
role shares at tempo end. Validator dividend names the validator-side reward credited inside the
validator share after Yuma runs ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

Keep emission for the full flow, emission split for role shares, and validator dividend for the
validator-side credited result.

## Distinction from Incentives

Validator dividends are validator-side emission outcomes from bonded evaluation. Miner incentives
are the miner-side emission share for evaluated work
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Validator dividends** — validator-side emission outcomes.
- **Miner incentives** — miner-side emission share for work.

Validator payout vocabulary and miner payout vocabulary name parallel role outcomes
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Take and Delegation Rewards

Validator dividends are consensus-produced validator-side outcomes. Validator take and delegation
rewards describe how that outcome is shared with delegators afterward
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Validator dividends** — validator-side outcome from bonded evaluation.
- **Take and delegation rewards** — post-dividend sharing with delegators.

Outcome vocabulary and sharing vocabulary answer different distribution steps
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Subnet Owner Take

A validator dividend and subnet owner take are both emission shares on a subnet such as netuid 1,
but they go to different roles. A validator dividend is the validator-side emission result a
validator earns through consensus evaluation, while subnet owner take is the documented share of
that subnet's emissions allocated to the subnet owner before the validator and miner sides are
subdivided ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Validator dividend depends on evaluation performance across the tempo; subnet owner take is a
documented allocation that does not depend on a validator's consensus result
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

On netuid 1 examples, both labels can appear in the same subnet emission flow, but they answer
different role questions.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Validator dividend names the validator-side emission outcome produced after consensus, while
autostaking names where a miner's earned emissions are routed after they are credited
([Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

- **Validator dividend** — the validator-side payout category from the emission pass.
- **Autostaking** — the routing of a miner's credited emissions into a staking position.

Autostaking applies to miners after miner incentives are credited; it does not rename validator
dividends on the validator side.

## Distinction from Miner Incentive

Validator dividend and miner incentive are the two reward sides of the same subnet emission pass.
Validator dividend is the share paid to validators for evaluating miner work and holding bonds,
while miner incentive is the share paid to miners for the work they produce. Both are drawn from the
same per-tempo emission but reward different roles
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Role-outcome vocabulary on opposite sides of the same pass answer different reader questions.
Validator dividend names the evaluation-side share; miner incentive names the work-production share.

- **Validator dividend** — the emission share paid to validators for evaluation.
- **Miner incentive** — the emission share paid to miners for their work.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Validator dividend names the validator-side emission outcome credited after Yuma Consensus runs on a
subnet. Yuma is the tempo mechanism that aggregates submitted validator weights through clipping and
bonding before those validator-side shares are assigned
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The documented emission split sets headline role buckets at tempo end, including the
validators-and-stakers share. Validator dividend vocabulary names the validator-side payout inside
that bucket after Yuma settlement, not the full 41/41/18 partition itself
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Miner incentives and validator dividends are parallel outcomes from the same Yuma pass. Dividend
vocabulary names the evaluation-side share; incentive vocabulary names the work-production share on
the opposite side of the same aggregation
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

Validator take and delegation rewards apply after validator dividends are credited. Yuma produces
the validator-side outcome; take vocabulary describes how much of the delegated portion the
validator keeps before stakers receive the remainder
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Coinbase and tempo timing accumulate alpha upstream of distribution. Validator dividend is a
downstream outcome label after pending emissions are distributed and Yuma turns submitted weights
into role shares on the selected netuid
([Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo)).

Readers should not read validator dividend as a staking yield quote or proof of validator weight
quality. The label names a credited validator-side outcome; live amounts depend on subnet emission,
stake context, and the Yuma pass on the relevant netuid
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)).

- **Validator dividend** — validator-side emission share after Yuma settlement.
- **Yuma Consensus** — tempo pass that turns submitted weights into role outcomes.

## Reader Boundary

Validator dividend is concept vocabulary for the validator's share of subnet emission. The term
should not be read as a staking yield estimate, a subnet ranking, or an endorsement of a specific
validator ([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

Actual dividend amounts depend on subnet context, emission configuration, stake weights, and the
selected environment. Mainnet, testnet, and localnet are separate contexts.
