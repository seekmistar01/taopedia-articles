# Subnet

A subnet is a specialized market inside Bittensor for a particular kind of digital work
([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term names the context where miners produce work, validators evaluate that work, and incentive
mechanisms turn evaluation into emission outcomes.

## Distinction from Digital Commodity

A subnet and a digital commodity name a market and the output it produces. A subnet is the
specialized incentive market where miners produce work and validators evaluate it under an incentive
mechanism, while a digital commodity is the specific kind of useful work that market is built to
deliver, so the subnet is the structure and the commodity is the product that comes out of it
([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Subnet** — the specialized incentive market for a kind of digital work.
- **Digital commodity** — the useful output that market is built to produce.

## Distinction from Bittensor Network

Bittensor is the full decentralized machine-learning network and platform. A subnet is one
specialized work market inside that network, not a separate blockchain
([Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction),
[Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet)).

- **Bittensor network** — the platform that hosts subnets, TAO, and shared chain infrastructure.
- **Subnet** — one incentive market context inside that network.

## Distinction from Netuid

A subnet is a Bittensor incentive network with its own participants, emissions, and tokenomics. A
netuid is the numeric identifier used to select that subnet in chain state and tooling
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid)).

- **Subnet** — the incentive network and its participant context.
- **Netuid** — the identifier used to select that network.

Network vocabulary names the whole subnet; netuid vocabulary names the selector readers use in APIs
and hyperparameters ([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

A subnet names a specialized Bittensor market for a particular kind of digital work, with miners
producing and validators evaluating. Yuma Consensus is the separate on-chain mechanism inside each
subnet that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Subnet** — specialized market for digital work and evaluation.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares. A
  subnet names a specialized work market; Yuma processes validator weights into rewards
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Distinction from Bittensor Networks

A Bittensor network and a subnet name an environment and a market within it. A network is the
mainnet, testnet, or localnet environment where activity is interpreted, while a subnet is an
incentive-based marketplace where miners produce work and validators evaluate it
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Bittensor network** — the environment where activity belongs.
- **Subnet** — a work-and-evaluation market running in that environment.

## Distinction from Incentive Mechanism

A subnet is the specialized work market. An incentive mechanism is the evaluation rule set inside
that market ([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)).

- **Subnet** — specialized market for digital work and evaluation.
- **Incentive mechanism** — rule set connecting work, evaluation, and rewards.

Market vocabulary names the subnet context; mechanism vocabulary names how work is evaluated there
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

## Distinction from Multiple-Mechanism

A subnet can host one or more incentive mechanisms with separate evaluation paths.
Multiple-mechanism vocabulary names that parallel structure inside one subnet market
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet)).

- **Subnet** — specialized market context for work and evaluation.
- **Multiple-mechanism** — parallel evaluation tracks inside one subnet.

Market vocabulary names the subnet; multiple-mechanism vocabulary names parallel tracks within it
([Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)).

## Distinction from Subnet Markets

Subnet names a specialized Bittensor work market with local staking pools and evaluation paths.
Subnet markets vocabulary emphasizes that local market frame inside Dynamic TAO design
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Subnet** — specialized market for work, validators, and incentives.
- **Subnet markets** — local market frame with pool and emission routing context.

Concept vocabulary names the market; markets vocabulary emphasizes local pool and routing context
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Stake

Subnet and stake name a market context and the support recorded inside it. A subnet is the
specialized Bittensor work market with local pools and evaluation paths, while stake is the
validator-associated token amount recorded behind validator positions in that subnet
([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Subnet** — specialized market context where stake and evaluation paths operate.
- **Stake** — validator-associated token amount recorded behind positions in that subnet.

## Reader Boundary

Subnet should be read as the general concept for a Bittensor work market. It is not a status report,
quality guarantee, launch guide, or complete description of a subnet's rules
([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The stable article-level point is that a subnet organizes specialized digital work, miner
production, validator evaluation, and incentive context inside Bittensor.
