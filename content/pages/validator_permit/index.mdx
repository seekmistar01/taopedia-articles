# Validator Permit

A validator permit is a Bittensor permission flag for validation rights inside a subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit) defines it as a
boolean flag for whether a specific subnet participant has validation rights.

## Subnet Context

Validator permits are subnet-specific. A participant can have validation rights in one subnet
without that statement automatically applying to every other subnet. This is why the glossary also
defines [VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit) as a delegate-level
view of which subnet IDs a delegate is authorized to validate on.

## Relationship to VPermit

Validator permit and VPermit name subnet validation permission at different scopes. The
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)
describes a boolean flag for validation rights inside one subnet, while the
[Glossary: VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit) describes the subnet
IDs where a delegate is authorized to validate.

For readers, validator permit answers a one-subnet permission question, while VPermit summarizes
those permissions across subnets for a delegate. A delegate's VPermit aggregates individual
validator permits rather than replacing them.

References:
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit)

## Relationship to Stake Weight

The glossary ties validator permits to stake weight by describing them as awarded to the top
participants by stake-weight position. At Taopedia level, the important point is that a permit is
not a general reputation label. It is a subnet validation permission connected to how validator
eligibility is represented for that subnet.

## Hyperparameter Context

Validator permit capacity on a subnet is bounded by the `max_allowed_validators` hyperparameter. The
[Subnet Hyperparameters: MaxAllowedValidators](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#maxallowedvalidators)
documentation lists this as the maximum number of validators on a subnet, with a documented default
of 64. Readers should treat validator permit as a subnet permission flag whose allocation is
constrained by that configured ceiling.

## Permit Versus Validator Registration

Validator registration places a hotkey in a subnet participant set with a UID slot. A validator
permit marks whether that participant has validation rights inside the subnet under stake-weight
rules described in the glossary. Registration and permit therefore answer different questions: entry
into the participant set versus permission to perform validation work there.

Readers should not treat a successful validator registration as automatic validation authority for
every subnet role. Permit status can change with stake-weight position and subnet hyperparameters
while the underlying UID slot relationship remains. Live permit tables and validating documentation
carry current permission state rather than this concept article. Weight submission and validation
duties still depend on permit context described in official validating references.

References:
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)

## Stake-Weight Position as Eligibility Context

The glossary ties validator permits to stake weight by describing them as awarded to top
participants by stake-weight position inside a subnet. That connection makes permit vocabulary an
eligibility flag tied to delegated stake context, not a general reputation score for unrelated
subnets.

The reader takeaway is scope. A permit answers whether validation rights exist on one netuid under
documented stake-weight rules. It does not transfer automatically to other subnets and should not be
read as a permanent badge independent of stake movement. Operational staking and validator
references explain how stake changes can affect permit standing over time. Delegators evaluating
validators should read permit status on the relevant netuid, not assume cross-subnet validation
rights.

References:
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)

## Permit Ceiling Versus UID Capacity

Subnet hyperparameter documentation lists max_allowed_validators as the maximum number of validators
on a subnet. Validator permits are therefore bounded by configured validator capacity on that
netuid, separate from the broader max_allowed_uids ceiling that limits miner and validator UID slots
together.

Readers evaluating subnet structure should keep validator permit limits distinct from general UID
slot limits. A subnet can contain many registered participants while only a subset hold validation
permits under stake-weight and hyperparameter rules described in official subnet documentation.
Current hyperparameter values belong in live subnet references rather than in a static article
quote. Permit counts can differ from total registered validators on the same netuid.

References:
[Subnet Hyperparameters: MaxAllowedValidators](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#maxallowedvalidators),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

A validator permit is usually discussed for one incentive mechanism at a time, but subnets can also
run multiple mechanisms in parallel. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that validators must evaluate miners separately for each mechanism.

For readers, a validator permit still marks validation rights inside a subnet, but the mechanism
context matters. Evaluation duties for one mechanism should be read separately when a subnet uses
more than one evaluation system.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Relationship to Subnet Deregistration

Validator permit and subnet deregistration address related but different parts of Bittensor subnet
vocabulary. A validator permit is the per-subnet right to submit validation weights, held by
high-stake validators inside a specific subnet, while subnet deregistration is the network mechanism
that removes an entire non-immune subnet when the subnet limit is reached and a new registration
attempt enters, selecting the existing subnet with the lowest EMA of alpha price for removal. The
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)
describes the right to validate inside a subnet, and the
[Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration) documentation
describes the network-level subnet removal process.

A validator can lose a permit within a continuing subnet if their stake weight drops below the
subnet's threshold — that is a permit-level change and the subnet continues operating. Subnet
deregistration, by contrast, removes the entire subnet: every validator permit issued within it ends
simultaneously because the subnet context no longer exists. The permit-loss pathway is governed by
within-subnet stake-weight rules; the subnet-removal pathway is governed by network-level selection
of the lowest-EMA-alpha-price subnet, which is a different criterion entirely.

References:
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration)

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the term at a high level. It does not list current permit holders, rank
validators, describe staking or registration steps, or restate operational requirements. Readers
should use official validator references for those details.
