# Autostaking

Autostaking is a Bittensor feature that automatically routes a miner's emissions into stake with a
validator selected by that miner
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The concept belongs to reward handling. Mining produces emissions, staking puts value behind a
validator, and autostaking connects those two ideas automatically after emissions are earned.

## Emission Routing Context

Autostaking acts on mining emissions after they are earned. The feature routes mining income into
staking, so the durable idea is emission routing rather than a new emissions category
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Emission names the reward flow. Autostaking names one treatment of that flow after the miner earns
it.

For a reader, the practical point is where the earned emission goes next. Autostaking sends it into
the selected validator-staking destination instead of leaving the routing as a separate manual
follow-up.

## Delegation and Destination

Autostaking uses Bittensor's existing staking and delegation vocabulary. Stake can be delegated to
validators, and a delegate is the validator-side recipient in that relationship
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

Autostaking does not create a separate validator role or a separate reward type. It is an automatic
path into the same validator-staking context used elsewhere.

The destination in autostaking is a validator chosen by the miner, but the term itself is not a
validator endorsement. It names the routing mechanism; validator selection remains a separate
delegation decision ([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Miner-Side Scope

The feature is miner-side because miners are the role receiving the emissions being routed. A subnet
miner performs work for a subnet and can receive emissions through the subnet reward process
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

For readers, autostaking is not a generic name for every form of staking. It is automation for
handling newly earned miner emissions.

## Relationship to Staking

Staking names the destination activity, while autostaking names the automation that routes new miner
emissions into that activity
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

The two terms are related but not interchangeable. Autostaking feeds staking; it is not a separate
staking category.

## Relationship to Dividends

Autostaking and dividends sit on different sides of the same emission flow. Autostaking routes a
miner's earned emissions into a validator-staking position, while dividends name the validator-side
emission result that Yuma Consensus produces from validator-miner bonds and miner outcomes
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

The two terms stay distinct. Autostaking names the routing step that places earned miner emissions
as stake behind a chosen validator, while dividends name the validator-side outcome of consensus
rather than the routing action, so an autostaking statement does not by itself describe a dividend
result
([Yuma Consensus: Validator Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Consensus and Token Context

Mining emissions that autostaking can route are produced through the subnet's incentive and
consensus process. [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) converts
validator weight vectors into per-miner emission shares each tempo, and those shares are the source
material that autostaking then routes into a validator-staking position
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

In Bittensor's [Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao) model,
subnet emissions can involve both TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. Autostaking routes miner
emissions into the selected validator's staking position; the exact asset type is determined by the
subnet's tokenomics context. Autostaking names the automatic routing step that follows the
consensus-derived emission
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Autostaking Applies After Miner Emissions Credit

Autostaking acts on mining emissions after they are earned through the subnet incentive and
consensus path. [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) converts
validator weight vectors into per-miner emission shares each tempo, and autostaking routes those
shares into the configured validator-staking destination
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

That ordering keeps autostaking on the post-emission side of mining. It does not create a separate
reward category or replace manual staking vocabulary elsewhere in the network.

## Destination Validator Remains a Miner Configuration Choice

Autostaking uses existing delegation vocabulary. The destination is a
[delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) chosen by the miner, but the
term names routing automation rather than validator endorsement
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

For readers, autostaking explains where earned miner emissions go next; delegation documentation
still governs how validator support relationships are formed.

## Yuma Emissions, Autostaking, and Delegate Stake on a Subnet

On a subnet such as netuid 1, a
[subnet miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) earns emission
shares through the subnet incentive and
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) path
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) then routes those earned shares
into stake with a configured [delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)
rather than requiring a separate manual staking transaction. In
[Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao) contexts, the routed
asset type follows the subnet's tokenomics rules while the routing step itself stays distinct from
holder-initiated [delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For autostaking on
a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how miner emission routing examples should be read.

In localnet, autostaking on netuid 1 can test routing configuration in isolation. Localnet emission
shares and delegate destinations reflect that test chain rather than production staking behavior.

On testnet, autostaking on netuid 1 applies in a shared non-production network where miner emissions
and delegation state are separate from mainnet.

On mainnet, autostaking on netuid 1 concerns production miner emissions routed to live validator
positions on the selected subnet
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Autostaking examples from one environment should not be
read as emission routing outcomes on another network.

## Autostaking Changes Where Emissions Go, Not How They Are Earned

A clarifying boundary is that autostaking is a routing convenience layered on top of emissions, not
a change to how rewards are produced. Documentation describes it as automatically directing earned
emissions into staking, so the underlying consensus and emission mechanics stay the same
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, this separates earning from compounding. The amount earned is still determined by
evaluation and the emission split, while autostaking only decides that the earned value is restaked
rather than left idle ([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

That framing also explains why autostaking is described as optional automation. Because it acts
after emissions are credited, turning it on or off changes what happens to rewards next, not whether
or how they were produced in the first place
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

## Reader Boundary

Autostaking names automatic routing of earned miner emissions into validator staking
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

It is not an earnings guarantee, validator recommendation, wallet guide, or instruction manual. It
is the routing concept that connects earned miner emissions with the selected validator-staking
destination.
