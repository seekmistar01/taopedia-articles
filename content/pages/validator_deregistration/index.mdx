# Validator Deregistration

Validator deregistration is the validator-specific case of deregistration in a Bittensor subnet. The
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)
documentation describes validators being removed when emissions are low and subnet registration
rules require displacement from a full subnet
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

## What It Represents

Validator deregistration narrows deregistration to validators: the participant being removed is a
validator occupying a subnet UID slot. Displacement ties removal to subnet participation and
emission standing when a full subnet accepts a new registration
([Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration),
[Glossary: UID slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's validator displacement path. They should not be
read as representing production removal outcomes on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Miner Deregistration

Deregistration uses the same emission-based pruning rule for every neuron on a full subnet. Miner
deregistration names the case where the displaced participant is a miner, while validator
deregistration names the case where the displaced participant is a validator
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

- **Miner deregistration** — displacement of a low-performing miner slot.
- **Validator deregistration** — displacement of a low-performing validator slot.

## Distinction from UID Trimming

Validator deregistration removes one participant when a full subnet accepts a new registration. UID
trimming is a subnet-owner bulk resize that removes many low emitters down to a chosen maximum
([Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration),
[UID Trimming](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/uid-trimming)).

- **Validator deregistration** — single-slot displacement at registration time.
- **UID trimming** — owner-initiated bulk reduction and compression.

## Distinction from Subnet Deregistration

Validator deregistration vacates one UID row inside a subnet that continues to exist. Subnet
deregistration removes an entire subnet to free a network slot when the global subnet cap binds
([Subnet deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration),
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

- **Validator deregistration** — participant removal inside a continuing subnet.
- **Subnet deregistration** — removal of the entire subnet context.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Validator permit authorizes qualifying neurons to submit weights on a subnet. Losing permit can
reduce validator emissions, but permit status is not the displacement selector when a full subnet
accepts a new registration
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

- **Validator permit** — authorization to submit counting weights.
- **Validator deregistration** — UID-slot removal when displacement rules apply.

## Distinction from Register

Register is the entry action that pays a dynamic burn to claim a UID. Validator deregistration is
the removal outcome when a full subnet must displace an eligible incumbent for that entry
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

- **Register** — entry action that claims a UID on a full subnet.
- **Validator deregistration** — validator-side exit when displacement is required.

## Distinction from Validator Registration

Validator registration gives a subnet validator a UID slot after registering a hotkey with that
subnet. Validator deregistration removes a low-performing validator slot when registration pressure
requires replacement, while the subnet itself continues
([Validator Registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration),
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

- **Validator registration** — entry into a subnet validator slot.
- **Validator deregistration** — removal of a low-performing validator slot.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

Validator deregistration removes a validator's UID position from a subnet. The immunity period is
the temporary window that protects a newly registered entrant from deregistration for poor
performance
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

- **Validator deregistration** — removal of a validator's UID position from a subnet.
- **Immunity period** — protection window shielding a new entrant from displacement.

## Distinction from Pruning Score

Pruning score ranks which incumbent is weakest when displacement is required on a full subnet.
Validator deregistration names the validator-specific removal event that follows that ranking
([Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score),
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — ranking that selects which UID is removed.
- **Validator deregistration** — removal that frees the selected validator's slot.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

A child hotkey is a separate key a parent hotkey authorizes to validate on its behalf. Validator
deregistration is the removal of a validator from a subnet when displacement rules apply
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

- **Child hotkey** — delegated validation key acting on re-delegated stake.
- **Validator deregistration** — removal of a registered validator's place in the subnet.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Validator deregistration names the removal of a validator neuron from a subnet when displacement
rules apply. It describes metagraph exit and loss of counting weight authority, not the settlement
mechanism that converts remaining validators' submissions into emission shares
([Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on the selected netuid. It
reads the weight matrix from eligible validators who still hold active slots, applies clipping and
bonding, and converts the result into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Deregistered validators no longer submit weights that count in consensus until they register again
on the subnet. Yuma settles signals from the remaining validator set; deregistration changes who is
eligible to contribute rows to the matrix—not how clipping or dividends are calculated
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

Readers should treat validator deregistration as a participant-removal outcome on one netuid, and
Yuma as the recurring settlement pass for eligible validators who remain.

- **Validator deregistration** — removal of a validator neuron from a subnet under displacement
  rules.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.
