# Alpha Reserve

An alpha reserve is the pool-held side of a subnet's alpha token in Bittensor's Dynamic TAO
tokenomics. The official
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet as an automated market maker with two liquidity reserves: a TAO reserve and a
subnet-specific alpha reserve.

## Pool Context

Alpha reserve belongs to the subnet liquidity-pool context. The same official subnet overview
describes alpha reserves as the amount of alpha available for purchase, while TAO reserves are the
TAO staked into the subnet. Together, those two reserves define the pool side of a subnet's economy.

This makes alpha reserve different from a participant balance. It is not the alpha held by subnet
participants as stake or rewards; it is the alpha held inside the pool that pairs with the subnet's
TAO reserve.

## Price Context

The subnet overview explains that a subnet pool's reserve ratio determines the price of its alpha
token. In reader terms, alpha reserve is one side of that ratio. A change in the reserve context can
affect the pool relationship between TAO and alpha, but the article-level concept is simply that the
alpha reserve is the pool-held alpha side.

This does not make alpha reserve a live market quote. Exact reserve amounts and prices are current
state values, while the concept describes where the alpha sits in the subnet tokenomics model.

## Emission Context

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)
documentation describes alpha reserve injection as adding alpha to the subnet's reserve in
proportion to TAO injection so the pool can grow without changing the alpha token price from the
injection itself.

For readers, the useful distinction is between pool-side and participant-side emission paths. Alpha
reserve injection supports the pool side, while alpha outstanding injection is the participant-side
context for rewards.

## Relationship to Alpha Outstanding

Alpha reserve and alpha outstanding describe where subnet alpha sits in Dynamic TAO accounting.
Alpha reserve is the pool-held alpha side paired with TAO reserve, while alpha outstanding is alpha
held outside that reserve context by subnet participants. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
uses both terms when explaining subnet liquidity and participant-held stake.

For readers, alpha reserve answers how much alpha is in the subnet pool, while alpha outstanding
answers how much subnet alpha is outside that pool. They are two locations for the same
subnet-specific alpha asset, not separate assets.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to ADR

Alpha reserve is part of the pool side behind Alpha Distribution Ratio. The
[Glossary: ADR](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#adr-alpha-distribution-ratio)
entry compares alpha emitted to participants with alpha injected into the automated market maker
pool. Alpha reserve belongs to that pool-side comparison, not to the participant-held side.

## Relationship to TAO Reserve

Alpha reserve and TAO Reserve are related but distinct pool-side terms in Bittensor subnet
tokenomics. Alpha reserve names the pool-held subnet-specific alpha side of a subnet's automated
market maker, while TAO Reserve names the pool-held TAO side of the same subnet. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet as an automated market maker with paired TAO and subnet-specific alpha
reserves, and the [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)
documentation describes TAO reserve injection as the process that adds TAO to the pool side that
pairs with the alpha reserve.

For readers, alpha reserve names one side of the subnet pool, while TAO Reserve names the other.
Together they define the pool side of a subnet's economy; the reserve ratio between them is the
price-setting context described on the official subnet overview. The two are not interchangeable:
alpha reserve is the subnet-specific asset side, TAO Reserve is the network asset side.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Alpha reserve names the pool-held side of subnet alpha in Dynamic TAO. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that when
multiple incentive mechanisms are used, each operates independently, allowing subnet creators to
distribute emissions across different types of work or evaluation criteria.

For readers, alpha reserve still describes alpha held inside the subnet pool. When a subnet runs
more than one incentive mechanism, participant-side emission paths can differ, but alpha reserve
remains the pool-held alpha side of that subnet market.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Reader Boundary

Alpha reserve is not a separate token, a staking instruction, or a recommendation about a subnet. It
is a tokenomics term for the pool-held alpha side of a subnet's liquidity context. Live reserve
amounts, prices, and trading outcomes should be read from current official tools or chain data.
