# Reserve Ratio

A reserve ratio is the relationship between the TAO reserve and alpha reserve inside a Bittensor
subnet pool. The official
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
explains that each subnet functions as an automated market maker with a TAO reserve and a
subnet-specific alpha reserve, and that the reserve ratio determines the subnet alpha token's price
context.

## Pool Context

The reserve ratio belongs to the pool side of subnet tokenomics. A subnet's TAO reserve records the
TAO side of the pool, while its alpha reserve records the pool-held subnet alpha side. The ratio
between those two reserves is the pool relationship readers need before interpreting staking,
unstaking, or alpha-token conversion claims.

This makes reserve ratio narrower than a general article about subnet liquidity pools. A liquidity
pool describes the whole reserve context; reserve ratio describes the relationship between the two
pool sides.

## Price Context

The official subnet overview describes subnet alpha price through the relationship between TAO in
reserve and alpha in reserve. In reader terms, the reserve ratio is the pool relationship that makes
alpha-denominated staking and unstaking different from a simple one-token balance view.

This does not make the reserve ratio a live market quote. Exact prices and reserve balances are
current state values, while the concept explains why TAO and alpha reserves have to be read
together.

## Slippage Context

[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage) explains why staking and
unstaking can return a different amount than a static expectation. Those actions interact with
subnet pool reserves, so the reserve ratio is part of the context behind received TAO or alpha
amounts.

For readers, the important boundary is simple: slippage describes the outcome difference, while
reserve ratio describes the pool relationship that helps produce conversion context.

## Emission Context

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection) documentation
connects subnet emission injection to reserve growth. TAO can be injected into a subnet's TAO
reserve, and alpha reserve injection is described as proportional to TAO injection so the pool can
grow without changing alpha price from the injection itself.

That emission context uses the same reserve relationship, but reserve ratio is not the same thing as
emission distribution. It describes the pool relationship, not the final allocation to miners,
validators, stakers, or subnet owners.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Reserve ratio describes the TAO and alpha reserve relationship in a subnet pool. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, reserve ratio still names pool-side price context. A subnet may run more than one
incentive mechanism, but the reserve ratio belongs to the shared pool rather than to one mechanism
label.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Relationship to Alpha Price

Reserve ratio and alpha price are closely related but name different parts of the same pool
relationship. Reserve ratio names the TAO-to-alpha proportion inside a subnet pool, while alpha
price names the TAO-denominated quote derived from that proportion. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes the subnet pool as an automated market maker with a TAO reserve and an alpha reserve, and
the [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation uses the same reserve
relationship when explaining subnet token price.

For readers, reserve ratio describes the pool state relationship; alpha price reads that state as a
TAO-per-alpha quote. A change in pool reserves can shift both quantities together, but they answer
different questions: one names the reserve proportion, the other names the implied price.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Reader Boundary

Reserve ratio is not a subnet ranking, a staking recommendation, or a guaranteed conversion rate. It
is a tokenomics term for reading the relationship between TAO and alpha reserves inside a subnet
pool. Live values should be checked in current official tools or chain data.

## Relationship to TAO Reserve

Reserve ratio and TAO reserve are related but different scopes in Bittensor pool vocabulary. TAO
reserve names the pool-held TAO side of a subnet liquidity pool, while reserve ratio names the
relationship between that TAO reserve and the paired alpha reserve. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet pool as holding a TAO reserve and a subnet-specific alpha reserve, and
explains that the reserve ratio between those two sides determines alpha token price context.

For readers, TAO reserve names one pool side, while reserve ratio names how that TAO side relates to
the alpha side inside the same subnet pool.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)

## Relationship to Alpha Reserve

Reserve ratio and alpha reserve are related but different scopes in Bittensor pool vocabulary. Alpha
reserve names the pool-held subnet alpha side of a subnet liquidity pool, while reserve ratio names
the relationship between that alpha reserve and the paired TAO reserve. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes subnet pools as automated market makers with TAO and alpha reserves, and the
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection) documentation
describes alpha reserve injection as proportional to TAO injection in that paired pool context.

For readers, alpha reserve names one pool side, while reserve ratio names how that alpha side
relates to the TAO side inside the same subnet pool.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)

## Relationship to Liquidity Positions

Reserve ratio and liquidity positions are related but different parts of Bittensor subnet liquidity
vocabulary. Reserve ratio names the subnet-pool-side split between the TAO and alpha reserves, while
a liquidity position is a user-deposited position on a subnet's automated market maker that
contributes to those reserves. The
[Liquidity Positions documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions) describes
liquidity positions as user-side deposits on subnet pools that earn fees from swaps through the
pool, and the same source distinguishes them from staking and unstaking by noting that liquidity
positions are held by users directly rather than delegated to validators.

For readers, reserve ratio is a subnet-pool-side measure of how the TAO and alpha sides of the AMM
pool are sized, while liquidity positions name the user-side deposits that add depth to those two
sides. The two are not interchangeable: reserve ratio describes the pool's internal composition,
while liquidity positions describe how much user-side depth sits on top of it.

References:
[Liquidity Positions documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Netuid

Reserve ratio and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A netuid
identifies a subnet on the network, while reserve ratio names the TAO-to-alpha reserve relationship
inside that subnet's liquidity pool. The
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level, and the
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet pool's reserve ratio as determining alpha token price context within that
subnet.

For readers, a netuid identifies which subnet pool is in scope, while reserve ratio names the
reserve relationship inside that subnet's pool.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Subtensor

Reserve ratio and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while reserve ratio names the pool-side
TAO-to-alpha relationship recorded through subnet liquidity state on that chain layer. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes subnet pools and their reserve ratios as part of subnet tokenomics on that layer.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while reserve ratio names a subnet pool relationship
read from chain-layer liquidity state.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Protocol Alpha

Reserve ratio and protocol alpha describe related but different aspects of subnet pool activity.
Reserve ratio is the proportion of TAO reserve to alpha reserve in a subnet's automated market maker
pool, determining alpha price and slippage context, while protocol alpha is the protocol-owned alpha
cached per subnet — the alpha the protocol holds from chain-side TAO buys during reserve injection
rather than immediately recycling it. The
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)
defines protocol alpha as the per-subnet protocol-owned alpha balance, and the
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)
documentation describes the reserve injection process that changes the pool balances reserve ratio
measures.

During reserve injection, the protocol's TAO purchases shift both the TAO reserve and alpha reserve
— the two inputs to reserve ratio — while adding to the protocol-owned alpha balance that protocol
alpha tracks. Reserve ratio names the current pool composition at any moment; protocol alpha names a
specific protocol-held alpha balance that reserve injection activity produces and caches per subnet.
The two are connected through reserve injection but answer different questions: one describes the
pool balance proportion, the other describes a protocol-owned alpha quantity within that pool
system.

References:
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)
