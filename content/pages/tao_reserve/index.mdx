# TAO Reserve

A TAO reserve is the TAO side of a Bittensor subnet liquidity pool. In Dynamic TAO, each subnet pool
pairs a TAO reserve with a subnet-specific alpha reserve
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term is a pool-location concept. TAO names the network token, while TAO reserve names where TAO
sits when it is part of a subnet's paired reserve system
([Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau)).

## What It Represents

Each alpha subnet maintains its own TAO reserve paired with an alpha reserve on a named netuid.
Emission injects TAO into the reserve side each block; staking routes TAO through the pool before
alpha is drawn from the paired side
([Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The root subnet has no alpha currency and therefore no paired TAO reserve structure. TAO reserve
applies to alpha subnets with both reserve sides
([Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)).

TAO reserve examples from one network environment should not be read as representing production pool
state on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Alpha Reserve

TAO reserve and alpha reserve are the paired sides of a subnet liquidity pool. TAO reserve names the
network-token side of that pair
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **TAO reserve** — pool-held TAO on the network-token side.
- **Alpha reserve** — pool-held subnet alpha on the paired side.

## Distinction from Reserve Ratio

Reserve ratio names the relationship between TAO and alpha reserves inside a pool. TAO reserve names
only the TAO side of that pair
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **TAO reserve** — the pool-held TAO quantity on one reserve side.
- **Reserve ratio** — the comparative relationship between both reserve sides.

## Distinction from Alpha Price

Alpha price is derived from TAO reserve divided by alpha reserve. TAO reserve names the pool-held
TAO quantity in that quote
([Understanding Subnets: Price/Rate of Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#pricerate-of-alpha-tokens)).

- **TAO reserve** — pool-held TAO inventory on the named netuid.
- **Alpha price** — TAO-denominated quote derived from paired reserves.

## Distinction from Slippage

Slippage names the received-amount gap when conversions move paired reserves. TAO reserve names the
pool-held TAO side those moves change
([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **TAO reserve** — pool-side TAO inventory.
- **Slippage** — conversion gap after pool movement.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking routes TAO into subnet alpha through the paired reserves, which changes TAO reserve balances
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — delegation action that moves value through the pool.
- **TAO reserve** — pool quantity on the TAO side that action can shift.

## Distinction from Emission

TAO reserve injection adds TAO to the pool side through emission processing. TAO reserve names the
pool-held quantity at observation time
([Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)).

- **TAO reserve** — pool-held TAO inventory on a subnet.
- **Emission injection** — flow that deepens the reserve side each block.

## Distinction from Liquidity Positions

Liquidity positions add range-based depth inside a subnet pool. TAO reserve names the pool-held TAO
inventory those positions interact with
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

- **TAO reserve** — core pool-held TAO on the subnet's TAO side.
- **Liquidity position** — provider range commitment inside that pool.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

TAO reserve names pool-held TAO inventory paired with alpha reserve. Yuma Consensus converts
validator weight submissions into emission shares after evaluation completes
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **TAO reserve** — pool-held TAO inventory on a subnet.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

Alpha outstanding is subnet alpha held outside the reserve pool. TAO reserve is TAO inside the
paired pool structure
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **TAO reserve** — TAO held inside the subnet's paired pool.
- **Alpha outstanding** — alpha held outside the reserve pool.

## Reader Boundary

TAO reserve is a reference term for the pool-held TAO side of a subnet liquidity pool. It is not TAO
itself, a staking tutorial, subnet ranking table, or return estimate
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau)).
