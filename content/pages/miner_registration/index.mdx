# Miner Registration

Miner registration is how a participant enters a Bittensor subnet as a miner. The
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)
documentation describes registration as the step that gives a miner a UID on a subnet so the miner
can take part in that subnet's work and reward flow.

## Why Registration Matters

Subnets organize participants around UID slots. The
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) defines a slot as
the position a subnet miner or validator occupies inside a subnet. Without registration, a hotkey is
not participating in that subnet's miner set.

For a reader, miner registration is therefore the entry point into subnet mining vocabulary. It does
not describe how well a miner performs, how much it earns, or which subnet is best to join.

## Entry Versus Ongoing Miner Performance

Registration grants a UID slot and begins subnet participation, but it does not fix performance rank
or emissions outcome. Registration is therefore a one-time entry event, while the validator
evaluation that drives rank and emission share continues for as long as the miner holds its slot
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The distinction keeps expectations aligned with subnet mechanics. A newly registered miner still
must produce work for the subnet task and receive validator evaluation like every other participant.
Registration opens the door to that evaluation flow; scoring, ranking, and emission allocation
remain downstream of entry. Immunity period rules may temporarily change removal timing after entry,
but they do not replace the ongoing evaluation described in mining and subnet documentation. Readers
evaluating whether to register should separate the one-time entry step from later subnet
performance.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For miner
registration on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret
evidence about UID slot acquisition and subnet participation
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

In localnet, miner registration on netuid 1 can be tested in an isolated environment. UID slots and
registration fees in localnet reflect local subnet configuration and do not represent live mainnet
registration costs or slot availability.

On testnet, miner registration on netuid 1 can be observed in a shared, non-production subnet.
Testnet UID assignments and immunity periods are separate from mainnet subnet participation.

On mainnet, miner registration on subnets such as netuid 1 is a live Subtensor extrinsic that
assigns a UID slot in a specific subnet. The
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)
documentation describes the registration burn, UID slot rules, and immunity period that apply on the
production network.

A miner registration example from one environment should not be read as representing UID state or
registration costs in another environment. Localnet, testnet, and mainnet each have separate subnet
UID registries, so registration belongs to the specific network where it was submitted.

## UID Scope

Miner registration establishes subnet participation and UID context, but it does not by itself prove
ongoing miner quality or reward outcome. Miner documentation explains registration, while
incentive-mechanism documentation explains how subnet work can later be evaluated
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

That boundary keeps registration claims separate from later scoring, emissions, and deregistration
outcomes.

## Distinction from Recycling

Miner registration names the process that grants a miner its UID slot. Recycling names the TAO-burn
mechanism that pays for that slot
([Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

## Distinction from Register

Register describes subnet entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO.
Miner registration names that entry process applied to a subnet miner who will take part in mining
work and evaluation
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

Register is the general subnet-entry term; miner registration names the miner-specific case of that
process.

- **Register** — the general on-chain action that purchases a UID slot on a subnet.
- **Miner registration** — the miner-specific entry path into subnet participation and evaluation.

## Distinction from Subnet Deregistration

Miner registration gives a miner a UID slot after paying a registration burn to enter a subnet.
Subnet deregistration is the network mechanism that removes an entire non-immune subnet when the
subnet limit is reached, selecting the subnet with the lowest EMA of alpha price for removal
([subnet deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

Miner deregistration vacates one low-performing miner slot while the subnet continues. Subnet
deregistration removes the entire subnet and every UID assignment within it at once.

- **Miner registration** — participant-level entry that assigns a miner UID slot on a subnet.
- **Subnet deregistration** — network-level removal of an entire subnet and all its UID slots.

Miner registration always happens on a specific subnet identified by
[netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid). The mining documentation treats
subnet choice as upstream of the registration burn: the participant selects which subnet market to
enter, then pays the dynamic registration cost for a miner UID slot on that subnet
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

That ordering matters because the burn purchases entry into one subnet, not a transferable option
across subnets. A miner who registers on the wrong netuid has entered that subnet's participant set
under that subnet's rules, not simply reserved TAO for a later subnet switch. Subnet selection and
registration cost belong together in live tooling and documentation rather than in a static article
quote. Changing subnets after a successful registration is a separate participation decision, not an
undo of the original entry burn.

When a subnet's miner slots are full, new registration can still occur because the network can
replace a low-performing miner to make room. Miner deregistration documentation describes how a full
subnet replaces the lowest-performing miner when a new registration arrives
([Miner Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

Readers should not treat registration as only an empty-slot action. On a full subnet, successful
registration implies both payment of the entry burn and displacement of an existing participant
under the subnet's performance rules. That replacement path is separate from the registration
transaction itself and belongs to subnet lifecycle documentation rather than to a guarantee about
which miner will be removed at submission time. Live subnet state and deregistration references
carry that detail at the time of registration.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

Immunity period names post-registration protection from deregistration. Miner registration names the
initial entry step into a subnet UID slot
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

After registration, a miner is not immediately subject to every low-performance removal path. The
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)
describes a grace window for newly registered participants, and
[Miner Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration) describes how a
full subnet can later replace a low-performing miner when a new registration arrives.

For readers, registration starts participation; immunity period and deregistration describe later
subnet lifecycle rules around that slot.

Miner registration names the miner-specific entry process, while a
[UID slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) names the participant
position inside a subnet that entry grants. Immunity period names the grace window that can follow
entry before ordinary low-performance removal rules apply.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Miner registration is the process by which a participant registers a hotkey to mine on a subnet and
claims a UID slot. It names subnet entry vocabulary, not the on-chain step that turns validator
weight submissions into emission shares
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that converts submitted validator weights into emission
shares after evaluation completes. Registration grants participant position; Yuma Consensus settles
emission shares from validator weights after evaluation
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers should keep registration vocabulary separate from the settlement step. Miner registration
answers how a miner enters a subnet; Yuma Consensus answers how validator weights become emission
shares in that subnet
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Miner registration** — paid entry process that grants a miner UID slot.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Distinction from Registration Cost

Registering a miner uses the same broad register vocabulary as other subnet participants. The
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes subnet
entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO. Mining documentation also
notes that this cost changes over time.

The [Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)
reference should not be read as a fixed price quote. It names the paid entry step that connects a
miner hotkey to a subnet UID slot.

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes
subnet entry as purchasing a UID slot, and the
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) states that the
UID is assigned to a hotkey when it registers as a miner or validator. Miner registration therefore
attaches subnet participation to an operational hotkey rather than to the coldkey balance account
alone.

Coldkey authority still governs wallet ownership and stake movement. The registered miner identity
that occupies the UID slot is the hotkey named in registration vocabulary
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

A [subnet miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) produces work for
the subnet's task and receives evaluation from validators. Registration makes that role possible by
placing the miner inside the subnet's participant set. Mining documentation treats registration as a
prerequisite before a miner can be evaluated and considered for emissions inside that subnet
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

This article does not describe mining setup, scoring formulas, or ranking tables. Those details
belong in mining references and live subnet data.

## Reader Boundary

Miner registration should not be read as a recommendation to join any particular subnet, as a
command tutorial, or as a guarantee of emissions. It is the concept term for how a miner enters a
subnet UID slot and begins participating in subnet mining vocabulary.
