# Burning

Burning is the removal of tokens from spendable circulation. In Bittensor tokenomics, the term keeps
permanent supply removal separate from recycling, emission, staking, fees, and ordinary transfer
activity ([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

## Distinction from Recycling

Burning and recycling describe opposite supply-accounting outcomes after a deducted amount. Burning
names permanent removal from spendable circulation, while recycling names protocol reuse that can
return tokens toward future distribution
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

- **Burning** — the deducted amount stays unavailable for future protocol distribution.
- **Recycling** — the deducted amount can return through later protocol emission paths.

## Distinction from Register

Burning and register name a supply outcome and the action that triggers one. Burning is the
permanent removal of TAO from spendable circulation, while register is the subnet-entry process that
purchases a UID slot by paying a registration burn in TAO
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Burning** — permanent removal of TAO from spendable circulation.
- **Register** — the subnet-entry process that pays a registration burn for a UID slot.

## Distinction from Issuance

Burning removes tokens from spendable circulation. Issuance names the broader supply-creation
picture for tokens entering the protocol
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

- **Burning** — permanent removal from spendable circulation.
- **Issuance** — supply-creation and injection vocabulary.

Removal vocabulary names what leaves circulation; issuance vocabulary names what enters it
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Subnet Stake Burn

Burning is the general supply-removal concept, while subnet stake burn is one specific mechanism
that performs it. Subnet stake burn documentation describes TAO moving into a subnet's reserve while
corresponding alpha is removed from circulation and burned, so stake burn is a concrete subnet case
of the broader burning idea
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)).

- **Burning** — the general removal of tokens from spendable circulation.
- **Subnet stake burn** — a specific subnet mechanism that burns alpha while TAO enters the reserve.

## Distinction from Transaction Fees

Burning names a supply-removal outcome after tokens leave spendable circulation. Transaction fees
name the cost side of state-changing actions
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

- **Burning** — supply outcome when tokens are permanently removed.
- **Transaction fees** — cost attached to a state-changing action.

Outcome vocabulary names what happens to deducted supply; fee vocabulary names action cost
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Permanent burning removes TAO from spendable circulation through registration and other protocol
actions. That supply-removal outcome is separate from the within-subnet pass that converts validator
weight submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Burning** — permanent removal of TAO from circulation.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares.
  Burning removes spendable supply; Yuma processes validator weights into rewards
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Reader Boundary

Burning is tokenomics vocabulary, not market advice, transfer guidance, or a report of exact network
totals. It describes the supply-accounting result of removing tokens from spendable circulation
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning)).
