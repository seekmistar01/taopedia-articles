# Bicameral Legislature

Bicameral legislature describes the two-body governance structure used in Bittensor proposal review.
The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature) defines it
as a two-tier system comprising the Triumvirate and the Senate for proposal approval.

## Two Governance Bodies

The Triumvirate and Senate have separate governance roles. The official
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) describes the Triumvirate as the
body that creates proposals and the Senate as the body that approves or rejects them. The
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation provides the Senate-side
context for reviewing proposals.

## How Proposals Move Through Both Bodies

Bicameral legislature is easiest to read as a sequence rather than as a list of separate governance
terms. The [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) describes the
Triumvirate as the body that creates proposals containing privileged call data. Those proposals then
enter Senate review, where senators — delegates with enough stake to participate — vote to approve
or reject them.

Approval alone does not execute the change. The same overview explains that a Triumvirate member
must close an approved proposal before its call data runs on chain. In plain terms, the Triumvirate
originates the item, the Senate decides whether it may proceed, and the Triumvirate still performs
the close step that actually executes the approved call data. Bicameral legislature names that split
review structure rather than any one proposal record or vote outcome.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)

## Why This Structure Replaced Direct Sudo Authority

Before Bittensor governance, privileged network changes could depend on a single administrative sudo
key. The [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance#security) explains that
the governance protocol replaced that model so approving privileged changes now requires Senate
majority support and Triumvirate closure of a proposal rather than direct sudo execution alone.

Bicameral legislature therefore names the review structure that sits in place of single-key
administration for the proposal path described in the official docs. It is not a claim that every
network action always passes through both bodies in every context readers may encounter elsewhere in
Bittensor documentation. For privileged changes in the documented governance flow, however, the
two-body model is the intended safeguard: proposal creation and proposal approval are separated, and
neither body can complete the process alone.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo)

## What Bicameral Legislature Does Not Mean

The term describes structure, not live governance state. It does not list current Triumvirate
members, name active senators, or report whether any proposal is approved, rejected, or closed.
Anyone can inspect proposals before the Senate, but that public visibility is separate from Senate
voting authority and separate from final execution after close.

Readers should also avoid treating bicameral legislature as a subnet incentive concept. Approved
governance changes may affect subnet rules, including designs that enable multiple incentive
mechanisms, but the term itself names network-level proposal review — Triumvirate plus Senate — not
how a subnet scores miners or validators after those rules are already in place. For subnet
incentive vocabulary, use the subnet articles; for governance review, bicameral legislature names
the Triumvirate-and-Senate proposal path.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)

## Reader Boundary

This term names the governance structure, not the status of any specific proposal or the membership
of either body. For proposal records, readers should use the official governance references rather
than treating a glossary article as live governance state.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Bicameral legislature names Bittensor's two-body governance review for proposals. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator) notes that subnet
creators can configure multiple incentive mechanisms to distribute emissions across different types
of work or evaluation criteria.

For readers, bicameral legislature still names governance structure. Approved subnet design changes
may enable more than one incentive mechanism in a subnet market.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)

## Relationship to Triumvirate

Bicameral legislature and the Triumvirate are related but different parts of Bittensor governance
vocabulary. Bicameral legislature names the two-body structure that separates proposal creation and
proposal approval, while the Triumvirate is the specific governance body that creates proposals in
that structure. The
[Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)
defines the two-tier system comprising the Triumvirate and the Senate, and the
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate) describes
the Triumvirate as responsible for creating proposals.

For readers, bicameral legislature names the overall governance architecture, while the Triumvirate
names the proposal-origin body within that architecture.

References:
[Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)

## Relationship to Senate

Bicameral legislature and the Senate are related but different parts of Bittensor governance
vocabulary. Bicameral legislature names the two-body structure that separates proposal creation and
proposal approval, while the Senate is the specific governance body that reviews and approves or
rejects proposals in that structure. The
[Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)
defines the two-tier system comprising the Triumvirate and the Senate, and the
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation describes the Senate as
the body where senators vote on Triumvirate proposals.

For readers, bicameral legislature names the overall governance architecture, while the Senate names
the approval-side body within that architecture.

References:
[Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)
