# Validator Take

Validator take is the percentage of a validator's delegated-stake emissions that the validator keeps
before the remaining emissions are passed to stakers. The
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)
entry frames it as the fee percentage validators charge delegators for validation services.

## Emission Context

Validator take belongs inside Bittensor's emissions distribution flow. The
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) documentation explains that
accumulated subnet emissions are distributed at the end of each tempo, with one portion going to
validators and their stakers. The validator's allocation is determined first, then the validator
receives its take from that allocation, and then the remaining TAO and alpha are emitted to stakers.

This makes validator take a distribution concept rather than a separate reward source. It describes
how one validator/staker allocation is split after the validator side of emissions has already been
calculated.

## Delegated-Stake Boundary

The glossary definition limits validator take to the portion of emissions that depends on delegated
stake. It is not described as applying to the validator's own self-stake emissions. For readers, the
important distinction is that validator take is about the relationship between a validator and
delegators who stake to that validator, not about every emission the validator may receive.

## Relationship to Validator Emissions

[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions) explains
how validator emissions are calculated from validator bonds and miner emissions. Validator take is a
later distribution step: it does not decide the validator's underlying consensus performance or
which validators earn emissions. It describes how the resulting validator/staker allocation is
shared between the validator and the stakers who delegated to it.

## Relationship to Dividends

Validator take and dividends describe different stages of validator-side tokenomics. Dividends name
the validator-side consensus result derived from bonds and miner emissions, while validator take
names the fee share a validator keeps from delegated-stake emissions before the remainder flows to
stakers. The
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)
documentation describes the consensus result, and
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) places
validator take inside the later distribution path.

For readers, dividends answer what validator-side emissions result from consensus, while validator
take answers how a delegated-stake allocation is split between validator and delegators.

References:
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Validator take describes how delegated-stake emissions are split between a validator and its
delegators after subnet allocation. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that subnets may run more than one incentive mechanism, each with separate miner evaluation and bond
pools for Yuma Consensus, as described in
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets).

Those mechanism rules decide subnet work and scoring. Validator take does not define them. For
readers, multiple-mechanism context belongs with per-mechanism evaluation and emissions calculation,
while validator take still names the later validator-delegator fee share on the delegated-stake
portion of distribution.

## Relationship to Child Hotkeys

Validator take and child hotkeys both involve a "take" percentage, but they describe different
relationships. Validator take is the fee a validator keeps from its delegated-stake emissions before
the remainder flows to the stakers who delegated to it, as framed in the
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-). A
child hotkey is a separate key that a parent hotkey authorizes to validate on its behalf, and the
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys) documentation describes a
child take as the share a child hotkey keeps for validating for the parent, paid from the dividends
the parent's stake would otherwise receive on that subnet.

For readers, validator take governs how a validator splits delegated-stake emissions with the
external delegators who staked to it, while child take governs how a parent key and the child key it
authorized split the parent's own dividend share. The two are separate rates that can apply at the
same time: a hotkey can hold a delegate take for its delegators and, where it acts as a child, a
distinct child take for validating on a parent's behalf. Validator take describes a validator and
the stakers who delegate to it; child take describes a parent key and the validation key it
re-delegates to.

References:
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys)

## Reader Boundary

Validator take should not be read as a recommendation, a live rate, or a complete measure of
validator quality. A lower take can leave more of the delegated-stake portion for stakers, but
staking decisions also depend on validator behavior, subnet participation, emissions, slippage,
fees, and other risks. This article defines the term only at the concept level.

## Relationship to Coinbase

Validator take and coinbase are related but different parts of validator-side emission economics.
Validator take is the fee percentage a validator keeps from its delegated-stake emissions before the
remainder flows to stakers. Coinbase is the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission,
injects TAO into emitting subnets' pool reserves, accumulates pending emissions, and checks epoch
boundaries to trigger Yuma Consensus rounds. The
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)
describes the fee share, and the
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes the per-block emission operation.

Validator take is downstream of coinbase. Coinbase is what creates the validator dividends in the
first place — the Yuma Consensus rounds it triggers each epoch determine how much a validator and
its stakers earn from emissions. Validator take then applies to that delegated-stake portion,
splitting it between the validator and its delegators. Coinbase decides the size of the
validator-side emission; validator take decides how that already-determined amount is divided. The
take cannot exist without the emissions coinbase produces.
