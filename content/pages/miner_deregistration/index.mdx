# Miner Deregistration

Miner deregistration is the miner-specific case of deregistration in a Bittensor subnet.
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)
describes it as the removal of a low-performing miner when a subnet is full and a new miner is
registered.

## Distinction from UID Trimming

Miner deregistration removes one low-scoring miner when a full subnet accepts a new registration.
UID trimming is a subnet-owner bulk resize that removes many low emitters down to a chosen maximum
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[UID Trimming](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/uid-trimming)).

- **Miner deregistration** — single-slot displacement at registration time.
- **UID trimming** — owner-initiated bulk reduction and compression.

## Distinction from Validator Deregistration

Deregistration applies to subnet miners and validators. Miner deregistration names the
miner-specific removal case when a low-performing miner loses a UID slot on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration),
[Glossary: Miner Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#miner-deregistration)).

Validator deregistration is the parallel removal path when the displaced participant is a validator
rather than a miner
([Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

The [Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)
describes removing a subnet miner or subnet validator from a subnet due to poor performance.
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)
describes that removal process applied to a low-performing miner when a full subnet accepts a new
registration.

Deregistration is the general removal term; miner deregistration names the miner-specific case of
that process.

## Distinction from Subnet Deregistration

Miner deregistration vacates one UID row inside a subnet that continues to exist. Subnet
deregistration removes an entire subnet to free a network slot when the global subnet cap binds
([Subnet deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

A low-performing miner can be displaced without dissolving the subnet itself.

## Distinction from Register

Register is the entry action that pays a dynamic burn to claim a UID. Miner deregistration is the
removal outcome when a full subnet must displace an eligible incumbent for that entry
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

Registration burn payment and displacement eligibility are linked decisions on a full subnet, not
independent steps
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

## Distinction from Pruning Score

Pruning score ranks which incumbent is weakest when a full subnet must displace one participant.
Miner deregistration names the miner-specific removal event that follows that ranking
([Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings ranking for displacement candidates.
- **Miner deregistration** — miner-specific slot removal after eligibility rules apply.

Ranking vocabulary names who is weakest; deregistration vocabulary names the removal event
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

## Distinction from Coinbase

Coinbase allocates epoch emissions to occupied UID slots after validator evaluations are processed.
Miner deregistration vacates the miner's slot, so that row no longer participates in the coinbase
round that credits emissions
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Coinbase** — epoch emission allocation to occupied subnet slots.
- **Miner deregistration** — removal that ends a miner's slot eligibility for those rounds.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Miner deregistration names displacement of a lowest-scoring miner when subnet UID slots are full.
That roster-removal lifecycle is separate from the tempo pass that converts validator weight
submissions into emission shares
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Yuma still aggregates submitted weights and credits miner incentives and validator dividends for
participants who remain on the netuid when the epoch closes
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Immunity period and pruning score sit in related lifecycle vocabulary. Deregistration removes a
slot; Yuma names the recurring settlement pass that reads evaluation signals
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

- **Miner deregistration** — miner slot removal when capacity rules apply.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain mechanism that uses validator weights to determine emission shares
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Distinction from UID Slot

Miner deregistration names removal of a low-performing miner from a subnet. A UID slot names the
indexed participant position that displacement can vacate
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Miner deregistration** — miner-specific removal from a subnet UID row.
- **UID slot** — indexed participant position on a netuid.

Removal-event vocabulary and position vocabulary answer different slot-lifecycle questions
([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the term at a high level. It does not list current miners, count available
slots, rank subnet participants, or describe registration steps. Current displacement eligibility
belongs in mining references and live subnet state at the time of observation
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

Miner deregistration should be read as removal from a subnet miner slot, not as proof that the
account has been removed from every Bittensor role or network context.
