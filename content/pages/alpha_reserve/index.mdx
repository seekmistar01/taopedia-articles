# Alpha Reserve

Alpha reserve is subnet alpha held inside a subnet liquidity pool. In Dynamic TAO, the pool pairs a
TAO reserve with a subnet-specific alpha reserve
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

The term is a location label. It identifies pool-held alpha, not every alpha token associated with
the subnet.

## Distinction from TAO Reserve

TAO reserve and alpha reserve are the paired sides of a subnet liquidity pool. Alpha reserve names
the subnet-token side of that pair
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Readers quoting alpha price need both reserve sides, not alpha reserve alone.

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

Alpha outstanding names subnet alpha outside the reserve pool. Alpha reserve names pool-held alpha
inside the paired reserves
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection)).

Outstanding is outside-pool alpha; alpha reserve is pool inventory.

## Distinction from Alpha Price

Alpha price is the TAO-denominated quote derived from TAO reserve divided by alpha reserve. Alpha
reserve names the pool-held alpha quantity in that quote
([Understanding Subnets: Price/Rate of Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#pricerate-of-alpha-tokens)).

The reserve is inventory; alpha price is the reading of that paired state.

## Distinction from Slippage

Slippage names the received-amount gap when conversions move paired reserves. Alpha reserve names
the pool-held alpha side those moves interact with
([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

Slippage is a conversion outcome; alpha reserve is pool-side inventory.

## Distinction from Liquidity Positions

Liquidity positions are range-based depth commitments inside a subnet pool. Alpha reserve names the
pool-held alpha inventory those positions interact with
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

A position is a provider commitment; alpha reserve is the pool quantity side.

## Distinction from Protocol Alpha

Protocol alpha is subnet alpha accumulated by the Bittensor protocol during reserve injection
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

Alpha reserve names pool-held alpha inventory; protocol alpha names protocol-attributed alpha.

Protocol-alpha statements and alpha-reserve statements both need a netuid, but they name different
accounting categories on that subnet.

## Distinction from Arbitrage

Arbitrage is profit-seeking trading that acts on a price gap between venues. Alpha reserve names
pool-held alpha whose ratio to TAO reserve sets the pool quote
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

Reserve vocabulary describes pool state; arbitrage vocabulary describes gap-closing trading.

Readers should not treat pool inventory movement as proof that arbitrage occurred on its own.

## Distinction from Alpha Tokens

Alpha tokens and alpha reserve name an asset and the pool-held side of it. Alpha tokens are the
subnet-specific asset more broadly, including participant-held alpha, while alpha reserve is the
alpha held inside the subnet's pool used in conversion math
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

Asset vocabulary names the subnet token broadly; alpha reserve vocabulary names the pool inventory
side used in staking conversions and price quotes.

- **Alpha tokens** — the subnet asset broadly, including participant-held alpha.
- **Alpha reserve** — the alpha held inside the subnet pool.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Alpha reserve is the alpha held inside a subnet pool for staking conversions and price quotes. It
names pool inventory used in Dynamic TAO math, not the recurring settlement step that converts
validator weight submissions into emission shares
([Glossary: Alpha Reserve](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-reserve),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on each subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate alpha reserve from general alpha tokens and from protocol alpha. Pool
inventory supports conversion quotes; Yuma settles evaluation signals from eligible validators on
that netuid—not the reserve balance itself
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Readers should treat alpha reserve as pool-inventory vocabulary on one netuid, and Yuma as the
epoch-boundary mechanism that allocates rewards from consensus while validators remain active.

- **Alpha reserve** — alpha held inside the subnet pool.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Distinction from Price

Alpha reserve names the alpha side of the pool reserve ratio. Price names the broader market quote
relationship between TAO and alpha in the pool
([Glossary: Alpha Reserve](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-reserve),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Alpha reserve** — pool-held alpha quantity in conversion math.
- **Price** — ratio quote derived from paired pool reserves.

Inventory vocabulary and quote vocabulary answer different pool-accounting questions
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Reader Boundary

Alpha reserve is a tokenomics term, not a separate token, staking guide, or pool strategy. It should
be used when the focus is subnet alpha inside the pool
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

When the focus is the paired TAO side, TAO reserve is more precise. When the focus is alpha outside
the pool, alpha outstanding is more precise. When the focus is the broader tokenomics system,
Dynamic TAO is more precise.
