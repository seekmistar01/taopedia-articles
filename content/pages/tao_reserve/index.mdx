# TAO Reserve

A TAO reserve is the pool-held TAO side of a subnet's liquidity context in Bittensor. The official
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet as an automated market maker with two reserves: one containing TAO and one
containing the subnet's alpha token.

## Pool Context

The TAO reserve belongs to the subnet pool rather than to a participant balance. The same official
subnet overview describes TAO reserves as the amount of TAO staked into the subnet, paired with an
alpha reserve that contains alpha available for purchase. Together, those reserves form the pool
side of a subnet's Dynamic TAO economy.

This makes TAO reserve a tokenomics location concept. It does not redefine TAO itself; it describes
where TAO sits when it is part of a subnet's reserve pair.

## Price Context

The official subnet overview explains that a subnet pool's reserve ratio determines the price of the
subnet's alpha token. TAO reserve is one side of that ratio, while alpha reserve is the other. For
readers, the important point is that reserve language describes the pool relationship between TAO
and subnet alpha, not a standalone price quote.

Exact reserve amounts and prices are current state values. The concept article only identifies the
TAO reserve as the pool-held TAO side used in that relationship.

## Emission Context

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection) documentation
describes TAO reserve injection as adding TAO to subnet reserves according to emission allocation.
Under flow-based emissions, subnet-level TAO reserve injection is determined from net TAO flow
rather than only from token price.

This puts TAO reserve in the injection stage of emissions. Later participant distribution still
depends on the subnet's reward and consensus processes, so TAO reserve injection should not be read
as a direct statement about any individual miner, validator, staker, or subnet owner payout.

## Relationship to Alpha Reserve

TAO reserve and alpha reserve are paired pool-side concepts. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) source
describes the two reserves together, and the
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection) documentation
explains alpha reserve injection as proportional to TAO injection so the pool can grow without
changing the alpha token price from the injection itself.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

TAO reserve names the TAO side of a subnet liquidity pool. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, TAO reserve still describes pool-held TAO in one subnet market.

## Reader Boundary

TAO reserve is not a staking tutorial, a live subnet ranking, or a yield estimate. It is a
tokenomics term for the pool-held TAO side of a subnet's liquidity context. Live reserve values,
prices, and trading outcomes should be checked in current official tools or chain data.
