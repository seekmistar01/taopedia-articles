# Alpha Tokens

Alpha tokens are subnet-specific assets in Bittensor. They are separate from TAO and give each
subnet its own tokenomics context for emissions, staking, and liquidity pools
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term is broad alpha-side vocabulary. It names the subnet-specific asset family rather than one
pool reserve, one price, or one staking action.

## Subnet Asset

An alpha token represents value inside a specific subnet rather than across the whole Bittensor
network. TAO remains the network asset, while alpha tokens belong to subnet-level tokenomics
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

TAO and alpha-token language stay separate. A TAO supply or emission statement is not automatically
a statement about a subnet's alpha-token context.

The subnet label is part of the asset context. Two subnets can both use alpha-token vocabulary
without sharing the same pool, reserve, staking, or emission setting.

That subnet label is the main reader cue. Alpha-token statements need the relevant subnet because
the asset is not a single network-wide pool token
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Emissions and Pools

Bittensor emission uses both TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. TAO enters subnet contexts, while
alpha tokens appear in subnet-level reward and reserve flows
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

Subnets use paired TAO and alpha reserve contexts. Each subnet can be read as an automated market
maker with TAO and subnet-specific alpha reserves
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That means alpha tokens are not only reward vocabulary. They are also part of the subnet pool design
that supports Dynamic TAO tokenomics. TAO is network-scoped; alpha tokens are subnet-scoped assets
used inside each subnet's emission and pool context.

## Reserve and Outstanding Context

Alpha reserve is related but narrower than alpha tokens. It points to the pool-held side of a
subnet's alpha context, while alpha tokens refer to the subnet-specific asset more broadly
([Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Alpha outstanding is another narrower term. It refers to alpha outside the reserve pool, while alpha
reserve refers to alpha inside the pool
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Together, reserve and outstanding vocabulary split where subnet alpha sits. Alpha-token vocabulary
stays wider: it names the asset that can appear in either context.

Protocol alpha is another neighboring phrase. It describes the protocol-side alpha vocabulary used
in tokenomics references, while alpha tokens remain the subnet-specific asset frame for readers
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

It does not replace the reserve, outstanding, price, or halving terms.

## Staking and Delegation

Outside the root subnet, stake can be represented in a subnet's alpha units. Delegation places
subnet alpha in staking context, while slippage explains why pool conditions matter when TAO and
alpha interact ([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

Alpha-token staking language stays scoped. It describes subnet-denominated stake context, not TAO
itself.

When TAO and alpha interact through a pool, the pool context matters for how the relationship is
read. Alpha-token vocabulary belongs with the subnet and pool setting being discussed.

## Halving and Price Context

Alpha tokens also have subnet-level halving context. Halving mechanisms distinguish network-level
TAO halving from subnet-level alpha halving
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

That distinction matters because the word "halving" can apply at different asset scopes. A TAO
halving statement and an alpha halving statement identify different asset scopes.

Alpha price is another narrower alpha-side term. It is the TAO-denominated quote derived from a
subnet's TAO reserve and alpha reserve, while alpha tokens refer to the asset being priced
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## A Subnet's Alpha Reserve Starts Near Zero

When a subnet is created, its alpha reserve does not begin with a meaningful starting balance.
Subnet documentation describes the reserve as initialized with a negligible amount of liquidity,
around 10^-9, before any staking activity builds it up
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That starting point matters for reading early subnet examples. An alpha-token price or reserve
figure observed immediately after a subnet's creation reflects this near-zero initialization rather
than a market-tested valuation.

## Narrower Alpha Labels Name Specific Pool Sides

Alpha price, alpha reserve, alpha outstanding, and alpha halving each name a more specific part of
subnet alpha vocabulary than alpha tokens alone
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Using the narrower label keeps pool-side and emission-side claims precise.

## TAO, Alpha Tokens, and Dynamic TAO on a Subnet Pool

On a subnet such as netuid 1, [TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau)
remains the network asset while alpha tokens name that subnet's local asset inside
[Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao) tokenomics
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Emission adds alpha through reserve injection and distribution paths, while staking and unstaking
move value through the paired TAO and alpha reserves that define alpha price
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)). Alpha-token vocabulary stays at the
asset level; alpha reserve, alpha outstanding, and alpha halving name narrower parts of that same
subnet context ([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For alpha tokens
on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how pool, reserve, and staking examples should
be read.

In localnet, alpha tokens on netuid 1 can be exercised in an isolated environment. Localnet reserve
values and staking conversions reflect that test chain rather than production Dynamic TAO behavior.

On testnet, alpha-token pools on netuid 1 apply in a shared non-production network where reserve
ratios and emission settings are separate from mainnet.

On mainnet, alpha tokens on netuid 1 concern live production subnet pools, emission paths, and
staking conversions on the selected subnet
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Alpha-token examples from one environment should not be
read as subnet asset behavior on another network.

## Subnet-Local Alpha After Pool Conversion

Staking documentation describes TAO entering a subnet liquidity pool and emerging as alpha-
denominated stake on the other side of the paired reserves. Alpha-token vocabulary therefore names
the subnet asset participants hold after that conversion path, not raw TAO sitting on a hotkey
balance
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Understanding Subnets: Liquidity Pools](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#liquidity-pools),
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

For readers, the same holder staking on two subnets ends up with two separate alpha positions
because each conversion happens in that subnet's pool. Root subnet staking remains the contrasting
path documentation keeps in TAO terms
([Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau)).

Unstaking reverses the path through the same pool context rather than withdrawing alpha as if it
were network-wide TAO
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

## Alpha Tokens Differ From Pool Reserve Labels

Understanding subnets documentation separates alpha tokens held by participants from alpha reserves
held inside the subnet pool. Alpha-token vocabulary names the subnet asset in participant and market
contexts, while alpha reserve vocabulary names the pool-held side used in conversion math
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

For readers, that keeps asset vocabulary distinct from reserve accounting. Emission injection,
registration recycling, and staking all move value through the paired reserves without renaming the
underlying subnet asset participants discuss as alpha tokens
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

Bridging vocabulary stays on the TAO and vTAO side and should not be read as moving subnet alpha
inventory through the EVM bridge path
([Token Bridging](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/evm-tutorials/bridge-vtao)).

## Distinction from TAO

Alpha tokens and TAO name a subnet-specific asset and the network asset. Alpha tokens give each
subnet its own token context, while TAO is the network-scoped asset that exists across the whole
Bittensor network ([Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Alpha tokens** — subnet-scoped assets local to each subnet.
- **TAO** — the network-scoped asset across Bittensor.

## Distinction from Alpha Reserve

Alpha tokens and alpha reserve name an asset and the pool-held side of it. Alpha tokens are the
subnet-specific asset more broadly, while alpha reserve is the alpha held inside the subnet's pool
used in conversion math
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

- **Alpha tokens** — the subnet asset broadly, including participant-held alpha.
- **Alpha reserve** — the alpha held inside the subnet pool.

## Reader Boundary

Alpha tokens are the right term when the focus is the subnet-specific asset. If the focus is
network-scoped TAO, TAO is more precise. If the focus is the pool-held side of the subnet asset,
alpha reserve is more precise. If the focus is cross-subnet emission share, subnet weights are more
precise
([Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights),
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Those distinctions keep alpha tokens focused on subnet asset vocabulary, not a staking walkthrough,
pool calculation, or full explanation of Dynamic TAO.
