# Root Proportion

Root proportion is a Bittensor tokenomics measure for a given subnet. The
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion)
entry defines it as the relative weight of TAO staked to validators through the root subnet, rather
than directly through that subnet's alpha-token context.

## What It Measures

Root proportion compares root-routed TAO stake with the subnet's alpha-token issuance context. The
official glossary describes it mathematically as a ratio between stake on Root and the total
issuance of the subnet's alpha token. For a reader, the important point is directional: the measure
shows how much of the subnet's dividend context belongs to root stakers rather than alpha stakers.

References:
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)

## Dividend Context

The official glossary gives root proportion a range from `0` to `1`, where the value represents the
proportion of dividends going to root stakers. A higher value means more of the subnet's total stake
context is held by stakers in root. A lower value means more dividends remain in the subnet's alpha
context.

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation provides the broader
setting: Bittensor emissions can reach both TAO stakers on the root subnet and alpha stakers on a
mining subnet. Root proportion belongs to that split, not to a live price quote or a general subnet
ranking.

References:
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to Root Subnet

Root proportion and the Root Subnet address related but different parts of Bittensor staking and
emission context. The
[Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)
describes the special subnet where TAO holders stake in a subnet-agnostic way, while the
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion)
measures how much of a subnet's dividend context is attributed to TAO staked through that root path.

For readers, the Root Subnet is the staking environment, while root proportion is a subnet-level
measure of how root-routed TAO stake shares in that subnet's dividend context.

References:
[Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion)

## Relationship to TAO Weight

Root proportion and TAO weight are related terms, but they answer different questions. TAO weight is
the global parameter for how TAO stake contributes relative to alpha stake. Root proportion is a
subnet-level measure of how much of a subnet's dividend context is attributed to root-staked TAO.

This distinction keeps the reader from treating root proportion as the same thing as validator stake
weight, TAO weight, or total subnet emissions.

References:
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Glossary: TAO Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-weight)

## Relationship to Alpha Staker

Root proportion and alpha staker describe the two sides of the subnet dividend split in Bittensor.
Root proportion measures how much of a subnet's dividend context belongs to stakers who stake
through the root subnet, while an alpha staker is someone who stakes directly through a subnet's
alpha-token context. The
[Glossary: Alpha Staker](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-staker) describes
alpha stakers as participants who stake into a specific subnet using that subnet's alpha token.

For readers, a higher root proportion means a larger share of subnet dividends flows to root
stakers; the remainder flows to alpha stakers. Root proportion is the measure that names how those
two staking contexts share dividend context within one subnet's emissions.

References:
[Glossary: Alpha Staker](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-staker),
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion)

## Relationship to Root Claim Type

Root proportion and root claim type are related but distinct parts of Bittensor dividend vocabulary.
Root proportion measures the share of a subnet's dividend context attributed to TAO staked through
the root subnet, while root claim type is the per-staker dividend-handling preference the official
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) names —
Keep, where dividends remain as the alpha of the originating subnet, or Swap, where dividends are
converted to TAO and restaked on Root. The
[Coinbase implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes root proportion as the input that determines how much of the subnet's alpha
emissions flow to root validators per block, while the
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
the same dividends being routed through Keep or Swap depending on each staker's setting.

For readers, root proportion names how a subnet's dividend context splits between root stakers and
alpha stakers, while root claim type names what the dividend actually becomes for a root staker once
a claim is processed. The two operate on different layers: proportion splits the dividend context
across staker groups, claim type routes the root side of that split through Keep or Swap.

References:
[Root Claim overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Coinbase implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)

## Reading the Term

A root-proportion claim should name the subnet and the context being measured. It should not be read
as a staking recommendation, a current chain value, or a claim about every subnet at once. The term
is safest when used as a supply-and-dividend proportion inside Bittensor's TAO and alpha-token
emission model.

References:
[Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)
