StreetVision by NATIX is Bittensor Subnet 72. Its on-chain description notes that, powered by
NATIX's "Internet of Cameras," StreetVision is advancing autonomous driving, Physical AI, and
map-making, and the project site is [natix.network](https://www.natix.network). The subnet applies
Bittensor's incentives to street-level computer vision: it rewards miners for building AI models
that look at road imagery and pull useful information out of it, starting with detecting
construction and roadwork sites. The codebase for the subnet is
[natixnetwork/streetvision-subnet](https://github.com/natixnetwork/streetvision-subnet).

## What StreetVision Rewards

The current task on the subnet is image classification: given a street image, decide whether it
shows roadwork. Miners build models that make that call, and they are rewarded according to how
accurately their model classifies the images the network puts in front of it. Detecting features
like roadwork reliably is the kind of building block that feeds larger uses such as keeping maps
current and supporting driving systems.

A distinctive part of the design is that rewards favor models that stay current. Miners publish
their classification models to a public model repository, and a submission counts at full strength
only for a limited window before its reward gradually decays. That decay pushes miners to keep
improving their models and resubmitting rather than relying indefinitely on an old one, which
steadily raises the quality of detection across the network.

## Miner and Validator Roles

Miners build and publish the image-classification models. Each model takes a street image and
predicts whether it contains the target feature, and a registered hotkey on netuid 72 is what ties a
miner's model and its results back to them. Keeping a model fresh through periodic resubmission is
part of staying competitive.

Validators test those models. They challenge miners with a balanced mix of real and synthetically
generated images drawn from a range of sources, so a model has to generalize rather than memorize,
and they score each miner on how accurately it classifies that mix. From those scores they set
weights on the network, which is how reward flows through [[Yuma Consensus]]. At the article level
the split is straightforward: miners supply the detection models, while validators measure their
accuracy and weight each miner accordingly.

## On-Chain Identity

Live SN72 subnet data is available on [TaoStats](https://taostats.io/subnets/72), which identifies
the subnet as StreetVision by NATIX. The live Finney identity for netuid 72 registers the subnet
name as StreetVision by NATIX and the GitHub repository as
[natixnetwork/streetvision-subnet](https://github.com/natixnetwork/streetvision-subnet).
