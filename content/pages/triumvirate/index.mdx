# Triumvirate

The Triumvirate is the Bittensor governance body associated with creating proposals. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate) defines it as a group
responsible for creating proposals, and the
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) places that role before Senate
approval or rejection.

## Composition

The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate) identifies the
Triumvirate as a group of three Opentensor Foundation employees. The
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate), by contrast, is a group of
[delegates](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) — validators that TAO
holders stake to — who control a significant portion of total network stake.

## Governance Role

In the governance flow, the Triumvirate originates proposals and the Senate reviews them. This split
keeps proposal creation separate from proposal approval. The
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation provides the review-body
context for proposals after they are created.

## Reader Boundary

This term names a governance role, not a live membership list, proposal status page, or statement
that any proposal has been approved. Readers should use the official governance references for
proposal records and Senate review context.

## Proposal Encapsulation

Governance proposals do not stand alone as empty labels. The official
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) describes proposals as
encapsulations of other extrinsics that run only after Senate approval conditions are met and a
Triumvirate member closes the proposal.

For readers, that means the Triumvirate's role includes packaging privileged call data for Senate
review. The body originates the proposal item; it does not by itself execute the enclosed extrinsics
without the later Senate and close steps.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)

## Senate Majority and Triumvirate Close

The same governance overview lists two execution conditions for approved proposals: the proposal
must obtain at least fifty percent plus one Senate approvals, and a Triumvirate member must close
the proposal. Both conditions are required before the enclosed call data can execute.

That split keeps proposal creation separate from final execution. The Triumvirate can originate a
proposal, but Senate consensus and an explicit close step still gate whether the privileged action
runs on chain.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)

## Replacing Single-Key Sudo Control

Before bicameral governance, privileged network actions relied on a single sudo key. The governance
overview explains that the protocol replaced that one-key model with Triumvirate proposal creation
plus Senate approval, so a malicious actor would need both a Triumvirate member and Senate majority
control to pass a privileged proposal.

For readers, the Triumvirate belongs to that security transition story. It names the proposal-origin
side of community governance rather than the old centralized sudo path for administrative actions.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo)

## Relationship to Senate

The Triumvirate and the Senate are related but different bodies in Bittensor governance vocabulary.
The Triumvirate creates proposals for privileged network changes, while the Senate reviews those
proposals and votes to approve or reject them. The
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate) describes
the proposal-origin role, and the [Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)
documentation describes the stake-based review body that votes on Triumvirate proposals.

For readers, the Triumvirate names proposal creation, while the Senate names proposal review before
any approved call data can be closed and executed.

References: [Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)

## Relationship to Proposal

The Triumvirate and a proposal are related but different scopes in Bittensor governance vocabulary.
The Triumvirate is the governance body that originates proposals, while a proposal is the governance
item containing privileged call data submitted for Senate review. The
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate) describes
the proposal-origin body, and the
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal) defines a proposal
as a suggestion or plan put forward by the Triumvirate for the Senate to vote on.

For readers, the Triumvirate names who creates governance items, while a proposal names the item
being reviewed.

References: [Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)

## Relationship to Bittensor Governance

The Triumvirate and Bittensor governance are related but different scopes in Bittensor governance
vocabulary. Bittensor governance names the full network process for reviewing and executing
privileged changes, while the Triumvirate names the proposal-origin body inside that process. The
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) describes the bicameral model in
which the Triumvirate creates proposals and the Senate approves or rejects them, and the
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate) defines the
Triumvirate as the group responsible for creating proposals.

For readers, Bittensor governance names the overall privileged-change review process, while the
Triumvirate names one body inside that process.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)

## Relationship to Subtensor

The Triumvirate and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary.
Subtensor names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while the Triumvirate names the
governance body that originates privileged proposals whose approved call data is executed on that
chain layer. The [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)
describes the blockchain layer, and the
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) explains that a successfully
closed proposal executes its call data in the same block as the close action.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while the Triumvirate names the governance origin for
privileged changes that may later execute on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For the
Triumvirate, that sequence changes how readers should interpret evidence about governance body
activity and proposal creation.

In localnet, Triumvirate governance mechanics can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet
proposals and governance flows do not represent production governance decisions.

On testnet, Triumvirate activity can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet
proposals are separate from mainnet governance state and live execution outcomes.

On mainnet, the Triumvirate is the live Bittensor governance body whose proposals enter the Senate
review flow before approved call data can execute on-chain. The
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) describes the proposal lifecycle
that applies on the production network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A Triumvirate example from one environment should not be
read as representing governance authority or proposal outcomes in another environment.
