# Bicameral Legislature

Bicameral legislature is the two-body governance structure used for Bittensor proposal review. The
term names a system made of the Triumvirate and the Senate, with proposal creation separated from
proposal approval
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

The term is structural. It names how governance responsibility is divided, not a proposal, proposal
hash, vote, or final governance outcome
([Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal),
[Glossary: Proposal Hash](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal-hash)).

This gives readers the governance map before the individual roles are discussed. Triumvirate and
Senate are the two bodies; proposal, proposal hash, senator, and vote records are neighboring terms
inside that structure.

## Two-Body Structure

The Triumvirate and Senate are separate governance bodies. The Triumvirate originates proposals, and
the Senate reviews those proposals for approval or rejection
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

Bicameral legislature is broader than either body alone. It names the arrangement that pairs one
origin side with one review side
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

Body-specific terms are narrower. Triumvirate names the proposal-origin body, Senate names the
review body, and bicameral legislature names the structure that connects them
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

The separation matters because the bodies do different work. One side creates proposals; the other
side reviews them for approval or rejection
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

## How the Senate Side Is Composed

The review body in this structure is not open-ended. Senate documentation states the Senate holds
twelve seats, and a coldkey becomes eligible for one once it has registered a hotkey-coldkey pair on
any subnetwork, holds hotkey stake greater than 2% of the network's total stake through delegation
or self-stake, and has elected to participate in the Senate
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

The seat list can still change after all twelve seats are filled. Senate documentation states that a
delegate who wishes to join replaces the lowest-stake sitting member as long as they hold more stake
in the network ([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

For the bicameral structure, this means the approval side is stake-gated and capped, while the
Triumvirate origin side is separate. The two-body division pairs a proposal-origin body with a
stake-weighted, size-limited review body rather than a single authority
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

## A Stage, Not a Final Destination

The bicameral legislature is framed as a step along a longer path rather than the end state of
Bittensor governance. Official documentation describes the governance protocol as transitioning
network management from foundation centralization toward community ownership over time, with the
bicameral legislature named as the first stage of that transition
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

That framing keeps the two-body structure from being read as a permanent governance design. It is
the documented starting arrangement for moving privileged control away from the foundation, not a
claim that later stages will keep the same Triumvirate-and-Senate shape.

## Proposal Path

In the governance flow, proposals originate with the Triumvirate and move to Senate review. The
Senate votes on whether to approve or reject them
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

Individual proposals are the items that move through the structure. A proposal hash identifies a
proposal, while the bicameral legislature names the governance arrangement around proposal creation
and review ([Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal),
[Glossary: Proposal Hash](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal-hash),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

Proposal hashes fit the path as identifiers for items under review. They do not create a third body
or replace the Triumvirate/Senate division
([Glossary: Proposal Hash](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal-hash)).

A senator belongs to the review side of the path. Senator names the delegate role that votes inside
the Senate, while bicameral legislature names the larger origin-and-review structure
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

## Neighboring Terms

Bicameral legislature is governance vocabulary, not subnet scoring vocabulary, proposal-status
tracking, or body-membership reporting. Approved governance changes may affect subnet rules, but the
term itself names the proposal-review structure made of the Triumvirate and Senate
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)).

The stable concept is the separation of proposal creation and proposal approval. Proposal records,
Senate votes, and applied changes have their own governance records
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

That gives the term a practical reading. Triumvirate is the more precise term for the origin body,
Senate for the review body, and proposal for the item under review
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)).

## Distinction from Senator

A bicameral legislature and a senator are a governance structure and a member within it. A bicameral
legislature is Bittensor's two-chamber governance design pairing the Senate and the Triumvirate,
while a senator is an individual delegate who holds a voting role inside the Senate chamber
([Bittensor Governance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#senate)).

- **Bicameral legislature** — the two-chamber governance structure of Senate and Triumvirate.
- **Senator** — an individual delegate voting within the Senate chamber.

## Distinction from Triumvirate

Bicameral legislature and the Triumvirate sit at different levels of governance vocabulary.
Bicameral legislature names the two-body structure that pairs proposal creation with proposal
review, while the Triumvirate names the proposal-origin body inside that structure
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

- **Bicameral legislature** — the full two-body governance arrangement.
- **Triumvirate** — the proposal-origin side of that arrangement.

## Distinction from Senate

Bicameral legislature and the Senate also differ in scope. Bicameral legislature names the paired
structure, while the Senate names the approval-side review body that votes on Triumvirate proposals
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Glossary: Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#senate),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

- **Bicameral legislature** — structural label for the two-body system.
- **Senate** — the review body that approves or disapproves proposals.

## Distinction from Proposal

Bicameral legislature names the two-body governance structure that pairs proposal creation with
proposal review. A proposal is the governance item the Triumvirate creates and the Senate votes on
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

- **Bicameral legislature** — structural label for the two-body governance system.
- **Proposal** — the item under review in that system.

Structure vocabulary and item vocabulary answer different governance questions
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

## Distinction from Sudo

Sudo and a bicameral legislature name an authority model and the structure that replaced it. Sudo is
the legacy single-key control concept, while a bicameral legislature is the two-body structure that
separates proposal creation from proposal approval
([Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo),
[Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)).

- **Sudo** — the legacy single-authority model being moved away from.
- **Bicameral legislature** — the two-body structure that replaced it.

Because of that replacement, privileged changes require the documented proposal-review path rather
than depending on one administrative key
([Governance Overview: security](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance#security),
[Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

The bicameral legislature names Bittensor's two-chamber on-chain governance structure. Triumvirate
proposal creation and Senate review belong to that voting design, not to subnet emission settlement
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

Yuma Consensus converts validator weight submissions into miner incentives and validator dividends
when the tempo boundary arrives on a selected netuid
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

A passed governance proposal can authorize privileged network changes. That outcome is still
separate vocabulary from the recurring weight-aggregation pass inside subnet markets
([Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)).

- **Bicameral legislature** — two-chamber on-chain governance structure.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Bicameral legislature is the governance structure connecting proposal creation and proposal
approval. Proposal records, hashes, votes, and downstream outcomes belong to neighboring governance
terms
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

The concise reading is two-body governance: the Triumvirate creates proposals, and the Senate
reviews them for approval or rejection.

## Triumvirate Originates, Senate Reviews

Governance documentation separates proposal creation from Senate approval
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

Bicameral legislature names that two-body structure rather than any single vote record.

## Development Stage Context

Bittensor separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet environments. Governance examples from one
environment belong to that environment because proposal records and vote outcomes can differ
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks),
[Introduction to Bittensor: Subnet development](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)).
