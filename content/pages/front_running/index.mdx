# Front-Running

Front-running is observing a pending transaction and submitting a similar transaction with higher
priority so that it executes first. In Bittensor it appears around subnet pool conversions visible
in the mempool before block inclusion
([Front-Running](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value),
[Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool)).

## What It Represents

A pending stake, unstake, or swap in the mempool can signal which way a subnet pool price is about
to move. An attacker submits a higher-priority trade ahead of the target to profit from that
movement ([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Price protection caps acceptable execution movement for the caller's own order. MEV Shield encrypts
sensitive pending contents so observers cannot read intent before inclusion
([Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection),
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield)).

Front-running examples from one network environment should not be read as representing production
mempool behavior on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from MEV

MEV names the broader value-extraction category built on transaction ordering and pre-block
visibility. Front-running is the specific tactic that submits ahead of a visible pending trade
([Glossary: MEV](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)).

- **Front-running** — ordering ahead of a visible pending trade.
- **MEV** — broader category covering front-running, sandwich attacks, and back-running.

## Distinction from Sandwich Attack

A sandwich attack wraps a victim trade with transactions before and after it. Front-running is the
before-only leg of that pattern
([Glossary: Sandwich Attack](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)).

- **Front-running** — ordering before the target swap.
- **Sandwich attack** — front-run plus back-run around one victim trade.

## Distinction from Back-Running

Back-running orders immediately after a target swap to profit from the move it just caused.
Front-running orders ahead of the target
([MEV](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)).

- **Front-running** — ordering before the target swap.
- **Back-running** — ordering immediately after the target swap.

## Distinction from Price Protection

Price protection is the trader-set tolerance that bounds how much adverse movement a swap will
accept. Front-running is the adversarial ordering tactic that exploits visible pending trades
([Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection)).

- **Front-running** — ordering ahead of a visible pending swap.
- **Price protection** — tolerance that caps loss from adverse execution.

## Distinction from MEV Shield

MEV Shield encrypts pending transaction data so it stays unreadable until finalization.
Front-running depends on reading visible pending details in the mempool
([MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield)).

- **Front-running** — ordering ahead of a visible pending swap.
- **MEV Shield** — encryption that hides pending data before inclusion.

## Distinction from Slippage

Slippage names the gap between expected and received amounts when a conversion moves through pool
reserves. Front-running is an adversarial ordering tactic that exploits that movement
([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Slippage** — received-amount gap from pool depth during a conversion.
- **Front-running** — adversarial ordering ahead of a visible pending trade.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Front-running names mempool ordering risk around pool conversions. Yuma Consensus converts submitted
validator weights into emission shares after evaluation completes
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Front-running** — trading ahead of a visible pending swap.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level and is not trading advice. Visible pending
transactions plus price-moving pools create the opening; price protection and MEV Shield narrow it
([Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection)).
