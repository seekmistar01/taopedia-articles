Investing is Bittensor Subnet 88. Its on-chain description is "Decentralized AUM," short for
decentralized assets under management, and the project site is
[Investing88.ai](https://investing88.ai). The subnet treats portfolio management itself as the work:
participants submit investment strategies, and the network rewards the ones that perform best when
measured against the risk they take. It began with staking strategies in the Tao/Alpha ecosystem and
has since extended to other asset classes such as US stocks. The codebase for the subnet is the
`mobiusfund/investing` repository.

## What Investing Rewards

A strategy on this subnet is essentially a plan for allocating capital across a set of assets.
Miners file these strategies, the subnet tracks how they would have performed over time, and rewards
flow to the strategies that hold up best. The emphasis is on risk-adjusted performance rather than
raw return: the scoring weighs factors such as how much a strategy earns, how volatile it is, and
how deep its losses get, so a steady strategy can outscore a riskier one with similar headline
returns.

Strategies are not set once and forgotten. A miner can revise or rebalance a strategy as conditions
change, and the network re-evaluates it accordingly. Because the subnet supports more than one asset
class, its rewards are divided among those classes, so strategies compete within their own market
rather than directly against unrelated ones.

## Miner and Validator Roles

Miners are the strategy providers. They submit a strategy — an allocation across the assets they
want to trade — and keep it updated as they refine their approach, with each strategy tied to a
hotkey registered on netuid 88. To discourage low-effort duplication, strategies that are too
similar to an existing one earn reduced scores.

Validators are the evaluation layer. They follow how each submitted strategy actually performs over
time, run it through the risk-adjusted scoring to rank it against the others in its asset class, and
set weights on the network based on the result. That weighting is how reward flows through
[[Yuma Consensus]]. At the article level the split is straightforward: miners supply the strategies,
while validators measure how well each one performs for the risk it takes and weight it accordingly.

## On-Chain Identity

Live SN88 subnet data is available on [TaoStats](https://taostats.io/subnets/88), which identifies
the subnet as Investing. Readers can reproduce the subnet identity fields directly with the
Bittensor CLI command documented in the
[btcli reference](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/btcli/#btcli-subnets-get-identity):
`btcli subnets get-identity --netuid 88 --network finney`. That Finney identity is the source of
truth for the subnet name and the GitHub repository associated with the subnet.
