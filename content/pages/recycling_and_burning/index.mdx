# Recycling and Burning

Recycling and burning are Bittensor token-accounting terms for different kinds of removal from
ordinary circulation
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

## What It Represents

Both terms describe removal from ordinary circulation. The accounting question is whether the amount
can return through future emission or stays unavailable for that route
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

Recycled tokens are subtracted from issuance so they can be emitted again. Transaction fees are one
official example: fees are recycled rather than permanently removed from future emission
([Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

Burned tokens remain in issuance accounting while unavailable for future emission. Subnet stake burn
illustrates the contrast: TAO moves into a subnet reserve while corresponding alpha is burned
([Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)).

Burn cost uses burn in its label. Local subnet creation guidance presents the TAO treatment as
recycled rather than destroyed
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Local subnet creation: Subnet creation cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/local-build/create-subnet#subnet-creation-cost)).

Recycling and burning examples depend on which network you connect to. Supply outcomes from one
environment should not be read as production behavior on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Issuance

Issuance tracks TAO circulating in the network. Recycling subtracts an amount so it can be emitted
again; burning leaves an amount unavailable for future emission
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

- **Issuance** — the circulating-supply record.
- **Recycling and burning** — removal treatments that adjust how supply can re-emit.

## Distinction from Transaction Fees

A transaction fee is the cost charged to process an action. Its collected amount is handled through
recycling rather than the fee label itself being a removal category
([Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

- **Transaction fee** — the cost charged to process an action.
- **Recycling and burning** — how the removed amount is treated for future emission.

## Distinction from Burn Cost

Burn cost is the dynamic TAO amount paid to register on a subnet. Recycling and burning describes
whether removed TAO can return through protocol emission
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling)).

- **Burn cost** — dynamic registration price for subnet entry.
- **Recycling and burning** — supply return and removal through protocol flows.

## Distinction from Halving

Halving cuts the rate at which new TAO enters circulation at supply thresholds. Recycling and
burning classify whether a specific deducted amount can be re-emitted
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

- **Recycling and burning** — removal treatments for specific deducted amounts.
- **Halving** — supply-threshold cut to the per-block emission rate.

## Distinction from Existential Deposit

An existential deposit is the minimum TAO balance an account must hold to exist on chain. Recycling
and burning are supply-accounting treatments for protocol-removed amounts
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).

- **Recycling and burning** — accounting treatment for protocol-removed amounts.
- **Existential deposit** — minimum balance below which an account can be reaped.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Recycling and burning classify whether deducted amounts can return toward future emission. Yuma
Consensus converts submitted validator weights into emission shares on a subnet
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Recycling and burning** — paired labels for re-emittable vs permanently removed amounts.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Recycling and burning are conceptual supply-accounting terms. The pair does not by itself prove an
exact amount, diagnose a transaction, or explain every mechanism that can move TAO or alpha
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).
