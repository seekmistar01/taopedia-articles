# Nominator

A nominator is a TAO holder who stakes with a validator in Bittensor delegation vocabulary
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term names the supplying side of the delegation relationship. It should be read separately from
the validator role that receives delegated stake and performs validation work.

## Role in Delegation

In Bittensor staking vocabulary, a nominator supplies TAO support to a validator. The delegate
glossary entry describes the receiving side as a subnet validator that receives staked TAO and
performs validation work
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

This keeps the relationship clear: nominator names the TAO-holder role, nominate names the staking
action, and delegate names the receiving validator role.

## Nominate and Stake Context

Nomination is the action vocabulary for staking TAO with a validator. The nominate glossary entry
defines the action, while the staking and delegation overview describes the broader staking support
relationship ([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

For readers, nominator should be read as the role label rather than a procedure. It does not name a
subnet miner, a validator permit, or a wallet guide.

## Emissions and Dividends Context

The nominator glossary places nominators in the consensus and staking-emissions picture through
delegated stake. The emission documentation provides the wider context for how Bittensor emissions
are distributed across validator and staker roles
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

This article does not calculate dividends or compare validators. It only defines the role that
supplies delegated TAO support.

## Token-Control Boundary

The glossary describes nominators as staking TAO with a validator while retaining control of their
tokens. That point matters because the nominator supplies support without becoming the validator
role itself ([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

In article prose, nominator should therefore not be collapsed into delegate. The two terms describe
opposite sides of a staking relationship.

## Subnet Context

Delegated stake is read inside a subnet staking context rather than as one undifferentiated network
balance. The staking and delegation overview describes stake in subnet alpha-token terms after the
staking conversion path
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

A nominator claim should therefore identify the validator and subnet context when those details
matter. The role label alone does not supply those details.

## Reader Boundary

Nominator should be read as a concept article term. It is not a validator recommendation, balance
report, staking instruction, or synonym for every staker in Bittensor
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The stable article-level point is that a nominator is the TAO-holder role that supplies delegated
stake to a validator.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. The nominator role
applies across the Bittensor lifecycle: staking and delegation relationships can be tested in
localnet for isolated development, exercised on testnet for shared non-production conditions, and
relied upon on mainnet for live operation with real emissions.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Nominator examples or staking outcomes from one
environment should not be read as representing production subnet performance in another environment.
