# Coinbase

Coinbase is Bittensor's per-block mechanism for emission flow across subnets
([Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The term belongs to tokenomics and chain-process vocabulary. It should be read separately from a
reward table, subnet strategy, or payout quote.

## Per-Block Role

The official coinbase documentation describes coinbase as running every block and coordinating TAO
and alpha emission distribution
([Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)).

That cadence is central to the concept. Coinbase is a recurring chain process, not a one-time event
or an article-level synonym for all Bittensor rewards.

## Liquidity Injection

Coinbase is described as injecting liquidity into subnet pools as part of the emission process
([Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

This makes coinbase part of the supply-flow side of Dynamic TAO. The article-level point is that
coinbase keeps the emission process moving at block cadence; the broader emissions documentation
explains how rewards fit into Bittensor tokenomics.

## Pending Emissions

The coinbase documentation also describes accumulation of pending emissions before distribution
([Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)).

Pending emissions are useful as a boundary concept. Coinbase can accumulate value toward later
distribution, but the final allocation depends on the relevant subnet consensus process.

## Yuma Consensus Handoff

Coinbase triggers subnet Yuma Consensus epochs. Yuma Consensus documentation describes the reward
distribution process that turns validator weight signals into miner incentives and validator
dividends ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)).

For readers, coinbase is the upstream per-block process and Yuma Consensus is the subnet consensus
step that allocates accumulated value.

## Block and Epoch Context

Coinbase is tied to block cadence, while an epoch names a subnet consensus period
([Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block),
[Glossary: Epoch](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#epoch)).

This distinction keeps timing vocabulary clear. A block is the chain progression unit; an epoch is
the subnet-level consensus cycle; coinbase is the recurring emission process that can trigger the
epoch handoff.

## Network Context

Bittensor documentation separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet contexts
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Coinbase claims should keep that environment boundary attached. A local or testnet observation is
evidence for that environment, while the concept article explains the mechanism described in the
official documentation.

## Reader Boundary

Coinbase should be read as the per-block Bittensor emission mechanism that injects liquidity,
accumulates pending emissions, and triggers subnet consensus epochs. It is not a payout quote, a
subnet performance claim, or a complete explanation of every reward outcome
([Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

That boundary keeps coinbase focused on the chain process rather than on unsupported claims about
specific subnets or future rewards.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. The coinbase
mechanism applies across the Bittensor lifecycle: per-block emission flows can be observed in
localnet for isolated chain testing, evaluated on testnet for shared non-production conditions, and
relied upon on mainnet for live operation with real emissions.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Coinbase observations or emission outcomes from one
environment should not be read as representing production subnet performance in another environment.
