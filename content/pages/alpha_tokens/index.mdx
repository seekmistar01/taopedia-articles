# Alpha Tokens

Alpha tokens are subnet-specific assets in Bittensor. They are separate from TAO and give each
subnet its own tokenomics context for emissions, staking, and liquidity pools
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term is broad alpha-side vocabulary. It names the subnet-specific asset family rather than one
pool reserve, one price, or one staking action.

## Distinction from TAO

Alpha tokens and TAO name a subnet-specific asset and the network asset. Alpha tokens give each
subnet its own token context, while TAO is the network-scoped asset that exists across the whole
Bittensor network ([Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Alpha tokens** — subnet-scoped assets local to each subnet.
- **TAO** — the network-scoped asset across Bittensor.

## Distinction from Alpha Reserve

Alpha tokens name the subnet-specific asset participants hold and trade. Alpha reserve names the
alpha held inside the subnet's pool used in conversion math
([Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-tokens),
[Glossary: Alpha Reserve](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-reserve)).

- **Alpha tokens** — subnet-scoped asset broadly, including participant-held alpha.
- **Alpha reserve** — alpha held inside the subnet pool.

Asset vocabulary and pool-inventory vocabulary answer different accounting questions
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Alpha tokens are the subnet-scoped asset traded and held inside one netuid's market. They name the
tokenomics unit for that subnet, not the recurring settlement step that converts validator weight
submissions into emission shares
([Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-tokens),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on each subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate alpha tokens from alpha reserve pool inventory and from TAO. Alpha names
the subnet asset; Yuma settles evaluation signals from eligible validators on that netuid—not the
token label or pool balance itself
([Glossary: Alpha Reserve](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-reserve)).

Readers should treat alpha tokens as subnet-market asset vocabulary on one netuid, and Yuma as the
epoch-boundary mechanism that allocates rewards from consensus while validators remain active.

- **Alpha tokens** — subnet-scoped asset inside a subnet market.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Distinction from Halving

Alpha tokens also have subnet-level halving context. Halving mechanisms distinguish network-level
TAO halving from subnet-level alpha halving
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

That distinction matters because the word "halving" can apply at different asset scopes. A TAO
halving statement and an alpha halving statement identify different asset scopes.

Alpha price is another narrower alpha-side term. It is the TAO-denominated quote derived from a
subnet's TAO reserve and alpha reserve, while alpha tokens refer to the asset being priced
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

Alpha reserve is related but narrower than alpha tokens. It points to the pool-held side of a
subnet's alpha context, while alpha tokens refer to the subnet-specific asset more broadly
([Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Alpha outstanding is another narrower term. It refers to alpha outside the reserve pool, while alpha
reserve refers to alpha inside the pool
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Together, reserve and outstanding vocabulary split where subnet alpha sits. Alpha-token vocabulary
stays wider: it names the asset that can appear in either context.

Protocol alpha is another neighboring phrase. It describes the protocol-side alpha vocabulary used
in tokenomics references, while alpha tokens remain the subnet-specific asset frame for readers
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

It does not replace the reserve, outstanding, price, or halving terms.

## Reader Boundary

Alpha tokens are the right term when the focus is the subnet-specific asset. If the focus is
network-scoped TAO, TAO is more precise. If the focus is the pool-held side of the subnet asset,
alpha reserve is more precise. If the focus is cross-subnet emission share, subnet weights are more
precise
([Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights),
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Those distinctions keep alpha tokens focused on subnet asset vocabulary, not a staking walkthrough,
pool calculation, or full explanation of Dynamic TAO.

## Distinction from Protocol Alpha

Alpha tokens and protocol alpha are different holdings of the same subnet currency. Alpha tokens are
the subnet's tradeable units that stakers receive and hold, while protocol alpha is alpha held by
the protocol itself rather than circulating among participants, so the two describe staker-held
versus protocol-held alpha
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Alpha tokens** — the tradeable subnet units stakers receive and hold.
- **Protocol alpha** — alpha held by the protocol rather than circulating.
