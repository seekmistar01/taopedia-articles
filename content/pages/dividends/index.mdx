# Dividends

Dividends are the validator-side emission result in Bittensor's consensus process. The
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)
documentation describes validator emissions as a validator's bonds to miners, weighted by the
miners' emission results.

## Consensus Context

Yuma Consensus turns validator weight signals into both miner-side and validator-side emission
outcomes. Miner emissions come from aggregate miner rankings after consensus filtering, while
validator emissions are calculated from the validator's bonds to miners and the miners' resulting
emissions.

This makes dividends a consensus outcome rather than a separate task performed by validators. The
validator first participates in evaluation, the consensus process resolves those signals, and the
dividend-like result belongs to the validator side of that resolution.

## Staker Payout Context

Validator dividends are one part of the broader emission distribution flow described in the official
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)
documentation. After Yuma Consensus resolves miner incentives and validator-side outcomes, delegated
stake can participate in downstream distribution paths that are separate from the dividend concept
itself.

For readers, this keeps dividends inside the validator-side consensus result while staker rewards
belong to the later distribution layer that follows consensus resolution.

Reference: [Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)

## Relationship to Bonds

Validator-miner bonds are the main context for dividends. The
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)
documentation explains that a validator's dividend is calculated from its EMA bond values with
miners and the miners' incentive values.

For readers, the important distinction is timing and role: bonds describe the smoothed relationship
between a validator and miners, while dividends describe the validator-side emission result that
uses those bonds.

## Relationship to Incentives

Incentives and dividends are related but not interchangeable. Incentives describe the miner-side
portion of the subnet result, while dividends describe the validator-side result derived from bonds
to those miner outcomes. A high-level reading is that miner incentives identify where miner-side
value landed, and dividends identify how validator-side emissions follow from bonded evaluation.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Dividends are usually discussed for one incentive mechanism at a time, but subnets can also run
multiple mechanisms in parallel. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) defines
multiple incentive mechanisms as independent evaluation systems, each with its own bond pool for
Yuma Consensus calculations.

For readers, dividends still name the validator-side consensus result connected to bonds and miner
incentives. When a subnet uses more than one mechanism, that bond and dividend context should be
read per mechanism rather than blended across the whole subnet.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Relationship to Yuma Consensus

Dividends and Yuma Consensus address related but different parts of Bittensor subnet incentive flow.
Yuma Consensus aggregates validator weight signals into miner incentives and validator dividends,
while dividends name the validator-side emission result produced from bonds and miner incentives
inside that process. The [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)
documentation describes clipping, bonding, and emission calculation, and the
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends) describes the
validator-side reward portion within emission distribution.

For readers, Yuma Consensus names the aggregation process, while dividends name one validator-side
outcome metric produced inside that process.

References: [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)

## Relationship to Validator-Miner Bonds

Dividends and validator-miner bonds address related but different parts of validator-side subnet
outcomes. Validator-miner bonds describe the smoothed relationship between a validator and miners
over time, while dividends name the validator-side emission result calculated from those bonds and
miner incentive values. The
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)
describes the bond relationship, and the
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends) describes the
validator-side reward portion within emission distribution.

For readers, validator-miner bonds name the smoothed relationship, while dividends name the
validator-side emission outcome that relationship helps shape.

References:
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)

## Relationship to Consensus-Based Weights

Dividends and consensus-based weights address related but different parts of validator-side subnet
outcomes. Consensus-based weights adjust validator-miner bond smoothing by consensus alignment so
validator dividends can better correlate with the miners a validator weights, while dividends name
the validator-side emission result calculated from bonds and miner incentive values. The
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)
documentation frames that dividend correlation goal, and the
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends) describes the
validator-side reward portion within emission distribution.

For readers, consensus-based weights name bond-path smoothing behavior, while dividends name the
validator-side emission outcome that smoothing helps shape.

References:
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)

## Relationship to Validator Weights

Dividends and validator weights address related but different parts of subnet evaluation structure.
Validator weights are the evaluation signals validators submit for miners, while dividends name the
validator-side emission result produced after those signals are processed through bonds and
consensus. The
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights)
describes the per-validator evaluation inputs, and the
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends) describes the
validator-side reward portion within emission distribution.

For readers, validator weights name the evaluation submissions, while dividends name the
validator-side emission outcome built from bonded evaluation inside consensus.

References:
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)

## Relationship to Autostaking

Dividends and autostaking address related but different parts of Bittensor emission and staking
vocabulary. Dividends are the validator-side emission result from Yuma Consensus, distributed to
validators based on their bonds and then further split to delegators who hold staked positions on
those validators, while autostaking is a mechanism that automatically routes a miner's emission
proceeds into a staking position on a chosen validator. The
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends) describes
dividends as the portion of emissions returned to delegators based on delegated stake, and the
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) documentation describes
autostaking as automatically staking a miner's emissions to a chosen validator.

A miner who enables autostaking accumulates a delegated staking position on the chosen validator
over time. That staked position is what makes the miner eligible for a share of the validator's
dividends in future emission rounds. Autostaking is the mechanism that builds the position;
dividends are the emission result that position can earn once it exists.

References: [Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)

## Distribution Boundary

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) documentation places
validator allocation inside a broader distribution flow that also includes validator take and staker
distribution. Dividends should not be read as the same thing as validator take. Validator take is a
later fee-share concept for delegated-stake emissions, while dividends are the validator-side
consensus result before those downstream splits are considered.

## Reader Boundary

Dividends are not a live yield quote, a validator recommendation, or an instruction for staking.
They are a Bittensor consensus and tokenomics term for the validator-side emission result connected
to bonds and miner incentives. Exact calculations and live values belong in the official references
and chain data.
