# Delegate Stake

Delegate stake is the TAO a Bittensor delegate stakes themselves, making it the self-staked portion
of the delegate's staking context
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

## Delegate Context

Delegate stake is read from the delegate side of the staking relationship. A delegate is a subnet
validator that receives stake from nominators
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)). Delegate stake
names the delegate's own staked TAO, not the TAO supplied by nominators.

That makes the term narrower than total stake associated with a delegate. It is useful when the
reader needs to separate self-staked TAO from delegated TAO in the staking model.

## Self-Stake and Effective Stake

Delegate stake and effective stake describe different totals. Effective stake is the total staked
TAO associated with a delegate, including the delegate's own TAO and TAO delegated by nominators
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

The boundary is simple: delegate stake is the self-staked component, while effective stake is the
combined total. This keeps delegate stake from being mistaken for every TAO amount associated with a
delegate.

## Nominator Boundary

Nominators stake TAO to validators
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)). That
nominated TAO can add to the delegate's broader staking context, but it is not delegate stake
because it did not come from the delegate themselves.

This distinction helps avoid role confusion. Delegate stake describes the delegate's own commitment,
while nominator stake describes TAO supplied by other accounts to the validator context.

## Stake-Weight Boundary

Delegate stake can contribute to stake-weight context, but it is not the same term as stake weight.
Stake weight is the computed stake value used to determine validator consensus power and emissions
in a subnet
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

Delegate stake therefore names one underlying staking amount. Stake weight names the consensus
influence measure that is computed from stake context inside a subnet.

## Staking Flow

The [Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation) documentation
places delegates and nominators inside the broader staking model. Delegate stake belongs to that
model as the delegate's self-staked TAO, which sits alongside but remains distinct from nomination
and effective-stake vocabulary.

For readers, the useful takeaway is that delegate stake is descriptive. It names who supplied the
TAO in a staking relationship, not a validator ranking or a complete account of subnet consensus.

## Reading Boundaries

Delegate stake is the right term when the focus is a delegate's self-staked TAO. If the question is
the combined total behind a delegate,
[effective stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake) is more
precise. If the question is validator influence inside subnet consensus,
[stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) is more precise.

These boundaries keep the article focused on a staking amount, not a staking guide, validator
selection advice, or a full explanation of reward outcomes.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For delegate
stake, that sequence changes how readers should interpret examples of validator self-stake amounts
and effective stake context.

In localnet, delegate stake mechanics can be observed in an isolated environment. Localnet delegate
self-stake figures reflect local chain settings and do not represent production validator stake
state.

On testnet, delegate stake can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet delegate
self-stake amounts are separate from mainnet validator stake state.

On mainnet, delegate stake is the live TAO a delegate validator stakes themselves in production
Bittensor operations. The
[Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation) documentation
describes the delegate stake context that applies on the production network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A delegate stake figure from one environment should not be
read as representing validator self-stake amounts in another environment.
