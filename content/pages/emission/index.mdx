# Emission

Emission is Bittensor's process for creating and allocating TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens.
The term covers both value entering subnet economic contexts and value reaching miners, validators,
stakers, and subnet creators through the protocol's allocation flow
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

The term is broader than a single reward label. Emission names the process that supplies and
distributes value; incentives, dividends, staking returns, and creator allocations name
role-specific outcomes within that process
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)).

## Injection and Distribution

Bittensor emission vocabulary separates injection from distribution. Injection concerns value
entering subnet contexts, while distribution concerns how that value is allocated across miners,
validators, stakers, and subnet creators during the emission process
([Emission: Injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

That distinction keeps emission from being mistaken for one balance, one role outcome, or one subnet
ranking. Emission is the combined tokenomics process; a miner incentive or validator dividend is a
narrower output of that process
([Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## TAO and Alpha Context

Emission in Bittensor involves both TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. TAO reserve injection and
alpha reserve injection are separate parts of the emission model, and the alpha side belongs to the
subnet-specific token context rather than to a universal alpha asset
([TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

This framing is important for tokenomics articles. A TAO-only explanation can miss the subnet pool
side of Dynamic TAO, while an alpha-only explanation can miss the network asset entering subnet
liquidity. Emission is the process that connects both sides to subnet economics
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Role Allocation

Emission reaches different role families through different vocabulary. Miner-side rewards are
usually discussed as incentives, validator-side rewards as dividends, and staking-related outcomes
as part of the broader staking and subnet-token allocation context
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Those names answer different questions. Emission identifies the full creation and allocation
process. Incentives and dividends identify role-specific slices of that process after subnet
evaluation and consensus have shaped the outcome
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Consensus Context

Yuma Consensus is one of the main contexts that turns validator evaluations into allocation
outcomes. Validators submit weights, consensus filters and aggregates those signals, and the result
affects miner incentives and validator dividends inside the subnet's emission flow
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights)).

Validator-miner bonds, consensus scores, and Commit Reveal belong near this context because they
shape how evaluations enter or move through consensus. They do not replace the word emission:
emission is the value-creation and allocation process around those consensus signals
([Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Glossary: Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal)).

## Supply Context

Emission is related to issuance and halving but is not the same term. Issuance concerns circulating
TAO supply, while halving concerns scheduled reductions in emission rate. Emission names the ongoing
process that creates and allocates value before those broader supply concepts are interpreted
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

Coinbase is another nearby term. Coinbase is the per-block mechanism involved in emission processing
and epoch-boundary activity, while emission is the broader tokenomics flow that readers see across
many blocks and epochs
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Mechanism and Network Context

Subnets can operate multiple incentive mechanisms, which means emission context can be split across
separate mechanism-specific evaluation and bond contexts inside the same subnet. Emission remains
the allocation process; mechanism count belongs to subnet incentive design
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)).

Network environment also matters. Mainnet, testnet, and localnet are separate contexts, so an
emission example from one environment is evidence about that environment rather than a universal
tokenomics statement for every Bittensor setting
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks),
[Introduction to Bittensor: Subnet development](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)).

## Reader Boundary

Emission is a reference term for Bittensor token creation and allocation. It is not a staking
recommendation, a subnet ranking table, or a role-specific operating guide. Exact amounts, role
outcomes, and subnet examples require the selected subnet, network environment, epoch context, and
relevant official source ([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).
