# Alpha Halving

An alpha halving is the subnet-scoped event that cuts a subnet's alpha-token emission rate in half
when that subnet's alpha supply reaches a threshold. The official
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes Bittensor
halvings as supply-threshold events that reduce emission rates, and the
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) overview places alpha-token emissions
inside per-subnet tokenomics.

In Taopedia terms, an alpha halving names a per-subnet event, not a network-wide one. It belongs
alongside the TAO halving concept because the two are parallel supply-threshold mechanisms operating
at different scopes.

## Why Alpha Halving Exists

Each subnet has its own alpha token with its own emission schedule. Without a per-subnet halving
mechanism, alpha-token emission would continue at a fixed rate indefinitely, expanding each subnet's
alpha supply without bound. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) frames halvings as
the mechanism that progressively reduces new token creation as supply grows.

For readers, an alpha halving is best understood as a release-valve on subnet-level token creation.
As a subnet's alpha supply crosses successive thresholds, the rate at which new alpha enters that
subnet slows down. This mirrors the TAO halving concept but operates independently inside each
subnet's own tokenomics.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## What an Alpha Halving Does

An alpha halving halves the emission rate of a single subnet's alpha token. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes the
threshold mechanism: when a specific supply level is reached, the emission rate cuts in half. For
alpha tokens, this threshold is tracked per subnet, so each subnet reaches its own alpha halvings on
its own supply timeline.

For readers, the key behavior is that an alpha halving slows alpha creation for one subnet without
affecting the network-wide TAO emission rate or any other subnet's alpha emissions. Two subnets can
be at different halving stages because their alpha supplies and emission histories differ.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Halving

Alpha halving and the TAO halving are related but different supply-threshold events. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes halvings
based on total token supply, and Bittensor applies that concept at two scopes: the TAO halving is
network-scoped, while the alpha halving is subnet-scoped.

For readers, the TAO halving names the network-wide reduction in TAO emission, while the alpha
halving names the per-subnet reduction in alpha emission. A TAO halving affects the whole network;
an alpha halving affects only one subnet. The two operate on independent supply thresholds and
timelines, so they should not be read as the same event.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)

## Relationship to Alpha Tokens

Alpha halving and alpha tokens are related because the alpha token is the asset whose emission the
halving reduces. The
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-tokens) describes
alpha tokens as subnet-specific assets used in emissions and staking, and the alpha halving is the
per-subnet mechanism that slows the creation of those assets over time.

For readers, alpha tokens name the subnet-scoped asset, while the alpha halving names the event that
reduces how quickly that asset is emitted. One names the token; the other names the supply-threshold
event that governs its emission rate.

References:
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-tokens),
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)

## Relationship to Emission

Alpha halving and emission are related because the halving directly changes the emission rate. The
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) overview describes emission as the
process of generating and allocating new tokens to participants, and the alpha halving is the
supply-threshold event that cuts the alpha side of that emission in half.

For readers, emission names the reward-flow process, while the alpha halving names the scheduled
reduction in the alpha portion of that flow. After an alpha halving, the subnet's alpha emission
rate is lower, which affects how much alpha participants can earn from that subnet.

References: [Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)

## Relationship to Subnet

Alpha halving and subnet are related because each halving is scoped to a single subnet. The
[Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet) describes a subnet as
an incentive-based competition market with its own digital commodity, and each subnet's alpha token
follows its own halving schedule based on that subnet's alpha supply.

For readers, subnet names the competition market, while the alpha halving names the per-subnet
emission-reduction event inside that market. Because the halving is subnet-scoped, the alpha halving
stage of one subnet says nothing about the stage of any other subnet.

References: [Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Subnet Stake Burn

Alpha halving and subnet stake burn are related but distinct supply-side mechanisms in Bittensor
subnet tokenomics. Alpha halving is the per-subnet supply-threshold event that halves a subnet's
alpha emission rate when the subnet's alpha supply reaches a threshold, while subnet stake burn is
the subnet-owner mechanism that adds TAO to a subnet's TAO reserve and burns the corresponding
alpha. The [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) documentation
describes the alpha-halving rule, and the
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn) documentation describes the
burn flow as a voluntary subnet buyback that moves alpha out of circulation.

For readers, alpha halving is a scheduled supply-side reduction tied to a subnet's alpha supply
milestones, while subnet stake burn is a subnet-owner-initiated pool action that removes alpha from
circulation in exchange for adding TAO to the subnet's reserve. The two are not interchangeable:
alpha halving slows future emission, subnet stake burn removes alpha that already exists. Both
reduce circulating alpha supply, but they operate on different points in the alpha lifecycle and are
triggered by different actors.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the alpha halving as a concept. It does not state current halving stages,
supply thresholds, exact dates, or per-subnet schedules. Readers should use the official Bittensor
tokenomics references for current halving data and exact parameters.
