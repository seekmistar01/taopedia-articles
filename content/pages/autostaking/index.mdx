# Autostaking

Autostaking is a Bittensor feature that automatically routes a miner's emissions into stake with a
validator selected by that miner
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The concept belongs to reward handling. Mining produces emissions, staking puts value behind a
validator, and autostaking connects those two ideas automatically after emissions are earned.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking names the destination activity, while autostaking names the automation that routes new miner
emissions into that activity
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

Routing vocabulary and destination vocabulary answer different questions. Autostaking feeds staking;
it is not a separate staking category.

- **Autostaking** — automatic routing of earned miner emissions into staking.
- **Staking** — attaching TAO support to a validator.

## Distinction from Dividends

Autostaking routes a miner's earned emissions into a validator-staking position, while dividends
name the validator-side emission result that Yuma Consensus produces from validator-miner bonds and
miner outcomes ([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

Routing vocabulary and validator-side outcome vocabulary answer different questions. Autostaking
names where miner emissions go next; dividends name validator-side payout categories from consensus.

- **Autostaking** — routing earned miner emissions into a staking position.
- **Dividends** — validator-side emission share from consensus distribution.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation is the broader action by which TAO support is attached to a validator. Autostaking is the
miner-side option that routes earned emissions into that delegated position automatically
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

- **Delegation** — attaching stake support to a validator.
- **Autostaking** — automatic routing of earned miner emissions into staking.

## Distinction from Validator Dividend

Validator dividend names validator-side emission outcomes after consensus. Autostaking names where a
miner's earned emissions are routed after they are credited
([Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Autostaking therefore concerns miner emission routing, while validator dividend vocabulary concerns
validator-side payout categories from the same broader emission pass
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Turning autostaking on or off changes what happens to credited miner emissions next; it does not
change how Yuma Consensus calculates those emissions in the first place
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

The routing destination still uses existing delegation vocabulary: autostaking selects which
validator position receives the restaked emissions after they are credited
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Delegation rewards are a delegator's proportional share of a validator's rewards after the
validator's take, while autostaking is the feature that automatically routes a miner's earned
emissions into a validator-staking position
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

Delegation rewards name what a staked position earns; autostaking names the automation that can fund
such a position from a miner's emissions.

## Distinction from Moving Stake

Moving stake is an operation that atomically transfers an existing delegated position from one
hotkey or subnet to another in a single transaction, without first withdrawing to the coldkey.
Autostaking is the mechanism that automatically routes a miner's emission proceeds into a staking
position on a chosen validator
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

Moving stake rearranges an existing position; autostaking accumulates a new position over time as
emissions arrive.

## Distinction from Unstaking

Autostaking routes miner emission proceeds into a staking position over time, while unstaking is the
exit path that removes from an accumulated delegated position through the ordinary withdrawal
process ([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

Autostaking adds to a position as emissions arrive; unstaking removes from that position when the
holder withdraws.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Autostaking is the miner setting that routes credited emissions into delegated stake on a chosen
validator hotkey. It names post-credit stake routing after Yuma credits miner incentives, not the
recurring settlement step that converts weight submissions into those credits
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on each subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate autostaking from unstaking and from manual nomination. Yuma credits miner
returns from consensus on that netuid; autostaking decides where those returns are restaked—not the
weight aggregation step itself
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

Readers should treat autostaking as post-emission routing vocabulary, and Yuma as the epoch-boundary
mechanism that allocates subnet rewards from consensus while validators remain active.

- **Autostaking** — routes credited miner emissions into validator stake.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Distinction from Emission

Autostaking names automatic routing of miner emissions into validator stake. Emission names the
broader creation and distribution flow that produces those miner returns
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Autostaking** — automatic stake routing after miner emissions credit.
- **Emission** — broader injection-and-distribution flow across the protocol.

Destination vocabulary names where miner returns go; emission vocabulary names the wider reward flow
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

## Distinction from Consensus

Mining emissions that autostaking can route are produced through the subnet's incentive and
consensus process. [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) converts
validator weight vectors into per-miner emission shares each tempo, and those shares are the source
material that autostaking then routes into a validator-staking position
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

In Bittensor's [Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao) model,
subnet emissions can involve both TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. Autostaking routes miner
emissions into the selected validator's staking position; the exact asset type is determined by the
subnet's tokenomics context. Autostaking names the automatic routing step that follows the
consensus-derived emission
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Reader Boundary

Autostaking names automatic routing of earned miner emissions into validator staking
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

It is not an earnings guarantee, validator recommendation, wallet guide, or instruction manual. It
is the routing concept that connects earned miner emissions with the selected validator-staking
destination.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Autostaking and effective stake relate as an action and a measured total. Autostaking is the
behavior of automatically restaking emissions back into stake so they compound, while effective
stake is a validator's combined self-and-delegated stake total that determines its consensus
influence; autostaked emissions add to the stake that feeds a validator's effective stake
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Autostaking** — automatically compounding emissions back into stake.
- **Effective stake** — the validator's combined self-and-delegated total that sets influence.
