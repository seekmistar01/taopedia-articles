# Alpha Low

Alpha low is the lower bound of the liquid alpha range, the pair of values that set how quickly
[validator-miner bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)
smooth when the liquid alpha feature is enabled. The
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters) reference
describes the alpha range as being set through `AlphaValues` via the `alpha_low` and `alpha_high`
parameters.

## What It Sets

Liquid alpha varies the bond-smoothing factor by how well a validator's weights align with
consensus, instead of holding that factor constant. Alpha low is the floor of that variable factor:
it caps how slow the smoothing can get at one end of the alignment range
([Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)).

The smoothing factor, often written as an EMA coefficient, controls how much each epoch's bond
update weighs new information against the prior bond. Alpha low pins the bottom of the allowed range
for that coefficient ([EMA](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema)).

## Distinction from Alpha High

Alpha low and alpha high are the two ends of the same range. Alpha low sets the lower bound of the
bond-smoothing factor, while alpha high sets the upper bound; together they bracket how far liquid
alpha may move the factor
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

- **Alpha low** — the lower bound of the smoothing factor.
- **Alpha high** — the upper bound of the smoothing factor.

## Distinction from Liquid Alpha

Liquid alpha is the feature that makes the bond-smoothing factor vary by consensus alignment, while
alpha low is one of the two bound values that feature uses. The feature decides that the factor
varies; alpha low decides how low it may go
([Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)).

## Why a Lower Bound Exists

Bounding the smoothing factor keeps liquid alpha from pushing bond adaptation to an extreme. Alpha
low guarantees the factor cannot fall below a chosen floor, which limits how heavily the system can
lean on prior bonds for validators at that end of the alignment range
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

For readers, alpha low is meaningful only as part of the liquid alpha range and applies when liquid
alpha is enabled for a subnet; its live value is per-subnet chain state and should be read for the
subnet in question
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).
