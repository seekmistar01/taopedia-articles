# Delegation Rewards

Delegation rewards are what a delegator earns for staking TAO to a validator instead of validating
itself. By backing a validator with stake, a delegator shares in the rewards that validator
produces, in proportion to how much stake it contributed
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## What It Represents

A delegator's reward is its proportional slice of validator-side returns, scaled by stake relative
to everything else delegated to the same validator. Before delegators are paid, the validator keeps
a take on the delegated portion, so delegation rewards are net of that commission at tempo
distribution
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

On netuid 1, examples illustrate one subnet's delegator return path. They should not be read as
representing production returns on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Autostaking

Delegation rewards are the proportional return delegated stake earns after distribution and take.
Autostaking automatically routes miner emissions into a validator-staking position
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation rewards** — proportional return delegated stake earns after distribution and take.
- **Autostaking** — routing path that can grow the delegated position over time.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Validator take is the commission a validator keeps from delegated-emission returns before nominators
receive the remainder. Delegation rewards are what remains for delegated stake after that split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Validator take** — commission kept from delegated returns before the nominator split.
- **Delegation rewards** — proportional share that remains for delegated stake.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is the participant role that delegates support to a validator. Delegation rewards are
the proportional returns that role earns on delegated stake after distribution
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Nominator** — role that supplies delegated support.
- **Delegation rewards** — proportional returns that role earns after distribution.

## Distinction from Nominate

Nominate is the action of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey. Delegation rewards are the
returns that attached support can earn after consensus distribution and take
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Nominate** — action of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Delegation rewards** — returns once distribution and take run at tempo end.

## Distinction from Dividends

Dividends are the validator-side emission outcomes Yuma Consensus produces from bonds and miner
incentives. Delegation rewards are the proportional share nominators receive after validator take is
applied to the delegated portion
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Dividends** — validator-side emission outcomes from consensus.
- **Delegation rewards** — nominator share after validator take on delegated emissions.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake is the combined total of delegate-owned and nominator-delegated TAO behind a
validator hotkey. Delegation rewards are the proportional returns delegated stake earns after
distribution and take
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Effective stake** — combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey.
- **Delegation rewards** — outcome vocabulary for what that support earns after take.

## Distinction from Liquidity Provider Rewards

Delegation rewards are a share of a validator's emissions earned by delegating stake to that
validator. Liquidity provider rewards are fee income from trades crossing a provided liquidity range
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

- **Delegation rewards** — share of validator emissions for delegated stake.
- **Liquidity provider rewards** — fee income from trades crossing a liquidity range.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking is the process of attaching TAO support to a validator. Delegation rewards are the share a
delegator earns from that validator's emissions for providing the support
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — process of attaching TAO support to a validator.
- **Delegation rewards** — share earned from that validator's emissions after take.

## Distinction from Emission

Emission names the broader subnet reward and injection flow. Delegation rewards name the nominator's
proportional share of validator-side returns after distribution and take on delegated stake
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Emission** — broader subnet reward and injection flow.
- **Delegation rewards** — nominator share inside one validator relationship.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Delegation rewards name the returns that flow to nominators who stake TAO to subnet validators after
distribution and validator take. They describe a delegator outcome inside one staking relationship,
not the consensus mechanism that builds emission shares from weight submissions
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary. It reads the weight matrix
from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result into miner
incentives and validator dividend shares that fund downstream delegation flows
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate delegation rewards from staking, emission, and validator take. Yuma
settles the validator-side pool from consensus; delegation rewards describe how nominators share
that pool after take—not how weights were aggregated
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Readers should treat Yuma as the settlement input to validator dividends, and delegation rewards as
the delegator-facing share of what remains after take on delegated stake.

- **Delegation rewards** — nominator returns after validator-side distribution and take.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level and is not financial advice. What a delegator actually
earns depends on the validator's performance, the take it sets, and the live distribution of stake
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation and delegation rewards relate as an arrangement and the income it produces. Delegation is
the act of assigning stake to a validator you do not operate, while delegation rewards are the
ongoing emissions that arrangement pays the delegator, after the validator's take is deducted
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Delegation** — the act of assigning stake to a validator you do not operate.
- **Delegation rewards** — the emissions that delegation pays, net of validator take.
