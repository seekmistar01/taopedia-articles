# Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator

Blockchain validator and subnet validator are different Bittensor roles. The official
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
distinguishes them by scope: a blockchain validator works at the network layer, while a subnet
validator works inside a specific subnet.

## Network-Layer Role

A blockchain validator is associated with Bittensor's base-chain activity. The glossary describes
this role in terms of validating transactions, producing blocks, and participating in network-wide
validation. This role is not the same as evaluating the work produced by miners inside a subnet.

## Subnet-Layer Role

A subnet validator operates inside one subnet. The glossary describes this role as evaluating miner
tasks and performance within that subnet. This makes the subnet validator role local to a subnet's
work market rather than a general base-chain validation role.

## Why the Distinction Matters

The shared word "validator" can make the two roles sound interchangeable, but they refer to
different layers of Bittensor. A blockchain validator is tied to network-level validation. A subnet
validator is tied to subnet-level evaluation of miner work. Reading the two terms separately avoids
confusing base-chain responsibilities with subnet evaluation responsibilities.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Subnet validators are usually discussed for one incentive mechanism at a time, but subnets can also
run multiple mechanisms in parallel. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that validators must evaluate miners separately for each mechanism.

For readers, a subnet validator still evaluates miner work inside one subnet market, but the
mechanism context matters. Subnet-level evaluation duties for one mechanism should be read
separately when a subnet uses more than one evaluation system.

selection, live validator sets, or role-specific performance metrics.

## Relationship to Subnet Miner

Blockchain validator and subnet miner address related but different layers of Bittensor vocabulary.
A blockchain validator is tied to network-level validation, while a subnet miner names the role that
produces work evaluated inside a subnet market. The
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
separates network-layer validation from subnet-layer evaluation, and the
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) describes
the work-producing role inside a subnet.

For readers, blockchain validator belongs to base-chain validation vocabulary, while a subnet miner
belongs to subnet work production that subnet validators evaluate.

References:

## Relationship to Validator Weights

Blockchain validator and validator weights address related but different layers of Bittensor weight
vocabulary. A blockchain validator is a network-layer role, while validator weights are evaluation
signals that subnet validators submit for miners inside a subnet's consensus process. The
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
places subnet validators inside subnet evaluation, and the
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights)
describes the per-validator evaluation inputs aggregated by Yuma Consensus.

For readers, blockchain validator names a base-chain role, while validator weights name subnet-level
evaluation submissions produced by subnet validators rather than blockchain validators.

References:
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator),
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights)

For readers, blockchain validator belongs to base-chain validation, while an incentive mechanism

## Relationship to Yuma Consensus

Blockchain validator and Yuma Consensus are related but different layers of Bittensor vocabulary. A
blockchain validator is a network-layer role tied to base-chain validation, while Yuma Consensus
aggregates subnet validator weight signals into miner incentives and validator dividends inside a
subnet. The
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
separates network validation from subnet evaluation, and
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) describes within-subnet
clipping, bonding, and emission calculation.

For readers, blockchain validator belongs to base-chain validation vocabulary, while Yuma Consensus
belongs to within-subnet evaluation aggregation performed by subnet validators.

References:
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator),

## Relationship to Commit Reveal

Blockchain validator and Commit Reveal are related but different layers of Bittensor vocabulary. A
blockchain validator is tied to network-level validation, while Commit Reveal names a subnet
mechanism that can conceal fresh validator weight evaluations until a later reveal time before
consensus uses them publicly. The
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
separates network validation from subnet evaluation, and the
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) documentation describes
concealed weight submission and delayed reveal inside subnet operation.

For readers, blockchain validator belongs to base-chain validation, while Commit Reveal belongs to
subnet-side weight visibility and timing around subnet validator submissions.

References:
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator),

## Relationship to Weight Copying

Blockchain validator and weight copying are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A
blockchain validator is a network-layer role, while weight copying describes when a subnet validator
relies on public weight information instead of producing an independent evaluation inside a subnet.
The
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
places subnet validators inside subnet evaluation, and the
[The Weight Copying Problem](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/weight-copying-in-bittensor)
documentation describes copied or derived weights as the problem behavior.

For readers, blockchain validator names a base-chain role, while weight copying names an
evaluation-quality problem that applies to subnet validators rather than blockchain validators.

References:
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator),

## Relationship to Subnet Deregistration

Blockchain validator, subnet validator, and subnet deregistration address related but different
layers of Bittensor network vocabulary. A blockchain validator is a network-layer role involved in
base-chain transaction validation and block production. A subnet validator evaluates miner work
inside a specific subnet market. Subnet deregistration is the mechanism that removes a subnet from
the network when the global subnet limit is reached, selecting the subnet with the lowest EMA of
alpha price for removal. The
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
separates the two validator scopes, and the
[Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration) documentation
describes how a subnet is removed and what happens to participant registrations.

Subnet deregistration affects subnet validators differently than blockchain validators. When a
subnet is deregistered, every subnet validator and miner registration inside that subnet is removed
simultaneously — the entire market those validators were evaluating no longer exists. A blockchain
validator's role is at the network layer, not inside one specific subnet, so a single subnet
deregistration event does not remove the blockchain validator's network-layer position, even if that
validator also had a subnet presence in the removed subnet. Subnet deregistration operates at the
scope where subnet validators work; blockchain validators operate at the network layer above it.

References:
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator),
[Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration)

## Relationship to Coinbase

Both validator roles relate to coinbase, but at different layers. A blockchain validator works at
the network layer producing blocks, while a subnet validator evaluates miner work inside a subnet.
The
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission,
accumulates pending emissions, and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch boundaries, while the
[Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator)
distinguishes the two roles.

Coinbase runs as part of per-block execution, which is the network-layer activity blockchain
validators are associated with — coinbase advances each block alongside ordinary block production.
Subnet validators sit on the other side of the relationship: they produce the weight signals that
coinbase-triggered epoch rounds read inside each subnet. The blockchain-validator layer is where
coinbase's per-block execution happens, while the subnet-validator layer supplies the evaluation
inputs those epoch rounds consume. Coinbase connects the two layers by running per block and acting
on subnet evaluation data at epoch boundaries.
