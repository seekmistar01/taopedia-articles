# Subnet Creator

A subnet creator is the role that defines what a Bittensor subnet is meant to produce and how that
work is evaluated
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term belongs to subnet design vocabulary. It identifies the role that gives a subnet its work
target before miners and validators participate in that subnet.

## Mechanism Scope

A subnet creator can shape more than one incentive mechanism inside a subnet. Multiple-mechanism
documentation describes separate mechanism contexts that can operate in parallel inside one subnet
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

For readers, one mechanism's task, protocol, or scoring model is mechanism-level context. A subnet
can contain more than one such context when multiple mechanisms are present.

That is why the creator term stays broader than one mechanism description. A mechanism explains a
particular evaluation path; the creator term names the role responsible for shaping the subnet that
contains those paths.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For the subnet
creator role on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret design
examples and mechanism outcomes.

In localnet, creators can design and validate work targets and evaluation rules in an isolated
environment. Local mechanism tests do not represent production subnet performance.

On testnet, creators can exercise those designs in a shared non-production network. Testnet subnet
behavior is separate from mainnet state on subnets such as netuid 1.

On mainnet, the subnet creator role applies to live incentive markets with real emissions on the
production Bittensor network
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Creator design examples or mechanism outcomes from one
environment are separate from production subnet performance in another environment.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

A subnet creator and the immunity period meet at configuration. A subnet creator is the role that
defines a subnet's task and evaluation and sets the subnet's hyperparameters, while the immunity
period is one such owner-settable hyperparameter that fixes how long a new UID is protected from
deregistration, so the creator chooses the immunity window its subnet enforces
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

## Distinction from Start Call

A subnet creator and a start call meet at activation. A subnet creator is the party that designs,
registers, and owns a subnet, while the start call is the activation step the creator issues to
begin the subnet's first emission period, so a created subnet does not emit until its creator makes
that call
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

## Distinction from Subnet Lease

Subnet creator names the party that registers and owns a subnet. A subnet lease is the
crowdloan-backed arrangement that distributes the creator's owner emission share among contributors
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans)).

- **Subnet creator** — owning party that originated the subnet design.
- **Subnet lease** — crowdloan arrangement splitting the owner emission share.

Origin vocabulary and ownership-arrangement vocabulary answer different funding questions
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Digital Commodities

A subnet creator defines a subnet's task and incentive mechanism. A digital commodity is the useful
work miners produce and validators evaluate inside that design
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Subnet creator** — role whose design decides what counts as product and how it is scored.
- **Digital commodity** — evaluated output those rules define.

Author vocabulary and product vocabulary answer different design questions
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Subnet creator names the role that defines a subnet's work and evaluation context. Yuma Consensus
converts validator weight submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

- **Subnet creator** — role that shapes work and evaluation rules on a subnet.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares. Subnet
  creator names design and evaluation context; Yuma processes validator weights into rewards
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Distinction from Registration Follows the Design

Subnet creator names who shaped the work target and evaluation frame. Registration vocabulary names
how miners and validators later join that already-defined subnet context
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

- **Subnet creator** — upstream design role behind the subnet's work context.
- **Registration** — downstream step where participants join that context.

Design vocabulary sits upstream of participant-entry vocabulary
([Glossary: Registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#registration)).

## Distinction from Protocol

Incentive-mechanism documentation separates task, protocol, and scoring model as related parts of
subnet design. A creator's design context can include each of those pieces without making them the
same term
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

The subnet protocol describes how miners and validators interact. The scoring model describes how
returned work is evaluated. The creator role sits around those design choices rather than replacing
them.

## Distinction from Subnet Task

A subnet creator defines the digital task miners are asked to perform. The subnet task names the
work target, while the creator role names who establishes that target for the subnet
([Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

The role and the task are not the same thing. The creator is the party responsible for the subnet's
design direction; the task is the work target miners try to satisfy
([Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task)).

## Distinction from Network

Subnet-creator examples belong to a network environment. The Bittensor Networks reference separates
mainnet, testnet, and localnet as different environments
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

The creator concept is stable, but observed subnet behavior belongs to the environment where it was
observed. A localnet design test, a testnet trial, and a mainnet subnet do not provide the same kind
of evidence.

## Reader Boundary

Subnet creator should not be read as a subnet launch guide, an operations checklist, a funding
explainer, or a quality rating for a specific subnet. It names the design role behind a subnet's
work target and evaluation method
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from Subnet Registration

A subnet creator and subnet registration relate as an actor and the process they perform. A subnet
creator is the entity that launches and owns a subnet, defining its task and incentive design, while
subnet registration is the on-chain process — paying the burn cost to claim a netuid — that brings
the subnet into existence
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Subnet Registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)).

- **Subnet creator** — the entity that launches and owns a subnet.
- **Subnet registration** — the on-chain process that brings a subnet into existence.
