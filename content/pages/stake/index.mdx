# Stake

Stake is the amount of currency tokens associated with a validator position in a Bittensor subnet
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term names a validator-associated amount. It should be read separately from the action of
staking and from the computed stake-weight measure used in consensus context.

## Validator Support Context

Stake is read in relation to validator support. The staking and delegation overview explains that
TAO holders can stake to a validator and that a validator's total stake includes stake delegated by
others
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

This makes stake a position-linked amount rather than a generic wallet balance. It describes support
associated with a validator in a subnet context.

## Subnet Context

Stake is local to subnet state. The staking-into-subnets documentation states that stake is always
local to a subnet and held in that subnet's alpha-token context
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

That locality matters because a stake amount does not stand alone as one network-wide number. It
needs the subnet and validator context attached.

## Own Stake and Delegated Support

The glossary definition includes both a validator's own stake and stake delegated from others. That
combined view matters when reading consensus and emission material
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

For readers, stake therefore names an aggregated support amount at the validator position. It is not
limited to tokens the validator personally supplied.

## Relationship to Stake Weight

Stake and stake weight are related but not identical. Stake names the underlying associated amount,
while stake weight is the computed measure used for validator consensus power and emissions
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

This distinction keeps amount vocabulary separate from consensus weighting vocabulary. Stake is an
input-side quantity; stake weight is the interpreted measure used downstream.

## Relationship to Delegation

Delegation is the process that attaches TAO support to a validator. Stake is the resulting
validator-associated amount that can include both self-supplied and delegated support
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

For readers, delegation is action vocabulary and stake is amount vocabulary. The two are closely
related but not interchangeable.

## Reader Boundary

Stake should be read as a reference term for validator-associated support inside subnet staking and
consensus. It is not a balance report, validator recommendation, or staking instruction
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The stable article-level point is that stake names the token amount associated with validator
support inside a Bittensor subnet.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. The stake concept
applies across the Bittensor lifecycle: validator stake amounts can be observed in localnet for
isolated testing, exercised on testnet for shared non-production conditions, and relied upon on
mainnet for live operation with real emissions.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Stake amount examples or consensus outcomes from one
environment should not be read as representing production subnet performance in another environment.
