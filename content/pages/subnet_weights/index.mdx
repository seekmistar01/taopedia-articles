# Subnet Weights

Subnet weights are Bittensor's way of expressing how much relative importance each subnet has when
emissions are allocated across subnets. The
[Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights) entry
defines the term through net TAO flows, while
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) explains how that flow signal is used
inside the broader emission process.

## Flow-based meaning

Under the flow-based emission model, subnet weights are tied to net TAO movement into and out of a
subnet through staking and unstaking activity. This makes the term different from a simple market
price label: it describes an allocation signal used to distribute emissions, not just the spot value
of a subnet's alpha token.

The official emission docs describe the flow-based model as replacing the previous price-based
approach for distributing TAO emissions across subnets:
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution-across-subnets).

## Smoothing context

Subnet weights should be read with their smoothing context. Bittensor does not treat every short
staking or unstaking movement as an immediate durable signal. The emission docs describe an
exponential moving average of net TAO flows, and the
[Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema#subnet-flow-emission-smoothing)
docs explain why this slow-moving signal reduces the effect of brief swings.

This means a subnet weight is best interpreted as a protocol-level allocation measure built from
sustained flow behavior, not as a live popularity score.

## Relationship to emissions

Subnet weights sit in the first stage of the emission process: allocation across subnets. After
liquidity is injected into subnet pools, Bittensor's participant-level distribution still depends on
subnet consensus and the rules that determine rewards within each subnet. The weight therefore
answers a narrow question: how much of the cross-subnet emission share is associated with a subnet.

The
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)
docs separate this allocation stage from the later distribution stage.

## Reader boundary

Subnet weights are not validator weights, weight vectors, or the weight matrix used inside subnet
consensus. Those terms concern how validators evaluate work within a subnet. Subnet weights concern
how Bittensor compares subnets when allocating emissions across the network.

## Staking Flow Signal

Subnet weights reflect net TAO movement from staking and unstaking activity across subnets. The
official [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation describes how
those flows feed the cross-subnet allocation stage, and the
[Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema) docs explain how short-term
swings are smoothed before they affect allocation.

For readers, inflow and outflow measure participation in subnet liquidity, not a subnet's task
quality or a validator's rank inside consensus.
