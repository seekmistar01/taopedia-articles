# Price Protection

Price protection is Bittensor's guardrail for adverse price movement during staking-related
activity. It is most relevant around staking and unstaking through subnet pools.

The official [Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection) page
describes strict, partial, and unsafe protection modes.
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage) explains why received
amounts can differ from static expectations during Bittensor staking and unstaking.

## Price-Movement Context

The [Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection) documentation ties
the feature to adverse price movement during staking-related activity. The guardrail belongs to
pool-price movement, not to every possible wallet or transaction risk.

This distinction keeps price protection close to slippage. Slippage describes the received-amount
difference, while price protection describes how staking-related activity responds when movement
crosses a selected boundary.

## Protection Modes

The [Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection) source describes
strict, partial, and unsafe modes. Strict protection rejects an action when the movement is outside
the allowed tolerance. Partial protection allows only the portion that stays inside the tolerance.
Unsafe mode proceeds without the guardrail.

These modes should be read as outcome boundaries, not interface instructions. The article-level
point is how the protection responds to adverse movement, not the syntax used by a specific tool.

## Submission and Execution Boundary

The [Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection) page describes price
movement relative to the submission reference and final execution. Pool conditions can change while
staking-related activity is pending or being processed, so the final result can differ from the
initial expectation.

That boundary matters because price protection is not a promise that pool conditions stay fixed. It
defines how the action should respond when the movement falls outside the selected tolerance.

## Relationship to Slippage

[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage) defines slippage as the
difference between expected and received amounts during Bittensor pool conversions. Price protection
is a guardrail around that kind of movement.

For readers, slippage names the outcome difference. Price protection names the response to
unacceptable movement. The two concepts are related, but they are not interchangeable.

## Relationship to MEV Shield

Price protection and MEV Shield address different parts of the same risk area.
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) hides sensitive pending details
before block inclusion, while price protection controls how staking-related activity handles adverse
price movement.

One protection does not replace the other. A source-backed claim should say whether it concerns
pending visibility, price movement, fee behavior, or final inclusion.

## Network Context

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Price-protection examples should keep network context
visible because pool state and observed outcomes belong to the environment being discussed.

Local examples can explain the concept, while testnet and mainnet examples belong to their own chain
histories. A claim about one environment should not be treated as evidence for another.

## Evidence Boundary

The [Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection) page supports claims
about strict, partial, and unsafe guardrail behavior. It does not guarantee a desired result, remove
pool movement, provide a fee quote, or replace sources about staking strategy.

When a claim depends on a concrete example, the article should identify the network, staking
direction, pool context, and source for any numeric amount. This page is a concept reference, not a
calculator or operating guide.
