# Burn Cost

Burn cost is the TAO amount associated with creating a new Bittensor subnet. Bittensor vocabulary
uses the term for TAO that is recycled during subnet creation, even though the amount is commonly
discussed through the familiar "burn cost" label
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)).

The term is narrower than a general fee. Burn cost belongs to the subnet creation path, not to every
transfer, validator action, or user interaction. It gives readers a specific name for the dynamic
TAO amount tied to bringing a subnet into existence
([Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost)).

## Distinction from Min Burn

Burn cost in this article names the recycled TAO amount for subnet creation. Min burn is a separate
neuron-registration hyperparameter that sets the lower bound on the dynamic TAO burn charged when a
hotkey registers on an existing subnet
([Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#minburn)).

- **Burn cost (subnet creation)** — recycled TAO required to open a new subnet market.
- **Min burn** — floor for neuron registration burn on an existing subnet.

The two should not be conflated when reading registration economics on a netuid that already exists
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

## Distinction from Max Burn

Subnet-creation burn cost is separate from max burn, the neuron-registration hyperparameter that
caps the dynamic registration burn on an existing subnet
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#maxburn),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)).

Subnet creation burn opens a new netuid; max burn bounds per-entry registration price inside a
subnet that already exists
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Readers comparing creation and entry costs should keep the lifecycle stage explicit. Burn cost funds
a new subnet market; max burn bounds joining a subnet that already has a netuid
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from Subnet Stake Burn

Burn cost and subnet stake burn are two different burns that should not be conflated. Burn cost is
the TAO amount recycled when creating a new subnet market, while subnet stake burn is a voluntary
subnet buyback that adds TAO to a subnet reserve while permanently removing alpha from circulation
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn),
[Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost)).

Creation vocabulary names the one-time cost to open a new netuid; stake-burn vocabulary names
ongoing reserve and supply adjustment on an existing subnet.

- **Burn cost** — recycled TAO required to create a new subnet market.
- **Subnet stake burn** — voluntary alpha buyback that adds TAO to subnet reserve.

## Distinction from Crowdloans

Burn cost names the TAO amount required for subnet creation, while a crowdloan names one collective
funding path that can help contributors pool TAO toward that creation action
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans),
[Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost)).

A crowdloan can be a path for meeting a creation cost, while burn cost remains the creation-side TAO
amount regardless of whether one creator or multiple contributors provide the funds.

- **Burn cost** — the dynamic TAO amount required to create a new subnet market.
- **Crowdloan** — collective funding toward a campaign-defined creation action.

## Chain Reads for netuid 1

Readers can verify the live subnet-creation burn cost for the active network with
`btcli subnets burn-cost --network finney`
([BTCLI: subnets burn-cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/btcli/#btcli-subnets-burn-cost),
[Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost)).

That read path keeps live subnet-creation cost claims tied to the network where creation is
observed. Neuron registration burn on netuid 1 is separate chain state reported through subnet
hyperparameters rather than through the subnet-creation burn-cost command
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

## Per-Subnet Live Value Boundary

Burn cost names the recycled TAO required to create a new subnet market. That amount is dynamic
network-level pricing rather than a hyperparameter stored on netuid 1
([Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)).

This article references netuid 1 when contrasting creation cost with neuron registration on an
existing subnet. That example helps readers separate subnet opening from joining a subnet that
already exists; it is not proof that creation cost equals registration burn on netuid 1
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Burn cost governs subnet creation; Yuma Consensus still converts validator weights into emission
shares on subnets that already exist
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Burn cost** — recycled TAO required to create a new subnet market.
- **Per-subnet boundary** — neuron registration burn on netuid 1 is separate chain state from
  creation cost.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Burn cost names the recycled TAO required to create a new subnet market. Yuma Consensus is the
separate tempo process that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares on subnets
that already exist
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Burn cost** — recycled TAO required to create a new subnet market.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares. Burn
  cost names subnet creation cost; Yuma processes validator weights into rewards
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

- **Dynamic pricing** — creation cost rises with demand and can decrease over time.
- **Recycling** — TAO is recycled rather than permanently destroyed.
- **Creation event** — subnet registration pays burn cost to receive a new netuid.

## Reader Boundary

Burn cost is a conceptual and accounting term, not timing advice, a price forecast, or a general
transfer fee. It names the dynamic TAO amount required for subnet creation, and Bittensor
documentation treats that TAO as recycled rather than simply destroyed
([Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)).

Live values belong in the tool or source that reports the relevant environment at the time of
observation.

## Distinction from Recycling

Burn cost and recycling describe a payment and the treatment of what is paid. Burn cost is the TAO
amount required to register a subnet through the registration burn path, while recycling and burning
describe what happens to consumed TAO afterward: recycled TAO is deducted from issuance and made
available to future emission, whereas burned TAO is removed from supply
([Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost),
[Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling)).

- **Burn cost** — the TAO price paid to register.
- **Recycling** — returning consumed TAO to future emission rather than destroying it.
