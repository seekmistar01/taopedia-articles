# Delegate

A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators and performs validation
tasks in one or more subnets
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)). The term sits
where Bittensor's validation role and delegated-stake vocabulary meet.

## Validation and Staking Role

Delegate is not only a staking label. It also points to the validating side of the relationship: the
delegate receives stake and performs validation work for subnet consensus. The
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)
places subnet validators in the miner-evaluation role, while the delegate glossary entry adds the
delegated-stake context.

This makes delegate more specific than a generic validator reference. It names a validator role that
can receive staked TAO from others while carrying out validation tasks.

## Delegator Side

A delegate is paired with the accounts that supply stake. The
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes the
TAO-holder role that stakes with validators, while the
[Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) entry names the validator
that receives that stake.

The relationship is directional: nominators supply delegated stake, and delegates receive that stake
while performing validation work. Keeping the two sides separate prevents delegate from being read
as the account role that supplies TAO.

## Delegate Stake and Effective Stake

Delegate stake and effective stake refine the staking side of the delegate role.
[Delegate stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake) is the TAO the
delegate stakes themselves.
[Effective stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake) is the broader
total associated with the delegate, including the delegate's own TAO and TAO delegated by
nominators.

These terms keep the delegate article from flattening every stake amount into one idea. Delegate
names the receiving validator role; delegate stake names the self-staked component; effective stake
names the combined total behind that role.

## Authorization Context

Validation rights are a separate question from the role label. A
[validator permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit) is the
permission flag for validation rights in a subnet, and
[VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit) lists the subnet IDs where a
delegate is authorized to validate.

That boundary keeps delegate from carrying every permission detail by itself. Delegate names the
staking-and-validation role; permit vocabulary describes whether validation rights exist for the
relevant subnet context.

## Reading Boundaries

Delegate is the right term when the focus is a validator that receives staked TAO from delegators.
If the focus is the action of staking TAO with a validator,
[nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) is more precise. If the
focus is the supplying role,
[nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) is more precise. If the
focus is the delegate's self-staked amount,
[delegate stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake) is more precise.

These boundaries keep delegate useful as a role term, not a staking procedure, a validator ranking,
or a complete explanation of reward outcomes.
