# Proposal

A proposal is a Bittensor governance item put forward by the Triumvirate for Senate review. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal) defines it as a suggestion
or plan put forward by the Triumvirate for the Senate to vote on.

## Governance Context

The [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) describes the same separation
of roles: the Triumvirate creates proposals, and the Senate approves or rejects them. In Taopedia,
the useful boundary is that a proposal is the item being reviewed, while the Triumvirate and Senate
are the governance bodies around it.

## Relationship to Proposal Hash

A proposal is referenced on chain by its proposal hash. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal-hash) defines the proposal
hash as the unique identifier for a proposal used in the voting process, so it is the reference for
the proposal rather than the proposal body itself. For readers, the proposal is the governance item
being reviewed, while the proposal hash is the identifier used to point at it during Senate voting.

References: [Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal),
[Proposal Hash](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal-hash)

## Reader Boundary

This article does not describe any live proposal, proposal record, or approval outcome. Readers
should use official governance references for current proposal details and Senate review context.

## Relationship to Triumvirate

A proposal and the Triumvirate are related but different scopes in Bittensor governance vocabulary.
The Triumvirate is the governance body that creates proposals, while a proposal is the governance
item put forward for Senate review. The
[Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate) describes
the proposal-origin role, and the
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal) defines a proposal
as a suggestion or plan put forward by the Triumvirate for the Senate to vote on.

For readers, the Triumvirate names who originates governance items, while a proposal names the item
submitted into Senate review.

References: [Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)

## Relationship to Senate

A proposal and the Senate are related but different scopes in Bittensor governance vocabulary. A
proposal is the governance item under review, while the Senate is the stake-based body that approves
or rejects Triumvirate proposals before execution. The
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal) defines the
proposal item, and the [Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation
describes how senators view proposals and cast approval or disapproval votes.

For readers, a proposal names the item being reviewed, while the Senate names the approval gate that
must pass before a Triumvirate member can close and execute it.

References: [Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)

## Relationship to Bittensor Governance

A proposal and Bittensor governance are related but different scopes in Bittensor governance
vocabulary. Bittensor governance names the full network process for reviewing and executing
privileged changes, while a proposal is the specific governance item that carries privileged call
data through that process. The [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)
describes the bicameral review flow, and the
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal) defines a proposal
as the item the Triumvirate puts forward for Senate vote.

For readers, Bittensor governance names the overall privileged-change process, while a proposal
names one item moving through that process.

References: [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)

## Relationship to Subtensor

A proposal and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while a proposal wraps privileged call
data that may execute on that chain layer after Senate approval and Triumvirate close. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) explains
that successfully closed proposal call data executes in the same block as the close action.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while a proposal names privileged call data that may
later execute on that layer after governance review.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)
