lium.io is Bittensor Subnet 51, a decentralized peer-to-peer GPU rental marketplace operated by
Datura. It connects miners, who contribute GPU-equipped machines, with renters, who pay to run
computational workloads such as machine learning or data analysis on them. The incentive-mechanism
code is maintained in the [Datura-ai/lium-io](https://github.com/Datura-ai/lium-io) repository, and
the rental front end is at lium.io.

## What the Subnet Provides

The subnet's product is rentable compute. Rather than producing a model or a dataset, it matches
supply (miners' GPUs) with demand (renters' jobs) and uses Bittensor to score the supply and route
payment. A renter browses available machines by GPU type, performance, and price, then rents the
capacity they need; the same network that ranks miners for emissions is what surfaces the better
machines to renters.

Because the resource being sold is real hardware, a miner's standing depends on what it actually
provides — GPU type, number of GPUs, bandwidth, and measured performance — rather than on a model
output.

## Miner and Validator Roles

Miners contribute GPU machines to the network. According to the repository, machines are scored on
factors such as GPU type, GPU count, bandwidth, and overall performance, and higher-performing
hardware earns better compensation. This ties a miner's reward to the quality of the compute it
makes available.

Validators maintain the integrity of the marketplace by securely connecting to miner machines to
verify their hardware specifications and performance, which both grades miners fairly and guards
against misrepresented or fraudulent machines. Those validator scores become the weights that feed
into [[Yuma Consensus]], which converts them into the incentive split across miners and validators,
paid in TAO.

## On-Chain Identity

Live SN51 data, including metagraph state and alpha token pool information, is available on
[TaoStats](https://taostats.io/subnets/51). The live Finney identity for netuid 51 registers the
subnet name as lium.io, with the description "revolutionizing the democratization of compute," and
points to the [Datura-ai/lium-io](https://github.com/Datura-ai/lium-io) repository, which is the
source of truth for the scoring and verification process described above.
