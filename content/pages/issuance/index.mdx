# Issuance

Issuance is the circulating amount of TAO in the Bittensor network. It includes directly held TAO
and TAO held in subnet liquidity pools
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## What It Represents

Issuance names a supply state, not a reward category. It tells readers what TAO amount is counted as
circulating before more specific emission, halving, recycling, or burning framing is applied
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

Emission, recycling, burning, and halving describe events or rules that can change how that supply
state is interpreted over time. Issuance is the supply record; those terms explain movement and rate
changes around it ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

TAO halving rules use issuance milestones. A network-level TAO halving reduces emission after total
TAO issuance reaches the relevant supply threshold. Alpha halving is separate: it concerns subnet
alpha-token emission rate on a named netuid, not this network TAO supply measure
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)).

Issuance examples depend on which network you connect to. Supply totals from one environment should
not be read as production circulation on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Emission

Emission names token creation and allocation flow. Issuance names the circulating TAO supply measure
those flows affect ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

- **Emission** — token creation and allocation flow over time.
- **Issuance** — circulating TAO amount counted at a moment.

## Distinction from Halving

Halving names supply-triggered emission-rate cuts as cumulative TAO issuance crosses documented
thresholds. Issuance is the live circulating measure halving rules reference
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

- **Issuance** — the circulating TAO supply measure.
- **Halving** — the threshold event that lowers future mint rates.

## Distinction from Maximum Supply

Maximum supply names the network-wide 21 million TAO ceiling. Issuance names circulating TAO counted
at a moment ([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

- **Maximum supply** — the fixed lifetime ceiling on total TAO issuance.
- **Issuance** — circulating TAO counted at a moment.

## Distinction from Block Reward

Block reward names the per-block network-wide TAO mint amount. Issuance names the cumulative
circulating total that mint path feeds
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Block reward** — the per-block network-wide TAO mint amount.
- **Issuance** — total circulating TAO including direct holdings and pool reserves.

## Distinction from Recycling and Burning

Recycling returns deducted TAO toward future emission accounting. Burning removes spendable
circulation without the same return path. Issuance is the supply record those adjustments interact
with
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning)).

- **Issuance** — the circulating TAO supply record.
- **Recycling and burning** — supply-flow adjustments that change how removed TAO relates to future
  emission.

## Distinction from Alpha Halving

Alpha halving is a subnet-scoped alpha emission-rate milestone. Issuance is the network TAO
circulating-supply measure used by TAO halving thresholds
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

- **Issuance** — network TAO circulating-supply measure.
- **Alpha halving** — subnet-scoped alpha emission-rate milestone.

## Distinction from Root Claims

Root claims route alpha dividends earned through Root Subnet staking into either alpha or TAO.
Issuance is the circulating TAO supply counted across the network
([Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

- **Root claims** — the rule that routes earned alpha dividends to alpha or TAO.
- **Issuance** — the circulating TAO supply measured network-wide.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Issuance names the circulating TAO supply record. Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that
converts submitted validator weights into emission shares on a subnet
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Issuance** — circulating TAO supply across wallets and subnet pools.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Issuance is high-level Bittensor tokenomics terminology. It is not a reward rule, subnet scoring
concept, or report of exact network totals
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Coinbase

Issuance and coinbase relate as an outcome and the process that produces it. Issuance is the
creation of new TAO added to total supply, while coinbase is the per-block process that computes and
applies emission, performing the issuance step each block
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Issuance** — the creation of new TAO added to total supply.
- **Coinbase** — the per-block process that computes and applies that issuance.

## Distinction from Burning

Issuance and burning name opposite directions in the same supply-accounting flow. Issuance is the
broader supply-creation picture for tokens entering the protocol, while burning is the permanent
removal of TAO from spendable circulation
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Issuance** — the broader supply-creation picture for tokens entering the protocol.
- **Burning** — the permanent removal of TAO from spendable circulation.
