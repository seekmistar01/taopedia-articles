# Alpha Outstanding

Alpha outstanding is subnet alpha outside a subnet's reserve pool. It describes the outside-reserve
side of subnet alpha in Bittensor tokenomics
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection)).

The term is a location label: it separates subnet alpha held in the automated market maker reserve
from subnet alpha outside that reserve.

## Outside-Pool Meaning

A subnet liquidity pool has reserve-side alpha and outside-reserve alpha. Alpha outstanding names
the outside-reserve side.

This does not make alpha outstanding a separate token. It is a way to describe where part of the
same subnet-specific alpha asset sits in the tokenomics model.

The subnet label stays attached. Alpha outstanding belongs to one subnet's alpha context, not to all
subnets at once and not to TAO itself.

## Accounting Role

The subnet overview describes a subnet economy as three pools: TAO reserves, alpha reserves, and
alpha outstanding
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That placement makes alpha outstanding an accounting term inside the subnet economy. It is the
outside-reserve alpha pool, while alpha reserve is the alpha side of the automated market maker and
TAO reserve is the TAO side.

The same overview describes alpha outstanding as alpha held in subnet hotkeys and as total stake in
the subnet. That is why the term can appear near staking and emission discussions without becoming a
staking procedure.

## Reserve and Protocol Alpha

Alpha reserve and alpha outstanding are opposite location terms for subnet alpha. Alpha reserve is
pool-held alpha, while alpha outstanding is alpha outside the reserve pool
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The distinction keeps reserve statements from being read as total-alpha statements. A subnet can
have alpha in reserve and alpha outstanding at the same time, but the phrases describe different
locations.

Protocol alpha identifies alpha gathered by the protocol during reserve-injection activity
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).
Alpha outstanding is broader outside-pool alpha context.

Keeping the terms separate avoids mixing a location label with a protocol-retained alpha category.
Protocol alpha can be outside ordinary staking context, but alpha outstanding remains the broader
outside-reserve term.

## Interpretation Pattern

Alpha outstanding is most useful when a sentence needs to separate pool inventory from outside-pool
subnet alpha. The term answers a location question inside a subnet's alpha economy.

That interpretation also limits what the term can carry. It does not identify the subnet's TAO
reserve, the subnet's alpha reserve, or the protocol alpha retained during an emission step.

The practical reading is therefore relational: alpha outstanding is read beside alpha reserve,
alpha-token price, ADR, and staking context rather than as a standalone operational instruction.

## Development Stage Context

Bittensor documentation separates localnet, testnet, and mainnet environments
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks),
[Introduction to Bittensor: Subnet development](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)).

Alpha outstanding examples keep that environment label attached. The concept is portable, but an
observed amount belongs to the subnet, reserve history, and network where it was observed.

Localnet examples are isolated development examples. Testnet examples are shared non-production
examples. Mainnet alpha-outstanding interpretation concerns production Dynamic TAO behavior.

## Distinction from Alpha Reserve

Alpha reserve names subnet alpha inside the liquidity pool. Alpha outstanding names subnet alpha
outside that reserve
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers comparing pool depth to stake-held alpha should use reserve vocabulary for pool-side alpha
and outstanding vocabulary for the outside-reserve side on the same netuid.

[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet economy as three pools: TAO reserve, alpha reserve, and alpha outstanding.
Alpha outstanding names the outside-reserve portion, while alpha reserve holds pool-side alpha and
TAO reserve holds the TAO side of the automated market maker
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, that three-pool frame is why alpha outstanding is a location label rather than a
separate token. The same subnet-specific alpha asset can sit in reserve or outside it; outstanding
vocabulary answers which side is being discussed without renaming the underlying subnet alpha
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

Understanding subnets describes three subnet pools: TAO reserve, alpha reserve, and alpha
outstanding. Alpha reserve names alpha inside the liquidity pool, while alpha outstanding names
alpha outside that reserve side
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The relevant distinction is which side of subnet alpha is being discussed, not whether alpha exists
at all.

## Distinction from Protocol Alpha

Protocol alpha is subnet alpha accumulated by the Bittensor protocol during reserve injection
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

Alpha outstanding is the broader outside-reserve location label. Protocol alpha names one
protocol-attributed category within that broader subnet accounting picture.

## Distinction from ADR

ADR compares alpha emitted outside the pool with alpha injected into the pool
([Glossary: ADR](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#adr-alpha-distribution-ratio)).

Alpha outstanding names where outside-pool alpha sits. ADR names the ratio between outside-pool
emissions and pool injections rather than a single balance field.

In that comparison, alpha outstanding belongs to the outside-pool side while alpha reserve belongs
to the pool-injection side, which is why naming alpha outstanding does not by itself state the ratio
([Glossary: ADR](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#adr-alpha-distribution-ratio),
[Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection)).

## Distinction from Emission

Emission names the subnet reward and injection flow. Alpha outstanding injection is one documented
emission path that adds alpha outside the reserve pool
([Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Emission covers the flow; outstanding names the outside-reserve location that flow can reach.

Emissions documentation describes alpha outstanding injection as part of subnet emission flow. That
makes outstanding balances part of emission accounting rather than a standalone tradable ticker
([Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, an outstanding amount should keep the subnet and emission context where it was
observed.

## Distinction from Alpha Halving

Alpha halving is a subnet milestone that reduces alpha emission rate after a supply threshold
([Halving Mechanisms: Alpha Halvings](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving#alpha-halvings)).

Alpha outstanding is a location label for subnet alpha outside the reserve pool. A halving changes
how quickly new alpha is emitted; outstanding names where existing subnet alpha sits in accounting.

## Distinction from Stake

Stake names tokens delegated to a validator in a subnet
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

Alpha outstanding can appear near total-stake discussion because subnet documentation connects
outside-reserve alpha with stake context, but outstanding remains a location label rather than a
staking procedure.

The subnet overview connects alpha outstanding with total stake in a subnet
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)). This use
stays narrow: alpha outstanding identifies the outside-reserve side of subnet alpha, not a staking
walkthrough, staking strategy, or return estimate.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Alpha outstanding names alpha held outside the subnet pool reserve on a subnet. It is an accounting
location label for subnet tokenomics, not the on-chain step that turns validator weights into
emission shares
([Glossary: Alpha Outstanding](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-outstanding),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that converts submitted validator weights into emission
shares after evaluation completes. It moves rewards through the subnet economy; it does not by
itself define where outstanding alpha sits in accounting
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers should keep the location label separate from the settlement step. Alpha outstanding answers
where subnet alpha sits outside the reserve pool; Yuma Consensus answers how validator weights
become emission shares in that subnet
([Glossary: Alpha Outstanding](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-outstanding),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Alpha outstanding** — alpha held outside the subnet pool reserve.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

The subnet overview describes alpha-token price as the ratio of TAO in reserve to alpha in reserve
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)). Alpha
outstanding is outside that reserve-side ratio, so it is not treated as alpha reserve when
explaining price.

The term still belongs in supply context because it is part of a subnet's alpha economy. A supply or
stake statement can mention alpha outstanding, but the surrounding text needs to distinguish total
alpha context from pool-held alpha reserve.

Alpha price is another nearby but separate term. Alpha price is based on the TAO reserve and alpha
reserve relationship, while alpha outstanding sits outside the reserve-side ratio.

## Reader Boundary

Alpha outstanding is a tokenomics term, not a separate token, operating guide, or return estimate.
It belongs where the focus is subnet alpha outside the reserve pool
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

When the focus is pool-held alpha, alpha reserve is more precise. When the focus is the subnet asset
broadly, protocol alpha is more precise. When the focus is the wider tokenomics system, Dynamic TAO
is more precise.

## Distinction from TAO Reserve

Alpha outstanding and the TAO reserve track different sides of a subnet's economy. Alpha outstanding
is the amount of a subnet's alpha circulating outside the protocol's reserves, while the TAO reserve
is the TAO-side balance of the subnet's liquidity pool that, with the alpha reserve, sets the alpha
price
([Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Alpha outstanding** — the subnet alpha circulating outside protocol reserves.
- **TAO reserve** — the TAO-side balance of the subnet's liquidity pool.
