# Reserve Ratio

A reserve ratio is the relationship between the TAO reserve and alpha reserve inside a Bittensor
subnet pool. The official
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
explains that each subnet functions as an automated market maker with a TAO reserve and a
subnet-specific alpha reserve, and that the reserve ratio determines the subnet alpha token's price
context.

## Pool Context

The reserve ratio belongs to the pool side of subnet tokenomics. A subnet's TAO reserve records the
TAO side of the pool, while its alpha reserve records the pool-held subnet alpha side. The ratio
between those two reserves is the pool relationship readers need before interpreting staking,
unstaking, or alpha-token conversion claims.

This makes reserve ratio narrower than a general article about subnet liquidity pools. A liquidity
pool describes the whole reserve context; reserve ratio describes the relationship between the two
pool sides.

## Price Context

The official subnet overview describes subnet alpha price through the relationship between TAO in
reserve and alpha in reserve. In reader terms, the reserve ratio is the pool relationship that makes
alpha-denominated staking and unstaking different from a simple one-token balance view.

This does not make the reserve ratio a live market quote. Exact prices and reserve balances are
current state values, while the concept explains why TAO and alpha reserves have to be read
together.

## Slippage Context

[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage) explains why staking and
unstaking can return a different amount than a static expectation. Those actions interact with
subnet pool reserves, so the reserve ratio is part of the context behind received TAO or alpha
amounts.

For readers, the important boundary is simple: slippage describes the outcome difference, while
reserve ratio describes the pool relationship that helps produce conversion context.

## Emission Context

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection) documentation
connects subnet emission injection to reserve growth. TAO can be injected into a subnet's TAO
reserve, and alpha reserve injection is described as proportional to TAO injection so the pool can
grow without changing alpha price from the injection itself.

That emission context uses the same reserve relationship, but reserve ratio is not the same thing as
emission distribution. It describes the pool relationship, not the final allocation to miners,
validators, stakers, or subnet owners.

## Reader Boundary

Reserve ratio is not a subnet ranking, a staking recommendation, or a guaranteed conversion rate. It
is a tokenomics term for reading the relationship between TAO and alpha reserves inside a subnet
pool. Live values should be checked in current official tools or chain data.
