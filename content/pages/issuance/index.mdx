# Issuance

Issuance is the circulating amount of TAO in Bittensor, including directly held TAO and TAO held in
subnet liquidity pools
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

The term belongs to supply-accounting vocabulary. It should be read separately from reward events,
subnet pool mechanics, and supply-flow mechanisms such as recycling or burning.

## What Issuance Counts

The glossary defines issuance as circulating TAO and explicitly includes TAO held in subnet
liquidity pools ([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

That makes issuance broader than a single holding location. It names the counted circulating supply
concept rather than the movement path that placed TAO in a particular part of the system.

## Pool-Held TAO

Pool-held TAO remains part of issuance because the glossary includes it in circulating TAO
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

This keeps subnet liquidity language separate from supply language. A subnet pool can hold TAO for
subnet mechanics while issuance names the broader circulating amount.

## Emission Boundary

Emission and issuance are related but distinct. Emission describes distribution over time, while
issuance describes the circulating TAO amount used in supply discussions
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

A reward distribution can affect supply context, but issuance is not the reward event itself.

## Halving Boundary

Halving uses issuance as supply context. Halving documentation connects emission-rate changes to
supply milestones, while issuance names the amount being counted for those milestones
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

The distinction keeps the terms precise: issuance is the counted supply concept, and halving is the
rule that changes emission rates at supply thresholds.

## Recycling and Burning Boundary

Recycling and burning describe different supply-flow outcomes around removed tokens
([Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)).

Issuance is the surrounding supply count. It should not be used as a substitute for whether a
removed amount is re-emittable or unavailable for future emission.

## Network Context

Bittensor documentation separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet contexts
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

An issuance example should keep the selected network attached when the claim depends on observed
supply accounting. A supply figure from one environment is not evidence for another environment.

## Reader Boundary

Use issuance when the topic is circulating TAO supply. Use emission for distribution over time,
halving for emission-rate changes at supply thresholds, and recycling or burning for removed-token
accounting ([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

This article defines the term; it does not report supply figures or interpret market conditions.

## Issuance and Emission Boundary

Issuance and emission are related tokenomics terms, but they should not be collapsed. The glossary
places issuance in supply accounting, while emissions documentation describes distribution flows to
participants and subnet contexts
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, issuance answers how supply is accounted for, while emission answers how protocol
rewards are distributed. A precise claim should identify which side of that boundary it is using.
