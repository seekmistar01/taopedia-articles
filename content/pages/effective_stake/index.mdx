# Effective Stake

Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake) defines it as the
total staked TAO amount of a delegate, including the delegate's own TAO and TAO delegated by
nominators.

## Delegate Context

A delegate is a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators. Effective stake is read
in that same staking context: it describes the stake associated with the delegate, not a separate
asset type or a standalone participant role.

## Own and Delegated TAO

The term combines two sources of staked TAO for the same delegate: the delegate's own staked TAO and
TAO supplied by nominators. This makes effective stake a combined staking measure rather than only a
description of self-stake.

## Relationship to Stake Weight

Effective stake is an amount of staked TAO. It is distinct from stake weight, which the
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) defines as the computed
total stake value for a validator that determines their consensus power and emissions in a subnet.

## Delegate Stake Context

The [Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)
defines delegate stake as the amount of TAO staked by the delegate themselves. Effective stake names
the combined total that includes that self-staked amount plus TAO delegated by nominators, so
delegate stake is the self-staked component within effective stake.

References:
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Effective stake describes staked TAO associated with a delegate inside a subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, effective stake still names the combined staking total for a delegate in one subnet.
That subnet may run more than one incentive mechanism, but effective stake remains part of the same
subnet context.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the term at a high level. It does not list live delegate balances, compare
delegates, recommend staking choices, or describe staking steps. Readers should use official staking
references for staking-specific actions.
