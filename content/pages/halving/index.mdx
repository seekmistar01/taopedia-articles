# Halving

Halving describes a reduction in token emission rate. In Bittensor tokenomics context, the term
helps readers understand how emission schedules can reduce the amount of newly emitted token supply
over time.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Emission Context

The term is about issuance rate, not market price. A halving changes the rate at which new emissions
are produced under a tokenomics schedule. It does not by itself describe demand, valuation, or a
staking recommendation.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to Issuance

Halving and issuance address related but different parts of Bittensor supply accounting. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes a
reduction in token emission rate, while the
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) describes TAO
circulating in the network.

For readers, halving names an emission-rate change tied to issuance milestones, while issuance names
the circulating supply record those milestones refer to.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Bittensor Emissions

Halving and Bittensor emissions address related but different parts of tokenomics. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes a
reduction in token emission rate, while [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)
describes how newly created TAO and subnet alpha tokens enter subnet and participant contexts.

For readers, halving names a supply-rate change, while Bittensor emissions name the broader
distribution process that halving rules can affect.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to Halving Mechanisms

Halving and halving mechanisms address related but different scopes in Bittensor tokenomics. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes a
reduction in token emission rate, while
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) explains TAO and alpha
halving rules in more detail.

For readers, halving is the high-level term, while halving mechanisms names the official rules for
when and how emission rates change.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Halving describes a reduction in token emission rate. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, halving still names a supply-rate change in tokenomics schedules. A subnet may later
run more than one incentive mechanism, but halving belongs to emission-rate rules rather than to a
single mechanism label.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Relationship to Burning

Halving and burning are related but different supply-reduction mechanisms. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes halving as
cutting the emission rate when a supply threshold is reached, while the
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning) describes burning as
removing tokens from circulation so they can no longer be transacted.

For readers, halving names the reduction in how fast new tokens are created, while burning names the
destruction of tokens that already exist. One slows future supply; the other removes current supply.
Both reduce circulation, but they operate at different points in the token lifecycle.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning)

## Relationship to Coinbase

Halving and coinbase address related but different parts of Bittensor emission scheduling. Halving
is a reduction in token emission rate triggered when a supply milestone is reached, while coinbase
is the per-block mechanism that accumulates pending emissions, injects liquidity into subnet pools,
and triggers Yuma Consensus epochs each block. The
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the process that runs every block to handle those emission
steps, while the [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)
describes halving as reducing the token emission rate at supply thresholds. After a halving event,
the block emission passed into coinbase is lower; coinbase continues running each block but at the
reduced rate. Halving names the emission-schedule rule; coinbase names the per-block execution that
operates under that rule—both belong to the emission picture, but halving changes the rate while
coinbase moves emissions at whatever rate is in effect.

References:
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the term at a high level. It does not make price predictions, give investment
advice, or report a live emission schedule. Readers should use current official tokenomics
documentation when they need exact network values.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For halving, that
sequence changes how readers should interpret evidence about emission-rate reductions at supply
thresholds.

In localnet, halving behavior can be tested in an isolated environment. Results reflect local supply
configuration and do not carry over to the production network schedule.

On testnet, halving can be observed in a shared, non-production network. This separates the result
from a private local environment while keeping it outside mainnet supply schedules.

On mainnet, halving reflects live emission-rate reductions tied to actual TAO and alpha supply
milestones. The [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)
describes halvings as supply-threshold events that reduce emission rates on the production network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A halving event observed in one environment should not be
read as identical evidence about emission schedules in another environment.

For readers, this keeps halving examples from being treated as carrying more weight than their
environment supports. Localnet, testnet, and mainnet contexts can all describe emission-rate
reductions, but they carry different interpretive weight.
