# Stake

Stake is the amount of currency tokens associated with a validator position in a Bittensor subnet
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).
It is amount vocabulary, not the action vocabulary of staking and not the downstream weighting
vocabulary of stake weight.

The term should stay attached to its validator and subnet context. A stake amount by itself is not a
wallet balance, validator recommendation, or network-wide performance signal.

## Validator Position Amount

Stake is read in relation to validator support. A TAO holder can stake to a validator, and a
validator's total stake can include both the validator's own stake and stake delegated by others
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

That combined view matters when reading Bittensor consensus and emission material. Stake names the
support amount associated with the validator position; it does not say who supplied every portion
unless the surrounding context identifies self-stake or delegated support.

## Subnet Context

Stake is local to subnet state. Staking into subnets places stake in that subnet's alpha-token
context, and subnet references use validator stake weight when explaining validator influence inside
a subnet
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets),
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-tokens),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

This locality keeps stake from becoming one universal account number. A precise statement about
stake should identify the subnet, validator, and asset context being discussed.

## Stake Weight and Delegation

Stake and stake weight are related but not identical. Stake names the validator-associated amount,
while stake weight is the interpreted measure used for validator consensus power and emissions
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

Delegation is the process that can attach holder support to a validator. Stake is the resulting
amount that can include both self-supplied and delegated support
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

## Environment Scope

Bittensor separates localnet, testnet, and mainnet environments. Stake examples from one environment
should not be treated as evidence for another because validator sets, balances, emissions, and
subnet state are environment-specific
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks),
[Introduction to Bittensor: Subnet development](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)).

Localnet stake examples are isolated development examples. Testnet examples are shared
non-production examples. Mainnet stake concerns production validator relationships and production
emissions.
