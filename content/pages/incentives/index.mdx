# Incentives

Incentives are the miner-facing reward portion of Bittensor emissions inside a subnet
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The term belongs to subnet emission vocabulary. It names the miner-side result of useful work and
validator evaluation, not every emission flow in Bittensor.

## Evaluation Shapes Incentive Outcomes

The link between work and incentives depends on subnet evaluation. Incentive-mechanism documentation
describes how validators evaluate miner work and how miner scores can affect emission allocation
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

The direction is important: miner work is evaluated first, evaluation affects allocation, and the
miner-facing allocation is the incentive outcome.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For incentives on
a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret reward examples,
scoring observations, and subnet-performance comparisons.

In localnet, incentive examples can be exercised in an isolated environment. Localnet observations
on netuid 1 reflect local chain state and local configuration rather than production Bittensor
behavior.

On testnet, incentive behavior can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet
incentive outcomes on a selected netuid are useful for checking interactions under shared chain
conditions while staying separate from mainnet state
([Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

On mainnet, incentives describe live production incentive behavior on the production Bittensor
network. Observed miner-facing amounts on netuid 1 depend on that subnet's consensus and evaluation
context.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. An example or outcome from one environment should not be
read as evidence of production behavior in another environment.

## Distinction from Consensus Score

Consensus score is the stake-weighted clipping benchmark Yuma uses while processing validator weight
input. Incentives are miner-facing emission outcomes credited after that input is clipped,
aggregated into rank, and normalized
([Glossary: Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Consensus score** — clipping benchmark inside Yuma.
- **Incentives** — miner-facing credited results after rank is normalized.

An incentive should not be read as the raw score a validator gives a miner. Validator scoring
belongs to the subnet's incentive mechanism, while incentives name the miner-facing emission outcome
after those evaluations are resolved
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

That boundary keeps evaluation vocabulary separate from payout vocabulary. A score can help shape an
allocation, but the incentive is the reward-side result.

## Distinction from Rank

- **Rank** — consensus standing after clipped weights are merged.
- **Incentives** — miner-facing emission credited in proportion to that standing.

Rank orders miners inside Yuma on the selected subnet; incentives name the miner reward share that
follows from normalized standing
([Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

## Distinction from Trust

Trust compares pre-rank support to final rank after clipping. Incentives are the credited miner-side
outcome after rank is normalized in the same Yuma pass
([Glossary: Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Trust** — pre-rank to final-rank support preservation after clipping.
- **Incentives** — miner-facing credited results after rank is normalized.

## Distinction from Emission

- **Emission** — creation and allocation of TAO and alpha across injection and distribution.
- **Incentives** — miner-facing reward outcomes inside the distribution stage.

Emission spans the full protocol flow; incentives stay on the miner branch after consensus resolves
weight signals ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

Incentives sit inside the broader emissions process. The emission documentation describes emissions
as distribution to roles that contribute value through mining, validation, staking, and subnet
creation ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

That broader process includes more than miner incentives. Incentives are the miner-facing part of
the emission picture.

## Distinction from Validator Dividends

Yuma resolves weight signals into separate miner and validator outcomes on the selected subnet.
Incentives belong to the miner-emission branch; validator dividends belong to the validator-side
branch documented under validator emissions
([Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Incentives** — miner-facing credited results.
- **Validator dividends** — validator-facing credited results from the parallel branch.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Documented emission distribution uses separate miner and validator branches. Incentives belong to
the miner-emission branch, while validator take applies only inside the validator-and-staker branch
when delegated-stake emissions are split
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

Validator take is not a later step in the miner reward path; it is a split inside the validator
portion after that branch is credited.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Incentives name the miner-facing emission share credited after Yuma resolves submitted validator
weights on a subnet. They label a payout outcome on the selected netuid, not the tempo step that
produced it ([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain tempo process that reads the weight matrix, applies clipping against
the consensus score benchmark, runs bonding, and then assigns miner incentives together with
validator dividends ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Weight Matrix](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The sections above already separate incentives from rank, trust, validator dividends, and the wider
emission flow. Incentive vocabulary names the miner branch of that settlement pass; Yuma vocabulary
names the full tempo step
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)).

Readers should not read incentives as one validator's raw score or as a fixed payout quote. Credited
amounts depend on clipped weights, subnet emission context, and the Yuma pass on the relevant netuid
([Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Incentives** — miner-facing emission share after Yuma settlement.
- **Yuma Consensus** — tempo step that turns submitted weights into emission shares.

Incentives are one miner-facing outcome of Yuma Consensus inside a subnet. Yuma Consensus aggregates
validator weight signals before subnet emissions are allocated
([Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

For readers, incentives should be read inside the consensus resolution path. Validators evaluate
miner work, consensus resolves those signals, and the miner-facing share is called incentives.

## Distinction from Miner Reward

Incentives belong to the mining side of a subnet. A subnet asks miners to produce useful work, and
validators evaluate that work under the subnet's rules
([Mining](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

This makes incentives a reward-allocation concept. It is narrower than the general idea of why
someone might participate in a network.

## Distinction from Multiple-Mechanism

When a subnet uses multiple incentive mechanisms, incentives should be read inside the mechanism
context being discussed. Multiple-mechanism documentation describes separate evaluation contexts
inside one subnet
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

An incentive earned in one mechanism should not be treated as a complete statement about miner work
in every mechanism.

## Reader Boundary

Incentives should be read as a concept article term. It is not a fixed rate, miner ranking, or
complete description of all Bittensor emissions
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The stable article-level point is that incentives name the miner-facing reward portion of subnet
emissions.
