# Incentives

Incentives are the miner-facing reward portion of Bittensor emissions inside a subnet. The official
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) defines incentives as a
portion of TAO emission received by subnet miners when they provide valuable services and compete
for UID slots.

## Miner Reward Context

Incentives belong to the mining side of a subnet. A subnet asks miners to produce useful work, and
validators evaluate that work under the subnet's rules. The official
[Mining](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners) documentation explains that validator scores
determine the proportion of subnet emissions allocated to each miner.

This makes incentives a reward-allocation concept. It is not just a general reason to participate in
the network; it names the emission share miners try to earn by producing work that validators rank
highly.

## Emission Context

Incentives sit inside the broader emissions process. The
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation describes emissions as the
distribution of newly created TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens to network participants who
contribute value through mining, validation, staking, and subnet creation.

That broader process includes more than miner incentives. Incentives are the miner-facing part of
the reward picture, while validator rewards, staking outcomes, and subnet-creator rewards are
separate emission contexts.

## Relationship to Evaluation

The link between work and incentives depends on subnet evaluation. The
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)
documentation describes incentive mechanisms as defining how validators evaluate miners' work and
how miner scores can result in higher emissions.

For Taopedia readers, the important point is the direction of the relationship: miner work is
evaluated, evaluation affects emission allocation, and the miner-facing allocation is what this
article calls incentives.

## Yuma Consensus Context

Incentives are one miner-facing outcome of Yuma Consensus inside a subnet. The official
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) documentation describes how
validator weight signals are aggregated and filtered before subnet emissions are allocated.

For Taopedia readers, this keeps incentives inside the consensus resolution path. Validators
evaluate miner work, consensus turns those signals into emission outcomes, and the miner-facing
share of that result is what this article calls incentives—not a separate reward layer outside
consensus.

Reference:
[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)

## Relationship to Dividends

Incentives and dividends name opposite sides of a subnet's consensus-derived emissions. Incentives
are the miner-facing portion, while dividends are the validator-facing result. The
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) page resolves validator
weights into both a miner-emissions vector and a validator-emissions vector, so the two terms
describe different participants in the same consensus outcome.

For readers, incentives identify where miner-side value landed, and dividends identify how
validator-side emissions follow from a validator's bonded evaluation of those miners. Because
validator emissions build on miner emission results, the two sides are connected, but they remain
distinct outputs measured for different participants.

References: [Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Incentives are usually discussed for one incentive mechanism at a time, but subnets can also run
multiple mechanisms in parallel. The official documentation notes that miners can receive incentives
separately from each mechanism, with total miner emissions for an epoch summed across the mechanisms
where they participate.

For readers, incentives still name the miner-facing reward portion inside a subnet, but the
mechanism context matters. Incentive earned in one mechanism does not describe miner performance in
another when a subnet runs parallel evaluation paths.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)

## Reader Boundary

Incentives should not be read as a fixed rate, a live miner ranking, or a complete description of
all emissions in Bittensor. Exact current amounts, formulas, and subnet-specific rules depend on the
official sources and current subnet context.
