# Mempool Visibility

Mempool visibility is the exposure of pending transaction details before block inclusion. In
Bittensor, that visibility matters because sensitive pending details can create MEV risk before the
chain processes the transaction. The
[glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) defines the mempool as the
temporary place where pending transactions sit, and
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) explains why hidden details can
matter before inclusion.

## What the mempool shows

A mempool is a waiting area for pending and unconfirmed transactions. The important Taopedia concept
is visibility: before block inclusion, some transaction details may be visible to other network
observers. That visibility can matter even when the transaction itself is valid. The
[glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) defines the pending
transaction context.

## Relationship to Mempool

Mempool visibility and the mempool are related but name different questions about the same
pre-inclusion window. The
[Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) defines the mempool
as the temporary area where pending transactions sit before block inclusion.

For readers, the mempool is where a transaction waits, and mempool visibility is whether sensitive
details of that pending transaction are exposed to observers. MEV risk can appear when visibility is
high during the wait, not merely because a transaction is still pending.

References: [Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool)

## Relationship to MEV Shield

Mempool visibility and MEV Shield address opposite sides of the same pre-inclusion risk. Mempool
visibility names whether pending transaction details are exposed before block inclusion, while
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) encrypts sensitive transaction
contents during that wait.

For readers, visibility is the exposure question, while MEV Shield is one official response to that
exposure. Using MEV Shield does not change what the mempool is; it changes what observers can see
before inclusion.

References: [MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/),
[Glossary: MEV](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)

## Why visibility can be risky

MEV risk comes from information available before a transaction is included. If sensitive pending
details are visible, another actor may try to use that timing advantage around the transaction. The
[glossary MEV entry](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)
connects MEV risk to pending visibility, and
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) frames encrypted mempool
protection as a Bittensor response to that risk.

## MEV Shield boundary

MEV Shield addresses mempool visibility by encrypting sensitive transaction contents while they are
pending and revealing them only when the chain can process them. That makes MEV Shield a visibility
protection, not a general guarantee of the final outcome. The
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) source defines this encrypted
mempool-protection model.

## Boundary with price protection

Mempool visibility and price protection are related but separate. Mempool visibility is about what
pending details can be seen before inclusion. Price protection is about how a staking-related action
handles adverse movement while it is processed. The
[Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection) source points to MEV
Shield as an additional protection, while defining price-protection behavior as a separate
guardrail.

## Interpretation Boundary

Mempool visibility does not mean every pending transaction is unsafe, and hiding details does not
remove every other risk. It describes the information-exposure window before block inclusion, when
pending transaction details may be visible soon enough to create MEV risk. The
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) documentation frames encrypted
mempool protection around that pending window.

That boundary should not be stretched into a post-inclusion privacy claim. Once a transaction is
included and the chain records relevant activity, ordinary chain evidence can still be inspected in
the public network context. Mempool visibility is about what observers can learn before inclusion,
not about erasing chain records after processing.

The same narrow reading keeps nearby safeguards separate. MEV Shield addresses pending-detail
exposure. Price protection addresses adverse price movement for staking and unstaking. Transaction
fees belong to submitted chain activity. A source-backed claim should therefore say whether it is
about pending visibility, price movement, or fee behavior rather than blending those concepts.

## Relationship to Extrinsics

Mempool visibility and extrinsics are related but different parts of Bittensor chain action
vocabulary. Extrinsics name signed transactions and calls submitted to Subtensor, while mempool
visibility names whether sensitive details of those pending submissions are exposed before block
inclusion. The [Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics)
describes signed transactions submitted to the chain, and the
[Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) describes the
pending-transaction context where visibility risk can appear.

For readers, extrinsics name the submitted chain actions, while mempool visibility names the
exposure question for those actions while they wait in the mempool.

References: [Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics),
[Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool)

## Relationship to Subtensor

Mempool visibility and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary.
Subtensor names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while mempool visibility names
whether pending transaction details are exposed on that chain layer before block inclusion. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) describes the
pending-transaction context where visibility risk can appear.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while mempool visibility names an exposure question
within the pre-inclusion window on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),

## Relationship to Block

Mempool visibility and a block are related but different parts of Bittensor chain timing vocabulary.
A block names one unit of chain progression on Subtensor, while mempool visibility names exposure of
pending transaction details before those submissions are included in a block. The
[Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block) describes a block as a
unit of chain data, and the
[Glossary: Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) describes the
pending-transaction context before inclusion.

For readers, a block marks chain progression and inclusion, while mempool visibility names what
observers may see before that inclusion step.

References: [Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block),

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For mempool
visibility, that sequence changes how readers should interpret evidence about pending transaction
exposure and MEV risk.

In localnet, mempool visibility can be tested in an isolated environment. The mempool exists
conceptually, but no real external observers are present to extract value from pending details.
Localnet examples show the visibility mechanics without representing the live MEV risk of a public
network.

On testnet, mempool visibility operates in a shared, non-production environment. MEV Shield behavior
and pending transaction handling can be observed on testnet, but the observer pool and risk profile
differ from a production context.

On mainnet, mempool visibility is a live concern. Real network actors may observe pending
transaction details before block inclusion, and MEV Shield operates on the production network to
address that exposure. The [MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/)
documentation describes the encrypted mempool-protection model in that production context.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A mempool visibility example from one environment should
not be read as representing the MEV exposure or observer activity of another environment.

For readers, this keeps mempool visibility examples grounded in their network context. Localnet and
testnet examples can illustrate the pending-exposure concept without implying the same risk profile
that applies on mainnet.
