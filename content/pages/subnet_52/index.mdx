Dojo is Bittensor Subnet 52, a subnet built by Tensorplex Labs. Its current version, Dojo V2, frames
the network as a competitive, decentralized Generative Adversarial Network (GAN): miners act as both
generators, producing outputs, and discriminators, judging outputs, and they compete against one
another to produce and identify the best work. The incentive-mechanism code is maintained in the
[tensorplex-labs/dojo](https://github.com/tensorplex-labs/dojo) repository.

## What the Subnet Produces

The subnet's output is high-quality work selected through competition. According to the repository,
Dojo V2 differs from a conventional GAN in its goal: instead of asking the generator to merely
reproduce ground-truth data, it challenges miners to create outputs that are not only
indistinguishable from a strong baseline but better than it. The design uses zero-sum incentives, so
producing or correctly identifying superior work is what earns rewards.

A practical feature of Dojo is its low barrier to participate as a miner: the repository notes that
miners do not run a server or custom code. A miner registers on the subnet, loads its hotkey into a
browser wallet, and then receives tasks to work on, which makes participation closer to a guided
workflow than to operating infrastructure.

## Miner and Validator Roles

Miners take on both sides of the adversarial setup. As generators they submit outputs in response to
the tasks they receive; as discriminators they assess outputs, and the zero-sum structure means a
miner gains when its work outperforms peers and loses ground when it falls behind. The aim is a
standing competition that keeps pushing the quality of submitted work upward.

Validators coordinate the competition and score participation, with setup and mechanism details kept
in the repository's documentation. The resulting validator scores become the weights that feed into
[[Yuma Consensus]], which converts them into the incentive split across miners and validators.

## On-Chain Identity

Live SN52 data, including metagraph state and alpha token pool information, is available on
[TaoStats](https://taostats.io/subnets/52). The live Finney identity for netuid 52 registers the
subnet name as Dojo, and points to the
[tensorplex-labs/dojo](https://github.com/tensorplex-labs/dojo) repository, which is the source of
truth for the competitive GAN mechanism described above.
