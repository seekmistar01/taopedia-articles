# Nominator

A nominator is a TAO holder who stakes with a validator. The official
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO on a validator hotkey so token holders can participate in
subnet consensus and receive staking-related emissions while retaining control of their tokens.

## Role in Delegation

In Bittensor staking vocabulary, the nominator is the supplying side of a delegation relationship.
The [Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) names the
validator that receives staked TAO and performs validation work. A nominator attaches TAO support to
that validator side; the delegate is the role that receives and validates with that support.

This keeps three terms distinct for readers: **nominator** is the account role, **nominate** names
the staking action, and **delegate** names the validator receiving delegated stake.

## Nominate and Stake Context

Nomination is the action vocabulary for how a nominator participates. The
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) describes
nomination as staking TAO on a validator hotkey. The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
uses delegation to describe the same support relationship in broader staking prose.

For readers, nominator should be read as the role label, not as a procedure. It does not name a
subnet miner, a validator permit, or a wallet file location.

## Emissions and Dividends Context

Delegated stake contributes to validator stake context that subnets use when interpreting consensus
and emissions. The official Nominator glossary places nominators in that participation picture: they
supply stake to validators so they can share in staking-related outcomes described in current
emission references.

This article does not calculate rewards, rank validators, or describe how to carry out nomination
steps. Those details belong in official staking documentation and live chain data.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

A nominator supplies stake to a validator inside a subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, nominator still names the TAO-holder role in delegation vocabulary. That support
applies to a validator position in one subnet, which may run more than one incentive mechanism.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Reader Boundary

Nominator should not be read as a recommendation to stake with any particular validator, as a
balance report, or as a synonym for delegate. It is a role term for a TAO holder who supplies
delegated stake.

## Relationship to Subtensor

Nominator and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while nominator names the TAO-holder role
that supplies delegated stake recorded through chain-layer staking state. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO on a validator hotkey.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while nominator names the supplying side of delegated
stake recorded on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)

## Relationship to Netuid

Nominator and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A netuid identifies
a subnet on the network, while nominator names the TAO-holder role that supplies stake to a
validator participating in that subnet context. The
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level, and the
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO on a validator hotkey.

For readers, a netuid selects which subnet context is in scope, while nominator names the
TAO-supplying role that supports a validator in that context.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)

## Relationship to Nominate

Nominator and nominate are related but different scopes in Bittensor staking vocabulary. Nominate
names the staking action of attaching TAO to a validator hotkey, while nominator names the account
role that performs that support relationship. The
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) describes
nomination as staking TAO on a validator hotkey, and the
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO on a validator hotkey.

For readers, nominate names the action, while nominator names the TAO-holder role carrying out that
delegation support.

References: [Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)

## Relationship to Delegate

Nominator and delegate are related but different sides of Bittensor delegation vocabulary. A
delegate is the subnet validator that receives staked TAO and performs validation work, while
nominator names the TAO-holder role that supplies that delegated stake. The
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) defines a delegate
as a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators, and the
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO on a validator hotkey.

For readers, delegate names the receiving validator role, while nominator names the supplying
TAO-holder role on the other side of that relationship.

References: [Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)

## Relationship to Autostaking

Nominator and autostaking address related but different parts of Bittensor delegated staking
vocabulary. A nominator is a TAO holder who stakes with a validator — supplying delegated stake to a
validator hotkey — while autostaking is a mechanism that automatically routes a miner's emission
proceeds into a staking position on a chosen validator. The
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO on a validator hotkey, and the
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) documentation describes
autostaking as automatically staking a miner's emissions to a chosen validator.

When a miner enables autostaking, their emissions flow automatically into a staking position on the
chosen validator — that accumulated stake appears as delegated stake on the validator hotkey, in the
same relationship a nominator holds. The difference is how the stake originates: a nominator
manually supplies TAO from their own wallet to support a validator, while autostaking routes a
miner's emission proceeds to that same kind of staking position without a manual staking step.
Nominator names any TAO-holder role that supplies delegated stake; autostaking is the specific
mechanism by which a miner can fill that nominator role automatically as emissions arrive.

References: [Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)

## Relationship to Coinbase

A nominator and coinbase are related but different parts of how delegated stake shares in emissions.
A nominator is the TAO holder who stakes on a validator hotkey to participate in consensus and
receive staking-related emissions. Coinbase is the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO
emission, injects TAO into emitting subnets' pool reserves, accumulates pending emissions, and
checks epoch boundaries to trigger Yuma Consensus rounds. The
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes the
staking account role, and the
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes the per-block emission operation.

A nominator's stake adds to a validator's effective stake, which raises that validator's influence
in the Yuma Consensus rounds coinbase triggers each epoch. Coinbase then distributes validator
dividends from those rounds, and the validator passes a share to its nominators in proportion to
delegated stake, after the validator take. A nominator influences coinbase-epoch outcomes only
indirectly, through the validator it backs; coinbase is the mechanism that produces the dividends
the nominator ultimately receives a portion of.
