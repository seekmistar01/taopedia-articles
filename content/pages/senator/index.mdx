# Senator

A senator is a delegate who participates in Bittensor Senate voting. The official
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation describes senators as
members of the Senate — delegates who have elected to participate in proposals and who control a
significant portion of total network stake.

## Role in the Senate

The Senate reviews proposals created by the Triumvirate. The
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) places the Senate after proposal
creation: the Triumvirate originates proposals, and the Senate approves or rejects them. A senator
is the participant role on the Senate side of that review flow.

This keeps three terms distinct for readers: **proposal** names the governance item, **Senate**
names the review body, and **senator** names the delegate who casts Senate votes.

## Delegate and Stake Context

Senators are delegates in Bittensor staking vocabulary. The
[Glossary: Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#senate) describes the Senate
as a group of elected delegates formed from top delegate hotkeys. The
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation adds participation
requirements: a coldkey must register a hotkey-coldkey pair, hold more than 2% of network stake
through delegation or self-stake, and elect to participate in the Senate before voting on
Triumvirate proposals.

For readers, senator should be read as a governance role tied to delegate stake context, not as a
subnet miner, a validator permit label, or a wallet file location.

## Voting Context

Senate voting is how senators act on proposals. The
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation describes approval or
disapproval votes submitted with `btcli sudo senate-vote`, with each vote included in the next block
after it is cast. The same page notes that anyone can view proposals with `btcli sudo proposals`.

This article does not list active senators, proposal hashes, or vote outcomes. Those details belong
in official governance references and live chain data.

## Representation Context

Senate participation also has a delegation representation angle. The
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate) documentation explains that network
holders who delegate stake to Senate members are represented by the delegate they chose. Senator
therefore names a voting delegate role inside a broader representation picture, not an instruction
to join the Senate or to delegate with any particular validator.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Senator names a Senate delegate who votes on governance proposals. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that subnets can run more than one incentive mechanism, each with its own bond pool for Yuma
Consensus calculations.

For readers, senator still names a governance voting role at the network level. When a proposal
touches subnet incentive rules, multiple-mechanism context belongs with the subnet policy being
reviewed, not with the senator role itself.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Reader Boundary

Senator should not be read as a recommendation to delegate with any particular validator, as a live
membership list, or as a synonym for Triumvirate. It is a role term for a Senate delegate who votes
on governance proposals.
