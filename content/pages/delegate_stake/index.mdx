# Delegate Stake

Delegate stake is the TAO a Bittensor delegate has staked themselves. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake) defines it as the
amount of TAO staked by the delegate themselves.

## Staking Context

The glossary describes a [delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) as
a subnet validator that receives staked TAO from delegators. In that context, delegate stake names
the delegate's own staked TAO rather than TAO supplied by other delegators.

## Relationship to Effective Stake

The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake) defines a related
term, effective stake, as the total staked TAO of a delegate — their own TAO plus TAO delegated by
others. Delegate stake is the self-staked portion of that total.

## Nominator Delegation Context

The [Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO on a validator's hotkey. Delegate stake names the delegate's
own TAO in that relationship, distinct from TAO supplied by nominators who nominate to validators or
delegates.

References:
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Delegate stake names the TAO a delegate stakes to themselves inside a subnet market. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that subnets may run more than one incentive mechanism, each with separate bond pools for Yuma
Consensus, as described in
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets).

Those mechanism rules shape miner evaluation and validator bonds. Delegate stake does not define
them. For readers, delegate stake still names self-staked TAO on a delegate, while
multiple-mechanism context belongs with per-mechanism evaluation when a subnet runs more than one
incentive system.

## Relationship to Stake Weight

Delegate stake and stake weight are related but distinct parts of Bittensor staking vocabulary.
Delegate stake names the TAO a Bittensor delegate has staked themselves, while stake weight is the
computed total stake value that determines a validator's consensus power and emissions in a subnet.
The [Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)
defines delegate stake as the delegate's own staked TAO, and the
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) entry
defines stake weight as the alpha-plus-TAO stake blend that feeds validator influence, with TAO
stake weighted by the global TAO weight parameter.

For readers, delegate stake names one input to stake weight on the validator side. Stake weight
combines the delegate's own alpha stake and any TAO stake they hold through the global TAO weight
parameter, so delegate stake is one of the components the stake weight measure reflects. The two are
not interchangeable: delegate stake names the underlying TAO a delegate has committed to themselves,
while stake weight names the validator-influence measure computed from that and other stake sources.

References:
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)

## Relationship to Autostaking

Delegate stake and autostaking address related but different parts of Bittensor staking vocabulary.
Delegate stake is the TAO a Bittensor delegate has staked themselves — the delegate's own commitment
to their validator position — while autostaking is a mechanism that automatically routes a miner's
emission proceeds to a staking position on a chosen validator. The
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)
defines delegate stake as the amount of TAO staked by the delegate themselves, and the
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) documentation describes
autostaking as automatically staking a miner's emissions to a chosen validator.

When a miner enables autostaking and emissions are routed to a chosen validator, those emissions
become delegated (nominator) stake on that validator rather than delegate stake. Delegate stake
names the validator's own self-staked TAO; autostaked emissions become external delegated stake from
miners that accumulates on the same validator. Both types of staked TAO contribute to the
validator's effective stake and staking context, but they have different origins: delegate stake
comes from the delegate's own TAO, while autostaked emissions come from miners routing their
proceeds to a chosen validator position.

References:
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)

## Reader Boundary

This article defines the term at a high level. It does not report live stake balances, rank
delegates, or describe transaction steps. Readers should use official staking references for
staking-specific details.

## Relationship to Coinbase

Delegate stake and coinbase are related but different parts of Bittensor's staking and emission
picture. Delegate stake is the TAO a delegate has staked itself, one component of the delegate's
total stake. The
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes coinbase as the per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission,
accumulates pending emissions, and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch boundaries, while the
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)
describes the self-staked amount.

Delegate stake adds to a delegate's total stake, which contributes to its stake weight and consensus
influence; coinbase is the per-block mechanism that triggers the epoch rounds and advances the
emission-distribution process those stake values feed into. Delegate stake is a staked-amount
measure on the staking side — a standing quantity behind a delegate going into consensus — while
coinbase is the protocol process that runs the epoch cycle each block. One names how much TAO a
delegate has committed to itself; the other names the recurring mechanism that drives the emission
cycle that stake takes part in.
