# Hotkey-Coldkey Pair

A hotkey-coldkey pair is the authentication pairing that links a participant's operational hotkey to
the coldkey that owns stake and signing authority. The official
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)
describes it as the authentication mechanism for delegates and nominators and for delegates
participating in the Senate.

## What It Represents

Bittensor separates long-term ownership from day-to-day network operations. A hotkey-coldkey pair
names how those two key roles are bound together for staking participation rather than either key
label on its own ([Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

The pair names an authorization relationship, not a requirement that both keys live on the same
machine. Coldkey material can stay off live miner or validator hosts while the hotkey performs
operational work
([Coldkey and Hotkey Workstation Security](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-hotkey-security)).

Subnet registration assigns a UID slot to a hotkey, not to a coldkey fund account. The pair still
applies because the registered hotkey remains owned by a coldkey
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

Senate requirements include registering as a hotkey-coldkey pair on a subnetwork before a coldkey
can participate in Senate voting ([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

Key-pair examples depend on which network you connect to. Authentication outcomes from one
environment should not be read as production behavior on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Coldkey

A coldkey names ownership authority over TAO and stake. A hotkey-coldkey pair names how an
operational hotkey is authorized under that owning coldkey
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

- **Coldkey** — ownership authority over funds and stake.
- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — bound authentication relationship between coldkey and hotkey.

## Distinction from Hotkey

A hotkey names operational authority for subnet roles. A hotkey-coldkey pair names which coldkey
owns and authorizes that operational key
([Glossary: Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

- **Hotkey** — operational key for subnet work and signing.
- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — authentication link between operational hotkey and owning coldkey.

## Distinction from Neuron

A neuron is a registered subnet participant record tied to a UID slot. A hotkey-coldkey pair names
the key relationship that authenticates that participation
([Glossary: Neuron](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#neuron),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

- **Neuron** — registered subnet participant record with a UID slot.
- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — key relationship that authenticates participation.

## Distinction from Register

Register is the subnet entry process that assigns a UID slot to a hotkey. A hotkey-coldkey pair
names which coldkey owns that hotkey
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — ownership and authentication relationship between the two keys.
- **Register** — subnet entry that binds a hotkey to a UID slot.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation vocabulary describes how nominators supply stake to delegates. The hotkey-coldkey pair is
the authentication frame those roles use when hotkey activity must remain accountable to the coldkey
that holds stake
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — authentication link between operational and custody keys.
- **Delegation** — stake supplied by nominators to validator delegates.

## Distinction from Coldkey Swap

Coldkey swap migrates on-chain identity to a new coldkey. A hotkey-coldkey pair names the current
ownership relationship between coldkey and hotkey
([Coldkey Swap](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — current authentication relationship between the two keys.
- **Coldkey swap** — migration of on-chain identity to a new coldkey.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

A hotkey-coldkey pair names staking identity vocabulary. Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that
converts submitted validator weights into emission shares on a subnet
([Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — authentication link between operational and custody keys.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Hotkey-coldkey pair should not be read as a wallet creation guide, a command sequence, or a
recommendation to register with any particular validator. It is a concept term for how operational
and ownership keys are paired in Bittensor staking and Senate authentication vocabulary.
