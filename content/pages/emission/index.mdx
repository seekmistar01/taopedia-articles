# Emission

Emission is Bittensor's process for creating and allocating TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. It
covers value entering subnet economic settings and value reaching miners, validators, stakers, and
subnet creators through protocol allocation flow. It is the process around role-facing reward terms,
not a synonym for any single reward label
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## Injection and Distribution

Bittensor emission vocabulary separates injection from distribution. Injection concerns value
entering subnet economic settings, while distribution concerns how that value is allocated across
miners, validators, stakers, and subnet creators.

That distinction keeps emission from being read as one balance, one role outcome, or one subnet
ranking. A miner incentive, validator dividend, staking-related outcome, or creator allocation is a
narrower result inside the larger creation-and-allocation process
([Emission: Injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For emission on a
subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret injection amounts,
tempo-bound distribution, and role-facing reward outcomes.

In localnet, emission examples can test allocation mechanics in an isolated environment. Local
injection and distribution reflect local chain configuration rather than production subnet rewards.

On testnet, emission can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet emission readings
on a selected netuid are separate from mainnet subnet pool and reward state
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

On mainnet, emission is the live token-creation and allocation process that feeds production subnet
pools and tempo-bound distribution on the connected Bittensor network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. An emission example from one environment should not be
read as representing production allocation behavior on another network.

## Injection Runs Every Block

[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation separates injection from
distribution. Injection adds new liquidity to each subnet's pools every
[block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block), while distribution waits until
the end of each [tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo).

The [Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission) states the
same rhythm in plain terms: every block, TAO is injected into each subnet, and every tempo the
accumulated value is extracted by miners, validators, stakers, and subnet creators.

## Yuma Allocates Distribution at Tempo Boundaries

Distribution uses [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) to turn
validator weight signals into miner
[incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) and validator
[dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission). Those role-facing outcomes
are credited when tempo-bound distribution runs rather than on every intermediate weight submission
([Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

Emission therefore names the broader creation-and-allocation process; Yuma names the consensus step
that shapes how accumulated value reaches miners and validators inside that process.

## Dynamic TAO Flow

In Dynamic TAO, emission connects network-level TAO allocation with subnet-level alpha distribution.
TAO reserve injection and alpha reserve injection describe pool-side growth, while distribution
describes role-facing rewards.

That creates two emission layers. The first layer is network-level: TAO is allocated into subnet
reserve settings. The second layer is subnet-level: subnet alpha tokens move through incentive,
dividend, and staking-related reward vocabulary.

Protocol alpha is one nearby category. During reserve-injection activity, the protocol can
accumulate alpha cached by subnet rather than placing all alpha into ordinary distribution. Protocol
alpha is therefore an emission-side category that sits alongside the standard distribution flow.

## Incentives Name Role-Facing Outcomes

Emission describes how value enters and moves through protocol allocation flow.
[Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) describe what miners,
validators, stakers, and subnet creators compete to earn from that flow. One is the upstream release
and allocation mechanism; the other is the downstream reward language attached to subnet roles.

A discussion of emission rates or allocation paths does not by itself define every incentive rule
inside a subnet, because incentive design can shape how emitted value is earned without replacing
the broader emission process itself.

## Network Emission Runs at a Per-Block Rate

Emission has a concrete base rate. Official emission material puts the current network emission at
half a TAO per block, minted roughly every twelve seconds, down from one TAO per block before the
first halving. That newly created TAO is what injection adds to subnet pools each block
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

That per-block figure is the input the rest of emission accounting builds on. The value injected and
later distributed across subnets is drawn from this network rate rather than minted separately by
each subnet.

## Distinction from Coinbase

Emission and coinbase describe different layers of the same supply flow on a subnet such as
netuid 1. Emission names the broader creation-and-allocation process for TAO and subnet alpha, while
coinbase names the per-block mechanism that advances emission accounting, injects liquidity, and
accumulates pending emissions before distribution
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Coinbase documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

For readers, coinbase is block-cadence process vocabulary inside emission. Coinbase can also hand
off to [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) epochs when subnet
timing conditions are met, but emission still names the wider injection-and-distribution flow that
coinbase advances each block on netuid 1
([Coinbase epoch execution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase#7-epoch-execution)).

- **Emission** — creation and allocation of TAO and alpha across the protocol flow.
- **Coinbase** — per-block engine that advances that flow before tempo distribution.

## Distinction from Tempo

Emission and tempo sit at different timing layers for subnet rewards on a subnet such as netuid 1.
Emission names how value is created, injected, and later allocated across roles, while tempo names
the recurring subnet interval when accumulated alpha is distributed after injection
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission: Injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

For readers, injection can run every block while miner incentives and validator dividends are
credited when tempo-bound distribution runs. Emission vocabulary therefore spans both stages, while
tempo vocabulary marks the boundary when role-facing outcomes finalize on netuid 1
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Incentives

Emission names the full creation-and-allocation process. Incentives name miner-facing outcomes
inside the distribution stage on the selected subnet
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

- **Emission** — injection and distribution across the protocol flow.
- **Incentives** — miner-facing credited results inside distribution.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Emission names the protocol-wide flow of value through injection and distribution stages. It covers
how TAO and alpha enter subnets and later reach participants, not the within-subnet mechanism that
turns submitted validator weights into role shares
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that reads the weight matrix on a selected netuid, applies
clipping and bonding, and then credits miner incentives and validator dividends at the epoch
boundary ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Weight Matrix](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The incentives and dividends sections above already separate participant-facing outcomes from the
wider emission model. Emission vocabulary names the full value path; Yuma vocabulary names
within-subnet settlement from submitted weights
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)).

Readers should not read emission labels as proof of one miner's payout on a subnet. Distribution on
a netuid still depends on the weight matrix Yuma processes at the relevant epoch boundary
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)).

- **Emission** — creation, injection, and allocation across the network.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain mechanism that uses validator weights to determine emission shares
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Distinction from Emission Split

Emission names the full protocol process. Emission split names only the documented role proportions
applied when tempo distribution divides accumulated alpha
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

The split describes documented partition shares; emission describes the broader
release-and-allocation path.

## Distinction from Flow-Based Emissions

Flow-based emissions compare net TAO movement across subnets before reserve injection. Emission
names the full value-creation and distribution process that includes both injection and tempo
distribution
([Emission: Distribution Across Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution-across-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Flow vocabulary belongs to cross-subnet injection comparison; emission vocabulary spans the full
process.

## Distinction from Halving

Halving names supply-milestone reductions in the network emission rate. Emission names the ongoing
creation-and-allocation process that continues under each rate step on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

Halving changes how much new TAO enters per block; emission still names the wider allocation flow.

The first halving already cut the per-block rate from one TAO to half a TAO, and later halvings
reduce it further at successive supply thresholds, so the same injection-and-distribution rhythm
continues while the quantity of new TAO entering each block shrinks over time
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Issuance

Emission and issuance describe the flow and the stock of network TAO. Emission is the process that
creates and allocates new TAO, and subnet alpha on a subnet such as netuid 1, each block and tempo,
while issuance is the resulting circulating amount of TAO in the network — the cumulative total that
emission adds to over time. One names the ongoing creation-and-allocation mechanism; the other names
the supply level it produces ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)).

The distinction matters for halving. Halvings are triggered by issuance crossing documented supply
thresholds, and each halving then steps down the emission rate, so issuance is the measured supply
that gates how fast emission proceeds. A statement about how much TAO exists is issuance; a
statement about how new TAO is created and distributed is emission
([Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

- **Emission** — the per-block and per-tempo process of creating and allocating TAO and alpha.
- **Issuance** — the circulating amount of TAO that emission accumulates over time.

## Distinction from Fees

Emission adds value through protocol allocation, while fees deduct TAO from a sender to complete a
state-changing operation ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

Use emission for rewards credited to miners, validators, and stakers after consensus and
distribution; use fees for the cost of submitting an operation.

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

Emission names the ongoing subnet reward and injection flow, while alpha outstanding injection is
one documented emission path that adds alpha outside the reserve pool
([Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Emission covers the wider flow; alpha outstanding names the outside-reserve location that flow can
reach.

## Distinction from Alpha Halving

Emission names the ongoing reward and injection flow on a subnet, while alpha halving names the
subnet milestone that reduces the alpha emission rate after a supply threshold
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

Emission covers the stream; alpha halving covers the step-down event on that stream for one subnet's
alpha.

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Emission names the broader subnet reward and injection flow, while delegation rewards name the
nominator's proportional share of validator-side returns after distribution and take on delegated
stake ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Emission is the upstream flow; delegation rewards are a downstream share a nominator receives from
the validator-side portion.

## Distinction from Incentive Mechanism

Emission names the subnet reward and injection flow that follows evaluation, while the incentive
mechanism names the subnet-side rules that decide what useful work means before rewards are
allocated ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

Emission distributes value; the mechanism defines the evaluation frame that makes distribution
meaningful.

## Distinction from Liquidity Positions

Emission names subnet reward and injection flow, while liquidity positions name pool-depth supply
for conversions and fee activity ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

Emission distribution and Yuma outcomes are separate from range-based pool supply.

## Distinction from Price Discovery

Price discovery and emission both attach numbers to a subnet's alpha, but from different systems.
Price discovery is the market price that emerges from the subnet pool's TAO-to-alpha reserve ratio
as trading and conversions move it, while emission is the protocol-driven distribution of reward
alpha that Yuma Consensus settles from validator evaluation, so emission sets how much reward alpha
is created and to whom rather than the pool price a reader sees
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Price discovery** — the market price emerging from the subnet pool's reserve ratio.
- **Emission** — the protocol distribution of reward alpha from validator evaluation.

## Distinction from Alpha Distribution Ratio

The alpha distribution ratio compares distributed subnet alpha against alpha injected into the pool.
Emission is the broader process by which TAO and subnet alpha enter circulation through protocol
schedules
([Glossary: ADR](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#adr-alpha-distribution-ratio),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Alpha distribution ratio** — comparative measure of distribution versus pool injection.
- **Emission** — protocol process that schedules TAO and alpha entering circulation.

Ratio vocabulary names one accounting comparison; emission vocabulary names the wider issuance path
([Emission: injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

## Distinction from Tao

Emission in Bittensor involves both TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. TAO reserve injection and
alpha reserve injection are separate parts of the model, and the alpha side belongs to the
subnet-specific token side.

This framing matters for tokenomics articles. A TAO-only explanation can miss the subnet pool side
of Dynamic TAO, while an alpha-only explanation can miss the network asset entering subnet liquidity
settings
([TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from Consensus

Yuma Consensus turns validator evaluations into allocation outcomes. Validators submit weights,
consensus filters and aggregates those signals, and the result affects miner incentives and
validator dividends inside the subnet's emission flow.

Consensus score and validator-miner bonds belong near this process because they shape how
evaluations move through consensus. They do not replace the word emission: emission is the
value-creation and allocation process around those consensus signals
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Glossary: Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)).

## Distinction from Supply

Emission is related to issuance and halving but is not the same term. Issuance concerns circulating
TAO supply, while halving concerns scheduled reductions in emission rate. Emission names the ongoing
process that creates and allocates value before those broader supply concepts are interpreted.

This keeps supply vocabulary and role-allocation vocabulary separate. Emission can affect the
broader tokenomics picture without becoming a substitute for issuance, halving, incentives, or
dividends ([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

## Distinction from RAO

Emission and RAO name a process and the unit scale it uses. Emission is the value-creation and
allocation flow that distributes TAO and subnet alpha tokens, while RAO is the finer TAO
denomination that expresses TAO-denominated amounts in that flow at sub-whole-TAO precision
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: RAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rao),
[Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau)).

- **Emission** — the value-creation and allocation flow for TAO and subnet alpha tokens.
- **RAO** — the finer TAO denomination used to express amounts in that flow precisely.

## Reader Boundary

Emission is a reference term for Bittensor token creation and allocation. It is not a staking
recommendation, subnet ranking table, market forecast, or role-specific operating guide
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

Emission names creation and allocation before a statement narrows to a miner incentive, validator
dividend, reserve injection, or supply metric.
