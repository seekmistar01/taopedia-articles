# Issuance

Issuance is the circulating amount of TAO in the Bittensor network. The
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) entry describes it
as TAO circulating in the network, including TAO held in wallets and TAO held in subnet liquidity
pools.

## Circulating TAO context

Issuance is a supply measure, not a reward category. It tells readers what amount of TAO is counted
as circulating in the network context. That circulating amount can include TAO held directly by
participants and TAO present in subnet pool reserves, so issuance should not be reduced to only
wallet balances or only newly emitted tokens.

The official glossary frames issuance at the network-token level:
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance).

## Relationship to emissions

Emissions are the process that creates and distributes new token amounts over time. Issuance is the
supply state those processes affect. In Bittensor, the
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) docs describe newly created TAO and
subnet-specific alpha tokens entering subnet and participant contexts. Issuance, by contrast, is the
circulating TAO amount after those network-level changes are accounted for.

This distinction keeps a statement about total circulating TAO separate from a statement about how a
block, subnet, or participant receives emissions.

## Relationship to halvings

TAO halvings depend on issuance thresholds rather than calendar dates alone. The
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) docs explain that TAO halving
reduces the TAO emission rate after total TAO issuance reaches supply-based thresholds. In that
context, issuance is the supply measure used to determine when the network-level halving rule
applies.

Alpha halvings are a separate subnet-level concept. They concern subnet alpha-token emission, while
TAO issuance concerns the network token.

## Recycling and burning boundary

Issuance can change when tokens are emitted, recycled, or burned. The
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
entry describes recycling as subtracting tokens from the chain's issuance record so the same
quantity can be emitted again, while burning removes tokens without making them available for future
emission.

For a reader, the important boundary is that issuance is not the same as a live price, a staking
return, or a subnet ranking. It is a supply-side term used to interpret tokenomics statements.

## Subnet Pool Context

Issuance counts TAO held in subnet liquidity pools as part of circulating supply. The official
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) entry includes
subnet pool TAO alongside wallet-held TAO, and the
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation describes how TAO moves
into subnet and participant contexts over time.

For readers, subnet pool TAO belongs to the issuance supply picture rather than to a single
participant reward category.

References: [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Halving Threshold Context

TAO halving rules are tied to issuance milestones rather than calendar dates alone. The official
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) documentation explains that
network-level halving reduces the TAO emission rate after total issuance reaches configured
thresholds.

For readers, issuance is the supply measure those thresholds refer to. It is not a live halving
schedule or a price forecast.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance)

## Relationship to Recycling

Issuance and recycling address related but different parts of Bittensor supply accounting. The
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) describes TAO
circulating in the network, while the
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
explains that recycled tokens are subtracted from the issuance record so the same quantity can be
emitted again.

For readers, issuance names the circulating supply record, while recycling describes a supply-flow
adjustment that can return deducted amounts to future emission.

References: [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Issuance counts circulating TAO across the network, including TAO in subnet liquidity pools. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, issuance still names network-level circulating TAO. Subnet pool TAO belongs to that
supply picture even when the subnet later runs more than one incentive mechanism.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)
