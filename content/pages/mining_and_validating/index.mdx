# Mining and Validating in Bittensor

Bittensor organizes work into specialized subnets. Within each subnet, **miners** produce a digital
commodity (e.g., model outputs, retrieval results, compute), and **validators** evaluate that work
according to the subnet’s incentive mechanism. Emissions of TAO are allocated based on
validator-submitted weights aggregated by consensus.

References: [Docs Home](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/),
[Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Miners

- **Role:** Produce the commodity requested by a subnet (e.g., respond to prompts, retrieve
  documents, provide compute).
- **Joining a subnet:** Miners generally register to an existing subnet; they do not need to create
  their own.
- **Registration cost:** Registration consumes TAO (cost varies dynamically based on time since the
  last registration). The TAO used to secure a UID is sunk (burned) during registration.
- **Competition:** Miners compete by improving the quality, timeliness, and reliability of their
  outputs according to the subnet’s evaluation criteria.

Miner work is subnet-specific. A miner's output only makes sense against the digital commodity and
evaluation rules of the subnet where it is registered. This is why mining articles usually pair the
role with subnet context rather than treating miners as a single network-wide worker type. The same
miner role can therefore look different across subnets because the requested commodity, evaluation
cadence, and quality signals are set by the subnet. Registration gives the miner a UID in that
subnet, but continued reward competition depends on the quality of the work that UID produces.

Reference: [Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)

## Validators

- **Role:** Evaluate miner outputs using the subnet’s incentive mechanism and produce a weight
  vector that reflects relative miner performance.
- **Weight submission:** Validators periodically submit weights to the blockchain; these are inputs
  to consensus for emissions allocation.
- **Dividends and stake:** Validators may receive dividends aligned with their performance and stake
  (depending on the mechanism archetype). Stakers can delegate TAO to validators.

Validator evaluation becomes chain-relevant through weight submission. The validator does not assign
final emissions alone; it contributes a view of miner performance that consensus can aggregate with
other validator views. This keeps validation connected to both subnet scoring and chain-level
emission accounting. The validator role also depends on timeliness: stale or misaligned evaluation
has less value when the subnet needs weights that reflect current miner performance.

Weights are relative scoring signals rather than miner outputs themselves. They describe how a
validator ranks or measures miners for the subnet's reward process. Because mechanisms differ,
useful validation is also subnet-relative.

References: [Validators (learnbittensor.org)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)

## Incentives and Emissions

- **Emission process:** Newly issued TAO is injected into subnet pools; allocation to miners and
  validators is driven by validator weights after consensus aggregation.
- **Aggregation:** Yuma Consensus aggregates validator-submitted weights, rewarding validators whose
  evaluations are consistent, timely, and predictive; miners with higher aggregate weights receive
  larger emission shares.
- **Staker participation:** TAO holders can stake to validators and share in validator rewards.

The emission path links the roles without merging them. Miners compete for incentive by producing
useful subnet work, validators compete for dividends by evaluating that work, and stakers
participate through validator delegation. These are connected outcomes in the same reward process,
not interchangeable role names. That distinction keeps reward vocabulary precise. Incentive points
toward miner-side emission, dividends point toward validator-side emission, and delegation explains
how stakers share in the validator side of the outcome.

References: [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Subnets and Mechanisms

- **Subnet creators:** Define the incentive mechanism for evaluating work and set the tasks
  validators use for scoring.
- **Mechanism flexibility:** Different subnets can adopt different mechanisms (e.g., accuracy-based
  scoring, reputation-based signals), enabling specialization.

This separates subnet design from subnet participation. Subnet creators define the task and
mechanism, while miners and validators operate inside that design by producing and evaluating work.
The boundary helps explain why two subnets can use the same role names while rewarding different
commodities. The names miner and validator describe who participates, while the incentive mechanism
describes what participation is measured against.

References:
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Practical Considerations

- **Quality matters:** Miners should optimize for the subnet’s published evaluation criteria and
  datasets; validators reward consistent high-quality outputs.
- **Costs and operations:** Miners should plan for registration costs, runtime costs (compute,
  bandwidth), and iteration to improve scores; validators need robust evaluation infrastructure and
  transparent methodology.
- **Transparency and security:** Use official docs and release announcements to avoid malicious
  lookalikes; validate endpoints and software sources.

References: [Docs Home](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Bittensor subnets can run more than one incentive mechanism in parallel. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that validators must evaluate miners separately for each mechanism and that miner performance in one
mechanism does not affect their rating in another.

For readers, miners still produce subnet work and validators still submit weights. When a subnet
uses multiple mechanisms, those roles span separate evaluation contexts inside the same subnet
market.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Relationship to Coinbase

Mining and validating and coinbase address related but different parts of Bittensor subnet
vocabulary. Mining and validating describes the roles inside a subnet: miners produce the work a
subnet asks for, and validators evaluate that work and submit weights. Coinbase is the per-block
protocol mechanism that accumulates pending emissions each block, injects TAO and alpha into subnet
pool reserves, and checks each subnet's epoch boundary to trigger the Yuma Consensus round that
turns validator weights into miner incentives and validator dividends. The official
[Mining](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners) and
[Validating](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators) documentation describes the roles, and the
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes the per-block operation that drives the emission outcome those roles compete
for.

Miners and validators perform their work between epoch rounds; coinbase determines when each round
fires and what emissions are distributed as a result. Miners answer what work is being produced;
validators answer how that work is being evaluated; coinbase answers what drives the per-block cycle
that schedules each round and delivers emission results to participants.

References: [Mining](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)

## Summary

- Miners generate outputs; validators evaluate them and submit weights.
- Emissions in TAO are distributed based on aggregated validator weights.
- Stakers delegate to validators; subnet creators define the rules of evaluation.
- This division of roles allows Bittensor to allocate incentives to useful work across many
  specialized subnets.
