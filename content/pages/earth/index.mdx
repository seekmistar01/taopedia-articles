**Earth** is the third planet from the Sun and the only known astronomical object known to harbor
life. About 29.2% of Earth's surface is land consisting of continents and islands, while the
remaining 70.8% is covered with water.

## Physical Characteristics

Earth is the fifth-largest planet in the Solar System. It has an equatorial diameter of about 12,742
kilometers and a mass of approximately 5.97 × 10²⁴ kg.

### Atmosphere

Earth's atmosphere consists mainly of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), with trace amounts of other
gases. This atmosphere is essential for life and protects the surface from harmful solar radiation.

### Gravity

The [[Gravity]] of Earth gives weight to physical objects and is approximately 9.8 m/s² at the
surface. This gravitational force keeps the atmosphere in place and the Moon in orbit.

## Life on Earth

Earth is the only known place in the universe where life exists. Life on Earth arose at least 3.5
billion years ago, and has since evolved into millions of species through the process of evolution.

## Moon

Earth has one natural satellite, the [[Moon]], which orbits Earth at an average distance of about
384,400 km. The Moon's gravitational influence produces the ocean tides and slightly lengthens
Earth's day.

## History of Study

Ancient civilizations believed Earth was flat, but the concept of a spherical Earth emerged in
ancient Greek philosophy. [[Isaac Newton]] explained how [[Gravity]] keeps planets in orbit around
the Sun.
