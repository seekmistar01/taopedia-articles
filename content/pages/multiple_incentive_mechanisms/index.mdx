# Multiple Incentive Mechanisms

Multiple incentive mechanisms are separate evaluation contexts inside one Bittensor subnet. The
glossary describes them as mechanisms with their own bond pools for Yuma Consensus calculations,
where validators evaluate miners separately for each mechanism
([Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)).

The term is narrower than the general incentive-mechanism concept. An incentive mechanism connects
work, evaluation, and rewards; multiple incentive mechanisms describe the case where more than one
of those evaluation contexts exists inside the same subnet
([Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism),
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)).

## Evaluation Separation

The core idea is separation. Miner performance in one mechanism should not be read as miner
performance in another mechanism unless the cited source says the contexts have been combined
([Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)).

That makes mechanism context part of the evidence. A claim about ratings, weights, or evaluation
results is incomplete if it does not preserve which mechanism produced the result.

## Bond Pool Context

Each mechanism has its own bond-pool context for Yuma Consensus calculations. Yuma Consensus is the
process that aggregates validator weight signals into miner incentives and validator dividends, so
separate bond pools mean separate consensus inputs for each mechanism context
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)).

For readers, bond-pool separation is not just extra vocabulary. It is what keeps one mechanism's
rating and bond history from being treated as the same evidence as another mechanism's records.

## Validator and Miner Reading

Validators and miners are still read through the selected mechanism context. A validator's
evaluation signal and a miner's rating can be mechanism-specific, so article prose should not turn
them into one subnet-wide claim without direct source support
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)).

This boundary is especially important when the same subnet contains more than one work or evaluation
path. The subnet is the container, while the mechanism context identifies which rating and bond-pool
evidence is being discussed.

## Emission Context

Emission language should also preserve the mechanism boundary. Bittensor emission documentation
gives the broader reward-flow context, while multiple-mechanism references explain why rewards and
ratings may need to be interpreted by mechanism inside a subnet
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)).

The safe article-level claim is structural: multiple incentive mechanisms separate evaluation
contexts. A stronger claim about actual reward distribution in a specific subnet needs a
mechanism-specific source.

## Evidence Boundary

The multiple-mechanism references support claims about separate evaluation contexts, separate miner
ratings, and separate bond pools. They do not by themselves prove how many mechanisms a specific
subnet contains, whether one mechanism is preferable, or how a subnet's work should be interpreted
outside the cited mechanism context.

For readers, the term should be used as a reference label for parallel evaluation context inside a
single subnet
([Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For multiple
incentive mechanisms, that sequence changes how readers should interpret examples of parallel
evaluation, separate bond pools, and per-mechanism emission outcomes.

In localnet, multiple-mechanism setups can be exercised in an isolated environment. Localnet
mechanism results reflect local chain state and locally configured incentive parameters rather than
production subnet evaluation.

On testnet, multiple-mechanism behavior can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet
mechanism outputs show how independent evaluation, bond-pool separation, and per-mechanism consensus
behave under shared chain conditions while staying separate from mainnet subnet state.

On mainnet, multiple incentive mechanisms are the live, parallel evaluation systems inside a
production Bittensor subnet. The
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)
reference describes the per-mechanism bond pool and evaluation flow that applies on the production
network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A per-mechanism emission or bond outcome from one
environment should not be read as evidence of production subnet behavior in another environment.
