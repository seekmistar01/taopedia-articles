# Reserve Ratio

A reserve ratio is the relationship between the TAO reserve and alpha reserve inside a Bittensor
subnet pool. Each subnet pool pairs TAO with a subnet-specific alpha token, and the ratio between
those reserve sides is central to Dynamic TAO pool behavior
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

## What It Represents

Reserve ratio names the proportion between TAO and alpha held inside a subnet's paired AMM pool. It
helps readers interpret alpha price, slippage, and staking conversions on alpha subnets that
maintain both reserve sides
([Glossary: Reserve Ratio](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#reserve-ratio),
[Understanding Subnets: Price/Rate of Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#pricerate-of-alpha-tokens)).

The root subnet (subnet zero) has no alpha currency and therefore no paired reserve ratio. Reserve
ratio applies to alpha subnets with both TAO and alpha reserve sides
([Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)).

Observed reserve-ratio values from one network environment should not be read as pool state on
another ([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Impermanent Loss

Reserve ratio is the proportion of TAO to alpha a subnet pool holds. Impermanent loss is the value
drift a liquidity provider can bear as that ratio moves away from where the provider entered
([Understanding Subnets: Liquidity Pools](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#liquidity-pools),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

- **Reserve ratio** — the proportion of TAO to alpha a subnet pool holds.
- **Impermanent loss** — the drift cost a liquidity provider can bear as that ratio moves.

## Distinction from Alpha Price

Alpha price is the TAO-denominated quote derived from paired reserves. Reserve ratio names the
proportion between those reserve sides before a reader turns the pool state into a per-alpha quote
([Understanding Subnets: Price/Rate of Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#pricerate-of-alpha-tokens)).

- **Reserve ratio** — paired reserve proportion inside the pool.
- **Alpha price** — TAO-denominated quote derived from those reserves.

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

Reserve ratio compares pool-held reserves. Alpha outstanding names alpha held outside the reserve
pool ([Glossary: Reserve Ratio](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#reserve-ratio),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Reserve ratio** — comparative pool reserve measure.
- **Alpha outstanding** — alpha held outside the reserve pool.

## Distinction from Slippage

Reserve ratio describes the TAO-to-alpha proportion in a subnet pool. Slippage is the
received-amount gap when a conversion moves through that pool
([Glossary: Reserve Ratio](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#reserve-ratio),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Reserve ratio** — paired reserve proportion in the pool.
- **Slippage** — conversion gap after pool movement.

## Distinction from Liquidity Positions

Liquidity positions add range-based depth inside a subnet pool. Reserve ratio still describes the
paired TAO-and-alpha proportion that staking, unstaking, and swaps move through
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Reserve ratio** — overall paired reserve proportion in the pool.
- **Liquidity position** — range-based depth a provider adds inside that pool.

## Distinction from Emission Injection

TAO reserve injection and alpha reserve injection add to one reserve side or grow the paired side in
relation to injection. Reserve ratio describes the relationship between the sides after those
quantities are considered together
([Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

- **Reserve ratio** — relationship between TAO and alpha reserves in the pool.
- **Emission injection** — additions to one or both reserve sides from emission.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Reserve ratio is the numeric relationship between TAO and alpha sides of a subnet liquidity pool. It
names pool-balance math used in conversion quotes, not the recurring settlement step that converts
validator weight submissions into emission shares
([Glossary: Reserve Ratio](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#reserve-ratio),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on each subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Earlier sections separate reserve ratio from emission injection and from alpha reserve inventory.
Pool-side ratios support staking conversions; Yuma settles evaluation signals from eligible
validators on that netuid—not the reserve ratio calculation itself
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Readers should treat reserve ratio as pool-math vocabulary on one netuid, and Yuma as the
epoch-boundary mechanism that allocates subnet rewards from consensus while validators remain
active.

- **Reserve ratio** — numeric TAO-and-alpha reserve relationship in a subnet pool.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Reserve ratio is a reference term for the relationship between TAO and alpha reserves inside a
subnet pool. Concrete examples require the selected subnet, network environment, and source for the
pool context
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from TAO Reserve

A reserve ratio and the TAO reserve relate as a derived ratio and one of its inputs. The TAO reserve
is the TAO-side balance held in a subnet's liquidity pool, while the reserve ratio expresses the
relationship between that TAO reserve and the alpha reserve — the proportion that governs price and
slippage
([Glossary: Reserve Ratio](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#reserve-ratio),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **TAO reserve** — the TAO-side balance in a subnet's liquidity pool.
- **Reserve ratio** — the proportion between the TAO and alpha reserves.
