# Subnet Liquidity Pools

Subnet liquidity pools are the paired-reserve context for subnet tokenomics in Bittensor. Each pool
connects a TAO reserve with the subnet's alpha reserve, giving the subnet a conversion surface for
staking and unstaking
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

## What It Represents

Documentation describes each subnet pool as paired TAO and alpha reserves used for staking
conversions, unstaking, and alpha price quotes. Alpha outstanding names alpha outside that reserve
context, not every alpha token associated with the subnet
([Understanding Subnets: Liquidity pools](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#liquidity-pools),
[Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection)).

Subnet pool examples from one network environment should not be read as representing production pool
state on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Reserve injection can add TAO and matched alpha to deepen both sides of a subnet pool. Cross-subnet
TAO reserve injection is flow-based, determined by net TAO inflows rather than token prices
([Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Emission: Distribution across subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution-across-subnets)).

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

A subnet liquidity pool names paired TAO and alpha reserves held for conversion. Alpha outstanding
names alpha held by participants outside that reserve context
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection)).

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — paired TAO and alpha reserves held for conversion.
- **Alpha outstanding** — alpha held outside the reserve context.

## Distinction from Reserve Ratio

The reserve ratio names how the TAO and alpha sides relate numerically at a given observation time.
The subnet liquidity pool names the paired structure itself
([Understanding Subnets: Liquidity pools](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#liquidity-pools)).

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — the paired reserve structure.
- **Reserve ratio** — numeric relationship between the two reserve sides.

## Distinction from Protocol Alpha

Protocol alpha names protocol-held alpha cached per subnet during reserve injection. A subnet
liquidity pool names the paired reserve structure
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — paired TAO and alpha reserve structure.
- **Protocol alpha** — protocol-held alpha balance cached per subnet.

## Distinction from Subnet Markets

A subnet liquidity pool is the paired reserve that prices and trades alpha. A subnet market is the
work-and-evaluation setting where miners produce and validators evaluate
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — TAO-and-alpha reserve for conversion and pricing.
- **Subnet market** — work-and-evaluation economic setting.

## Distinction from Constant Product AMM

Bittensor subnet pools use constant product automated market maker behavior. Staking and unstaking
pass through paired reserve movement rather than fixed one-sided conversion
([Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — paired reserve conversion surface.
- **Constant product AMM** — pool rule that moves both sides during conversions.

## Distinction from Staking and Unstaking

Staking passes TAO through the pool and produces alpha-denominated stake. Unstaking reverses that
path through the same reserve context
([Staking and Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — reserve surface both directions traverse.
- **Staking / unstaking** — user-facing conversion actions through that surface.

## Distinction from Slippage

Slippage is the difference between a simple expected conversion and the result produced by the pool
state used for the action ([Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — paired reserve structure.
- **Slippage** — gap between expected and realized conversion through that structure.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Subnet liquidity pools are the paired reserve structure for TAO and alpha conversions on one netuid.
They name the pool surface used for staking and unstaking quotes, not the recurring settlement step
that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on each subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate subnet liquidity pools from slippage and from staking actions. Pool
reserves support conversion math; Yuma settles evaluation signals from eligible validators on that
netuid—not the paired reserve balances themselves
([Glossary: Alpha Reserve](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-reserve)).

Readers should treat subnet liquidity pools as reserve and conversion vocabulary, and Yuma as the
epoch-boundary mechanism that allocates subnet rewards from consensus while validators remain
active.

- **Subnet liquidity pool** — paired reserve structure for conversions.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Subnet liquidity pools are reserve and conversion vocabulary. They name the paired TAO and alpha
reserve context for a subnet, not exact balances or timing at observation
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).
