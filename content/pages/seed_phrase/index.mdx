# Seed Phrase

A seed phrase is the human-readable word sequence that can recover access to Bittensor wallet key
material. The official
[Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase) describes it
as a series of words generated with a wallet's cryptographic key pair and used to recover the
coldkey private key offline.

## Recovery Material Boundary

A seed phrase is recovery material, not a public wallet address or ordinary account label. The
glossary ties it to restoring coldkey private-key access, while the wallet documentation separates
wallet identity from the software used to view or manage it
([Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase),
[Wallets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

That boundary keeps the term in custody context. Sharing a seed phrase should be understood as
exposing recovery authority over the protected wallet material, not as sharing a harmless
identifier.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For seed phrase
vocabulary, that sequence changes which wallet recovery material a reader should assume when judging
whether recovery words protect production custody.

In localnet, wallets and recovery phrases are often generated for development and testing. Localnet
seed phrases protect isolated key access rather than production
[coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey) custody on Finney.

On testnet, recovery words still gate wallet access on a shared non-production network. Testnet
wallet material is separate from mainnet key custody
([Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

On mainnet, a seed phrase can restore live coldkey private-key access to
[TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau) and
[stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake) the wallet controls
([Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Recovery-phrase examples from one environment should not
be read as describing production wallet custody on another network.

## A Seed Phrase Is at Least Twelve Words

The official [Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)
gives the form a concrete minimum: a series of at least twelve words, generated together with the
wallet's cryptographic key pair. The word count is part of the definition rather than a display
choice a wallet makes.

For readers, that minimum is why a seed phrase is recorded as an ordered list of whole words instead
of a short password. The full ordered sequence is what carries the information needed to regenerate
the keys, so the complete set of words in their original order is what restores access
([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).

## Losing Every Backup Is Unrecoverable

The same security page states that losing all access to a seed phrase or initialized wallets
permanently and unrecoverably loses access to the account, including
[TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau) and
[stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake) the coldkey controlled.

Seed phrase vocabulary therefore includes a custody obligation: redundant offline backups are part
of the concept because the network cannot restore lost recovery material for the holder.

## Leaked Material Should Be Rotated, Not Reused

Official guidance distinguishes losing a secret from leaking one. A leaked seed phrase should be
treated as compromised, and the owner can rotate access through a
[coldkey swap](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap) rather than continuing to rely on
exposed recovery words
([Handle your Seed Phrase Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

That response keeps seed phrase reading in wallet-security vocabulary. The term names recovery
material; leak handling belongs to rotation and replacement of coldkey control.

## Distinction from Mnemonic

Mnemonic and seed phrase are adjacent recovery terms in Bittensor wallet documentation. Both name
the word sequence that can regenerate wallet keys
([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic),
[Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)).

Phrase-form vocabulary and mnemonic vocabulary describe the same recovery layer with different
labels. Official key documentation describes a mnemonic as a word sequence used to regenerate keys
and restore both coldkeys and hotkeys, so seed phrase emphasizes the recovery-words form while
mnemonic names the same recovery concept in Bittensor key vocabulary
([Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets),
[Working with Keys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/working-with-keys)).

## Distinction from Bittensor Wallet

A Bittensor wallet is the identity container for keys and stake. A seed phrase is offline recovery
material for that wallet's key access
([Glossary: Bittensor Wallet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bittensor-wallet),
[Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)).

Wallet vocabulary names the on-chain identity structure; seed phrase vocabulary names backup words
that can restore it.

## Distinction from Coldkey Swap

Coldkey swap migrates wallet identity after recovery material is compromised. A seed phrase is the
recovery material that leak-response guidance may require replacing
([Coldkey Swap](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap),
[Handle your Seed Phrase Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

Recovery-word vocabulary and identity-migration vocabulary answer different post-leak actions.

The [Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)
describes a series of words generated with a wallet's cryptographic key pair and used to recover the
[coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey) private key offline.
[Handle your Seed Phrase Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)
documentation treats whoever holds those words as having full control over the wallet they restore.

That makes the seed phrase key-equivalent recovery material rather than a display label. Disclosing
the words is closer to handing over wallet control than to sharing a public address.

A seed phrase is most closely tied to coldkey recovery in official prose. The glossary definition
focuses on recovering the coldkey private key, which is the ownership root for TAO and stake in
Bittensor wallet design. Hotkeys remain operational keys controlled by that ownership context
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey),
[Glossary: Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey)).

The term names the recovery-word role inside wallet design. It does not name a display format, a key
management standard, or a backup schedule. Those questions belong to the wallet's own key security
context.

## Distinction from Wallet Address

A wallet address is a public destination identifier. A seed phrase is secret recovery material that
must never be shared like an address
([Glossary: Wallet Address](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#wallet-address),
[Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)).

Public destination vocabulary and secret recovery vocabulary require opposite handling.

## Distinction from Phishing

Phishing names deceptive attempts to obtain recovery secrets. A seed phrase is the custody object
those attacks target
([Handle your Seed Phrase Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase),
[Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)).

Attack-pattern vocabulary and recovery-material vocabulary answer different security questions.

## Distinction from Regenerating a Key

Regenerating a key names the recovery action that rebuilds a lost coldkey or hotkey. A seed phrase
is the secret input that can enable that action
([Glossary: Regenerating a Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#regenerating-a-key),
[Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase)).

Procedure vocabulary names the recovery step; seed phrase vocabulary names the backup words behind
it.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

A seed phrase is the human-readable word sequence that can recover Bittensor wallet key material
offline. It names recovery-word vocabulary inside wallet design, not subnet scoring or emission
settlement ([Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase),
[Handle your Seed Phrase Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that converts submitted validator weights into emission
shares after evaluation completes on a subnet. Seed phrases restore signing keys locally; they do
not submit weights or settle emissions by themselves
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers should keep recovery-word vocabulary separate from subnet settlement vocabulary. Seed phrase
answers which word sequence can restore coldkey access; Yuma Consensus answers how validator weights
become emission shares in a subnet
([Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Seed phrase** — recovery word sequence for wallet key material.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Seed phrase should not be read as a public identifier, a validator label, or a transferable token.
It is secret recovery material. The term names the recovery-word concept inside Bittensor wallet
design, not a balance, a role, or a network address.
