# Seed Phrase Security

Seed phrase security is the practice of treating a Bittensor wallet recovery phrase as sensitive
wallet access material. The wallet docs describe a seed phrase as a human-usable recovery phrase
generated with a wallet, while the wallet overview explains that wallets are used to sign
transactions, access TAO, and manage stake.

## Why seed phrases matter

A seed phrase matters because control of it can become control of the wallet it recovers. The
[seed-phrase page](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase) frames the two main
failure modes as losing the phrase or disclosing it to someone else. For Taopedia readers, the
important distinction is simple: loss can remove access, while disclosure can give another party
access.

## Wallet access context

The wallet overview explains that Bittensor wallets are used for signing transactions, accessing
TAO, and managing stake in subnets. That makes recovery material different from an ordinary label or
public identifier. It is connected to the ability to regain wallet access, so readers should treat
seed-phrase claims as wallet-safety claims rather than generic account advice.

## Loss and disclosure

Loss and disclosure are different risks. Loss means the recovery phrase is no longer available when
the wallet needs to be restored. Disclosure means someone else may be able to use the phrase. The
source also warns that outside intervention may not be available after theft, scam, or accidental
key loss, so an article about seed phrases should avoid treating recovery as guaranteed.

## What this article does not publish

This article does not publish examples, recovery walkthroughs, storage recipes, screenshots, or
wallet material. That boundary matters because the seed-phrase page is about sensitive wallet access
material, and Taopedia should explain the concept without asking readers to expose anything that
controls a wallet.

## Reading seed-phrase claims

Seed-phrase claims should be read as claims about wallet access and wallet safety. A good claim says
whether it is talking about recovery, loss, disclosure, or daily wallet use. A claim that asks for,
shows, or casually handles recovery material is not just a documentation detail; it changes the
security context of the wallet.

## Each Key Gets Its Own Recovery Words

Bittensor wallet creation can produce separate recovery material for the coldkey and for each
hotkey. The [Working with Keys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/working-with-keys) guide notes
that a unique mnemonic is generated for each key and shown at creation time.

That structure matters for seed phrase security because protecting one phrase does not automatically
protect every key in the wallet. A coldkey recovery phrase and a hotkey recovery phrase are
different secrets tied to different signing roles in the
[Wallets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets) model.

For readers, wallet backup review should name which key each stored phrase belongs to before
treating one copy as complete protection for the whole wallet identity. Regenerating-a-key
vocabulary describes recovering the specific key tied to a given recovery phrase rather than every
key in a wallet at once, so each phrase restores only the key it was created for and coldkey phrase
exposure must be compared separately from hotkey phrase exposure
([Glossary: Regenerating a Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#regenerating-a-key),
[Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

## Phrase Recovery Rebuilds Key Access

Wallet guidance treats the seed phrase as the offline path back to protected key material when local
wallet files or devices are lost. The
[Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase) describes
those words as generated with the wallet key pair so access can be saved and later imported into
wallet software or hardware.

The wallet overview also notes that the cryptographic wallet can outlive any one application install
and be recovered from seed phrase material after device loss
([Wallets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

That recovery role sits behind routine signing rather than replacing it. Miners, validators, and
holders still operate through coldkeys and hotkeys day to day; the phrase matters when access must
be rebuilt from backup material rather than during ordinary subnet actions.

## Rotation Paths Differ by Key Type

The seed-phrase page ties leaked coldkey recovery material to coldkey rotation through a coldkey
swap
([Handle your Seed Phrase/Mnemonic Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

Hotkey exposure follows a different workstation-security path. Guidance describes rotating the
hotkey when operational key material is suspected while the coldkey remains protected
([Coldkey and hotkey workstation security](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-hotkey-security)).

Seed phrase security therefore includes knowing which key a phrase restores, not only whether the
words are stored safely. The appropriate response after suspected disclosure depends on whether the
affected material came from coldkey or hotkey creation output.

## Recovery Phrases Should Stay Offline

Seed-phrase guidance treats recovery material as sensitive wallet access information that should be
stored offline and protected from disclosure. The documented failure modes are loss and disclosure,
not merely forgetting a display label
([Handle your Seed Phrase/Mnemonic Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

For readers, seed phrase security is about custody of recovery words rather than about routine
subnet operation or public address sharing.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. Seed-phrase
security examples should be read against that network boundary rather than as proof of live mainnet
behavior.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Recovery-handling vocabulary is the same across
environments, but exposure consequences differ by network.

## Distinction from Mnemonic

Mnemonic names the recovery phrase itself. Seed phrase security names the handling practice that
protects that material from loss and disclosure
([Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic),
[Handle your Seed Phrase/Mnemonic Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

## Distinction from Coldkeys

Coldkey names the custody key whose recovery words carry the highest-consequence authority. Seed
phrase security names how those words are stored and protected
([Glossary: Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#coldkey),
[Handle your Seed Phrase/Mnemonic Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

## Distinction from Regenerating a Key

Regenerating a key names the restore action recovery words enable. Seed phrase security names the
prevention discipline that keeps only the owner able to perform that restore
([Glossary: Regenerating a Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#regenerating-a-key),
[Handle your Seed Phrase/Mnemonic Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase)).

## Distinction from Public Key

Seed phrase security and the public key name opposite sides of the same wallet's cryptography. Seed
phrase security is the handling discipline around the secret recovery words that can rebuild private
key material, while the public key is the publicly available, shareable component of the key pair
used to verify signatures and identify accounts so others can interact with the wallet
([Glossary: Public Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#public-key)).

- **Seed phrase security** — protects secret recovery material that must never be disclosed.
- **Public key** — the openly shareable identifier that is meant to be seen by others.

## Distinction from Private Key

Seed phrase security and the private key both concern secret wallet material, but they sit at
different layers. Seed phrase security is the practice of safeguarding the human-readable recovery
words from loss and disclosure, while the private key is the underlying private component of the
cryptographic key pair that secures and authorizes transactions and operations in the network
([Glossary: Private Key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#private-key)).

- **Seed phrase security** — guards the recovery words that can regenerate the underlying secret
  key.
- **Private key** — the secret key-pair component that actually authorizes transactions once
  derived.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Seed phrase security is the practice of protecting recovery words from theft, loss, and exposure. It
names wallet safety vocabulary, not the on-chain step that turns validator weight submissions into
emission shares
([Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase),
[Wallets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that converts submitted validator weights into emission
shares after evaluation completes. Protecting seed phrases guards custody offline; Yuma Consensus
settles scoring outcomes on a subnet
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers should keep recovery-security vocabulary separate from the settlement step. Seed phrase
security answers how recovery words are protected; Yuma Consensus answers how validator weights
become emission shares in a subnet
([Glossary: Seed Phrase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#seed-phrase),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Seed phrase security** — protecting recovery words from theft and loss.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Seed phrase security should not be read as a wallet brand, a subnet role, or proof that a phrase was
stored correctly. It names the handling discipline around recovery material that can regenerate
wallet keys
([Handle your Seed Phrase/Mnemonic Securely](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/handle-seed-phrase),
[Glossary: Mnemonic](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mnemonic)).
