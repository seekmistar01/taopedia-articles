# Validator Registration

Validator registration is how a participant enters a Bittensor subnet as a validator. The
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)
reference describes registration as registering a hotkey with a subnet to receive a UID on that
subnet so the validator can take part in evaluation and validation work there
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

## What It Represents

Subnets organize participants around UID slots. Without registration, a hotkey is not part of that
subnet's validator set. A subnet validator evaluates miner work inside a subnet and participates in
the subnet's consensus and emissions picture after entry succeeds
([Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's validator entry path. They should not be read as
representing production registration costs or permit outcomes on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Register

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes
subnet entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO. Validator
registration names the validator-specific case of that process
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

- **Register** — general subnet-entry vocabulary.
- **Validator registration** — validator-specific entry process.

## Distinction from UID Slot

Validator registration names the validator-specific entry process. A UID slot names the participant
position inside a subnet that entry grants
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Validator registration** — paid entry process for a validating participant.
- **UID slot** — numbered position granted inside a subnet after entry.

## Distinction from Netuid

Register describes the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot, while a netuid selects which
subnet that entry targets
([Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

- **Netuid** — identifier of the target subnet market.
- **Validator registration** — validator entry process inside that subnet context.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Validator registration names the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot. The validator permit
describes whether a participant has validation rights inside a subnet after entry
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

- **Validator registration** — entry that places a hotkey in the subnet.
- **Validator permit** — rights flag for validation work after entry.

## Distinction from Validator Deregistration

Validator registration gives a subnet validator a UID slot after registering a hotkey with that
subnet. Validator deregistration removes a low-performing validator slot when registration pressure
requires replacement, while the subnet itself continues
([Validator Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Validator registration** — entry into a subnet validator slot.
- **Validator deregistration** — removal of a low-performing validator slot.

## Distinction from Subnet Deregistration

Validator registration concerns entry into a continuing subnet. Subnet deregistration removes an
entire non-immune subnet from the network when the subnet limit is reached and a new subnet
registration attempts to enter
([Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration)).

- **Validator registration** — participant entry into one continuing subnet.
- **Subnet deregistration** — removal of the entire subnet context.

## Distinction from Burn Cost

Validator registration is the validator-specific subnet entry process that grants a UID slot. Burn
cost is the dynamic TAO amount paid to register on a subnet
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)).

- **Validator registration** — paid entry process for a validating participant.
- **Burn cost** — dynamic TAO price for that registration.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

Validator registration is how a participant enters a subnet as a validator by registering a hotkey
to receive a UID. A child hotkey is a separate key a parent hotkey authorizes to validate on its
behalf by re-delegating a portion of the parent's stake
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

- **Validator registration** — entry that gives a hotkey a validator UID.
- **Child hotkey** — authorized worker key validating with re-delegated stake.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Validator registration is the process of obtaining a validator UID slot on a selected netuid. It
names how a hotkey enters the validator role on the metagraph, not the recurring settlement step
that converts weight submissions into emission shares
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary after validators hold
active slots. It reads the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and
converts the result into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate validator registration from child hotkeys and from miner registration.
Registration grants a validator slot; Yuma settles evaluation signals from validators who meet
permit and stake-weight rules on that netuid—not the registration transaction itself
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Readers should treat validator registration as an entry step onto one subnet, and Yuma as the
epoch-boundary mechanism that allocates rewards from consensus while validators remain active.

- **Validator registration** — obtaining a validator UID slot on one netuid.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Validator registration should not be read as a recommendation to join any particular subnet, as a
command tutorial, or as a guarantee of emissions
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).
