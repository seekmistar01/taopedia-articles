# Staking and Delegation (TAO)

TAO holders can stake any amount of liquidity to validators. Staking aligns a staker with a
validator’s performance and typically entitles the staker to a share of validator dividends.

References:
[Managing Your Stakes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/managing-stake-sdk),
[Docs Home](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/)

## Key Concepts

- **Stake to validators:** Stakers choose validators to support by delegating TAO to them.
- **Minimum transaction amount:** 500,000 RAO (0.0005 TAO) for stake/unstake/move/transfer (per
  docs).
- **Wallets:** You need a wallet to manage stake (CLI or Wallet SDK).
- **Rewards:** Validator dividends are shared with their stakers, per network rules and validator
  setup.

Reference:
[Managing Your Stakes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/managing-stake-sdk)

## Typical Workflow (Conceptual)

- Create or import a wallet (hotkey/coldkey; see keys article).
- Select a validator to stake to, based on performance, transparency, and risk tolerance.
- Use the CLI to stake TAO to the validator; confirm transaction and monitor rewards over time.
- Rebalance by moving or unstaking as conditions change.

## Considerations

- **Risk:** Validator performance and mechanism outcomes vary; rewards are not guaranteed.
- **Costs:** Transaction fees and minimums apply; plan for operational cadence rather than frequent
  micro-movements.
- **Security:** Protect wallet keys and verify official CLI/docs to avoid phishing or malicious
  lookalikes.

## Relationship to Stake

Staking and stake are closely related but not interchangeable terms. In the glossary, staking is the
process of attaching TAO to a validator hotkey, while stake is the token amount associated with a
validator position after that support exists. The
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking) describes the
process, and the [Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)
describes the associated tokens.

For readers, staking is action language and stake is amount language. This article covers the broad
staking and delegation area; a specific stake claim should still identify the validator and subnet
context being discussed.

References: [Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)

## Relationship to Alpha Staker

Staking and delegation can describe more than one staking path. The
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) documentation
distinguishes stakers on the Root Subnet from stakers holding subnet alpha in a mining subnet. An
alpha staker belongs to the subnet-local alpha-token side of that distinction.

This keeps ordinary staking language from becoming too broad. Staking and delegation name the
general validator-support area, while alpha staker names a participant whose stake is held in a
specific subnet's alpha context.

Reference: [Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)

## Relationship to Root Staker

Root staker is the Root Subnet side of staking vocabulary. The
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)
describes Subnet Zero as a special subnet where validators can register and TAO holders can stake in
a subnet-agnostic way. A root staker is therefore a TAO holder using that root-subnet path.

For readers, staking and delegation is the umbrella topic. Root staker is narrower: it identifies
who is staking through Root Subnet validators rather than directly through one ordinary subnet's
alpha-token context.

References:
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)

## Relationship to Root Claims

Root claims are downstream of Root Subnet staking. The
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims) describes
how alpha dividends earned through root stake can be swapped into TAO or kept as subnet alpha. That
claim-handling step is separate from the initial act of staking to a validator.

For readers, staking and delegation explains how validator support is attached. Root claims explain
what can happen later to alpha dividends connected to the Root Subnet staking path. They should not
be read as a separate validator choice, live reward quote, or general staking instruction.

References:
[Root Claim Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)
