# Subnet Miner

A subnet miner is the task-performing role inside a Bittensor subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) defines a subnet miner
as the entity that performs tasks given by subnet validators, while
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) describes
subnets as markets where miners produce a specific digital commodity and validators measure the
work.

## Role in a Subnet

The miner role is tied to the subnet's work target. A miner in one subnet may produce a different
kind of output than a miner in another subnet because each subnet can define its own goals and
standards. [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)
explains that subnets specialize around different digital commodities, and the
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task) connects subnet tasks to
the work miners perform.

## Relationship to Validators

Subnet miners and validators form the basic work-and-evaluation relationship inside a subnet. Miners
provide work for the subnet, and validators measure that work against the subnet's standards. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) frames subnet miners
around tasks from validators, and
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) describes
validators as the role that measures miner work.

## Relationship to the Task

A subnet miner is the role performing the work; the subnet task is the work target itself. Keeping
those ideas separate makes the term easier to read: the miner is the role, while the task describes
what that role is expected to produce.
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)
places the task, protocol, and evaluation method together as parts of an incentive mechanism, and
the [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task) defines the task as
the work miners perform.

## Incentive Mechanism Context

Miner work becomes meaningful inside the subnet's incentive mechanism. The task says what work is
requested, the protocol shapes miner-validator interaction, and the evaluation method gives
validators a way to measure the result.
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)
describes these pieces as part of subnet design, while
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) explains why
subnet goals and standards differ.

## Reader Boundary

Subnet miner should be read as a role concept, not as a procedure or claim about any particular
miner. The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner) keeps the
term scoped to task-performing behavior, and
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) provides the
broader subnet context without requiring procedural detail in this article.
