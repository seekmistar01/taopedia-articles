# Triumvirate

The Triumvirate is the Bittensor governance body responsible for creating proposals. It originates
governance items that the Senate reviews before privileged changes can proceed
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

## Composition

The glossary identifies the Triumvirate as a three-person group associated with the Opentensor
Foundation
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

That composition is separate from the Senate. The Senate is the review body that votes on
Triumvirate proposals, while the Triumvirate is the body that creates those proposals
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

## Governance Role

Bittensor governance separates proposal origin from proposal review. The Triumvirate creates
proposals, and the Senate approves or rejects them
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

This separation replaced the earlier single-key sudo model for privileged network actions. In the
bicameral model, a privileged proposal needs both Triumvirate origin and Senate approval before it
can proceed through the governance process
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo)).

## Proposal Relationship

A proposal is the governance item put forward by the Triumvirate for Senate voting
([Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)).

The Triumvirate is therefore not the proposal itself. It is the body that originates the proposal,
while the proposal is the item being reviewed.

## Senate Relationship

The Senate reviews Triumvirate proposals and casts approval or disapproval votes
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)). That relationship keeps governance
authority divided between creation and review.

For readers, Triumvirate language should point to proposal creation. Senate language should point to
proposal review and voting.

## Subtensor Context

Subtensor is Bittensor's layer-one blockchain and system of record
([Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)). Triumvirate
proposals matter to Subtensor because approved privileged changes can affect that chain layer
through governance ([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

That does not make the Triumvirate a chain layer. It is a governance body whose proposals can lead
to privileged Subtensor changes after review.

## Network Context

Bittensor documentation separates localnet, testnet, and mainnet environments
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Triumvirate examples should keep that environment boundary attached. A localnet or testnet example
can illustrate governance mechanics, but it is not evidence of a mainnet governance outcome.

## Reader Boundary

The Triumvirate is not the Senate, a proposal, a proposal hash, or Subtensor itself. It names the
proposal-origin body inside Bittensor governance
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

When the focus is the item being reviewed, proposal is more precise. When the focus is the voting
body, Senate is more precise. When the focus is the chain layer affected by governance, Subtensor is
more precise.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For Triumvirate,
this sequence gives readers a boundary for interpreting governance examples and role-boundary notes.

Localnet examples are isolated and reflect local chain state, so they are useful for controlled
experiments rather than evidence of live Bittensor behavior. Testnet examples add shared
non-production conditions, which can reveal integration behavior without touching mainnet state.

On mainnet, Triumvirate examples should be read as live production governance behavior on the
production Bittensor network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet, so outcomes from one environment should not be treated as
proof of behavior in another.
