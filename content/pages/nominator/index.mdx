# Nominator

A nominator is a TAO holder who stakes with a validator in Bittensor delegation vocabulary
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term names the supplying side of the staking relationship. It should be read separately from the
validator role that receives delegated stake and performs validation work.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

On a subnet such as netuid 1, nominator and delegate stake name opposite sides of the same staking
relationship behind one validator hotkey. A nominator supplies nominated TAO support, while delegate
stake names only the TAO the delegate stakes personally
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

For readers comparing figures on netuid 1, nominator vocabulary identifies who attached support,
while delegate stake reports the validator-supplied amount only. Effective stake counts both
together when a reader needs the full backing total behind the hotkey
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Nominator** — TAO-holder role supplying delegated support.
- **Delegate stake** — validator self-staked TAO on the same hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

A nominator supplies TAO support behind a delegate validator. Delegation rewards name the return
path for that nominated support after validator take and emission settlement
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegation Rewards](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation-rewards)).

- **Nominator** — supplying role that attaches nominated TAO to a hotkey.
- **Delegation rewards** — return outcomes for nominated support after settlement.

Role vocabulary names who supplies support; reward vocabulary names what returns after distribution
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Senate

A nominator is the TAO-holder role that stakes behind a delegate validator. Senate is the governance
body formed from top delegate keys that approves or disapproves proposals
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#senate)).

- **Nominator** — supplying role that delegates TAO behind a validator.
- **Senate** — governance body of top delegate keys reviewing proposals.

Staking-relationship vocabulary and governance-body vocabulary answer different questions
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Delegate

A nominator is the TAO-holder role that stakes behind a delegate. A delegate is the subnet validator
role that receives that nominated stake and performs validation work
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Nominator** — supplying role that attaches TAO support to a chosen validator.
- **Delegate** — receiving validator role that performs validation and earns validator take.

The two words name opposite ends of the same staking relationship rather than interchangeable labels
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

A nominator supplies delegated TAO behind a validator hotkey through staking markets. That
supply-side role is separate from the tempo pass that converts validator weight submissions into
emission shares on a subnet
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus reads submitted weights, applies clipping and bonding, and then credits miner
incentives and validator dividends when the epoch closes
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Nomination moves stake toward a chosen delegate; weight setting is how that delegate records
evaluation scores for consensus to aggregate. The two actions should not be read as interchangeable
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

Effective stake counts nominated support together with delegate self-stake when readers need the
full backing behind a hotkey. Yuma still settles from submitted weights rather than from who
supplied that backing
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Nominator** — TAO-holder role that stakes with validators through delegation.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Distinction from Nominate

Nominate is the action vocabulary for staking TAO with a validator. Nominator is the role label for
the holder associated with that action
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

Stake is the related amount vocabulary. Delegated TAO contributes to the validator's stake context,
while the nominator term identifies who supplied that support
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Reader Boundary

Nominator should not be collapsed into delegate. The nominator supplies support, and the delegate
receives support as the validator role
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The role label does not by itself identify a subnet, validator, balance, or reward outcome.

## Distinction from Delegation

A nominator and delegation name a role and the action that defines it. Delegation is the act of
assigning stake to a validator you do not operate, while a nominator is the participant who performs
that delegation — the holder staking to a validator's hotkey in return for a share of rewards
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation** — the act of assigning stake to a validator you do not operate.
- **Nominator** — the participant who delegates their stake to that validator.
