# Burning

Burning is the removal of tokens from spendable circulation. In Bittensor tokenomics, the term helps
readers distinguish tokens that are destroyed or otherwise removed from normal circulation from
tokens that are distributed, staked, transferred, or recycled through another mechanism.

References: [Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Tokenomics Context

Burning is a supply-side concept. It describes what happens to tokens, not who earned them or
whether a subnet is valuable. That distinction matters because Bittensor articles may discuss
registration costs, emissions, staking, and recycling in the same tokenomics area, but each term
describes a different movement or accounting treatment of TAO.

References: [Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Recycling

Burning and recycling are related but not identical. Burning emphasizes removal from spendable
circulation. Recycling emphasizes reuse or redistribution through a protocol process. Keeping the
terms separate helps readers avoid treating every tokenomics sink as the same event.

Reference:
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Effect on Issuance

The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning) explains
that burned tokens remain counted in the record of token issuance even though they no longer
effectively circulate. Because they stay in that record, they are not re-emitted.

## Circulation Removal Context

The
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
describes removal from circulation as tokens that exist in neither wallet nor liquidity pool and
cannot be transacted. Burning names one form of that removal rather than recycling tokens back into
the record of issuance for future emission.

References: [Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Issuance

Burning and issuance address related but different parts of Bittensor supply accounting. The
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) describes TAO
circulating in the network, while the
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
explains that burned tokens remain in the issuance record even though they no longer effectively
circulate.

For readers, issuance names the circulating supply record, while burning describes removal from
transactable circulation without making that quantity available for future emission.

References: [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Burning removes tokens from spendable circulation. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, burning still names a network tokenomics sink. Subnet incentive mechanisms distribute
emissions among participants, but burning describes supply removal rather than per-mechanism miner
evaluation.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the term. It does not report live burn amounts, predict token price, or provide
investment advice. Readers should use current official sources when they need exact network values.
