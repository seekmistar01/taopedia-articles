# Maximum Supply

Maximum supply is the fixed ceiling on how much TAO can ever exist
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

## What It Represents

[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) documentation states that TAO
has a hard cap of 21 million, similar to Bitcoin's maximum supply. Issuance can approach this
ceiling over time but cannot exceed it.

The cap is a protocol-level maximum across the network's whole lifetime. Circulating supply changes
continuously while the ceiling stays fixed
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation describes the block reward
as the per-block TAO mint for the whole network. Halving cuts that rate when cumulative issuance
crosses documented supply thresholds on the way toward 21 million
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

Registration recycling and paid transaction fees both deduct from `TotalIssuance`, the same issuance
accounting halving thresholds measure against, slowing the measured approach to the cap
([Transaction Fees in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

Maximum supply examples depend on which network you connect to. Supply figures from one environment
should not be read as production issuance history on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Circulating Supply

Maximum supply is the fixed 21 million lifetime ceiling. Circulating supply is the live TAO amount
in circulation at a moment ([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Maximum supply** — the fixed 21 million lifetime ceiling on total TAO.
- **Circulating supply** — the live amount in circulation, changing with issuance and recycling.

## Distinction from Halving

Maximum supply names the lifetime ceiling. Halving names the emission-rate reduction when cumulative
issuance crosses supply thresholds
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving)).

Bittensor halves on supply thresholds rather than block numbers, so the per-block mint slows as
total issuance climbs toward 21 million
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

- **Maximum supply** — the fixed 21 million lifetime ceiling on total TAO.
- **Halving** — the rate-cut event when cumulative issuance crosses supply thresholds.

## Distinction from Block Reward

Maximum supply names the lifetime ceiling. Block reward names the per-block TAO mint that approaches
it ([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Maximum supply** — the total TAO that can ever exist.
- **Block reward** — the per-block mint rate that approaches that ceiling.

## Distinction from Recycling

Maximum supply is the fixed 21 million TAO ceiling. Recycling returns deducted TAO toward the
emission pool, which can delay threshold crossings without changing the cap
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Maximum supply** — fixed lifetime ceiling on total TAO issuance.
- **Recycling** — reuse path that can slow the approach toward that ceiling.

## Distinction from Alpha Tokens

The 21 million TAO cap bounds network-wide TAO issuance. Each subnet also emits its own alpha for
role-facing rewards ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Maximum supply** — fixed ceiling on total network TAO.
- **Alpha tokens** — per-subnet emission track separate from the TAO cap.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Maximum supply names the lifetime TAO ceiling. Yuma Consensus converts validator weight submissions
into emission shares at tempo boundaries
([Glossary: Maximum Supply](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#maximum-supply),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Maximum supply** — lifetime TAO ceiling for cumulative issuance.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level. It does not report current circulating supply, the
next halving date, or live recycling rates
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

## Distinction from Issuance

Maximum supply and issuance relate as a ceiling and the process approaching it. Maximum supply is
the fixed 21 million TAO lifetime cap on how much can ever exist, while issuance is the ongoing
creation of new TAO that accumulates toward — but never exceeds — that cap
([Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Maximum supply** — the fixed 21M TAO lifetime ceiling.
- **Issuance** — the ongoing creation of new TAO toward that ceiling.
