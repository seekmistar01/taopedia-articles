# Staking

Staking is the process of attaching TAO support to a validator in Bittensor. The official
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking) defines staking as attaching
TAO to a validator so the validator's total stake, consensus power, and dividend context can
increase.

## Validator Support

Staking is read through validator support. The
[Staking/delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
explains that staking, also known as delegation, supports validators because their total stake in a
subnet includes stake delegated by others. That support affects validator consensus power and
emission share.

This makes staking a participation process, not the validator role itself. The validator performs
validation; staking attaches support to that validator's subnet position.

## Relationship to Stake

Staking and stake are closely related but not identical. Staking names the process. Stake names the
associated token amount after support has been attached to a validator position. The
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake) entry defines stake as
the currency-token amount delegated to a validator UID in a subnet, including both self-stake and
stake delegated from others.

For readers, the distinction prevents a common collapse: staking is the action context, while stake
is the amount that becomes relevant for consensus and emissions.

## Relationship to Delegation

Official Bittensor documentation uses staking and delegation as closely related terms. The
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation) describes
delegation as staking TAO to a validator, and the
[Staking/delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
uses the same pairing.

In Taopedia, staking is the broad process name, while delegation emphasizes the support relationship
between a TAO holder and the validator receiving that support.

## Hotkey Attachment Context

The [Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking) describes
staking as attaching TAO to a validator hotkey to increase the validator's total stake, consensus
power, and share of dividends. For readers, staking names the support action and its validator-side
target in subnet consensus vocabulary.

References: [Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Staking attaches TAO support to a validator inside a subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that each mechanism operates with its own bond pool for Yuma Consensus calculations.

For readers, staking still names validator support in one subnet market. When that subnet runs more
than one incentive mechanism, bond-pool context should be read per mechanism.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Reader Boundary

Staking should not be read as a validator recommendation, a balance report, or a complete guide to
staking operations. Exact balances, fees, slippage, validator take, and subnet-specific behavior
depend on current official staking references.
