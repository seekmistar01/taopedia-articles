# Nominate

To nominate is to stake TAO with a validator or delegate in Bittensor. The
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) describes
nomination as the staking process by which TAO holders participate through validators.

## Delegation Context

Nominate is the action side of the nominator relationship. A
[nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) is the account role, while
nominate names the act of staking TAO with the validating side of a delegation relationship.
[Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation) names the support
relationship that nomination establishes on the validator hotkey receiving that stake
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Use nominate when the article covers the staking action and delegation when it covers the support
relationship that action creates on the validator hotkey being supported
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

Governance nomination language refers to separate Senate or proposal roles and should not be mixed
with staking nominate prose without naming that context
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

## Staking Context

The
[staking and delegation documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
uses delegation to describe staking TAO with validators. In Taopedia prose, nominate should be read
as delegation vocabulary rather than as a subnet role, account count, or chain-state claim.

## Validator Hotkey Context

The [Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) describes
nomination as staking TAO on a validator's hotkey so token holders can participate in subnet
emissions through active validators. The term names the delegation action and its validator-side
target in staking vocabulary, distinct from the nominator account role
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

For readers, nomination prose should name the validator hotkey or delegation context being
supported.

## The Nominating Coldkey Can Later Lock That Stake for Conviction

Nominate attaches a coldkey's stake to a validator hotkey on a subnet. The conviction and locked
stake feature works at that same coldkey level: documentation describes locked staking as letting
coldkey holders lock alpha stake to a specific hotkey on a subnet
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

A coldkey that nominates a validator hotkey is therefore the same coldkey that can subsequently lock
that staked position to build a conviction signal on that hotkey. Nominate names the staking action
that creates the position; locking is a separate, later action available to the same coldkey on a
position that already exists
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Staking Documentation Covers Procedures, Not This Label

This article is a concept boundary. It does not provide staking procedures, CLI commands, or live
chain data. Staking and delegation documentation covers how nomination is submitted on chain
([Staking and delegation documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Readers should keep procedural claims tied to those references rather than to nominate vocabulary
alone.

## Nomination Applies Within a Subnet Context

A [netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) identifies which subnet is in
scope, while nominate names the delegation action that supports a validator participating in that
context ([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

A precise claim should name both the subnet context and the validator hotkey receiving delegated
support.

## Nomination Is a Chosen Staking Action

Nomination names a deliberate delegation act by a TAO holder. The
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) describes a
nominator as an account that stakes TAO with validators, while nominate names the act of making that
delegation choice.

That keeps the action separate from automatic routing mechanisms. A holder chooses nomination; it is
not the same vocabulary as autostaking or miner emission routing.

The [Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) also ties
nomination to staking TAO on a validator hotkey so token holders can participate through active
validators.

## Nomination Versus Autostaking

Nominate and autostaking both result in staked TAO on a validator, but they describe different
things. To nominate is the act of staking TAO with a validator or delegate, as the
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) describes — a
staking action a TAO holder chooses to take.
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) is a mechanism that automatically
routes a miner's emission proceeds into a staking position on a chosen validator.

Nominate names a deliberate staking action by a token holder, while autostaking names an automatic
routing of a miner's emissions into stake. Nomination is a chosen act of staking existing TAO with a
validator, whereas autostaking continuously converts newly earned emissions into stake on a chosen
validator without a separate action each time.

## Nomination Versus Unstaking

Nomination adds or adjusts delegated support for a validator. Unstaking removes stake from the
delegation model. The
[Glossary: Unstake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking) describes
unstaking as withdrawing delegated stake.

Using the right verb keeps staking prose precise. Nominate is not a synonym for unstake or transfer.

Unstaking ends or reduces delegated exposure, while nomination establishes or changes which
validator hotkey receives that delegated support.

## Nomination Versus Moving Stake

Moving stake rearranges an existing delegated position between hotkeys or subnets without treating
the action as a fresh nomination from scratch. The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
lists moving stake alongside other stake-management operations separate from the initial delegation
choice.

Nominate therefore belongs with first-time or adjusted delegation support, while moving stake
belongs with rearranging where an existing staked position already sits.

Both verbs concern stake management, but they name different kinds of delegation changes.

## Multiple Mechanisms Inside One Subnet

Nomination attaches TAO support to a validator inside a subnet. The
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)
notes that each subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, nominate still names the delegation action in staking vocabulary. That action supports
a validator position in one subnet, which may run more than one incentive mechanism
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)).

## Subtensor Records Nomination on Chain

Nominate names the staking action of attaching TAO to a validator hotkey recorded through
chain-layer delegation state.
[Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) names Bittensor's layer 1
blockchain and system of record
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

Subtensor names the chain layer, while nominate names the delegation action recorded on that layer.

## Delegate Vocabulary Names the Receiving Role

Nominate names the action of staking TAO with a validator hotkey, while
[delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) names the subnet validator
role that receives that staked TAO and performs validation work
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

Nominate names the supplying action, while delegate names the receiving validator role on the other
side of that relationship.

## Coinbase and Yuma Consensus After Nomination

Nominate is the staking action that attaches a TAO holder's stake to a validator hotkey.
[Coinbase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase) is the per-block
protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission, injects TAO into emitting subnets' pool reserves,
accumulates pending emissions, and checks epoch boundaries to trigger
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) rounds.

Nominating increases a validator's effective stake, which Yuma Consensus uses to weight that
validator's influence during coinbase-triggered epoch rounds. Coinbase then distributes validator
dividends from each epoch's emissions, and a share of those dividends flows back to nominators in
proportion to the stake they delegated. Nominate is the action that positions stake behind a
validator; coinbase and Yuma Consensus convert that validator's consensus participation into the
emissions a nominator can ultimately share in.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For nomination on
a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret evidence about TAO
delegation and validator stake relationships
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

In localnet, nomination on netuid 1 can be tested in an isolated environment. Stake delegated to
validators in localnet reflects local chain configuration and does not represent production
validator associations or real TAO.

On testnet, nomination on netuid 1 can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet
nomination affects testnet validator stake but remains separate from mainnet delegation state and
real nominator relationships.

On mainnet, nominating on subnets such as netuid 1 is a live Subtensor staking action that changes
real TAO positions and validator stake on the production network. The
[Staking and delegation documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
describes the delegation mechanics that apply on mainnet.

A nomination example from one environment should not be read as representing validator stake or
delegation associations in another environment.

## Governance Role Scope

Nominate language should keep the governance role being nominated visible. Governance sources
separate proposal flow, Senate participation, and role-specific authority, so nomination claims
should identify the body or process they affect
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

This scope prevents a nomination from sounding like completed governance approval. A nomination can
be part of selecting or authorizing a role, while voting, approval, closure, and execution remain
separate steps.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is the participant role that delegates TAO to a validator. Nominate is the staking
action that attaches that support to a chosen validator hotkey
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

Role vocabulary names who supplies support; nominate vocabulary names the attachment action.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation names the broader staking relationship between nominator and validator. Nominate names
the specific action of staking TAO with a chosen validator hotkey
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

Relationship vocabulary is wider than the single support action that creates or increases it.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking is the general process of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey. Nominate is the
glossary action label for a nominator performing that attachment to a delegate
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

General process vocabulary and nominator-action vocabulary answer different reader questions.

## Distinction from Delegate

A delegate is a validator that can receive nominated stake. Nominate is the action a nominator takes
to attach TAO to that validator hotkey
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

Receiving-role vocabulary and support-action vocabulary sit on opposite sides of the same delegation
relationship.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Nominate is the action of staking TAO with a chosen validator. Effective stake is the combined total
recorded behind that validator hotkey after nomination is included
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

Action vocabulary names support being added; effective stake vocabulary names the measured total
once it is recorded.

## Distinction from Unstaking

Unstaking withdraws delegated stake back toward the coldkey. Nominate attaches TAO support to a
validator hotkey
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

Entry and exit are opposite directions on the same delegation path.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

To nominate is to stake TAO with a validator or delegate in Bittensor. Yuma Consensus is the
separate on-chain mechanism that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares each
tempo ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Nominate** — nominate describes staking TAO with validators in Bittensor delegation.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Nominate should not be read as a subnet role, a completed governance appointment, or proof that
emissions were received. In staking vocabulary it names the delegation action of attaching TAO to a
validator hotkey ([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Staking and delegation documentation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).
