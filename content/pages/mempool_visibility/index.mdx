# Mempool Visibility

Mempool visibility is the exposure of pending transaction details before block inclusion. In
Bittensor, that exposure matters because pending details can create MEV risk.

The [Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) entry places pending and
unconfirmed transactions before block inclusion.
[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) explains Bittensor's encrypted
mempool-protection model for sensitive transaction contents.

## What Visibility Means

The [Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) entry places pending
transactions before block inclusion. Mempool visibility asks what details are exposed during that
waiting period. The concept is about information exposure before block inclusion, not about whether
a transaction is valid.

That distinction keeps the term narrow. A transaction can be pending without every detail being
equally visible, and visibility can matter before the chain has produced the later block record.

## Why Visibility Can Be Risky

MEV risk comes from information available before a transaction is included. The
[MEV glossary entry](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mev-maximal-extractable-value)
connects maximal extractable value to transaction ordering and pending visibility. If sensitive
pending details are exposed, another observer may try to use that timing advantage.

The risk is about the pre-inclusion window. Mempool visibility should not be stretched into a claim
about every later chain record, every submitted transaction, or every transaction-fee outcome.

## MEV Shield Boundary

[MEV Shield](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/mev-shield/) addresses mempool visibility by
encrypting sensitive transaction contents while they are pending and revealing them when the chain
can process them. That makes MEV Shield a visibility protection, not a general guarantee about the
final outcome of a submitted action.

This boundary matters because shielding and validity are separate questions. MEV Shield changes what
can be seen during the pending window; it does not replace the runtime rules that determine whether
a submitted action succeeds.

## Price Protection Boundary

Mempool visibility and price protection are related but separate. Visibility is about what pending
details can be seen before inclusion.
[Price Protection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/price-protection) is a guardrail for
adverse price movement around staking-related actions, and that source points to MEV Shield as an
additional protection.

The terms should not be blended. A source-backed claim should say whether it is about pending
visibility, price movement, fee behavior, or transaction inclusion rather than treating all safety
terms as one mechanism.

## Network Context

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A mempool visibility example should therefore keep its
network context visible because pending-exposure evidence belongs to the environment being observed.

Local examples can explain mechanics, and testnet examples can show shared testing context. Claims
about mainnet risk need mainnet-appropriate source support.

## Evidence Boundary

Mempool visibility supports claims about information exposure before block inclusion. It does not
prove final inclusion, later state, wallet intent, or every reason another observer could act on
pending information.

The [Mempool](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#mempool) and
[Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block) entries help separate the
pre-inclusion window from the later chain record. MEV Shield and price-protection sources explain
specific protections, but each protection has its own scope.
