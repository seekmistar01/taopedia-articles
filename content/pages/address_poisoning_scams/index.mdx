# Address Poisoning Scams

Address poisoning is a wallet scam that relies on lookalike destination addresses. In Bittensor,
[address-poisoning guidance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/address-poisoning-scams) treats
the risk as a wallet-safety problem around routine value-bearing actions.

## Lookalike Address Risk

The scam works by making a wrong destination look familiar enough to trust. Long wallet addresses
are often shortened in interfaces, and a malicious address can resemble a legitimate one at the
visible edges. The
[address-poisoning guide](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/address-poisoning-scams) connects
that visual similarity to mistakes during everyday wallet use.

## Wallet Context

Bittensor wallets are used for actions involving TAO and subnet participation. That makes
destination-address review part of wallet safety rather than a cosmetic interface detail. The
[wallets guide](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets) places wallets around signing, access,
and stake management, while address poisoning targets the destination chosen for a value-bearing
action.

## Recent-History Trap

Address poisoning often abuses convenience. A lookalike address may appear near legitimate wallet
activity, so selecting from recent history can feel safer than it is. The
[Bittensor address-poisoning guide](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/address-poisoning-scams)
frames recent activity and abbreviated display as the conditions that make the scam plausible.

## Destination Verification

The core safety boundary is destination verification before an action is signed. The relevant
question is not whether an address is syntactically valid; it is whether that valid address is the
intended destination. Bittensor's
[address-poisoning guidance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/address-poisoning-scams) points
readers toward checking the destination rather than trusting visual familiarity alone.

## Wallet Address Relationship

Address poisoning targets wallet-address interpretation. A
[wallet address](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#wallet-address) is a
public-key-derived identifier for sending and receiving TAO, and a
[public key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#public-key) is shareable key
material used for account reference. Address poisoning exploits confusion between different valid
identifiers, not a single address becoming invalid.

## Recovery Expectations

Wrong-destination transactions are best understood as prevention-first risks. The
[address-poisoning guide](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/address-poisoning-scams) cautions
that outside organizations may not be able to recover tokens lost through theft, scam, or accidental
key loss. That makes the topic a wallet-safety concept rather than a post-transaction recovery
process.
