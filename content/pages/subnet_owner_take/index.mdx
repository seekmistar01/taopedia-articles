# Subnet Owner Take

Subnet owner take, which the documentation also calls the owner cut, is the share of a subnet's
alpha emissions that goes to the subnet's owner. Documentation states that the ratio of all subnet
alpha emissions given to the subnet owner as stake is 18 percent, applied globally across subnets
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Validator Take

Owner take is the subnet creator's documented emission share, while validator take is a separate
configurable cut a validator or delegate keeps from rewards of those who stake to it
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

Role vocabulary and commission vocabulary answer different questions. Both can be near 18 percent in
documentation, but owner take funds the subnet creator and validator take applies per-validator on
delegated-stake emissions.

- **Subnet owner take** — documented owner share of subnet alpha emissions.
- **Validator take** — configurable commission on delegated-stake rewards.

## Distinction from Crowdloans

Crowdloans name shared subnet ownership, while subnet owner take names the documented owner share of
subnet alpha at tempo distribution on netuid 1 and other subnets
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans)).

Ownership-structure vocabulary and emission-allocation vocabulary answer different questions. When
ownership is shared through crowdloans, the same owner take percentage is divided among contributors
rather than paid to one owner.

- **Crowdloans** — shared subnet ownership with proportional owner-cut splits.
- **Subnet owner take** — documented owner share of subnet alpha at tempo distribution.

## Chain Reads for netuid 1

The documented 18-41-41 split, including the 18 percent owner take, is illustrated on netuid 1
throughout this article. Readers can verify live subnet state for that example with
`btcli subnets metagraph --netuid 1 --network finney` or
[TaoStats netuid 1](https://taostats.io/subnets/1)
([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

That read path keeps live owner-take examples tied to a parseable netuid. Statements about tempo-end
owner stake on netuid 1 should be checked against the metagraph and emission documentation for
netuid 1 rather than inferred from the documented percentage alone.

## Per-Subnet Live Value Boundary

Subnet owner take is documented at 18 percent globally, but the alpha amount credited to an owner on
netuid 1 still depends on that subnet's live emission and tempo distribution state
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

This article uses netuid 1 when illustrating the documented 18-41-41 split. That example helps
readers verify one subnet's metagraph; it is not proof that every subnet's live owner stake matches
the same observation
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

The documented owner share is allocated at tempo distribution; Yuma Consensus subdivides the miner
and validator buckets after that owner allocation
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Subnet owner take** — documented owner share of subnet alpha at tempo distribution.
- **Per-subnet boundary** — live owner stake outcome is chain state on one netuid.

## Distinction from Validator Dividend

Subnet owner take names the documented owner allocation before validator and miner buckets are
subdivided on netuid 1, while validator dividend names the validator-side emission result from
consensus evaluation ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Allocation vocabulary and earned-outcome vocabulary answer different questions. Owner take is a
documented protocol bucket; validator dividend depends on consensus performance across the tempo.

- **Subnet owner take** — documented owner share allocated at tempo distribution.
- **Validator dividend** — validator-side emission earned through consensus evaluation.

## Distinction from Child Hotkeys

On a subnet such as netuid 1, a child hotkey is an optional validation role: a delegate re-delegates
a portion of its stake to that hotkey, which then earns a childkey take on the re-delegated stake.
Subnet owner take is instead the documented protocol share of that subnet's alpha emission allocated
to its owner, given as 18 percent within the documented 18-41-41 split on a subnet such as netuid 1
([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The two are credited through different paths. A childkey take arises when stake is re-delegated to a
hotkey for validation, while the owner take is part of that documented tempo-end emission split
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Child hotkey** — an optional validating key earning a childkey take on re-delegated stake.
- **Subnet owner take** — the documented protocol share of a subnet's emission paid to its owner.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Subnet owner take names the documented owner share carved from a subnet's emission split before
validator and miner buckets are subdivided. Yuma Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts
validator weight submissions into the miner and validator portions of that split each tempo
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Subnet owner take** — documented owner allocation from the tempo emission split.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into miner and validator shares.

- **Tempo distribution** — documented 18-41-41 split runs at tempo end on a subnet.
- **Injection** — alpha enters subnet pools each block before distribution applies.
- **Protocol allocation** — owner share is carved from emissions, not charged as a user fee.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level. It does not report the owner take actually accruing
on any particular subnet or name a specific owner. Those are live chain state and should be checked
for the relevant netuid. The 18 percent figure is the documented value and is the protocol's stated
owner share at the time of writing ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).
