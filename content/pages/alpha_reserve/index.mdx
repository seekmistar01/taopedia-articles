# Alpha Reserve

Alpha reserve is subnet alpha held inside a subnet liquidity pool. In Dynamic TAO, the pool pairs a
TAO reserve with a subnet-specific alpha reserve
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

The term is a location label. It identifies pool-held alpha, not every alpha token associated with
the subnet.

## Pool-Side Meaning

Each subnet pool has two reserve sides: TAO reserve and alpha reserve
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Alpha reserve is the subnet-alpha side of that pair. It belongs to the automated-market-maker pool
context, while other alpha vocabulary can describe alpha outside the pool.

That boundary keeps reserve vocabulary precise. Alpha reserve is not total subnet alpha supply and
not the broader alpha-token category; it is the pool-held alpha side.

## Accounting Role

The subnet economy can be read through TAO reserves, alpha reserves, and alpha outstanding. Alpha
reserve is the pool-held alpha in that grouping
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

It is paired with the subnet's TAO reserve, while alpha outstanding is the alpha outside the reserve
pool.

That makes alpha reserve narrower than total alpha supply. A subnet can have alpha in reserve and
alpha outside reserve at the same time; the reserve figure only names the pool side.

The distinction matters because a subnet's pool is the exchange context for staking TAO into that
subnet's alpha token. Reserve language is therefore about pool accounting rather than a general
supply label
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Emissions and Injection

Alpha reserve injection adds alpha to the reserve side of the pool
([Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

That separates reserve-side alpha from alpha allocated outside the pool. Alpha reserve injection
supports the pool side of subnet tokenomics, while outside-pool alpha belongs to other emission
contexts.

In the emissions overview, the injection stage adds TAO to a subnet's TAO reserve, adds alpha to the
subnet's alpha reserve, and sets aside alpha for alpha outstanding
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

That split keeps the pool's reserve growth separate from the alpha later distributed outside the
pool. Both are subnet alpha context, but they answer different questions.

## Reserve Vocabulary

Alpha reserve and alpha outstanding describe subnet alpha by location. Alpha reserve is pool-held
alpha; alpha outstanding is alpha outside the reserve context
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

This distinction prevents pool-reserve statements from being read as total-alpha statements. The
same subnet asset can appear in reserve-side and outside-reserve contexts, but those contexts are
not interchangeable.

Protocol alpha is related to reserve injection, but it is not the same term. Protocol alpha refers
to alpha gathered by the protocol during reserve-injection activity, while alpha reserve is the
pool-held alpha side itself
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

## Liquidity Position Boundary

Alpha reserve names the pool-held alpha side, not an individual user's liquidity position. User
liquidity positions describe how liquidity is placed around the subnet pool, while alpha reserve is
the reserve quantity inside the paired TAO-and-alpha pool
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That boundary keeps pool accounting separate from participation strategy. A liquidity position can
interact with the pool, but the alpha reserve term still refers to the pool-side alpha amount rather
than to one user's chosen range or position.

## Price Context

Alpha-token price is the ratio of TAO in reserve to alpha in reserve. Alpha reserve is the alpha
side of that relationship
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That does not make alpha reserve the whole economic value of a subnet. It means the reserve-side
alpha is the pool quantity used when explaining the subnet's alpha price.

When a statement is about market-cap-style supply or total stake, alpha outstanding also matters.
When a statement is about reserve ratio or pool liquidity, alpha reserve is more precise.

## Slippage Boundary

Slippage depends on how a trade interacts with the subnet pool's reserve balances. Alpha reserve is
one side of that pool state, while slippage names the price movement that can occur as a trade
changes the reserve ratio
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

That keeps the terms adjacent but distinct. Alpha reserve names the pool-held alpha quantity;
slippage describes execution impact around trades through the pool.

## Alpha Injection Tracks TAO to Preserve Pool Price

[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)
documentation explains that alpha is injected in proportion to TAO injection so subnet pool price
does not change from reserve growth alone. On a subnet such as netuid 1, alpha reserve therefore
grows with paired TAO reserve movement rather than as an unrelated supply label.

That pairing keeps reserve-side accounting coherent. Alpha reserve names the pool-held alpha
quantity; injection rules describe how it changes alongside TAO reserve updates.

## Reserve Ratio Defines Alpha Price

[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) describes a
subnet's [alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha) price as the
ratio of TAO in reserve to alpha in reserve. Alpha reserve is the alpha side of that relationship,
so price statements on netuid 1 should name both reserve sides rather than alpha reserve alone.

When prose discusses slippage or staking conversions, the same paired reserves move together through
pool activity ([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

## Staking Conversions Move the Paired Reserves

[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage) describes staking and
unstaking as conversions that pass through subnet pool reserves. Those actions therefore change the
TAO and alpha reserve balances that define alpha price on a subnet such as netuid 1, even when the
focus is delegation rather than a discretionary swap.

Alpha reserve vocabulary stays on the pool-accounting side; staking vocabulary names participant
actions that can shift the reserves holding that alpha.

## Reserve Injection, Outstanding Alpha, and Pool Price

Dynamic TAO emission splits subnet alpha across reserve injection, alpha outstanding, and the paired
TAO reserve on a subnet such as netuid 1
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

Alpha reserve injection grows the pool-held alpha side in proportion to TAO reserve injection so the
reserve ratio—and therefore alpha price—does not shift from injection alone. Alpha outstanding names
alpha allocated outside that pool context, while protocol alpha names alpha gathered during
injection activity
([Alpha Outstanding](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection),
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

Staking and unstaking then move value through those paired reserves, which is why reserve-ratio and
slippage vocabulary belong in the same pipeline as alpha reserve rather than as separate supply
claims ([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For alpha reserve
on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how reserve-side pool accounting should be read.

In localnet, alpha reserve can be observed on isolated subnet pools such as netuid 1. Localnet
reserve balances reflect that test chain's emission and staking activity rather than production pool
state.

On testnet, alpha reserve on netuid 1 applies in a shared non-production network where TAO and alpha
reserve pairs are separate from mainnet. Testnet reserve ratios describe that environment's chain
history.

On mainnet, alpha reserve on netuid 1 is live pool-held alpha inside production Dynamic TAO pools,
where injection, staking conversions, and slippage all move the paired reserves that define alpha
price ([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. An alpha-reserve example from one environment should not
be read as reserve state on another network.

## Relationship to Arbitrage

An alpha reserve and arbitrage describe a pool's inventory and the trading that acts on its price.
The alpha reserve is the alpha side of a subnet pool, on a subnet such as netuid 1, that, paired
with the TAO reserve, sets the pool's price, while arbitrage is trading that profits from price
differences between that pool and other venues by buying the underpriced side and selling the
overpriced one
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

The two terms answer different questions. The alpha reserve names the pool quantity whose ratio to
the TAO reserve determines the alpha price, while arbitrage names the external trading behavior that
responds to gaps in that price, so reserve vocabulary describes the pool state and arbitrage
describes the trading that pushes a mispriced pool back toward the broader market
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Reader Boundary

Alpha reserve is a tokenomics term, not a separate token, staking guide, or pool strategy. It should
be used when the focus is subnet alpha inside the pool
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

When the focus is the paired TAO side, TAO reserve is more precise. When the focus is alpha outside
the pool, alpha outstanding is more precise. When the focus is the broader tokenomics system,
Dynamic TAO is more precise.
