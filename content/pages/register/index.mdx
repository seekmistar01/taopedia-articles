# Register

Register is the subnet-entry process that gives a Bittensor participant a UID slot inside a subnet.
The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) defines it as
registering with a subnet and purchasing a UID slot by paying a dynamic registration burn in TAO.

## UID Slot Context

Registration matters because subnet participation is organized around UID slots. The
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) entry defines a
UID slot as a position occupied by a subnet miner or subnet validator within a subnet. In practical
reading, register is the step that connects a participant to that subnet position.

[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration) uses miner
registration as a concrete example: a miner must register with a subnet to receive a UID on that
subnet. This article keeps the concept at that level rather than expanding into operational steps.

## The Registration Burn Is Not Recoverable

Registering is not a refundable deposit. Official mining documentation states that once a UID slot
is secured on the main chain, the burned TAO is sunk cost and cannot be recovered
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

That makes the registration burn different from holding-based TAO requirements that remain a
participant's own balance. Register names a one-way spend that purchases the UID slot; the TAO does
not return even if the participant later deregisters or stops participating.

## Burn Price Bounds and Decay

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes the
neuron registration burn as dynamic with explicit parameter bounds. The burn price is bounded by
`MinBurn` and `MaxBurn`, decays over time according to `BurnHalfLife`, and increases on each
successful registration through `BurnIncreaseMult`.

That parameter set explains why registration cost is neither fixed nor arbitrary. The price falls
when registrations are quiet and rises when slots fill, while the bounds keep the burn inside subnet
hyperparameter limits. Static articles should describe that adjustment behavior rather than quoting
a live registration amount
([Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

## Alpha Pool Recycling From Registration Fees

Neuron registration fees are paid in TAO, but the fee also interacts with subnet pool accounting.
Glossary wording states that alpha tokens worth the current swap value of the registration fee are
taken from the subnet's alpha liquidity pool and recycled when a hotkey registers on a subnet.

For readers, register therefore connects subnet entry to Dynamic TAO pool mechanics, not only to a
UID assignment. The burn is a TAO payment, while the recycling step moves equivalent alpha value out
of the subnet pool context described in subnet liquidity documentation
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Register and Burned_Register Extrinsics

The glossary notes that the `register` and `burned_register` extrinsics share the same non-root burn
path for subnet entry. Both routes purchase a UID slot through the dynamic registration burn rather
than describing separate admission economics for ordinary subnets.

Root registration is treated separately in documentation. When reading register at Taopedia level,
the shared non-root burn path keeps miner and validator registration examples aligned on the same
subnet-entry cost model unless the article explicitly discusses Subnet Zero
([Subtensor extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subtensor-api/extrinsics)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For register on a
subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret UID slot assignments and
burn-cost examples.

In localnet, registration can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet UID slots and burn
parameters reflect local chain configuration rather than production subnet state.

On testnet, registration can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet slot readings
on a selected netuid are separate from mainnet metagraph state.

On mainnet, register is the live Subtensor operation that purchases a UID slot through the dynamic
burn on the production network
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A registration example from one environment should not be
read as representing UID state or burn costs in another.

## Deregistration Opposes Registration When Subnets Are Full

Registration adds a participant to a UID slot, while
[deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration) removes an
eligible low-performing participant from a slot when subnet capacity and replacement rules require
it ([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

This relationship does not mean every registration removes another participant. It means register
should be read in the context of finite subnet UID slots and the subnet's participation rules.

## Miner Registration Is the Miner-Specific Entry Case

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes
subnet entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO.
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration) describes
that entry process applied to a subnet miner who will take part in mining work and evaluation.

Register is the general subnet-entry term; miner registration names the miner-specific case of that
process.

## Validator Registration Is the Validator-Specific Entry Case

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes
subnet entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO.
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)
describes that entry process applied to a subnet validator who will take part in evaluation and
validation work.

Register is the general subnet-entry term; validator registration names the validator-specific case
of that process.

## Multiple Mechanisms Require Separate Evaluation After Entry

Register gives a participant a UID slot inside a subnet. The
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)
notes that validators must evaluate miners separately for each mechanism.

Registration still names subnet entry into a UID slot. When that subnet runs more than one incentive
mechanism, evaluation after registration happens under separate mechanism contexts
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets)).

## UID Slot Is the Position Entry Grants

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes the
subnet-entry process that grants a participant a UID slot, while the
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) describes the
participant position inside a subnet that entry provides.

Register names the entry process; a UID slot names the position a miner or validator occupies after
that entry succeeds.

## Netuid Selects the Destination Subnet

Register describes the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot, while a
[netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) selects which subnet that entry
targets.

Register names how a participant enters a subnet; netuid names which subnet market that entry
applies to. Registration language is incomplete without the netuid that selects the destination
subnet context.

## Immunity Period Follows Successful Entry

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes the
subnet-entry process, while the
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)
describes a temporary protection window that begins after a participant registers into a subnet.

Register names the entry event; immunity period names the grace window that can follow that entry
before ordinary low-performance removal rules apply.

## Active UID Tracks Status After Entry

Register describes the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot, while an
[active UID](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#active-uid) describes ongoing
activity status after a participant already holds a slot.

Register names how a participant enters a subnet; active UID names whether that slot currently meets
the activity conditions tracked on the metagraph after entry.

## Child Hotkeys Require Their Own Registration

Register is the subnet-entry process that gives a hotkey a UID slot by paying a dynamic registration
burn in TAO. [Child hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys) configuration
lets a registered parent hotkey allocate a portion of its stake weight to one or more child hotkeys
for validation, where those child hotkeys must already be registered on the netuid on which they
validate.

Register answers how a hotkey obtains a UID slot in a subnet; child-hotkey configuration answers how
a registered parent assigns stake weight to other registered hotkeys to validate on its behalf.

## Coinbase Processes Emissions After Entry

Register grants a UID slot, while
[coinbase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase) is the per-block
emission operation that accumulates pending emissions and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch
boundaries.

Registration places a participant into the UID slot set that later epoch processing can consider. It
does not by itself guarantee emissions; rewards still depend on activity, validator evaluations,
stake, and runtime logic.

## Subnet Entry Boundary

Register should be read as subnet entry, not as a general account creation event. The glossary
defines registration around purchasing a UID slot with a dynamic registration burn, and mining
documentation uses miner registration as a concrete subnet-entry example
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

Registering for one subnet grants a UID slot in that subnet context; it does not imply the same role
or slot across every subnet.

## Reader Boundary

Register should be read as a concept about entering subnet participation. It is not a current cost
quote, a guarantee of future rewards, or a status report on any specific subnet's available
capacity. Exact registration steps, prices, and subnet-specific details belong in current
documentation at the time of observation.

## Relationship to Existential Deposit

Register and existential deposit are related but different terms. Register is the subnet entry
process that places a miner or validator into a UID slot on a netuid, while existential deposit is
the minimum TAO balance threshold for retaining a Bittensor account record
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).

For readers, these sit on different layers of participation. Register names subnet membership entry
after an account can fund the required costs, while existential deposit names the account-balance
boundary that keeps the account represented in the first place
([Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau)).

## Relationship to Validator Permit

Register and the validator permit are separate subnet states a UID can hold. Register is the
subnet-entry action that places a hotkey on a UID slot, while the validator permit is the rights
flag, awarded to the top neurons by stake weight, that records whether that registered UID may
submit counting validator weights, so registration alone does not grant the permit
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

- **Register** — the entry action that places a hotkey on a subnet UID slot.
- **Validator permit** — the separate right to submit counting weights, earned by top stake weight.

## Relationship to Immunity Period

Register is the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot on a netuid. Immunity period is the
block-measured protection window that starts after that entry
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

A participant must register before immunity period timing begins on netuid 1. Register names the
admission step, while immunity period names how long ordinary deregistration protection lasts after
admission ([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#immunityperiod)).

Use register when covering subnet admission; use immunity period when covering post-entry protection
rather than the entry step itself
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration),
[Mining: Immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#immunity-period)).

Entry action and post-entry protection window answer different reader questions on netuid 1
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).
