# Root Proportion

Root proportion is a Bittensor tokenomics measure for a single subnet: the relative weight of TAO
staked to validators through the root subnet, rather than through that subnet's alpha-token side
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion)).

The term is useful when a reader needs to distinguish root-routed TAO stake from the subnet's own
alpha-token context. It belongs to subnet dividend interpretation, not to a general account balance,
staking recommendation, or whole-network ranking.

## Distinction from TAO Weight

Root proportion measures how much of a subnet's dividend context is attributed to TAO staked through
the root path. TAO weight scales how TAO-side stake counts inside stake-weight calculations
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Glossary: TAO Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-weight)).

- **Root proportion** — subnet-level share of dividend context from root-routed TAO.
- **TAO weight** — scaling factor for TAO-side stake in weight calculations.

Dividend-attribution vocabulary and stake-counting vocabulary answer different scope questions
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Dividends

Root proportion names the share of a subnet's dividend context attributed to root-routed TAO.
Dividends name validator-side emission returns after Yuma settlement
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

- **Root proportion** — attribution share for root-routed TAO on a subnet.
- **Dividends** — validator-side emission returns after consensus settlement.

Attribution vocabulary names how root stake weighs on a subnet; dividend vocabulary names payout
flow ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Alpha Staker

Alpha staker and root proportion describe different parts of a subnet's dividend context. Alpha
staker names the role on the subnet-alpha side, while root proportion measures how much of a
subnet's dividend context is attributed to TAO staked through the root path
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

- **Alpha staker** — who holds subnet alpha through staking on a mining subnet.
- **Root proportion** — how much of a subnet's dividend context is attributed to root-routed TAO.

## Distinction from Root Claims

Root claims and root proportion both concern root-subnet staking but measure different things. Root
proportion is the relative weight a validator's stake carries through the root subnet rather than
through a subnet's alpha side, while root claims handle the alpha dividends that root staking earns
— whether they stay as alpha or convert to TAO and restake on root. One measures root-routed stake
weight; the other handles the dividends it produces
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)).

## Distinction from Root Subnet

Root proportion and the root subnet are a measure and the path it measures. Root proportion is the
relative weight of TAO staked to validators through the root subnet, while the root subnet, also
called Subnet Zero, is the special Bittensor subnet that provides that subnet-agnostic staking path
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)).

- **Root proportion** — the relative weight of TAO staked through the root subnet.
- **Root subnet** — Subnet Zero, the subnet-agnostic staking path itself.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Root proportion names the relative weight of TAO staked through the root subnet when reading
dividend and emission context for a selected netuid. It describes root-routed stake mix in
cross-subnet staking views, not the within-subnet settlement that allocates miner and validator
shares
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary inside one subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into incentive and dividend shares from that subnet's accumulated pool
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections tie root proportion to TAO weight and root subnet vocabulary. Root-routed stake
influences how root TAO counts in stake-weight calculations; Yuma still settles validator
submissions on the active netuid after injection and accumulation.

Readers should treat root proportion as a root-staking reading aid, and Yuma as the per-subnet
mechanism that turns weights into payouts
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

- **Root proportion** — relative weight of root-routed TAO in subnet dividend context.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares on one
  netuid.

## Reader Boundary

Root proportion is most useful as interpretation vocabulary. It can explain the root-staked side of
a subnet dividend split, but it should not be used as a standalone account-management rule, yield
forecast, validator-quality signal, or live subnet-status claim
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion)).

When the focus is the parameter that compares TAO stake with alpha stake, TAO weight is the more
direct term. When the focus is emitted rewards and their allocation context, emissions vocabulary is
more direct. Root proportion sits between those ideas as the subnet-level reading of the root side
of dividends ([Glossary: TAO Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-weight),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For a casual reader, the safest interpretation is narrow: root proportion says where a subnet's
dividend split leans, not whether the subnet is good, whether a validator is trustworthy, or whether
an account should take action.

## Distinction from Root Staker

Root proportion and a root staker relate as a metric and a participant. A root staker is a
participant who stakes on the root subnet, while root proportion is the share of a stake or emission
that derives from root (TAO-denominated) stake rather than subnet alpha
([Glossary: Root Proportion](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-proportion),
[Glossary: Root Subnet/Subnet Zero](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Glossary: TAO Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-weight)).

- **Root staker** — a participant who stakes on the root subnet.
- **Root proportion** — the share of stake or emission deriving from root stake.
