InfiniteHash is Bittensor Subnet 89. Its on-chain description is "BTC Pool + Lightning Network," and
the subnet brings real Bitcoin mining onto Bittensor as a shared, decentralized mining pool while
also building Lightning Network infrastructure alongside it. Miners contribute the work that secures
Bitcoin and are rewarded through the subnet rather than through a traditional centralized pool. The
codebase for the subnet is the `backend-developers-ltd/InfiniteHash` repository.

## What InfiniteHash Rewards

Bitcoin mining is the process of dedicating specialized hardware to compute work that helps secure
the Bitcoin network, measured as hashrate. InfiniteHash runs a mining pool that aggregates the
hashrate of its participants, and rewards each miner in proportion to the share of hashrate they
contribute. The Bitcoin the pool earns is converted into the subnet's token and burned, so the
mining work flows back into the subnet's own economy rather than paying out as bitcoin directly.

The subnet pairs that mining pool with Lightning Network participation. Beyond contributing raw
hashrate, miners can also run Lightning nodes and earn additional rewards based on the quality of
that service, which is how the subnet builds out a payment layer next to the mining operation.

## Miner and Validator Roles

Miners take part by pointing Bitcoin mining hardware at the subnet's pool. They embed their
Bittensor hotkey in the worker identifier their hardware reports, which is what links the hashrate
back to them, so no subnet-specific mining software is required beyond that configuration. Miners
who also operate Lightning nodes can earn on top of their hashrate rewards.

Validators measure those contributions. They track how much hashrate each miner's hotkey is
delivering to the pool, assess the quality of the Lightning service where it applies, and translate
that into rewards by setting weights on the network. That weighting is how reward flows through
[[Yuma Consensus]]. At the article level the split is straightforward: miners supply mining
hashpower and Lightning service, while validators measure each contribution and weight it
accordingly.

## On-Chain Identity

Live SN89 subnet data is available on [TaoStats](https://taostats.io/subnets/89), which identifies
the subnet as InfiniteHash. Readers can reproduce the subnet identity fields directly with the
Bittensor CLI command documented in the
[btcli reference](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/btcli/#btcli-subnets-get-identity):
`btcli subnets get-identity --netuid 89 --network finney`. That Finney identity is the source of
truth for the subnet name and the GitHub repository associated with the subnet.
