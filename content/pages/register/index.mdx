# Register

Register is the subnet-entry process that gives a Bittensor participant a UID slot inside a subnet.
The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) defines it as
registering with a subnet and purchasing a UID slot by paying a dynamic registration burn in TAO.

## What It Represents

Register names the on-chain admission step that binds a hotkey to a subnet UID slot after paying the
dynamic registration burn
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's registration path. They should not be read as
representing burn costs or slot availability on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Registration Allowed

Register is the subnet-entry process that purchases a UID slot through the dynamic burn.
Registration allowed is the hyperparameter Boolean `NetworkRegistrationAllowed` that decides whether
new neuron registration is open on a subnet before that burn path can execute
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

- **Register** — the entry action and burn-priced UID assignment.
- **Registration allowed** — whether the subnet currently accepts new registrations.

## Distinction from Max Allowed UIDs

Register adds one participant to a subnet UID set. Max allowed UIDs is the hyperparameter ceiling on
how many UID slots can coexist on that subnet at once
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#maxalloweduids),
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

When slots are below the ceiling, registration fills an open position. When all slots are occupied,
documented deregistration rules can still allow a new registration to displace the lowest-standing
incumbent
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Register** — adds one participant through the burn path.
- **Max allowed UIDs** — coexistence ceiling on the subnet slot set.

## Distinction from Deregistration

Register adds a participant to a subnet UID slot through the dynamic registration burn.
Deregistration removes an eligible low-performing participant when subnet capacity and replacement
rules require it ([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

- **Register** — entry that purchases admission to a UID slot.
- **Deregistration** — removal that clears a slot under documented rules.

## Distinction from Burn Cost

Burn cost names the TAO charge for subnet entry through the dynamic registration burn. Register
names the subnet-entry process that pays that charge to obtain a UID slot
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Register** — the admission process that claims a slot.
- **Burn cost** — the dynamic price mechanism for that admission.

## Distinction from UID Slot

A UID slot is the subnet position a participant occupies after successful entry. Register is the
process that purchases that position through the dynamic burn
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Register** — how admission happens on a selected netuid.
- **UID slot** — the subnet position admission grants.

## Distinction from Subnet Task

Register is the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot after the dynamic registration burn.
Subnet task names the work miners are expected to perform after they hold that slot
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task)).

- **Register** — admission step that places a participant on a netuid.
- **Subnet task** — work miners perform after holding a slot.

## Distinction from Existential Deposit

Register places a miner or validator into a subnet UID slot. Existential deposit is the minimum TAO
balance threshold for retaining a Bittensor account record
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).

- **Register** — subnet membership purchased through the burn.
- **Existential deposit** — account-balance threshold for chain representation.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Register places a hotkey on a subnet UID slot. The validator permit is the separate rights flag,
awarded to top neurons by stake weight, that records whether that registered UID may submit counting
validator weights
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

- **Register** — slot acquisition on a subnet.
- **Validator permit** — authorization to submit counting weights.

## Distinction from Sybil Attack

Register is the subnet-entry process that assigns a UID slot after paying the registration burn.
Sybil attack vocabulary names mass fake-identity creation; rising burn prices limit slot flooding
through cost ([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Sybil Attack](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sybil-attack)).

- **Register** — legitimate subnet-entry process purchasing a UID slot.
- **Sybil attack** — mass creation of fake participant identities.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Register names the subnet-entry process that places a miner or validator into a UID slot on a
selected netuid. It describes how a participant joins the metagraph, not the epoch-boundary
settlement that converts weight submissions into emission shares
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: UID slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary after participants hold
active slots. It reads the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and
converts the result into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections tie register to burn pricing and sybil resistance. Registration grants a slot; Yuma
settles evaluation signals from validators and miners who already occupy slots on that netuid.

Readers should treat register as a prerequisite entry action, and Yuma as the recurring settlement
pass that allocates rewards from consensus on an active subnet.

- **Register** — subnet-entry process that claims a UID slot on one netuid.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from validator weights to emission shares.

## Distinction from Adjustment Interval

Register names the subnet entry process that places a miner or validator into a UID slot. Adjustment
interval names how often the dynamic registration burn is recalibrated against demand
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

- **Register** — the subnet entry action claiming a UID slot.
- **Adjustment interval** — how often the dynamic burn is recalibrated against demand.

## Distinction from Hotkey-Coldkey Pair

A hotkey-coldkey pair names the authentication relationship between operational hotkey and owning
coldkey. Register is the subnet entry process that assigns a UID slot to a hotkey on a netuid
([Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — ownership and authentication relationship between the two keys.
- **Register** — subnet entry that binds a hotkey to a UID slot.

## Distinction from Miner Registration

Register describes subnet entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO.
Miner registration names that entry process applied to a subnet miner who will take part in mining
work and evaluation
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

- **Register** — the general on-chain action that purchases a UID slot on a subnet.
- **Miner registration** — the miner-specific entry path into subnet participation.

## Reader Boundary

Register should be read as a concept about entering subnet participation. It is not a current cost
quote, a guarantee of future rewards, or a status report on any specific subnet's available
capacity. Exact registration steps, prices, and subnet-specific details belong in current
documentation at the time of observation.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

Registering and the immunity period are a step and the window it opens. Registration is the action
of joining a subnet by acquiring a UID slot, typically by paying the recycle/burn cost, while the
immunity period is the grace window granted immediately after registering during which the new UID
cannot be pruned for a low score
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration)).

- **Register** — the action of acquiring a UID slot in a subnet.
- **Immunity period** — the post-registration window that protects the new UID from pruning.

## Distinction from Burning

Register is the subnet-entry action that purchases a UID slot through the dynamic registration burn,
while burning is the permanent supply outcome of that action. Register records slot assignment on
the metagraph after the burn is paid; burning names what happens to the deducted TAO, which is that
those tokens leave spendable circulation
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)).

- **Register** — the subnet-entry action that pays the registration burn to claim a UID slot.
- **Burning** — the permanent removal of TAO from spendable circulation after that burn is deducted.
