# Effective Stake

Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate. It combines the delegate's own
staked TAO with TAO delegated by nominators
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The term is amount vocabulary inside the staking model. It is not a separate asset, a new role, or a
validator-quality score.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking names the process of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey, while effective stake
names the combined total of delegate-owned and nominated TAO behind that hotkey after support is
recorded ([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind that hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation names how nominated TAO becomes attached to a validator hotkey, while effective stake
names the combined total of delegate-owned and nominated TAO behind that hotkey
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation** — nominated TAO attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind that hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

For netuid 1 examples, effective stake and delegate stake name different figures behind the same
validator hotkey. Effective stake is the combined total of delegate-owned and nominator-delegated
TAO behind that hotkey, while delegate stake names only the TAO the delegate stakes personally
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Effective stake** — combined self-stake plus nominated support behind the hotkey.
- **Delegate stake** — validator self-staked TAO only.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Effective stake and validator take describe a delegate's economics as amount and split. Effective
stake is the total TAO associated with a delegate after self-stake and nominator-delegated stake are
counted, while validator take is the commission the delegate keeps from emissions earned on
delegated stake before the remainder flows to nominators
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Effective stake** — combined backing total behind the delegate hotkey.
- **Validator take** — commission on delegated-stake returns at distribution.

## Distinction from Delegated Stake

Effective stake and delegated stake answer different questions about support behind a validator
hotkey. Delegated stake names TAO nominators attach to a delegate, while effective stake names the
combined total after that nominated support is added to the delegate's own stake
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

- **Delegated stake** — nominator-supplied TAO attached to the delegate.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is a participant role that delegates TAO to a validator hotkey. Effective stake is the
combined total recorded behind that hotkey after nomination is included
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Nominator** — participant role that delegates TAO to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined total recorded behind that hotkey after nomination.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Autostaking routes miner emissions into delegated stake automatically. Effective stake is the
combined total behind a delegate hotkey that those routed amounts can increase over time
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Autostaking** — automatic routing of miner emissions into validator stake.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

Routing vocabulary names how emissions become stake; effective-stake vocabulary names the measured
total ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Dividends

Dividends are validator-side emission outcomes after consensus processing. Effective stake remains
the combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey before payout vocabulary enters the discussion
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Dividends** — validator-side emission outcomes after consensus processing.
- **Effective stake** — combined staking total behind the hotkey before payout vocabulary.

## Distinction from Hotkey-Coldkey Pair

Effective stake and the hotkey-coldkey pair describe different parts of the same staking picture.
Effective stake is the aggregate stake a validator's hotkey carries across its own and all delegated
stake, while the hotkey-coldkey pair is the authentication relationship between the operational
hotkey and the ownership coldkey under which that validator acts
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — ownership-and-operation link for one wallet identity.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Effective stake and stake weight are related but different delegate terms. Effective stake names the
total staked support behind a delegate after self-stake and nomination are combined, while stake
weight names how strongly that delegate's signals count when validator inputs are merged in Yuma
Consensus
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Effective stake** — combined staked support behind a delegate hotkey.
- **Stake weight** — computed influence used in subnet consensus processing.

## Distinction from Transferring Stake

Transferring stake and effective stake separate ownership from validator backing totals.
Transferring stake is the operation that changes which coldkey owns a staked position while leaving
it on the same hotkey, whereas effective stake is the combined delegate-owned plus nominated total
associated with the validator that position backs
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview: Transferring stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#transferring-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

Transferring ownership does not move effective stake between validators the way moving stake does.

- **Transferring stake** — changing the coldkey that owns a position on the same validator.
- **Effective stake** — combined backing total behind the validator hotkey.

## Distinction from Alpha Staker

An alpha staker holds subnet alpha by staking to a validator on that subnet. Effective stake is the
validator's combined self-and-delegated total that those positions feed
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

- **Alpha staker** — participant holding subnet alpha through validator staking.
- **Effective stake** — combined backing total behind the validator hotkey.

Holder vocabulary names who supplies alpha; effective-stake vocabulary names the validator total
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Effective stake names the combined staking total behind a validator on one subnet. Yuma Consensus
settles stake-weighted validator submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Effective stake** — combined self-and-delegated stake backing a validator.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from weighted submissions to emission shares.

Amount vocabulary names backing behind a delegate; Yuma vocabulary names reward settlement
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

## Reader Boundary

Effective stake should be read as a delegate-associated staked TAO total, not as stake weight,
validator weights, rank, trust, dividends, or validator-selection advice
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

Use effective stake when the focus is the combined total behind a delegate. Use delegate stake for
the delegate's own stake, delegated stake for nominator-provided stake, and stake weight for subnet
consensus influence
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Stake

Stake and effective stake describe a raw amount and an aggregated influence figure. Stake is TAO
committed to a hotkey, while effective stake is a validator's combined self-and-delegated total that
the protocol uses to weigh its consensus influence, so individual stake contributions sum into the
effective stake of the validator they back
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Stake** — TAO committed to a hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — the validator's combined self-and-delegated total that sets influence.
