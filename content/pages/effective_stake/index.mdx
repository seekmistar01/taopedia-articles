# Effective Stake

Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake) defines it as the
delegate's own staked TAO plus TAO delegated by nominators, making it a combined staking total
rather than only a measure of self-stake.

## Delegate Context

Effective stake is read from the delegate side of the staking relationship. The
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) describes a
delegate as a subnet validator that receives stake from nominators. Effective stake names the total
staked TAO associated with that delegate after both sources of stake are considered.

That framing keeps the term narrow. Effective stake is not a separate asset type and not a new role.
It is a way to describe how much staked TAO stands behind a delegate in the staking model.

## Combined Stake Total

The [Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)
defines delegate stake as the TAO staked by the delegate itself. Effective stake includes that
self-staked amount and adds TAO delegated by nominators, as described in the
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake).

This distinction helps readers avoid mixing up the component and the total. Delegate stake is the
delegate's own contribution. Effective stake is the combined total associated with the delegate
after delegated TAO is included.

## Effective Stake and Stake Weight

Effective stake and stake weight answer different questions. Effective stake describes the staked
TAO amount associated with a delegate, while
[Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) describes the
computed stake value used to determine validator consensus power and emissions in a subnet.

The boundary matters because a staking total is not the same as a consensus influence measure.
Effective stake helps explain the staking base behind a delegate. Stake weight explains how staking
context is translated into influence inside subnet consensus.

## Staking Flow

The [Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation) documentation
places delegates, nominators, and delegated stake inside the broader staking model. Effective stake
belongs to that model as a summary of the total TAO associated with a delegate after nomination is
accounted for.

It is therefore a descriptive staking term. It can help explain why a delegate has more staking
backing than its own delegate stake alone, without turning the article into instructions for staking
actions or validator selection.

## Reading Boundaries

Effective stake is the right term when the focus is the combined staked TAO behind a delegate. If
the question is only the delegate's self-staked amount, delegate stake is more precise. If the
question is consensus influence inside a subnet, stake weight is more precise. The
[Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake), and
[Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) glossary entries
separate those terms.

These boundaries keep effective stake useful as a reference term: it describes a combined staking
total, not a recommendation, a validator quality score, or a complete explanation of reward
outcomes.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For effective
stake, that sequence changes how readers should interpret examples of delegate stake totals and
nominator delegation amounts.

In localnet, effective stake can be observed in an isolated environment. Localnet delegate stake
totals reflect local chain settings and do not represent production validator stake state.

On testnet, effective stake can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet delegate
stake totals are separate from mainnet validator stake state.

On mainnet, effective stake is the live total of TAO staked with a delegate, combining the
delegate's own stake and all nominator delegations, in production Bittensor operations. The
[Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation) documentation
describes the stake mechanics that apply on the production network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. An effective stake figure from one environment should not
be read as representing delegate stake totals in another environment.

## Weighting Boundary

Effective stake should be read as a stake value after protocol weighting, not simply as the nominal
amount a participant supplied. Staking documentation explains the base delegation context, while
subnet and emission references describe how stake contributes to validator and subnet outcomes
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

This boundary helps readers distinguish wallet balances, raw stake, and stake as interpreted by
protocol mechanisms. The exact effective value belongs to the selected network and calculation
context rather than to a standalone balance statement.
