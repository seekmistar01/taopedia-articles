# Validator Registration

Validator registration is how a participant enters a Bittensor subnet as a validator. The
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)
reference describes registration as registering a hotkey with a subnet to receive a UID on that
subnet so the validator can take part in evaluation and validation work there.

## Why Registration Matters

Subnets organize participants around UID slots. The
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) defines a slot as
the position a subnet miner or validator occupies inside a subnet. Without registration, a hotkey is
not part of that subnet's validator set.

For a reader, validator registration is the entry point into subnet validation vocabulary. It does
not describe validator quality, delegated stake totals, or which subnet to join.

## Validator Role After Registration

A
[subnet validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-or-subnet-validator)
evaluates miner work inside a subnet and participates in the subnet's consensus and emissions
picture. Validating documentation treats registration as the step that connects a validator hotkey
to that role through a subnet UID slot
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

This article does not describe validator setup, weight submission commands, or live permit tables.
Those details belong in validating references and live subnet data.

## Registration Cost Context

Validator registration uses the same broad register vocabulary as other subnet participants. The
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes subnet
entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO. Validating documentation
points readers to current registration cost references rather than fixed values
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

Validator registration should not be read as a price quote. It names the paid entry step that
connects a validator hotkey to a subnet UID slot.

## Permit and Lifecycle Context

Registration is only the entry step. A validator also needs validation-permit context to perform
validation work inside a subnet. The
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)
marks whether a participant has validation rights, and
[Validator Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)
describes how a full subnet can later remove a low-performing validator when registration pressure
requires replacement.

For readers, registration starts validator participation; permit status and deregistration describe
later lifecycle rules around that slot.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For validator
registration on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret
evidence about UID slot assignments and validator permit context.

In localnet, validator registration can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet UID slots and
validator permit outcomes reflect local chain configuration and do not represent production subnet
state.

On testnet, validator registration can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet
registration cost and permit outcomes are separate from mainnet subnet state.

On mainnet, validator registration is a live action that connects a validator hotkey to a real
subnet UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A validator registration example from one environment
should not be read as representing UID slot availability or registration costs in another
environment.

## Register Names the Shared Entry Vocabulary

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes
subnet entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO.
[Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)
describes that entry process applied to a subnet validator who will take part in evaluation and
validation work.

Register is the general subnet-entry term; validator registration names the validator-specific case
of that process.

## UID Slot Is the Position Registration Grants

The [Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes the
subnet-entry process, while the
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot) describes the
participant position inside a subnet that entry grants.

Validator registration names the validator-specific entry process; a UID slot names the position a
validator occupies after that entry succeeds.

## Netuid Selects the Target Subnet

Register describes the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot, while a
[netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) selects which subnet that entry
targets
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

Netuid names which subnet market the validator is entering; validator registration names the
validator-specific entry process inside that subnet context.

## Validator Permit Follows Entry

Validator registration names the subnet-entry process that grants a UID slot, while the
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)
describes whether a participant has validation rights inside a subnet after entry.

Registration places a hotkey in the subnet; validator permit names whether that participant can
perform validation work under the validator-permit rules.

## Validator Deregistration Versus Subnet Deregistration

Validator registration is the process that gives a subnet validator a UID slot after registering a
hotkey with a subnet.
[Subnet deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration) is the
mechanism that removes an entire non-immune subnet from the network when the subnet limit is reached
and a new subnet registration attempts to enter, selecting the subnet with the lowest EMA of alpha
price for removal.

Validator registration concerns entry into a continuing subnet. Validator deregistration is the
participant-level process where a low-performing validator slot is vacated while the subnet itself
continues. Subnet deregistration removes the entire subnet: every validator registration within it
is lost because the subnet context no longer exists.

When a subnet is deregistered, individual validators are not removed one by one — the whole subnet
disappears and every UID assignment within it ends simultaneously.

## Child Hotkeys Delegate Validation From a Parent Slot

Validator registration is how a participant enters a subnet as a validator by registering a hotkey
to receive a UID
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).
A [child hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys) is a separate key that a
parent hotkey authorizes to validate on its behalf by re-delegating a portion of the parent's stake
to it, without transferring ownership of that stake.

Registration answers how a hotkey enters a subnet's validator set; a child hotkey answers which key
a parent authorizes to carry out validation on its behalf. Registration gives a hotkey a validator
UID; a child hotkey validates using a parent's re-delegated stake weight.

## Coinbase Drives Emissions After Entry

Validator registration gives a subnet validator a UID slot after registering a hotkey with a subnet.
[Coinbase](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase) describes the
per-block protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission, accumulates pending emissions, and triggers
Yuma Consensus rounds at epoch boundaries.

Registration lets a validator enter a subnet and begin participating; coinbase advances chain-level
emissions and triggers the epoch calculations on a recurring basis. Registration by itself is only
the entry step and does not determine rewards. What a validator actually earns is determined by
subnet and Yuma Consensus mechanics — stake, weights, and related eligibility — rather than by
registration or by coinbase alone.

## Reader Boundary

Validator registration should not be read as a recommendation to join any particular subnet, as a
command tutorial, or as a guarantee of emissions. It is the concept term for how a validator enters
a subnet UID slot and begins participating in subnet validation vocabulary
([Validating: Validator registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-registration)).

Current chain values, wallet state, subnet-specific outcomes, or production measurements belong to
the relevant network, block, account, or tool output at the time of observation.
