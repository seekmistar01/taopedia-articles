# Subnet Stake Burn

Subnet stake burn is a Bittensor mechanism for subnet owners to permanently remove alpha from
circulation. The official [Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)
page describes the mechanism's behavior and its effect on a subnet.

## What Stake Burn Does

The official page says stake burn acts as a voluntary subnet buyback. In that mechanism, TAO is sent
into the subnet's TAO reserve, while alpha is taken out of circulation. The important reader-facing
point is the reserve change: the subnet reserve receives TAO and the corresponding alpha is removed
from circulating supply.

Reference: [Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)

## Reserve Flow

The source describes a sequence where TAO is added to the subnet's reserve in its AMM pool. The pool
then calculates an alpha amount, removes that alpha from its alpha reserve, and the alpha is burned
within the same chain action. As a result, circulating alpha decreases and the subnet pool reserves
adjust.

Reference: [Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)

## Relationship to Recycling and Burning

Subnet stake burn is one concrete use of the broader burning vocabulary. Recycling and burning
describe supply-accounting outcomes for removed TAO and alpha, while subnet stake burn names the
subnet-owner mechanism that adds TAO to a subnet reserve and burns the corresponding alpha. The
[Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
glossary entry distinguishes re-emittable recycled tokens from burned tokens, and the
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn) documentation describes the
burn flow for this subnet action.

For readers, recycling and burning answer how removed tokens are accounted for, while subnet stake
burn answers which subnet mechanism performs a specific alpha burn.

References:
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)

## Tempo Limit

The same source states that stake burn is rate-limited per subnet. A subnet owner can use it once
per tempo, described on the official page as once every 360 blocks. A second attempt during the same
tempo is rejected with the documented rate-limit error.

Reference: [Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)

## Careful Interpretation

The official page also includes execution examples and troubleshooting details. This Taopedia
article does not reproduce those instructions. Readers should treat stake burn as a protocol
mechanism whose exact effect depends on the current subnet pool state and the official mechanism
documentation.

Reference: [Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Subnet stake burn adjusts a subnet pool by adding TAO to the reserve and removing alpha from
circulation. The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)
notes that each subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, stake burn still names a subnet-owner pool action. It belongs to shared pool tokenomics
rather than to per-mechanism miner evaluation.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Relationship to Alpha Outstanding

Subnet stake burn and alpha outstanding name different aspects of alpha token supply in Bittensor.
Alpha outstanding describes the total amount of alpha in circulation for a given subnet, while
subnet stake burn names the mechanism that permanently reduces that supply. The
[Glossary: Alpha Outstanding](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-outstanding)
describes the total alpha in supply for a subnet, and the
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn) documentation describes how
the burn mechanism removes alpha from circulation.

For readers, alpha outstanding is the supply figure; subnet stake burn is the action that lowers it.
A stake burn event directly decreases the alpha outstanding for that subnet, making the two concepts
complementary: one names the current quantity, the other names a mechanism that changes it.

References:
[Glossary: Alpha Outstanding](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-outstanding),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)
