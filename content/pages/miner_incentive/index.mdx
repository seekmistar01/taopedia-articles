# Miner Incentive

A miner incentive is the emission share received by Bittensor miners for their work in a subnet. It
is the miner-facing side of subnet emission distribution
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

The term is reward vocabulary. Mining is the process of performing subnet tasks; miner incentive is
the emission-side term for what miners receive when validators evaluate their work positively.

## Distinction from Incentives

Incentives is the glossary term for miner-facing emission outcomes. Miner incentive names the same
miner-side credited share in article vocabulary
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Miner Incentive](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#miner-incentive)).

- **Incentives** — glossary term for miner-facing emission outcomes.
- **Miner incentive** — article label for the miner-side credited share.

Glossary vocabulary and article vocabulary name the same miner-side outcome at different scopes
([Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

## Distinction from Rank

Rank is the consensus standing after clipped weights are merged. Miner incentive is the emission
share credited in proportion to that standing
([Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Rank** — ordering after Yuma aggregation.
- **Miner incentive** — credited miner reward share that follows.

## Distinction from Trust

Trust compares pre-rank support to final rank after clipping. Miner incentive is credited after rank
is normalized in the same Yuma pass
([Glossary: Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Trust** — pre-rank support compared with final rank after clipping.
- **Miner incentive** — credited miner reward share after normalization.

Clipping-impact vocabulary and credited-outcome vocabulary answer different post-consensus layers
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Emission Split

For netuid 1 examples, emission split names the documented tempo partition in official emission
documentation. Miner incentive names outcomes inside the miner bucket after Yuma divides that share
by rank ([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution),
[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)).

The split sets the documented miner bucket size on netuid 1; miner incentive names credited results
inside it.

## Distinction from Validator Emissions

Yuma resolves weight signals into separate miner and validator branches. Miner incentive belongs to
the miner-emission branch; validator emissions belong to the validator-side branch
([Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

- **Miner incentive** — miner-facing credited results.
- **Validator emissions** — validator-facing credited results from the parallel branch.

Parallel branch outcomes from the same Yuma pass answer different role-facing questions
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

## Distinction from Dividends

Miner incentive is the emission share miners receive for evaluated work in a subnet. Dividends are
the validator-side emission share tied to consensus role and bonded support
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)).

- **Miner incentive** — the emission share paid to miners for their work.
- **Dividends** — the validator-side emission share paid for evaluation and bonded support.

Miner-side outcome vocabulary and validator-side outcome vocabulary answer different role branches
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Weight Setting

Miner incentive is the per-miner emission share after consensus. Weight setting is the validator
`set_weights` action that records scores for the epoch
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Weight Setting](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-setting)).

- **Miner incentive** — miner-side emission share after consensus.
- **Weight setting** — validator action recording miner scores.

Outcome vocabulary and submission-action vocabulary answer different stages of the same flow
([Glossary: Weight Matrix](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix)).

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Miner incentive is the miner-side emission outcome; stake weight is the validator-side influence
measure in subnet consensus arithmetic
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

- **Miner incentive** — per-miner emission share after aggregation.
- **Stake weight** — validator consensus influence from staked support.

Outcome vocabulary and influence vocabulary answer different sides of the same flow
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Miner incentive names the miner-side emission share credited after Yuma runs on a netuid. Yuma
Consensus settles validator weight submissions into miner incentives and validator dividends each
tempo ([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Miner incentive** — miner-side emission share after the pass.
- **Yuma Consensus** — settlement aggregating weights into emission shares.

Outcome vocabulary and settlement vocabulary answer different tempo layers
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Validator Trust

Miner incentive is the miner-side emission outcome; validator trust measures how much of a
validator's weight contribution survived consensus clipping
([Glossary: Validator Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-trust),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Miner incentive** — miner-side emission share after consensus.
- **Validator trust** — validator-side clipped-weight alignment measure.

Miner outcome vocabulary and validator standing vocabulary answer different consensus steps
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Weight Vector

Miner incentive is the miner-side emission outcome; a weight vector is the scored payload a
validator submits through `set_weights`
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

- **Miner incentive** — miner-side emission share after consensus.
- **Weight vector** — validator-submitted scored targets for the epoch.

Outcome vocabulary and submission vocabulary answer different stages of the same flow
([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

## Reader Boundary

Miner incentive is concept vocabulary for the miner's share of subnet emission. The term should not
be read as a mining profitability estimate, a subnet ranking, or an endorsement of a specific subnet
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

Actual incentive amounts depend on subnet context, emission configuration, validator evaluations,
and the selected environment. Mainnet, testnet, and localnet are separate contexts
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Miner incentive names the miner-facing emission outcome after Yuma Consensus resolves ranks. It does
not replace rank, weight, or trust vocabulary used inside the consensus process
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Validator Dividend

Miner incentive and validator dividend are the two reward streams of subnet emission. Miner
incentive is the share paid to miners according to the value of their task performance, while the
validator dividend is the share paid to validators and their stakers for evaluating miners and
setting weights
([Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Miner incentive** — the emission share paid to miners for task performance.
- **Validator dividend** — the emission share paid to validators and their stakers.
