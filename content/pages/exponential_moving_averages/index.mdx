# Exponential Moving Averages

An exponential moving average, or EMA, is a smoothing method that blends a new measurement with a
running value from earlier steps. In Bittensor, EMAs help dampen brief fluctuations before they
affect subnet-level emissions, validator-miner bonds, and related consensus incentives.

References: [Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## What EMA smoothing means

EMA smoothing gives newer measurements more influence while still retaining part of the earlier
running value. A higher smoothing factor reacts faster to new information, while a lower smoothing
factor changes more slowly. This makes EMAs useful when a protocol needs to respond to real trends
without overreacting to brief noise.

Reference: [Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema)

## Subnet flow smoothing

Bittensor's emission process uses EMA-smoothed net TAO flow when determining how emissions are
allocated across subnets. This makes subnet allocation less sensitive to brief staking or unstaking
swings. The broader effect is that sustained flow matters more than a brief movement that quickly
reverses.

References: [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema),
[[dynamic_tao|Dynamic TAO]]

## Validator-miner bond smoothing

EMAs also appear inside [[yuma_consensus|Yuma Consensus]] through validator-miner bond smoothing. A
validator's bond to a miner should not swing entirely from one evaluation step. Bond smoothing lets
relationships evolve over time, which helps reward validators that identify useful miner work while
reducing the effect of sudden, noisy changes.

References: [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema)

## Liquid alpha context

Liquid alpha, also called consensus-based weights, makes the bond-smoothing factor depend on how a
validator's weights align with consensus. This connects EMA behavior to validator incentives:
alignment can make the bond response more direct, while weaker alignment keeps the response more
conservative.

References:
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Why smoothing matters

Without smoothing, a noisy signal can create sharp incentive changes before the network has enough
evidence that the change is meaningful. With smoothing, Bittensor can make incentives responsive
without making them brittle. This is important for both the market-level flow signals described by
[[dynamic_tao|Dynamic TAO]] and the subnet-level evaluations described by
[[mining_and_validating|mining and validating]].

References: [Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## How to read EMA claims

An EMA claim should identify the smoothed quantity and the behavior affected by that smoothing. In
Bittensor, the same general technique can apply to subnet flow, protocol-cost tracking, or
validator-miner bonds, but those uses are not interchangeable. The right interpretation depends on
which process is being smoothed and which incentive path uses the result.

References: [Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Exponential moving averages are usually discussed for one incentive mechanism at a time, but subnets
can also run multiple mechanisms in parallel. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that each mechanism operates with its own bond pool for Yuma Consensus calculations.

For readers, EMA smoothing still helps dampen noisy signals inside a subnet, but the mechanism
context matters. Bond smoothing in one mechanism should be read separately when a subnet uses more
than one evaluation system.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Relationship to Subnet Weights

Exponential moving averages and subnet weights address related but different parts of emission
signal processing. Subnet weights are the allocation measure built from net TAO flow behavior, while
exponential moving averages smooth that flow signal so brief staking swings do not dominate
cross-subnet allocation. The
[Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights)
describes relative emission importance through net TAO flows, and the
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes the smoothing method applied to noisy measurements over time.

For readers, subnet weights name the allocation outcome, while exponential moving averages name the
smoothing technique applied to the flow signal behind that outcome.

References:
[Glossary: Subnet Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-weights),
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)

## Relationship to Validator-Miner Bonds

Exponential moving averages and validator-miner bonds address related but different parts of bond
processing inside Yuma Consensus. Validator-miner bonds describe the smoothed relationship between a
validator and miners over time, while exponential moving averages provide the smoothing method that
keeps bond values from jumping entirely on one evaluation step. The
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)
describes the bond relationship, and the
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes EMA smoothing in bond updates.

For readers, validator-miner bonds name the smoothed relationship, while exponential moving averages
name the technique that helps that relationship evolve gradually across epochs.

References:
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)

## Relationship to Liquid Alpha

Exponential moving averages and liquid alpha address related but different parts of bond smoothing.
Exponential moving averages smooth validator-miner bonds using an alpha smoothing factor, while
liquid alpha varies that factor per validator-miner pair based on consensus alignment. The
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes the general smoothing technique, and the
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)
documentation describes liquid alpha as adjusting the smoothing response per pair.

For readers, exponential moving averages name the general smoothing method, while liquid alpha names
a per-pair adjustment to the smoothing factor inside that method.

References:
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema),
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights)

## Relationship to Yuma Consensus

Exponential moving averages and Yuma Consensus address related but different parts of Bittensor
subnet incentive flow. Yuma Consensus aggregates validator weight signals into miner incentives and
validator dividends, while exponential moving averages smooth validator-miner bond updates inside
that consensus path. The
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes smoothing in bond evolution, and
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) describes clipping, bonding,
and emission calculation.

For readers, Yuma Consensus names the aggregation process, while exponential moving averages name
one smoothing technique used when bond values evolve inside that process.

References:
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Relationship to Consensus Score

Exponential moving averages and a consensus score refer to related but different parts of Yuma
Consensus. A consensus score is the stake-weighted median used to filter outlier validator weight
submissions, while exponential moving averages smooth validator-miner bond updates inside the
consensus path after those inputs are processed. The
[Glossary: Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score)
describes the agreement threshold applied to validator inputs, and the
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes the smoothing method applied to bond evolution over time.

For readers, a consensus score names input filtering inside consensus, while exponential moving
averages name the smoothing technique used when bond values evolve inside that process.

References:
[Glossary: Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)

## Relationship to Validator Weights

Exponential moving averages and validator weights address related but different parts of subnet
evaluation structure. Validator weights are the evaluation signals validators submit for miners,
while exponential moving averages smooth validator-miner bond updates that evolve from those
evaluations inside Yuma Consensus. The
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights)
describes the per-validator evaluation inputs, and the
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes EMA smoothing in bond updates.

For readers, validator weights name the evaluation submissions, while exponential moving averages
name the smoothing technique applied when bond relationships evolve from those submissions.

References:
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights),
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)

## Relationship to Commit Reveal

Exponential moving averages and Commit Reveal address related but different parts of
validator-weight processing. Commit Reveal can conceal validator weight evaluations until a later
reveal time before consensus uses them publicly, while exponential moving averages smooth
validator-miner bond updates inside the consensus path after those evaluations are processed. The
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) documentation describes
concealed weight submission and delayed reveal, and the
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes smoothing in bond evolution.

For readers, Commit Reveal names a visibility and timing layer around weight submissions, while
exponential moving averages name the smoothing technique used when bond values evolve inside
consensus.

References: [Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal),
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)

## Relationship to Rank

Exponential moving averages and rank address related but different parts of Yuma Consensus
vocabulary. Rank names the final aggregate judgment produced for a miner after consensus filtering
and aggregation, while exponential moving averages smooth validator-miner bond updates inside the
consensus path that leads to those outcomes. The
[Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank) describes the final
aggregate judgment from validator ratings, and the
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
describes smoothing in bond evolution.

For readers, rank names a miner-side consensus outcome, while exponential moving averages name the
smoothing technique used when bond relationships evolve inside the same process.

References: [Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank),
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)
