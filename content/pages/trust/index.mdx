# Trust

Trust is a Bittensor consensus metric that describes how much of a miner's original validator
support remains after consensus clipping. The official glossary defines trust as the relationship
between final rank and pre-rank in Yuma Consensus.

References: [Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## What Trust Represents

Validators evaluate miner work by submitting weights. Before those signals become a consensus
result, outlier assignments can be clipped. Trust describes the effect of that clipping on the
evaluated miner: high trust means the original support was largely preserved, while lower trust
means more of that support was reduced by the consensus process.

References: [Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score)

## Relationship to Rank

Rank and trust are closely related but not the same concept. Rank is the consensus-adjusted
evaluation result. Trust describes how much the path from pre-rank to final rank changed during
consensus filtering. In practical reading, rank says where the miner landed after aggregation; trust
says how much clipping affected that result.

References: [Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank),
[Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust)

## Relationship to Validator Trust

Trust is read from the evaluated miner's side of the process. Validator trust is read from the
validator's side, describing how much of a validator's submitted weight signal remains after
filtering. Keeping the two separate avoids treating trust as a general reputation score for every
participant.

References: [Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Validator Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-trust)

## Relationship to Consensus Score

Consensus score is the threshold-like agreement signal used during clipping. Trust is an outcome
metric after that filtering has affected an evaluated miner's support. The two concepts are related
because trust depends on how much validator support survives the consensus threshold.

References: [Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust)

## Careful Interpretation

Trust should be read inside a subnet's evaluation context. It is not a universal quality label, not
an operating instruction, and not a promise about future results. The Taopedia-level meaning is the
role it plays in explaining how consensus clipping changes miner-side evaluation.

References: [Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)
