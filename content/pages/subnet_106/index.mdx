VoidAI is Bittensor Subnet 106. Its on-chain description is "Multi-chain liquidity protocol enabling
interoperability for Bittensor by leveraging Chainlink CCIP," and the project site is
[voidai.com](https://voidai.com). The subnet's role is to connect Bittensor's token economy to
outside decentralized-finance markets: it moves Bittensor tokens onto another chain so they can be
traded there, and rewards miners for supplying the liquidity that makes those markets work. The
codebase for the subnet is the `v0idai/SN106` repository.

## What VoidAI Rewards

For a token to trade smoothly on a decentralized exchange, someone has to supply liquidity — pools
of the two tokens that buyers and sellers trade against. VoidAI brings Bittensor's alpha tokens and
TAO to another chain in wrapped form, using Chainlink's cross-chain protocol to move them, and then
rewards miners for providing liquidity to the resulting trading pairs.

Rewards favor liquidity that is actually useful. A miner earns more when their liquidity is
concentrated near the current trading price, where it can absorb real trades, and less when it sits
far from the market or is spread too thin to matter. The amount supplied and the performance of the
chosen Bittensor token also factor in. The aim is to build deep, well-placed markets for Bittensor
tokens rather than simply locking up funds. Liquidity is currently provided on Solana, with the
protocol designed to extend to additional chains.

## Miner and Validator Roles

Miners bridge their Bittensor tokens to the connected chain, supply them as concentrated liquidity
to a trading pair, and lock that position to the subnet so it can be tracked and rewarded. A
registered hotkey on netuid 106 is what ties a miner's positions back to them.

Validators read those positions directly from the connected chains rather than relying on miners to
report them. They assess each position's quality — how concentrated it is, how close it is to the
current market price, and whether it is actively in range — and from that set weights on the
network, which is how reward flows through [[Yuma Consensus]]. At the article level the split is
straightforward: miners supply the liquidity, while validators measure how useful each position is
and weight each miner accordingly.

## On-Chain Identity

Live SN106 subnet data is available on [TaoStats](https://taostats.io/subnets/106), which identifies
the subnet as VoidAI. Readers can reproduce the subnet identity fields directly with the Bittensor
CLI command documented in the
[btcli reference](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/btcli/#btcli-subnets-get-identity):
`btcli subnets get-identity --netuid 106 --network finney`. That Finney identity is the source of
truth for the subnet name and the GitHub repository associated with the subnet.
