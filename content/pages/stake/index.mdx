# Stake

Stake is the amount of currency tokens associated with a validator position in a Bittensor subnet
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).
It is amount vocabulary, not the action vocabulary of staking and not the downstream weighting
vocabulary of stake weight.

The term should stay attached to its validator and subnet context. A stake amount by itself is not a
wallet balance, validator ranking, or network-wide performance signal.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking names the process of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey, while stake names the
measured token amount associated with that supported validator position after support is recorded
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Stake** — measured validator-associated amount after support is recorded.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation names how nominated TAO becomes attached to a validator hotkey, while stake names the
measured token amount behind that position after support exists
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

- **Delegation** — support relationship attaching nominated TAO to a hotkey.
- **Stake** — combined support amount recorded behind that hotkey.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Stake names the validator-associated amount in a subnet context, while effective stake summarizes
the combined total of delegate-owned and nominator-delegated TAO behind a delegate hotkey
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Stake** — validator-associated amount in the discussed subnet context.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Stake names the validator-associated token amount, while stake weight is the interpreted measure
used for validator consensus power and emissions inside a subnet
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Stake** — validator-associated token amount behind the position.
- **Stake weight** — consensus-power interpretation of that amount.

## Distinction from Alpha Stake

After dynamic TAO, stake on a subnet is recorded as subnet-specific alpha behind a validator hotkey
rather than as one pooled balance across every network
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

- **Stake** — amount vocabulary for support recorded behind a validator position.
- **Alpha stake** — subnet-local alpha recorded after staking into that subnet's pool.

## Distinction from Alpha Outstanding

Stake names tokens delegated to a validator in a subnet, while alpha outstanding is a location label
for subnet alpha that sits outside the reserve pool rather than a staking procedure
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Emission: Alpha outstanding injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection)).

- **Stake** — delegated support recorded behind a validator position.
- **Alpha outstanding** — location label for subnet alpha outside the reserve pool.

## Distinction from Protocol Alpha

Stake names tokens delegated to a validator in a subnet, while protocol alpha names alpha attributed
to the protocol itself during reserve-injection activity
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

- **Stake** — delegated support behind a validator position.
- **Protocol alpha** — protocol-held alpha during reserve-injection activity.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Stake names the validator-associated amount behind a position, while the validator permit names the
binary right to submit counting validator weights that top-stake neurons earn
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Stake** — validator-associated token amount behind the position.
- **Validator permit** — right to submit counting validator weights.

## Distinction from Subnet Stake Burn

Stake and subnet stake burn name different actions on the subnet token economy. Stake is the
delegator-supplied amount delegated to a validator on a subnet, while subnet stake burn is the
subnet-owner action that adds TAO to a subnet's TAO reserve and burns the corresponding alpha
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets),
[Subnet Stake Burn](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/stake-burn)).

- **Stake** — participant-side amount delegated to a validator.
- **Subnet stake burn** — subnet-owner reserve action that changes pool composition.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Stake names TAO or alpha committed behind a validator position. That support vocabulary describes
committed backing, not the tempo pass that converts validator weight submissions into emission
shares ([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus reads submitted weights, applies clipping and bonding, and then credits miner
incentives and validator dividends on the selected netuid
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Stake weight scales how strongly a validator's signals count inside aggregation. The stake move
itself is not the settlement step that produces credited shares
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

Readers should keep backing vocabulary and settlement vocabulary separate. Stake names what is
committed; Yuma names how evaluation signals become emission outcomes each tempo
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Stake** — committed TAO or alpha behind a participant position.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement turning validator weights into emission shares.

## Distinction from Subnet

Stake is local to subnet state. A subnet is the broader market context where that stake is recorded
behind validator positions
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Stake** — validator-associated token amount in a subnet context.
- **Subnet** — specialized market where that support is recorded and evaluated.

Amount vocabulary names support behind a position; subnet vocabulary names the market context
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

## Reader Boundary

Stake is amount vocabulary for validator-associated support. It is not wallet custody, a validator
ranking, or stake weight itself
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

A stake amount should stay attached to its validator and subnet context.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

Stake and delegate stake relate as the general concept and a specific portion of it. Stake is TAO
committed to a hotkey to back its consensus weight, while delegate stake is the portion contributed
by nominators who stake to a validator's hotkey they do not own, as opposed to the validator's own
self-stake ([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Stake** — TAO committed to a hotkey to back its weight.
- **Delegate stake** — the portion staked by nominators to a hotkey they do not own.
