# Existential Deposit

Existential deposit is the minimum TAO balance threshold for a Bittensor account to remain
represented
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).
The term belongs to account-balance vocabulary. It is separate from staking amounts, fee amounts,
and subnet scoring terms.

## Balance Threshold

The concept is a retained-balance threshold, not a reward category. It names the minimum amount of
TAO required for an account to exist
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).

That makes the term useful when a reader needs account-retention context. It does not describe miner
evaluation, validator influence, or a subnet's scoring model.

## TAO Context

The threshold is expressed in TAO. The TAO glossary gives the token context, while existential
deposit narrows the question to the minimum account-balance boundary
([Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau),
[Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).

The two terms are related but not interchangeable. TAO names the network token; existential deposit
names a threshold measured in that token.

## Dust-Account Boundary

Existential deposit helps separate meaningful account records from dust-account buildup. Balances
below the threshold are connected with account removal to conserve resources
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).

That resource boundary is the main reason the concept matters. It explains why a very small balance
can be treated differently from an account balance above the threshold.

## Wallet-Balance Boundary

Existential deposit concerns the account's retained TAO balance, not the wallet key material itself.
Wallet documentation describes coldkeys and hotkeys as key roles, while fee documentation describes
TAO costs attached to state-changing actions
([Wallets and Keys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets),
[Transaction Fees](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

For readers, that keeps account retention separate from custody and action cost. The existential
deposit threshold answers whether enough TAO remains for account representation; wallet-security and
fee questions need their own sources.

## Network Scope

Bittensor network references separate localnet, testnet, and mainnet environments
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks),
[Introduction to Bittensor: Subnet development](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)).
Existential-deposit examples should keep that environment attached because account-state examples
from one network do not automatically describe another network.

Localnet examples can show isolated behavior, testnet examples can show shared non-production
behavior, and mainnet references describe production-network context. The term stays the same, while
the observed account state belongs to the network where it was observed.

## Account-State Scope

Existential deposit is an account-state concept, not a transaction fee or staking threshold. Wallet
and chain-inspection references help explain account context, while fee and delegation references
answer different questions ([Wallets and Keys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets),
[Subtensor Storage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subtensor-api/storage)).

This keeps the term focused on account existence rather than broad balance-management advice.

Account retention is also separate from wallet identity. A wallet's
[public key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#public-key) and
[private key](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#private-key) identify and authorize
that wallet regardless of balance; existential deposit only determines whether the underlying
account stays represented in chain state once its balance falls below the threshold.

## Account Balance, Transfer, Staking, and Fee Pipeline

Existential deposit sits at the account-balance floor of Bittensor wallet activity. The
[Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)
states that accounts with balances below the threshold can be reaped, or removed, to conserve
network resources and prevent blockchain bloat from dust accounts. The term therefore names an
account-retention boundary, not a recommended wallet balance.

The same glossary entry describes existential deposit as a runtime constant in the Balances pallet
configuration. Documentation gives a default of 500 RAO in runtime code while noting that the actual
on-chain value should be read from the `Balances::ExistentialDeposit` constant rather than assumed
from examples alone ([Subtensor Storage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subtensor-api/storage)).

The useful pipeline order is therefore free-balance observation, comparison against the queried
retention threshold, and downstream effects on whether the account remains represented. A balance
can still be valid [TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau) while falling
below the retention threshold that keeps the account represented on chain.

[Transfer](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#transfer) vocabulary sits on the same
balance path. Outbound transfers reduce the sender's free balance, while inbound transfers increase
the receiver's balance, so ordinary movement can leave an account closer to the retention boundary.
Transfer names the movement action; existential deposit names the minimum retained balance required
for account representation ([Transaction Fees](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

[Staking and delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation) concerns
TAO placed behind validator support, while existential deposit concerns the minimum account balance
boundary. A staking amount and an account-retention threshold answer different questions. Both
involve TAO balances, but they describe different balance states and should not be combined into one
threshold.

[Transaction Fees](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees) belong to a separate extrinsic-cost
path. Fees relate to the cost of submitting a state-changing action, while existential deposit is a
retained-balance threshold. Subtensor storage is the better reference point for reading whether
account state is represented, while fee documentation explains the separate cost of submitting an
action ([Subtensor Storage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subtensor-api/storage)).

An account can be discussed in storage terms without turning the existential deposit into the fee
for a particular transaction. The deposit names the retained-balance condition; fees name the cost
side of an extrinsic.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For existential
deposit, that sequence changes how readers should interpret minimum-balance thresholds and account
retention examples. Existential deposit is a network-level account rule rather than a
subnet-specific action on a subnet such as netuid 1, but the environment separation still applies to
every balance observation and to fee or staking actions that can move balances toward the threshold.

In localnet, existential-deposit examples can be exercised in an isolated environment. Local
threshold values reflect local chain configuration rather than production account rules. Local fee
and staking examples on netuid 1 can move balances without representing production retention
thresholds.

On testnet, existential-deposit behavior can be observed in a shared, non-production network.
Testnet minimum-balance thresholds are separate from mainnet account state
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit)).

On mainnet, existential deposit is the live minimum TAO balance rule for retaining account
representation on the production Bittensor network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A balance-threshold example from one environment should
not be read as representing production account-retention rules in another environment.

## Distinction from Min Burn

Existential deposit is the minimum TAO balance required to keep a Bittensor account represented on
chain, while min burn is a subnet hyperparameter floor on the registration burn paid to obtain a UID
slot
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Account-retention threshold vocabulary and registration-burn floor vocabulary answer different
balance questions.

## Distinction from Register

Existential deposit is the minimum TAO balance threshold for retaining a Bittensor account record.
Register is the subnet entry process that places a miner or validator into a UID slot on a netuid
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

## Distinction from Transfer

Existential deposit is the minimum TAO balance threshold for retaining a Bittensor account on chain.
Transfer is wallet vocabulary for sending TAO from one address to another
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit),
[Glossary: Transfer](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#transfer)).

- **Existential deposit** — minimum retained balance required for account representation.
- **Transfer** — movement of TAO between wallet addresses.

Outbound transfers reduce free balance and can move an account toward the retention boundary; the
two terms name different parts of the same balance path
([Transaction Fees](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/fees)).

## Reader Boundary

Existential deposit should be read as balance-threshold vocabulary. It identifies the minimum TAO
amount required for account retention, while TAO, fees, staking, storage, and network references
provide nearby context
([Glossary: Existential Deposit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#existential-deposit),
[Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-tau)).

Existential deposit reading should treat the figure as chain-configured state. Wallet and fee prose
can mention the concept without quoting a live threshold unless the value was queried for the
network in scope.
