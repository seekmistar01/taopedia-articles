# Subnet Registration

Subnet registration is the process of creating a new subnet on the Bittensor network. The
[Subnet Registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet) reference describes
it as the step where a participant pays a registration burn to open a new incentive market, and the
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost) defines that
burn as the cost of registering a new subnet.

In Taopedia terms, subnet registration names a creation process, not a participant role or a token
amount. It is distinct from neuron registration, which adds a miner or validator to an existing
subnet rather than creating a new one.

## Why Subnet Registration Exists

Bittensor is organized around subnets, each running its own incentive mechanism for a specific kind
of work. [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)
explains that the network grows by adding new subnet markets, and subnet registration is the process
that opens them ([Subnet Registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

For readers, subnet registration is best understood as the network-expansion step. Without it, no
new subnet markets would appear. The registration burn is the mechanism's way of making creation
deliberate rather than free, so that new subnets represent a committed investment.

## What Subnet Registration Does

Subnet registration takes a registration burn from the participant and produces a new subnet. The
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost) describes the
burn as TAO that is recycled when a new subnet is created, and the
[Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
glossary entry explains that recycled tokens are subtracted from total issuance so they can be
emitted again.

For readers, the important behavior is that subnet registration converts a TAO burn into a new
subnet identity. The new subnet receives a unique netuid, begins participating in the network's
incentive structure, and becomes a destination for staking and emission activity.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For subnet
registration, that sequence changes how readers should interpret examples of subnet creation and
burn-cost outcomes.

In localnet, subnet registration can be exercised in an isolated environment. Localnet registration
events reflect local chain state and locally configured burn-cost parameters rather than production
subnet creation.

On testnet, subnet registration can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet
registration shows how burn-cost, recycle behavior, and subnet identity fields behave under shared
chain conditions while staying separate from mainnet subnet state.

On mainnet, subnet registration is the live action that brings a new subnet into the production
Bittensor network ([Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet),
[Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A subnet registration observed in one environment should
not be read as evidence of production subnet creation in another environment.

## Distinction from Register

Register is the process of purchasing a UID slot on an existing subnet by paying a neuron
registration burn. Subnet registration creates an entirely new subnet
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

Register joins an existing subnet as a miner or validator. Subnet registration opens a new subnet
for others to join.

- **Subnet registration** — creating a new subnet and receiving a new netuid.
- **Register** — purchasing a UID slot on an existing subnet.

## Distinction from Burn Cost

Burn cost is the TAO required to create a new subnet. Subnet registration is the process that pays
that burn to open the subnet
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

Burn cost names the price. Subnet registration names the act of paying that price to create the
subnet.

- **Subnet registration** — the on-chain process that creates a new subnet.
- **Burn cost** — the TAO amount required to register a new subnet.

## Distinction from Subnet Creator

A subnet creator defines the subnet's task and incentive mechanism. Subnet registration is the step
that brings that subnet into existence on the network
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

The creator defines what the subnet does. Registration makes it exist on chain.

- **Subnet creator** — the participant role that defines the subnet's task and mechanism.
- **Subnet registration** — the on-chain process that creates the subnet.

A creator can fund a new subnet directly, or fund it indirectly through a
[crowdloan](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans), which describes a collective
funding path that can finalize into a leased subnet. When registration happens through that path,
the registration produces both a new subnet and a subnet lease — split profit ownership of a subnet
where the crowdloan creator becomes the lease beneficiary and contributors become lease
shareholders.

Not every subnet registration produces a lease. A creator who funds a subnet directly creates the
subnet without the lease structure. When a crowdloan funds the registration, the lease is the
layered ownership arrangement the crowdloan produces on top of the new subnet.

## Distinction from Netuid

A netuid is the unique identifier assigned to a subnet. Subnet registration is the process that
creates the subnet and triggers that assignment
([Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

Netuid names the identifier. Subnet registration names the creation event that assigns it.

- **Subnet registration** — the process that creates a new subnet on chain.
- **Netuid** — the unique identifier assigned to that subnet.

## Distinction from Crowdloans

Subnet registration is the creation event that opens a new subnet. A crowdloan is one funding path
that can pay for that registration and, when successful, can also produce a subnet lease structure
([Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

Direct registration opens the subnet without a lease. A crowdloan-funded registration can add
beneficiary and shareholder roles on top of the new subnet.

- **Subnet registration** — the on-chain event that creates a new subnet.
- **Crowdloan** — a collective funding path that can pay for registration and produce a lease.

## Distinction from Subnet Deregistration

Subnet registration creates a new subnet by paying a registration burn and receiving a unique
netuid. Subnet deregistration removes an entire non-immune subnet when the subnet limit is reached
([Subnet deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

A new registration attempt at capacity can trigger deregistration of the subnet with the lowest EMA
of alpha price. The newly registered subnet then receives an
[immunity period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period).

- **Subnet registration** — creating a new subnet and opening a new incentive market.
- **Subnet deregistration** — removing an entire subnet when the network subnet limit is reached.

## Distinction from Coinbase

Subnet registration brings a subnet into existence. Coinbase is the per-block protocol mechanism
that drives TAO emission, injects TAO into subnet pool reserves, and triggers Yuma Consensus rounds
at epoch boundaries
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

Registration opens the market. Coinbase supplies and distributes the emissions that flow through it
after the subnet exists.

- **Subnet registration** — creating the subnet slot on chain.
- **Coinbase** — the per-block emission mechanism that feeds the subnet after it exists.

## Distinction from Subnet Is Active

Subnet registration and subnet is active mark different points in a subnet's life. Registration is
how a subnet slot is created on-chain, while subnet is active is the flag reporting that the
subnet's emissions have started. A subnet can be registered before its emission path begins, so
existing on-chain is not the same as actively emitting
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Subnet registration** — creating the subnet slot on-chain.
- **Subnet is active** — whether that subnet's emissions have started.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Subnet registration names the on-chain process that creates a new netuid by paying the registration
burn. Yuma Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts validator weight submissions into
emission shares each tempo once the subnet is running
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

- **Subnet registration** — on-chain creation of a new netuid slot.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares. Subnet
  registration names roster admission; Yuma processes validator weights into rewards
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

- **Burn cost** — TAO cost to register on a subnet when slots are scarce.

## Reader Boundary

This article defines subnet registration as a concept. It does not state current burn amounts,
describe registration steps, list active subnets, or compare subnet-creation strategies. Current
costs and operational detail belong in subnet documentation at the time of observation.
