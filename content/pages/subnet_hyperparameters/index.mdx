# Subnet Hyperparameters

Subnet hyperparameters are protocol state variables that shape how a Bittensor subnet behaves. They
do not define what useful work means on the subnet; that is the role of the subnet's incentive
mechanism. Instead, hyperparameters set boundaries and timing around validator weights, consensus
processing, emission handling, and miner or validator lifecycle rules.

References:
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)

## What they control

Hyperparameters cover several kinds of subnet behavior. Timing settings such as `tempo` define a
block-based cadence. Weight-related settings influence how validator weight submissions are paced or
processed. Consensus-related settings can affect bonding, clipping, and Yuma behavior.
Emission-related settings can influence how subnet rewards are handled once the broader emission
system reaches the subnet.

References:
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal)

## Relationship to incentive mechanisms

An incentive mechanism tells miners what to produce and validators how to evaluate that work.
Hyperparameters do not replace that mechanism. They set the protocol envelope around it: how often
subnet epochs advance, how validator signals are submitted, how consensus interprets those signals,
and how rewards are ultimately distributed. This is why hyperparameters are closely related to
[[subnet_creation_mechanisms|subnet mechanism design]] without being the same thing.

References:
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Timing and reward cadence

Tempo is the clearest timing example. The emission process accumulates rewards over blocks and
distributes them at the end of a subnet tempo through [[yuma_consensus|Yuma Consensus]]. Commit
Reveal also depends on tempo-based timing: its waiting period is measured in tempos, and converting
that interval to blocks requires the subnet's tempo value.

References: [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal)

## Authority and change control

Not every hyperparameter is controlled the same way. Some values are intended for subnet-level
administration, while others require broader protocol authority. Readers should avoid assuming that
any named value can be changed by the same actor or through the same process. Permission and update
rules are part of the parameter definition, not just operational detail.

Reference: [Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)

## How to read hyperparameter claims

A hyperparameter claim should name the behavior being described rather than treating the value as a
universal property of Bittensor. The important questions are whether the claim is about timing,
consensus, emissions, or subnet administration, and whether the cited source supports that exact
scope. Exact values should be checked against the relevant subnet source before making operational
decisions.

References:
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Subnet hyperparameters set protocol boundaries around validator weights and consensus cadence. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix) notes that each
incentive mechanism has its own weight matrix for independent consensus calculations.

For readers, hyperparameters still name subnet-level settings. Per-mechanism weight matrices remain
separate when a subnet runs more than one incentive mechanism.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Weight Matrix](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix)

## Relationship to Tempo

Subnet hyperparameters and tempo address related but different parts of subnet timing vocabulary.
Tempo names the block-based cadence that governs epoch transitions inside a subnet, while subnet
hyperparameters include the configurable setting that defines that cadence for a selected subnet.
The [Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo) describes tempo as
the subnet-specific cadence for epochs, and the
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters) reference
lists tempo among subnet-specific settings.

For readers, tempo is the cadence concept, while subnet hyperparameters are the configurable inputs
that express that cadence for one subnet.

References: [Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)

## Relationship to Epoch

Subnet hyperparameters and an epoch address related but different parts of subnet timing vocabulary.
An epoch names the consensus period inside a subnet, while subnet hyperparameters such as `tempo`
configure the block-based cadence that determines when those periods run. The
[Glossary: Epoch](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#epoch) describes the subnet
consensus period, and the
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters) reference
lists tempo and related subnet settings for a selected subnet.

For readers, an epoch names the consensus period, while subnet hyperparameters are the configurable
settings that help produce that cadence for one subnet.

References: [Glossary: Epoch](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#epoch),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)

## Relationship to Yuma Consensus

Subnet hyperparameters and Yuma Consensus address related but different parts of subnet behavior
configuration. Subnet hyperparameters set protocol boundaries around validator weights, consensus
cadence, and emission handling, while Yuma Consensus describes how validator weight signals are
aggregated into miner incentives and validator dividends inside that configured context. The
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters) reference
lists consensus-related settings for a selected subnet, and
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) describes clipping, bonding,
and emission calculation.

For readers, subnet hyperparameters name configurable protocol settings, while Yuma Consensus names
the aggregation process those settings help frame inside a subnet.

References:
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Relationship to Commit Reveal

Subnet hyperparameters and Commit Reveal address related but different parts of validator-weight
timing configuration. Commit Reveal names the subnet mechanism that can conceal fresh validator
weights until a later reveal time, while subnet hyperparameters include settings that shape how that
mechanism is configured for a selected subnet. The
[Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal) documentation describes
concealed weight submission and delayed reveal, and the
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters) reference
lists related subnet-specific settings.

For readers, Commit Reveal names the weight-concealment mechanism, while subnet hyperparameters are
the configurable settings that shape how that mechanism operates inside one subnet.

References: [Commit Reveal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/commit-reveal),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)
