# Effective Stake

Effective stake is the total staked TAO associated with a delegate. It combines the delegate's own
staked TAO with TAO delegated by nominators
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The term is amount vocabulary inside the staking model. It is not a separate asset, a new role, or a
validator-quality score.

## Delegate and Nominator Context

A delegate is a validator that can receive stake from nominators. A nominator stakes TAO to a
validator rather than becoming that validator
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

Effective stake summarizes the total TAO associated with the delegate after both sides of that
relationship are counted. It is broader than delegate stake because delegate stake only names the
TAO supplied by the delegate themselves
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

That makes effective stake a relationship-aware staking amount. It reflects delegate support from
both self-stake and nomination, without turning the delegate and nominator into the same role.

## Delegation Boundary

Delegation places delegates, nominators, and delegated stake inside the broader staking model.
Effective stake belongs to that model as the combined total behind a delegate after nomination is
included ([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

This makes effective stake descriptive rather than advisory. It can explain why a delegate has more
staking support than its own delegate stake alone, but it does not choose a validator or quote a
reward outcome.

The delegation boundary also keeps effective stake separate from broader ownership terminology. The
term summarizes stake associated with a delegate in the staking model; it does not describe every
account relationship around staking actions.

## Stake Weight Boundary

Effective stake and stake weight answer different questions. Effective stake describes the staked
TAO amount associated with a delegate. Stake weight is the computed stake value used for validator
consensus power and emissions in a subnet
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

This boundary keeps a staking total separate from a consensus influence measure. Effective stake
helps explain the staking base behind a delegate, while stake weight explains how stake context is
interpreted inside subnet consensus.

TAO weight and alpha stake are also stake-weight context terms. They belong to how validator
influence is computed inside a subnet, while effective stake remains a delegate-associated staking
total ([Glossary: TAO Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao-weight),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

## Validator Influence Context

Effective stake can matter to validator discussions because delegates participate in validation and
can receive nominated stake. That does not make effective stake the same thing as validator weights,
rank, trust, or dividends
([Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

Validator weights are miner-evaluation signals. Dividends are validator-side emission outcomes.
Effective stake stays on the staking-total side of the vocabulary.

Keeping those terms separate prevents a delegate's staking support from being mistaken for a direct
miner-evaluation result or a payout forecast.

## Autostaking and Emission-Derived Stake Context

Autostaking adds emission-derived entries to effective stake. When miners configure autostaking,
mining emissions are automatically routed as new delegated stake to the configured validator. That
delegated stake adds to the validator's effective stake total each time emissions are routed,
without requiring a separate staking transaction from the miner
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, effective stake increases from two sources: delegate-provided stake and externally
delegated stake from nominators and autostaking miners. Tracking only one source understates the
total. The effective stake total captures both components as a single staking-context measure.

The Dynamic TAO model extends this context to alpha tokens. In alpha-enabled subnets, effective
stake can be measured in subnet alpha rather than TAO, and autostaked emissions in those subnets can
add alpha-denominated stake to the validator's effective stake
([Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

That makes effective stake a multi-asset term in Dynamic TAO contexts. The concept — combined
delegate-provided and externally delegated stake — stays the same, but the asset denomination
follows the subnet's tokenomics rather than always being TAO
([Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao)).

## Distinction from Staking

Staking names the process of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey, while effective stake
names the combined total of delegate-owned and nominated TAO behind that hotkey after support is
recorded ([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Action vocabulary and combined-total vocabulary answer different questions. Staking names support
being attached; effective stake names the measured total once delegate-owned and nominated support
are counted together
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

- **Staking** — attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind that hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation names how nominated TAO becomes attached to a validator hotkey, while effective stake
names the combined total of delegate-owned and nominated TAO behind that hotkey
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Relationship vocabulary and combined-total vocabulary answer different questions. Delegation
describes the support attachment; effective stake reports the full hotkey backing total once that
support is recorded
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

- **Delegation** — nominated TAO attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind that hotkey.

## Effective Stake, Stake Weight, and Yuma on netuid 1

On a subnet such as netuid 1,
[delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation) and
[autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) build the combined backing total
recorded as effective stake behind a
[delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) hotkey
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

That backing total feeds into
[stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight) interpretation,
while [validator weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector) and
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) handle miner-evaluation
signals and emission outcomes separately
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Effective stake therefore names how much support sits behind a validator on netuid 1; it does not
replace validator-weight scoring or dividend allocation vocabulary.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For effective
stake on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how delegation examples should be read.

In localnet, effective stake on netuid 1 can be tested in isolation. Localnet delegate totals and
nomination relationships reflect that test chain rather than production staking behavior.

On testnet, effective stake on netuid 1 applies in a shared non-production network where validator
sets, balances, and delegation state are separate from mainnet.

On mainnet, effective stake on netuid 1 concerns production validator relationships and production
TAO or alpha-denominated backing on the selected subnet
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. An effective-stake example from one environment should not
be read as evidence for another environment.

## Effective Stake Totals Differ From Stake Weight Influence

Effective stake and stake weight are often read together because both describe support behind a
delegate, but they answer different questions. Effective stake is the total staked amount associated
with a delegate after self-stake and nomination are combined, while stake weight is the computed
influence measure used inside subnet consensus and emissions
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

That distinction keeps a backing total separate from a consensus multiplier. A delegate can hold a
large effective stake total while stake weight reflects how that support is interpreted for
validator influence inside [Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
including alpha-denominated recording on alpha subnets after pool conversion
([Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

For readers, effective stake is descriptive amount vocabulary: it explains how much support sits
behind a hotkey. Stake weight is influence vocabulary: it explains how strongly that delegate's
[validator weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector) count when
consensus aggregates evaluations
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Higher effective stake does not automatically mean proportionally higher stake weight, because stake
weight is the term consensus uses rather than a duplicate balance label. Use effective stake when
the article needs the combined backing total; use stake weight when it needs consensus influence
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

For netuid 1 examples, effective stake and delegate stake name different figures behind the same
validator hotkey. Effective stake is the combined total of delegate-owned and nominator-delegated
TAO behind that hotkey, while delegate stake names only the TAO the delegate stakes personally
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

For readers comparing backing totals, effective stake counts both buckets together and delegate
stake reports the validator-supplied amount only. The two can diverge sharply when nomination is
large or when self-stake is modest on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

- **Effective stake** — combined self-stake plus nominated support behind the hotkey.
- **Delegate stake** — validator self-staked TAO only.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Effective stake and validator take describe a delegate's economics as amount and split. Effective
stake is the total TAO associated with a delegate after self-stake and nominator-delegated stake are
counted, while validator take is the commission the delegate keeps from emissions earned on
delegated stake before the remainder flows to nominators
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

For netuid 1 readers, effective stake answers how much support sits behind the hotkey, and validator
take answers what share of delegated-emission returns the delegate keeps at distribution. Two
delegates with the same effective stake can still leave different nominator outcomes if their take
rates differ
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Take applies to delegated-emission returns rather than relabeling the delegate's own self-staked
portion, so amount vocabulary and commission vocabulary should be read together rather than
collapsed into one figure
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

## Distinction from Delegated Stake

Effective stake and delegated stake answer different questions about support behind a validator
hotkey. Delegated stake names TAO nominators attach to a delegate, while effective stake names the
combined total after that nominated support is added to the delegate's own stake
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

For netuid 1 readers, delegated stake is the nominator-supplied portion only, and effective stake is
the full hotkey backing total used when readers need one combined figure. Delegation vocabulary
describes how that support is attached; effective stake vocabulary describes the measured total once
it is recorded on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

A rise in delegated stake can increase effective stake without changing delegate stake, which is why
the three terms stay separate in staking documentation
([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

## Distinction from Nearby Staking Figures

Several staking labels describe support behind one delegate hotkey. They are easy to merge when an
article needs only one number, but each answers a different reader question
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

| Term            | What it measures                             |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Stake           | TAO locked to a hotkey in the staking model  |
| Delegate stake  | TAO the delegate stakes personally           |
| Delegated stake | TAO nominators attach to the delegate        |
| Effective stake | Combined delegate-owned plus nominated total |
| Stake weight    | Computed influence used in subnet consensus  |

Use effective stake when the article needs the full backing total behind a hotkey. Use stake weight
when the article needs consensus influence rather than a balance-style total
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is a participant role that delegates TAO to a validator hotkey. Effective stake is the
combined total recorded behind that hotkey after nomination is included
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

Role vocabulary names who supplies support; effective stake vocabulary names how much support the
delegate hotkey carries once that relationship is recorded. A nominator can increase effective stake
without changing delegate stake
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Autostaking

Autostaking routes miner emissions as new delegated stake to a configured validator. Those routed
amounts add to effective stake over time without a separate manual staking transaction from the
miner ([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

Autostaking is a configuration path; effective stake is the measured total that path can increase.
Readers comparing delegate totals should remember both nominator delegation and autostaked emissions
can sit behind the same hotkey ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Dividends

Dividends are validator-side emission outcomes after consensus processing. Effective stake remains
the combined staking total behind a delegate hotkey before payout vocabulary enters the discussion
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

A large effective stake total does not forecast dividend amounts by itself. Dividends depend on
validator performance, take settings, and emission distribution rules on the selected subnet
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

## Distinction from Hotkey-Coldkey Pair

Effective stake and the hotkey-coldkey pair describe different parts of the same staking picture.
Effective stake is the aggregate stake a validator's hotkey carries across its own and all delegated
stake, while the hotkey-coldkey pair is the authentication relationship between the operational
hotkey and the ownership coldkey under which that validator acts
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Hotkey-Coldkey Pair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey-coldkey-pair)).

A hotkey-coldkey pair is a single ownership-and-operation link for one identity, while effective
stake sums contributions from many nominators delegating to that hotkey.

- **Hotkey-coldkey pair** — the ownership-and-operation link for one wallet identity.
- **Effective stake** — combined delegate-owned plus nominated total behind the hotkey.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Effective stake and stake weight are related but different delegate terms. Effective stake names the
total staked support behind a delegate after self-stake and nomination are combined, while stake
weight names how strongly that delegate's signals count when validator inputs are merged in Yuma
Consensus
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Effective stake is a backing total; stake weight is a consensus influence measure. A higher
effective stake total does not automatically mean the same stake weight reading.

- **Effective stake** — combined staked support behind a delegate hotkey.
- **Stake weight** — computed influence used in subnet consensus processing.

## Reader Boundary

Effective stake should be read as a delegate-associated staked TAO total, not as stake weight,
validator weights, rank, trust, dividends, or validator-selection advice
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight)).

Use effective stake when the focus is the combined total behind a delegate. Use delegate stake for
the delegate's own stake, delegated stake for nominator-provided stake, and stake weight for subnet
consensus influence
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).
