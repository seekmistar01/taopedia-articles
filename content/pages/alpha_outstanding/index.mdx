# Alpha Outstanding

Alpha outstanding is the subnet alpha that sits outside a subnet's reserve pool in Bittensor's
Dynamic TAO tokenomics. The official
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes alpha outstanding as alpha held by subnet participants and also refers to it as the total
stake in the subnet.

## Reserve and Outstanding Context

A subnet has a liquidity-pool side and a participant-held side. The liquidity-pool side includes the
subnet's TAO and alpha reserves. Alpha outstanding describes the alpha outside that reserve context,
which is why it is useful when reading subnet supply, stake, and emission descriptions.

This does not make alpha outstanding a separate token. It is a location-and-accounting concept for
the same subnet-specific alpha asset: some alpha is in reserve, and some alpha is outstanding with
participants.

## Emission Context

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-outstanding-injection)
documentation describes alpha outstanding injection as alpha set aside each block to be emitted to
participants. In that context, alpha outstanding is connected to miner, validator, staker, and
subnet-owner outcomes after the subnet's emission process is applied.

For readers, the useful point is the split: alpha reserve injection supports the pool side, while
alpha outstanding injection supports participant-side emission context.

## Relationship to Alpha Tokens

Alpha outstanding should be read as a state of subnet alpha, not as a new asset class. Alpha tokens
are the subnet-specific assets used in Dynamic TAO; alpha outstanding describes the portion of that
subnet alpha that is outside the reserve pool and associated with participant-side stake and
emission accounting.

This keeps the term narrower than a general article about alpha tokens. A claim about alpha tokens
can describe the asset broadly, while a claim about alpha outstanding describes where part of that
asset sits in the subnet tokenomics model.

## Relationship to Alpha Reserve

Alpha outstanding and alpha reserve describe opposite sides of subnet alpha accounting. Alpha
outstanding is the participant-held alpha outside the reserve pool, while alpha reserve is the
pool-held alpha paired with the subnet's TAO reserve. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes both terms in the Dynamic TAO subnet market context.

For readers, alpha outstanding answers how much subnet alpha is outside the pool, while alpha
reserve answers how much alpha is held inside the pool. The distinction is about location and
accounting, not about two different alpha token types.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to ADR

Alpha outstanding is also part of the context behind Alpha Distribution Ratio. The
[Glossary: ADR](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#adr-alpha-distribution-ratio)
entry compares alpha emitted to participants with alpha injected into the AMM pool. Alpha
outstanding belongs on the participant-held side of that comparison, while alpha reserves belong on
the pool side.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Alpha outstanding names subnet alpha set aside for participant-side emission. The official
documentation notes that miners can receive incentives separately from each mechanism when a subnet
runs more than one incentive mechanism.

For readers, alpha outstanding still describes alpha outside the reserve pool. When a subnet uses
multiple mechanisms, that participant-side emission path can reflect more than one mechanism
context.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)

## Relationship to Protocol Alpha

Alpha outstanding and protocol alpha are both subnet alpha that sits outside the reserve pool, but
they belong to different holders. Alpha outstanding is subnet alpha associated with participant
positions—stakers, validators, and miners in a subnet—while protocol alpha is subnet alpha that the
protocol itself holds. The
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)
describes protocol alpha as alpha tokens the protocol gathers as a result of chain-side TAO buys
during reserve injection, cached per subnet rather than immediately recycled, while the
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
frames alpha outstanding as the participant-held side of the subnet alpha model.

For readers, alpha outstanding and protocol alpha together describe the two non-reserve holders of
subnet alpha: participant roles on one side and the protocol itself on the other. A staker's or
miner's alpha position contributes to alpha outstanding; the protocol's reserve-injection byproduct
contributes to protocol alpha. The glossary further notes that at subnet dissolution, the protocol's
alpha share participates in pro-rata TAO settlement, reducing staker payouts proportionally while
the protocol's TAO share returns to the chain. Both sides are part of the same subnet's total alpha
accounting, but participant-held and protocol-held alpha serve different purposes in that
accounting.

References:
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Conviction and Locked Stake

Alpha outstanding and conviction both concern staked subnet alpha, but they describe different
things. Alpha outstanding is the subnet alpha held by participants outside the reserve pool — also
described as the total stake in the subnet — as the
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes. Conviction and locked stake, by the
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)
documentation, describe locking subnet alpha so that it builds a time-based commitment score and the
staked balance cannot be silently reduced below the locked amount.

For readers, alpha outstanding names how much subnet alpha is held as stake by participants, while
conviction names whether some of that staked alpha has been locked to signal commitment. The two are
not interchangeable: alpha outstanding is a total-amount category of participant-held alpha, whereas
conviction is a lock applied to staked alpha that constrains its reduction. Locked alpha is still
part of alpha outstanding; conviction adds a commitment constraint that the outstanding-amount
measure does not itself capture.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)

## Reader Boundary

Alpha outstanding is not a live balance, a staking instruction, or a yield estimate. It is a
tokenomics term for reading the difference between alpha in reserve and alpha associated with
participant-side subnet stake and emissions. Exact live amounts belong in current chain data or
official tools.
