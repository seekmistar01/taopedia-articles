# Emission

Emission is Bittensor's process for creating and allocating TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. It
covers value entering subnet economic contexts and value reaching miners, validators, stakers, and
subnet creators through protocol allocation flow
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

The term is broader than a single reward label. Emission is the process; incentives, dividends,
staking-related outcomes, and creator allocations are role-specific results within that process
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends)).

## Injection and Distribution

Bittensor emission vocabulary separates injection from distribution. Injection concerns value
entering subnet contexts, while distribution concerns how that value is allocated across miners,
validators, stakers, and subnet creators
([Emission: Injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

That distinction keeps emission from being mistaken for one balance, one role outcome, or one subnet
ranking. A miner incentive or validator dividend is a narrower output of the process.

## TAO and Alpha Context

Emission in Bittensor involves both TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens. TAO reserve injection and
alpha reserve injection are separate parts of the model, and the alpha side belongs to the
subnet-specific token context
([TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection),
[Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

This framing matters for tokenomics articles. A TAO-only explanation can miss the subnet pool side
of Dynamic TAO, while an alpha-only explanation can miss the network asset entering subnet liquidity
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Role Allocation

Emission reaches different role families through different vocabulary. Miner-side rewards are
usually discussed as incentives, validator-side rewards as dividends, and staking-related outcomes
as part of broader staking and subnet-token allocation context
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Those names answer different questions. Emission identifies the full creation and allocation
process; incentives and dividends identify role-specific results after subnet evaluation and
consensus have shaped the outcome
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Consensus Context

Yuma Consensus turns validator evaluations into allocation outcomes. Validators submit weights,
consensus filters and aggregates those signals, and the result affects miner incentives and
validator dividends inside the subnet's emission flow
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Validator Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-weights)).

Consensus score and validator-miner bonds belong near this context because they shape how
evaluations move through consensus. They do not replace the word emission: emission is the
value-creation and allocation process around those consensus signals
([Glossary: Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)).

## Supply Context

Emission is related to issuance and halving but is not the same term. Issuance concerns circulating
TAO supply, while halving concerns scheduled reductions in emission rate. Emission names the ongoing
process that creates and allocates value before those broader supply concepts are interpreted
([Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)).

This keeps supply vocabulary and role-allocation vocabulary separate. Emission can affect the
broader tokenomics picture without becoming a substitute for issuance, halving, incentives, or
dividends.

## Network Context

Bittensor documentation separates localnet, testnet, and mainnet environments
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Emission examples should keep that environment boundary attached. A localnet or testnet example can
illustrate emission behavior, but it is not evidence of a mainnet emission outcome.

## Reader Boundary

Emission is a reference term for Bittensor token creation and allocation. It is not a staking
recommendation, a subnet ranking table, or a role-specific operating guide
([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

Exact amounts, role outcomes, and subnet examples need the selected subnet, network environment,
epoch context, and relevant source.

## Coinbase and Dynamic TAO Architecture

In Dynamic TAO, emission depends on the coinbase mechanism that bridges network-level TAO allocation
and subnet-level alpha distribution. The coinbase process converts network TAO into subnet reserve
injections and produces distributed alpha for subnet participants
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao)).

That process creates two emission layers. The first layer is network-level: TAO is injected into
subnet reserves through the coinbase process, establishing the TAO side of each subnet's liquidity
pool. The second layer is subnet-level: alpha tokens are produced from that injection and
distributed as miner incentives, validator dividends, and staking rewards
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Emission: Injection and distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#injection-and-distribution-two-stages-of-the-emissions-process)).

Protocol alpha is one result of this architecture. During coinbase reserve injection, the protocol
can accumulate alpha that is cached by subnet rather than immediately distributed to participants.
Protocol alpha is therefore an emission-side category that sits alongside the standard distribution
flow
([Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

For readers, Dynamic TAO emission is not just about TAO supply. It involves the relationship between
TAO reserve flow and subnet alpha production, which is why emission vocabulary separates injection
and distribution stages rather than treating the process as a single token release
([Dynamic TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#dynamic-tao),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).
