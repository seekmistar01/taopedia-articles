# Subnet

A subnet is a specialized market inside Bittensor. The official
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet) defines a Bittensor subnet as
an incentive-based competition market that produces a specific kind of digital commodity. The same
definition places miners and validators inside the subnet: miners produce the commodity, and
validators measure the work.

## Market Scope

A subnet narrows Bittensor's work into a particular market instead of treating all useful work as
one undifferentiated task. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet as having its own goal and standards, while the
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction) places subnets
beside the blockchain and TAO incentive layer as a core platform component.

This makes subnet a root concept for reading Bittensor: the subnet names where a specific kind of
work is requested and evaluated, while the broader network supplies shared protocol and incentive
context.

## Miner and Validator Roles

The miner and validator roles give a subnet its work-and-evaluation structure. Miners produce the
requested digital commodity, and validators measure whether the work meets the subnet's standards.
The official [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet) and
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) sources both
use this producer-and-evaluator framing.

For a reader, this means a subnet is not only a label for a topic area. It is the setting where
participants compete to produce useful work and where evaluation determines how that work is
interpreted within Bittensor.

## Relationship to the Network

Subnets are part of Bittensor, not separate blockchains. The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction) describes the
platform as including a pool of subnets, the Bittensor blockchain, TAO, and open-source tools. Those
pieces are related but distinct: subnets organize work markets, while the blockchain records network
activity and supports incentive flow.

Keeping that distinction clear helps avoid treating subnet as a synonym for the whole Bittensor
network. A subnet is one market context inside the network.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

A subnet is usually discussed with one incentive mechanism at a time, but Bittensor also allows more
than one mechanism inside the same subnet. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, a subnet still names one specialized work market, but that market can include more than
one evaluation path. Rules and standards for one mechanism should be read separately rather than
assumed to apply across the whole subnet.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Relationship to Yuma Consensus

A subnet and Yuma Consensus are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A subnet names
the specialized work market where miners and validators operate, while Yuma Consensus is the
aggregation step that turns the validator weight signals produced inside that subnet into miner
incentives and validator dividends. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes the subnet as the market and role boundary, and
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) describes the clipping,
bonding, and emission calculation applied to the weights gathered within it.

For readers, a subnet names where evaluation happens and who participates, while Yuma Consensus
names the on-chain process that resolves that subnet's validator weights into emission outcomes.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Reader Boundary

Subnet should be read as the general concept for a Bittensor work market. It is not a live status
report, a guarantee about any particular market's quality, or a complete description of a subnet's
rules. Specific market behavior depends on the subnet's own goal, standards, and official
documentation.
