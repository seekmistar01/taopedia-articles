# Subnet Miner

A subnet miner is the task-performing role inside a Bittensor subnet. Miners perform the work that
subnet validators evaluate
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term belongs to subnet incentive vocabulary. It names the work-producing role in the subnet
flow; validator, scoring, consensus, and emission language describe later parts of that flow.

In plain language, subnet miner answers: who is doing the work that validators score in this subnet?
A miner produces the output; a validator evaluates it; consensus aggregates those evaluations into
miner incentives. The miner role is the entry point in that sequence
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner)).

For a reader, the distinction from subnet validator matters most when reading evaluation results. A
miner produces; a validator evaluates. Their roles are complementary and subnet-specific, so a miner
from one subnet does not automatically perform in another subnet
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Work Role

The miner role is tied to the subnet's work target. A miner in one subnet may produce a different
kind of output than a miner in another subnet because each subnet can define its own goals and
standards ([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task)).

This keeps miner vocabulary scoped to the subnet being discussed. The role is defined by the work a
subnet asks miners to perform.

That scope is the main reason the word miner is not interchangeable across subnet examples. A
general miner claim still needs the subnet task or mechanism that gives the work its meaning.

## Work Product Versus Incentive Outcome

The subnet miner supplies work that validators later evaluate under the subnet's rules. The
incentive mechanism then connects task, protocol, and evaluation method into a scoring path
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner)).

For readers, miner names who produces requested work. It does not by itself define emissions,
weights, or final incentive outcomes.

The incentive path depends on more than the work product. Returned work can be scored, converted
into validator signals, aggregated through consensus, and connected to emissions by the subnet's
mechanism design.

## Validators Evaluate Miner Work

Subnet miners and subnet validators form the core work-and-evaluation relationship inside a subnet.
Miners provide work, and validators measure that work against the subnet's standards
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

Subnet miner names the producing side; subnet validator names the evaluating side, including
measurement, weights, and consensus signals.

## Subnet Task Names the Work Target

A subnet miner is the role performing the work, while the subnet task is the work target itself. The
glossary connects subnet tasks to the work miners perform, and the incentive-mechanism documentation
places task design inside the broader subnet mechanism
([Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

The miner is the role; the task describes what that role is expected to produce. Two miner examples
can both be valid Bittensor examples while still describing different work because their subnet
tasks differ.

## Mechanism Scope

Some subnets can run multiple incentive mechanisms in parallel. In that case, miner work and
validator evaluation belong to the mechanism being discussed
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)).

This prevents a result in one mechanism from being treated as a complete statement about miner work
across every mechanism in the subnet.

Mechanism scope also keeps emissions language precise. A miner result from one mechanism does not
automatically describe every work path, evaluation path, or incentive path inside the same subnet.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For subnet miners
on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret work examples and
evaluation outcomes.

In localnet, miner role mechanics can be demonstrated in isolation. Local miner examples reflect
local chain configuration rather than production subnet performance.

On testnet, miner work can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet evaluation
outcomes are separate from mainnet subnet state.

On mainnet, subnet miner is the live work-producing role inside production subnet markets on the
connected Bittensor network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Miner examples from one environment should not be read as
evidence for production subnet performance in another environment.

## Registration Grants a UID Before Mining Work

[Mining: Miner registration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-registration) describes
registering with a subnet to receive a UID before a miner can take part in mining work.
[Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) vocabulary names that
subnet-entry step; subnet miner names the work-producing role that follows once entry succeeds
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

Miner reading therefore starts after registration. The role produces subnet task output;
registration places the hotkey into the participant set validators can evaluate.

## Validators Score Miner Output Through Weight Vectors

[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) describes validators
periodically submitting vectors that rank miner work they have evaluated. Those
[validator weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector) are the
evaluation signals subnet miners receive after producing output under the subnet task
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

Subnet miner names who produces work; validator weights name how that work is scored before
consensus merges many validator signals.

## Miner Incentives Follow Rank After Consensus

[Yuma Consensus: Miner emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#miner-emissions)
documentation divides miner-side subnet emissions by aggregate
[rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank) after validator weights are clipped
and combined. Miner incentives therefore sit downstream of both evaluation and consensus processing
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives)).

Producing useful subnet work is the input; rank and incentives describe the emission outcome after
consensus runs.

## Axon Receives Miner-Side Requests

Subnet miner names the role that produces work for the subnet task, while
[axon](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#axon) names the server-side endpoint that
receives incoming [synapse](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#synapse) objects from
[dendrite](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dendrite) clients
([Understanding Neurons](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/neurons#neuron-to-neuron-communication)).

Subnet miner belongs to the work-producing role; axon belongs to the endpoint that receives
miner-side requests in the axon-dendrite communication path. A subnet miner mention does not by
itself describe endpoint configuration.

## A Miner Competes for Standing, Not Just for a One-Time Slot

Documentation frames subnet mining as ongoing participation in a competitive subnet rather than a
static registration. A subnet miner performs the work validators evaluate each tempo, and its
standing — reflected in emissions and pruning score — determines whether it keeps its UID when the
subnet is full
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)).

For readers, this is why the miner role and pruning score belong in the same discussion.
Registration places a miner on the subnet, but continued low emissions can make that slot vulnerable
when someone new registers and every UID is occupied
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

That competitive framing also keeps miner vocabulary separate from validator vocabulary. A subnet
miner is evaluated for task performance, while validators score that performance; the two roles face
different responsibilities even though both can be displaced by pruning score
([Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

## Distinction from Subnet Validator

Subnet miner names the work-producing role inside a subnet. Subnet validator names the evaluating
role that measures that work against the subnet's standards
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

Production and evaluation are complementary sides of the same subnet flow. A miner produces output;
a validator scores it before consensus and emissions processing.

## Distinction from Subnet Task

Subnet task names the work a mechanism is meant to evaluate. Subnet miner names the role that
performs that work inside the subnet
([Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

Task vocabulary describes what work means; miner vocabulary describes who produces it.

## Distinction from Subnet Protocol

Subnet protocol names the exchange rules for how validators request work and miners return
responses. Subnet miner names the work-producing role that returns responses within those rules
([Glossary: Subnet Protocol](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-protocol),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

Protocol vocabulary describes how interaction is organized; miner vocabulary describes who produces
work under that organization.

## Distinction from Axon

Subnet miner names the work-producing role. Axon names the server-side endpoint that receives
incoming synapse objects from validator dendrite clients
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Glossary: Axon](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#axon),
[Understanding Neurons](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/neurons#neuron-to-neuron-communication)).

Role vocabulary describes who produces work; axon vocabulary describes where requests arrive on the
miner side.

## Distinction from Synapse

Subnet miner names the work-producing role. Synapse names the structured message object that carries
challenge and response fields through the axon-dendrite exchange
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Glossary: Synapse](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#synapse)).

Role vocabulary and message-object vocabulary answer different questions on the same exchange path.

## Distinction from Pruning Score

A subnet miner and the pruning score describe a miner as the role and the value that governs its
removal. A subnet miner is the participant that performs the work validators evaluate, while the
pruning score is the per-neuron value that ranks which UID is displaced when a full subnet takes a
new registration, so a miner that earns little and holds the lowest pruning score is the first
removed once its immunity ends
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

## Distinction from Mining and Validating

Mining and validating is overview vocabulary for both subnet roles, while subnet miner names the
participant that produces subnet work inside a selected netuid
([Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction)).

- **Mining and validating** — paired role overview for subnet participation.
- **Subnet miner** — the work-producing participant role on one subnet.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Subnet miner names the participant role that supplies work inside a subnet incentive mechanism. Yuma
Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares
each tempo ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner)).

- **Subnet miner** — work-producing role on a selected netuid.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Subnet miner names the work-producing role inside a subnet. It describes the work side of subnet
incentives rather than how-to steps, performance reports, account labels, or claims about a specific
miner's output
([Glossary: Subnet Miner](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-miner),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

A specific miner's rank or incentive is a consensus-and-network outcome to read from live subnet
data, not something the term itself reports.
