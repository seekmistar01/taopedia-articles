# Nominator

A nominator is a TAO holder who stakes with a validator in Bittensor delegation vocabulary
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term names the supplying side of the staking relationship. It should be read separately from the
validator role that receives delegated stake and performs validation work.

## Delegation Role

In Bittensor staking vocabulary, a nominator supplies TAO support to a validator. A delegate is the
receiving validator role, while a nominator is the holder role supplying delegated stake
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

This keeps the relationship clear: nominator names the TAO-holder role, nominate names the staking
action, and delegate names the receiving validator role.

The [Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) entry is
specific about the target: a nominator stakes TAO on a validator's
[hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey), not on the validator as a
general identity. The hotkey is the staking destination that ties nominator support to a particular
validator instance.

## Nominate and Stake Context

Nominate is the action vocabulary for staking TAO with a validator. Nominator is the role label for
the holder associated with that action
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

Stake is the related amount vocabulary. Delegated TAO contributes to the validator's stake context,
while the nominator term identifies who supplied that support
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Wallet Authority Boundary

Nominator describes the holder side of a delegated-stake relationship, not control over the
validator's wallet. Bittensor wallet documentation keeps wallet authority tied to key ownership,
while delegation documentation describes staking TAO to validators
([Bittensor wallets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/getting-started/wallets/),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

That boundary keeps role vocabulary separate from custody. A nominator can supply delegated stake
without receiving the validator's private keys or becoming the validator account.

## Emissions and Dividends

Nominators sit in the broader staking-emissions picture through delegated stake. Emissions
documentation distinguishes validator dividends and staking roles from miner incentives
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

That does not make nominator a reward quote or validator ranking term. It names the role that
supplies delegated TAO support; dividends are an outcome term in the emission system.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. Nominator examples
from one environment should not be treated as evidence for another because validator sets, balances,
and delegation relationships are environment-specific
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Localnet examples can test nomination wiring in isolation. Testnet examples add shared
non-production staking state. Mainnet nominator interpretation concerns production delegation
relationships on the active network.

## Delegated Stake Adds to Effective Stake

Delegation vocabulary describes nominators as TAO holders who stake with validators. That delegated
TAO combines with a delegate's own stake to form
[effective stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake), the total
staked amount associated with the receiving validator
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

Nominator language names the supplying side of that total. Effective stake names the combined
backing behind the validator after both self-stake and nomination are counted.

## Validator Take Splits the Nominator Return

[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) documentation
places [validator take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-) inside
the distribution stage that follows consensus. The validator keeps its configured share from
emissions tied to delegated stake before the remainder flows to stakers.

That split explains why nomination does not automatically mean receiving the full validator-side
emission total. Take vocabulary names the validator-kept portion; nominator vocabulary names the
TAO-holder role whose return depends on that later split
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Nominate Is the On-Chain Staking Action

The [Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate) names the
action of staking TAO with a validator, while nominator names the holder role associated with that
relationship. Delegation documentation treats nomination as attaching TAO support to a chosen
validator rather than transferring wallet control
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

Readers comparing terms should keep action and role separate. Nominate describes what a TAO holder
does; nominator describes who supplies the delegated support in staking vocabulary.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

On a subnet such as netuid 1, nominator and delegate stake name opposite sides of the same staking
relationship behind one validator hotkey. A nominator supplies nominated TAO support, while delegate
stake names only the TAO the delegate stakes personally
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

For readers comparing figures on netuid 1, nominator vocabulary identifies who attached support,
while delegate stake reports the validator-supplied amount only. Effective stake counts both
together when a reader needs the full backing total behind the hotkey
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Nominator** — TAO-holder role supplying delegated support.
- **Delegate stake** — validator self-staked TAO on the same hotkey.

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Nominator and delegation rewards answer different questions on a subnet such as netuid 1. Nominator
names the holder role that supplies delegated support, while delegation rewards are the proportional
returns that delegated stake earns after validator take at tempo distribution
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

For readers, nominator is role vocabulary on the supplying side of delegation, and delegation
rewards are outcome vocabulary for what that support earns after consensus and take. Nomination
attaches support; rewards describe the credited return that follows on netuid 1
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

## Distinction from Senate

A nominator is a TAO holder who stakes with a validator. The Senate is the governance body formed
from the top delegate keys by stake. Delegated stake a nominator supplies counts toward the total
stake that can qualify a delegate for a Senate seat, but nomination does not make the nominator a
senator ([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

- **Nominator** — TAO-holder role supplying delegated stake to a validator.
- **Senate** — governance body whose seats are gated by delegate stake totals.

## Distinction from Delegate

A nominator is the TAO-holder role that stakes behind a delegate. A delegate is the subnet validator
role that receives that nominated stake and performs validation work
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Nominator** — supplying role that attaches TAO support to a chosen validator.
- **Delegate** — receiving validator role that performs validation and earns validator take.

The two words name opposite ends of the same staking relationship rather than interchangeable labels
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

## Reader Boundary

Nominator should not be collapsed into delegate. The nominator supplies support, and the delegate
receives support as the validator role
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The role label does not by itself identify a subnet, validator, balance, or reward outcome.
