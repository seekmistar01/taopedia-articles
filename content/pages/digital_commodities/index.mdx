# Digital Commodities

Digital commodities are the useful outputs that Bittensor subnets are designed to produce. A subnet
sets the work target, miners produce work for that target, and validators evaluate the results
([Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term keeps attention on the product of a subnet. It is not only a label for token incentives or
for the software that performs the work.

## Distinction from Subnet

A digital commodity and a subnet name an output and the market that produces it. A subnet is the
incentive-based competition market that defines a task and rewards the participants who serve it,
while a digital commodity is the specific kind of useful work that market is built to produce, so
the subnet is the mechanism and the commodity is what comes out of it
([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Subnet** — the incentive-based competition market that defines and rewards the work.
- **Digital commodity** — the useful output that market is built to produce.

## Distinction from Subnet Task

A digital commodity and a subnet task name an output and the work request that produces it. A subnet
task is the work miners are asked to perform under a subnet's incentive design, while a digital
commodity is the useful output produced when miners respond to that task
([Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

- **Subnet task** — the work miners are asked to perform.
- **Digital commodity** — the useful output produced in response.

## Distinction from Incentive Mechanism

A digital commodity and an incentive mechanism name an output and the rules that define and reward
it. The incentive mechanism specifies what work counts, how it is evaluated, and how scores feed
rewards, while a digital commodity is the evaluated output that mechanism is built to produce
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)).

- **Incentive mechanism** — the task, evaluation, and reward rules.
- **Digital commodity** — the evaluated output those rules produce.

## Distinction from Subnet Creator

Digital commodities name the work outputs miners produce inside a subnet. A subnet creator is the
participant role that registers and operates a subnet market
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Digital commodities** — useful outputs miners produce under subnet rules.
- **Subnet creator** — participant role that creates and runs a subnet market.

Product vocabulary names miner output; creator vocabulary names who operates the subnet
([Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)).

## Distinction from Subnet Markets

Digital commodities name the useful outputs a subnet asks miners to produce. Subnet markets
vocabulary emphasizes the local incentive market frame with pool and emission routing
([Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Digital commodities** — evaluated work products miners produce for a subnet.
- **Subnet markets** — local market frame for incentive competition and routing.

Product vocabulary names what work means; markets vocabulary names the competitive market context
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Digital commodities name the useful digital work Bittensor subnets ask miners to produce. Yuma
Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts validator evaluations of that work into emission
shares each tempo
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Digital commodities** — useful digital work produced in subnets.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares.
  Digital commodities name miner work products; Yuma processes validator weights into rewards
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Distinction from Subnet Work

A digital commodity is subnet-specific. Different subnets can target different kinds of useful
digital output, while still using Bittensor's shared role and incentive vocabulary
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That subnet boundary matters because the commodity is not a universal object across the entire
network. It is the work category a particular subnet asks miners to produce and validators to judge.

## Distinction from Multiple-Mechanism

Some subnets can use multiple incentive mechanisms. In that case, each mechanism can define a
separate work-and-evaluation path, so the commodity context should stay tied to the mechanism being
discussed
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)).

The broader idea stays the same. A digital commodity is the useful output a subnet asks for, while
the mechanism defines how that output is assessed and rewarded.

## Distinction from Mechanism

Digital commodities name the evaluated work product. Mechanism vocabulary names the evaluation rule
set that defines how that product is scored inside a subnet
([Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

- **Digital commodities** — useful outputs miners produce for a subnet.
- **Mechanism** — evaluation rule set defining how work is scored.

Product vocabulary names what is produced; mechanism vocabulary names how it is evaluated
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Reader Boundary

Digital commodities should be read as Bittensor subnet-output vocabulary
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)). The stable
point is the relationship among requested work, miner production, validator evaluation, and emission
incentives.

This article does not rank subnets, measure output quality, provide mining instructions, or claim
that every submitted output is useful. Commodity claims should be read through the relevant subnet
and incentive mechanism.
