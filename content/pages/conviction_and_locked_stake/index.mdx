# Conviction and Locked Stake

Conviction and locked stake describe a Bittensor staking feature where subnet alpha stake can be
locked so that it builds a time-based commitment signal
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The feature is commitment vocabulary. It does not create a separate token, a separate subnet role,
or a general staking recommendation.

## Locked Alpha Stake

Locked stake is subnet alpha stake constrained by a lock. The conviction staking documentation
explains that the locked amount limits how far the staked alpha balance on that subnet can fall
while the lock is active
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

That makes locked stake narrower than ordinary stake. The stake still belongs to a subnet alpha
context, but the locked portion carries an additional commitment constraint.

The subnet context should stay visible whenever the term is used. Conviction is tied to locked alpha
in a subnet position, not to every staking balance in Bittensor.

## Conviction Score

Conviction is the score that grows from locked stake over time. The locked amount and the passage of
time both matter, so conviction should be read as a maturing commitment signal rather than a simple
balance number
([Conviction score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#the-conviction-score),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

This keeps conviction separate from stake itself. Stake names the amount associated with a staking
position; conviction names the time-based signal built from locked stake.

## Conviction Approaches the Locked Amount on a Curve, Not a Straight Line

In perpetual mode, conviction does not climb toward the locked amount at a constant rate. It rises
quickly at first and then levels off, approaching but never quite reaching the full locked mass. The
conviction-staking documentation gives concrete checkpoints along that curve: conviction reaches
about 63% of the locked amount after one maturity time constant has passed, and about 90% after
roughly 2.3 time constants
([Conviction score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#the-conviction-score)).

That maturity time constant is itself a governance-settable value rather than a fixed duration, so
the same elapsed time can represent different progress toward full conviction on different subnets
or after a parameter change. The shape of the curve is stable; the pace at which any specific lock
climbs it is not.

## Lock Modes

The conviction model includes decaying and perpetual modes. Decaying mode reduces the locked amount
over time, while perpetual mode keeps the locked amount from decaying until the mode changes
([Decaying and perpetual modes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#decaying-and-perpetual-modes),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

The mode affects how the commitment signal evolves. It should not be treated as a separate asset
type or as a promise about validator rewards.

## Movement Boundary

Locked stake should be read as a movement constraint on an existing subnet alpha staking position,
not as a new wallet or a separate token balance. The conviction-staking documentation describes the
lock in terms of how far the staked alpha balance can fall, while subnet staking keeps the position
inside subnet-local alpha context
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

That distinction keeps the commitment signal separate from custody language. The lock constrains
movement of the staked alpha position; it does not rename the asset or make conviction a wallet
authority term.

## Surplus Stake Above the Lock Stays Movable

[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)
documentation describes the lock as a floor on how low total staked
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha) on a
subnet can fall for one coldkey. Stake above that locked floor is not itself locked and can leave
through ordinary [Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)
paths
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

The same guide also states that a coldkey can keep staking additional alpha on the subnet after a
lock exists. The lock blocks only reductions below the locked amount; it does not cap how much new
alpha can be added to the position.

That reading separates the commitment floor from the full staking position. Conviction names the
signal built from the locked portion, while surplus alpha above the floor remains ordinary movable
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake) on the same subnet
position.

## Exiting a Lock Starts With a Public Signal

New locks begin in decaying mode by default under conviction documentation. A holder who wants the
locked mass to stop shrinking must explicitly switch to perpetual mode; returning to decaying mode
starts the unwind path instead
([Decaying and perpetual modes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#decaying-and-perpetual-modes),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

The documentation treats that mode change as visible commitment vocabulary rather than a silent
local setting. Switching from perpetual back to decaying is recorded on chain immediately, and the
following decay period exists so other stakers can observe a large holder winding down before the
lock fully clears.

That timing keeps conviction aligned with its role as a public commitment signal. A reader watching
subnet stake should treat a decaying-mode switch as advance notice that locked mass will shrink over
time rather than as an instant release of the whole position.

## Decaying Conviction Can Outlast the Remaining Lock

In decaying mode, conviction documentation describes two linked curves: locked mass erodes over time
while conviction first rises and then falls. Because conviction accumulates maturing time from the
lock, it can peak partway through the decay and later remain higher than the alpha still locked at
that moment
([The conviction score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#the-conviction-score),
[Decaying and perpetual modes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#decaying-and-perpetual-modes)).

That behavior makes decaying conviction a record of past commitment rather than a live mirror of
current locked holdings. After the lock has been shrinking for long enough, the score can reflect
duration already served even when much of the original locked mass is gone.

Readers comparing conviction to wallet balances should therefore keep the two separate. Stake names
what is held now; decaying conviction can still carry weight from earlier locked periods until both
values finish falling toward zero.

## Locked Stake, Conviction, and Yuma on netuid 1

On a subnet such as netuid 1, locked
[alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha) stake constrains how far
a staked position can fall while building a public conviction signal
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

That commitment path sits on subnet staking vocabulary rather than on
[validator weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector) or
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) scoring
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Conviction therefore names a time-based commitment signal on netuid 1; it does not replace emission
allocation or validator evaluation vocabulary.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For conviction and
locked stake on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how lock-state examples should be
read.

In localnet, conviction and locked stake on netuid 1 can be tested in isolation. Local lock state
and alpha balances reflect that test chain rather than production subnet staking behavior.

On testnet, conviction examples on netuid 1 apply in a shared non-production network where lock
state and subnet conditions are separate from mainnet.

On mainnet, conviction and locked stake on netuid 1 concern production subnet alpha and production
subnet state ([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A conviction example from one environment should not be
read as evidence for another environment.

## Locked Stake Constrains Unstaking Below the Floor

Locked stake limits how far a subnet alpha staking position can fall while the lock remains active.
Conviction-staking documentation describes the lock as a floor on total staked alpha for one coldkey
on a subnet, so reductions below that locked amount are blocked until the lock unwinds
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

That constraint is the practical unstaking delay for the locked portion. Stake above the locked
floor can still leave through ordinary
[unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking) paths, but the locked mass
itself cannot be withdrawn until decaying mode erodes it or the holder changes lock mode according
to conviction documentation
([Decaying and perpetual modes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#decaying-and-perpetual-modes)).

For readers, the delay is intentional rather than a wallet bug. Switching from perpetual back to
decaying mode is recorded on chain immediately, and the following decay period gives other stakers
time to observe a large holder winding down before the lock fully clears
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

Conviction then names the time-based signal built while that locked mass remains in place. It grows
from locked stake over time and can outlast the remaining locked amount in decaying mode because it
records maturing commitment rather than mirroring every movable alpha unit in the wallet
([Conviction score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#the-conviction-score)).

Locked stake therefore combines movement constraint with public commitment vocabulary. It is not a
separate token and does not replace validator evaluation or emission allocation terms
([Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Stake weight names the influence measure derived from staked value. Conviction names the separate
time-based commitment signal produced by locking stake
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

## Distinction from Staking

Staking and locked stake act on a position at different points. Staking is the act of attaching
alpha support inside a subnet, while locked stake applies a commitment constraint to alpha that is
already staked, so a position can be staked without being locked
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

- **Staking** — attaching alpha support inside a subnet.
- **Locked stake** — a commitment constraint on already-staked alpha.

## Distinction from Unstaking

Locked stake and unstaking sit on opposite sides of withdrawal. Unstaking is the ordinary path that
converts staked alpha back toward TAO, while locked stake is the floor that blocks reductions below
the locked amount until the lock unwinds; surplus above the floor can still unstake normally
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking)).

- **Unstaking** — the ordinary withdrawal of staked alpha.
- **Locked stake** — the floor that blocks withdrawal below the locked amount.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Conviction and locked stake describe a staker locking alpha to build a time-based commitment that
cannot be reduced below the locked amount, while validator take is the share of delegated-stake
emissions a validator keeps before the rest flows to stakers
([Conviction Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Glossary: Validator Take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take)).

Staker-commitment vocabulary names a locked position; validator-take vocabulary names the
validator's commission on emissions.

## Distinction from Conviction Score

Locked stake is the floor on subnet alpha that cannot be reduced below the locked amount. Conviction
score is the time-based commitment signal built while that locked mass remains in place
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[The conviction score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#the-conviction-score)).

- **Locked stake** — minimum alpha mass that cannot be reduced below the locked floor.
- **Conviction score** — time-based signal built from locked commitment over time.

In decaying mode, conviction can outlast the remaining locked amount because it records maturing
commitment rather than mirroring every movable alpha unit
([Decaying and perpetual modes](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking#decaying-and-perpetual-modes)).

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Conviction and locked stake name time-locked alpha staking that builds a public commitment signal.
Yuma Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts validator weight submissions into emission
shares each tempo
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Conviction and locked stake** — time-locked alpha with a commitment signal.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Distinction from Subnet

Conviction and locked stake apply to subnet alpha already staked inside a specific subnet. Alpha
tokens are subnet-specific, and staking into subnets places stake in subnet-local context
([Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).

The concept is therefore separate from Root Subnet TAO staking and from ordinary unlocked stake. An
alpha position can be staked without being locked; conviction begins when part of that staked alpha
is locked for commitment signaling.

## Reader Boundary

Conviction and locked stake are commitment vocabulary for subnet alpha, not a separate token, a
validator recommendation, or a reward forecast
([Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking),
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

Conviction names the time-based signal built from locked stake; stake names the amount in the
position. Root Subnet TAO staking and ordinary unlocked alpha stake are separate paths
([Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)).
