# Delegation

Delegation is the process of staking TAO to a validator. It names the support relationship between a
TAO holder and the validator receiving that stake
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term should be read separately from the validator role and from stake as an amount. Delegation
is relationship vocabulary inside the staking model.

## What It Represents

Delegation creates an ongoing support position: nominated TAO stays attached to a validator hotkey
and earns a proportional share over time until the holder moves or removes it
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

On an alpha subnet, delegated TAO enters that subnet's pool and is tracked in alpha behind the
validator hotkey. On the root subnet, delegation keeps stake TAO-denominated instead
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets),
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)).

Delegation examples from one network environment should not be read as representing production
staking relationships on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Delegate

Delegation names staking TAO to support a validator hotkey. Delegate names the receiving validator
role that nominators support
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

- **Delegation** — the support relationship created by staking TAO behind a validator hotkey.
- **Delegate** — the validator role that receives that nominated support.

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Delegation is TAO support staked behind a validator hotkey on a subnet. Delegation rewards are the
share credited back to nominators after the validator keeps its configured take from delegated-stake
emissions
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Delegation** — support attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Delegation rewards** — the credited share after validator take.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is the TAO holder who delegates. Delegation is the stake relationship to a validator
hotkey on a subnet, not the holder's wallet role
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

- **Delegation** — the support relationship to a validator hotkey.
- **Nominator** — the TAO holder who supplies that support.

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Delegation attaches TAO support to a validator hotkey. Validator permit is the separate on-chain
flag that allows a hotkey to validate and set weights on a subnet
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

- **Delegation** — nominated TAO support attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Validator permit** — the on-chain flag that allows a hotkey to validate and set weights.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

Delegate stake is the TAO a validator stakes from their own balance. Delegation is nominated TAO
that other holders attach to that validator hotkey
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

- **Delegation** — nominated TAO support from other holders.
- **Delegate stake** — TAO the validator stakes from their own balance.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Delegation is the process of attaching nominated TAO to a validator hotkey. Effective stake is the
resulting total TAO associated with that delegate after self-stake and nominations are counted
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Delegation** — the process of attaching nominated TAO to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — total TAO associated with a delegate after self-stake and nominations.

## Distinction from Nominate

Delegation names the ongoing support relationship after TAO is staked to a validator. Nominate is
the extrinsic action that attaches that support to the chosen validator on a subnet
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate)).

- **Delegation** — the ongoing support relationship after TAO is staked to a validator.
- **Nominate** — the on-chain action that attaches that support to a validator hotkey.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Delegation is attaching TAO support to a chosen validator. Autostaking is automation that routes a
miner's newly earned emissions into such a staking position without a manual step
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

- **Delegation** — attaching TAO support to a validator to share its rewards.
- **Autostaking** — automatically routing earned emissions into a staking position.

## Distinction from Coldkey

Delegation describes where stake support is assigned, not a transfer of wallet signing authority
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

- **Delegation** — the support relationship created by staking TAO behind a validator hotkey.
- **Coldkey** — the custody anchor that controls balances and authorizes sensitive wallet actions.

## Distinction from Stake

Stake names the amount associated with a validator position after support exists. Delegation is one
path by which that support becomes attached
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

- **Delegation** — the process of attaching nominated TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Stake** — the amount associated with a validator position after support exists.

## Distinction from Dividends

Delegation establishes the support position behind a validator hotkey. Dividends describe an
emission share that can follow from delegated-stake relationships after validator-side distribution
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

- **Delegation** — the support relationship created by staking TAO behind a validator hotkey.
- **Dividends** — the emission share credited to stakers after validator-side distribution.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Delegation is the support relationship created when a TAO holder stakes toward a validator hotkey.
Yuma Consensus converts submitted validator weights into emission shares after evaluation completes
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Delegation** — support relationship from staking TAO toward a validator.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Reader Boundary

Delegation is not a validator recommendation, balance report, fee explanation, or staking guide. It
names staking TAO to a validator as support inside Bittensor's staking model
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Staking

Delegation and staking overlap but are not identical. Staking is the general act of committing TAO
to a hotkey to back its consensus weight, while delegation is staking specifically to a validator
you do not operate, assigning your stake's weight to that validator in return for a share of its
rewards ([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — committing TAO to a hotkey to back its weight.
- **Delegation** — staking to a validator you do not operate, for a share of its rewards.
