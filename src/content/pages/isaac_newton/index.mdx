**Sir Isaac Newton** FRS (25 December 1642 – 20 March 1726/27) was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, alchemist, theologian, and author who is widely recognized as one of the greatest mathematicians and physicists of all time.

## Early Life

Newton was born in Woolsthorpe Manor in Lincolnshire, England. His father died three months before he was born. When Newton was three, his mother remarried and went to live with her new husband, leaving her son in the care of his maternal grandmother.

## Scientific Work

### Mathematics

Newton made seminal contributions to Mathematics, including:

- Development of Calculus (independently discovered alongside Leibniz)
- Generalized binomial theorem
- Newton's method for finding roots of functions

### Physics

His work in [[Physics]] revolutionized the field:

- **Laws of Motion**: Newton formulated the three laws of motion, forming the foundation of classical mechanics
- **Universal Gravitation**: His law of [[Gravity]] explained planetary motion and terrestrial physics
- **Optics**: Discovered that white light is composed of a spectrum of colors

## Principia Mathematica

In 1687, Newton published *Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica* (Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy), often called the *Principia*. This work laid the foundations for classical mechanics and is considered one of the most important works in the history of science.

## Later Life

Newton was elected as a member of Parliament, served as Master of the Royal Mint, and was knighted by Queen Anne in 1705, becoming Sir Isaac Newton.

He never married and died in his sleep in London on 20 March 1726/27, at age 84.

## Legacy

Newton's work dominated the scientific view of the physical universe for over three hundred years, until [[Albert Einstein]]'s theory of General Relativity provided a more comprehensive explanation.
