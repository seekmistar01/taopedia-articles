# Liquidity Provider Rewards

Liquidity provider rewards are the trading fees a provider earns for supplying liquidity to a
subnet's pool. Positions accumulate fees from staking and unstaking activity that flows through the
provider's selected price range
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

## What It Represents

On netuid 1, documentation describes concentrated liquidity positions earning fees when other
participants stake or unstake inside the provider's range. Accumulated fees stay in the position
until the provider modifies or removes it; a position does not earn fees from staking by the owning
coldkey ([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions),
[User Liquidity Positions: Fee Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions#fee-distribution)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's fee-accrual path. They should not be read as
representing production provider returns on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Fee income sits opposite impermanent loss, the price-drift exposure a provider carries while
liquidity remains in the pool
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Liquidity provider rewards are fee income from trades crossing a provided range. Delegation rewards
are a share of a validator's emissions for delegated stake
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Liquidity provider rewards** — fees from activity crossing a liquidity range.
- **Delegation rewards** — share of validator emissions for delegated stake.

## Distinction from Impermanent Loss

Impermanent loss is the hold-versus-pool opportunity cost from price drift. Liquidity provider
rewards are fee income that can offset that cost
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

- **Impermanent loss** — opportunity cost from price drift while in the pool.
- **Liquidity provider rewards** — fee income from supported activity.

## Distinction from User Liquidity Positions

User liquidity positions define where and how much liquidity a provider supplies. Liquidity provider
rewards are the fee income those positions earn
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

- **User liquidity positions** — concentrated position structure and range placement.
- **Liquidity provider rewards** — fee income from others' activity in range.

## Distinction from Emission

Subnet emissions route miner and validator shares through tempo distribution and Yuma Consensus.
Liquidity provider rewards accumulate as trading fees inside a supplied range and withdraw
separately ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Liquidity provider rewards** — pool fee balances from supported activity.
- **Emission** — broader subnet reward and injection flow.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Liquidity provider rewards are returns earned from supplying liquidity to a subnet pool within a
chosen price range. They name pool fee income from trading activity, not the recurring settlement
step that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on each subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

Earlier sections separate liquidity provider rewards from general subnet emissions. Pool fees
accumulate from swaps inside a supplied range; Yuma settles evaluation signals from eligible
validators on that netuid—not the LP position balance itself
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Readers should treat liquidity provider rewards as pool-trading fee vocabulary, and Yuma as the
epoch-boundary mechanism that allocates subnet rewards from consensus while validators remain
active.

- **Liquidity provider rewards** — returns from pool liquidity provision.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level and is not financial advice. What a position actually
earns depends on live trading activity, the chosen range, and prices at the time of observation
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).
