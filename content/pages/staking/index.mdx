# Staking

Staking is the process of attaching TAO support to a validator in Bittensor
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## What It Represents

Staking is process vocabulary. Stake is the related amount vocabulary after support is attached
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

The term names validator support, not a validator recommendation, balance report, fee explanation,
or full staking guide. Delegation describes staking TAO to a validator; staking is the broader
process name ([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Staking examples depend on which network you connect to. Delegation and stake-weight readings from
one environment should not be read as production behavior on another
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Delegation

Staking names attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey. Delegation names the recorded support
relationship behind that hotkey
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

- **Staking** — the action of attaching TAO support.
- **Delegation** — the recorded support relationship behind a hotkey.

## Distinction from Stake

Stake names the amount of tokens delegated to a validator. Staking names the process of attaching
that support ([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

- **Staking** — the process of attaching support.
- **Stake** — the token amount after support is attached.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake names the combined delegate-owned and delegated TAO behind a hotkey. Staking names
the act of adding that support
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

- **Staking** — the act of adding support behind a hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — the combined TAO total behind that hotkey.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Stake weight is the interpreted stake measure used for validator consensus power and emissions in a
subnet ([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Staking** — attaches support behind a validator.
- **Stake weight** — the downstream consensus measure derived from stake.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

Delegate stake names only the TAO a validator stakes from their own holdings. Staking names the
broader act of attaching support behind a hotkey
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

- **Staking** — the broader act of attaching support behind a hotkey.
- **Delegate stake** — validator-owned TAO staked from their own holdings.

## Distinction from Liquidity Positions

Liquidity positions commit range-based TAO and alpha depth inside a subnet pool. Staking converts
TAO through the pool into a delegated validator position
([User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — delegation action through pool conversion.
- **Liquidity positions** — range-based pool depth commitment.

## Distinction from Slippage

Slippage names the conversion gap when TAO moves through a subnet pool. Staking names the delegation
action that triggers that pool move
([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Staking** — the delegation intent behind pool conversion.
- **Slippage** — the received-amount difference on that conversion.

## Distinction from Dividends

Dividends name validator-side rewards from consensus outcomes. Staking names how TAO support is
attached behind a validator hotkey
([Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Staking** — support attachment behind a validator hotkey.
- **Dividends** — validator-side rewards from consensus outcomes.

## Distinction from Moving Stake

Staking adds new TAO to a validator's hotkey on a subnet. Moving stake rearranges stake already
delegated to a different hotkey and/or subnet
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — brings new value into a delegated position.
- **Moving stake** — re-points an existing attachment without adding fresh TAO.

## Distinction from TAO Reserve

Staking routes TAO into subnet alpha through paired reserves. The TAO reserve is the pool quantity
that action shifts
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Staking** — the delegation action behind pool conversion.
- **TAO reserve** — the pool quantity that action can move.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Staking names where stake is assigned toward subnet participation. Yuma Consensus turns validator
weights into credited emission shares when a tempo closes
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Staking** — committing TAO or alpha to support participation.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Autostaking routes miner emissions into a staking position automatically. Staking is the general
process of attaching TAO support to a validator
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)).

- **Staking** — the general support-attachment process.
- **Autostaking** — automatic routing of miner proceeds into a staking position.

## Reader Boundary
