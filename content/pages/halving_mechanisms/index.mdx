# Halving Mechanisms

A halving is a supply-based emission rule that reduces the rate of new token creation by 50%. In
Bittensor, halving has two scopes: TAO halvings reduce network-level TAO emission, while alpha
halvings reduce the emission rate of a specific subnet's alpha token. This distinction matters when
reading [[dynamic_tao|Dynamic TAO]] tokenomics because TAO and subnet alpha tokens do not describe
the same asset.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## What halving changes

Halving changes the rate at which new tokens are created and distributed. It does not by itself
define how miner, validator, or staker rewards are compared inside a subnet. Bittensor emission docs
describe distribution of newly created TAO and subnet-specific alpha tokens, while
[[yuma_consensus|Yuma Consensus]] explains the consensus process that ranks subnet work for reward
distribution.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## TAO halvings

A TAO halving occurs when total TAO issuance reaches supply thresholds. The official halving page
describes the event as reducing the TAO block reward by 50%, which lowers daily TAO emission and
subnet pool injections. Because timing depends on issuance rather than a fixed calendar statement,
the concept should not be reduced to a date, countdown, or short-term status note.

Reference: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Alpha halvings

Alpha halving operates at subnet scope. It reduces a subnet alpha token's emission rate after that
subnet's total alpha issuance reaches a threshold, and the official source says the adjustment
applies across miners, validators, and subnet creators in that subnet. This is different from a TAO
halving, which applies at network scope.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## TAO halvings and alpha pool injections

The halving docs separate alpha pool injections from alpha rewards during a TAO halving. When TAO
injection into subnet pools halves, the portion of alpha injected alongside TAO also halves so the
pool relationship can be maintained. Alpha rewards to miners, validators, and subnet creators do not
halve from the TAO halving alone.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)

## Relationship to Dynamic TAO

[[dynamic_tao|Dynamic TAO]] gives subnets their own alpha-token reserves and outstanding alpha, so a
halving statement needs to say whether it is about TAO, alpha pool injections, or alpha emitted to
miners, validators, and stakers. Staking and unstaking interact with subnet pools that hold TAO and
alpha reserves, while halving rules describe how new issuance into that system changes over time.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## How to read halving claims

A halving claim should state the asset, scope, and source. "TAO halving" describes a network-level
supply threshold for TAO emission. "Alpha halving" describes a subnet-level supply threshold for
that subnet's alpha token. Claims about exact emission amounts or timing should stay tied to the
official source that supports them, because those details depend on issuance and recycling.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)

## Relationship to Halving

Halving mechanisms and halving address related but different scopes in Bittensor tokenomics. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes a
reduction in token emission rate, while
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) explains TAO and alpha
halving rules in more detail.

For readers, halving is the high-level term, while halving mechanisms names the official rules for
when and how emission rates change.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Issuance

Halving mechanisms and issuance address related but different scopes in Bittensor tokenomics. The
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) describes the
circulating TAO amount in the network, while
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) explains that TAO halving
reduces the emission rate after total TAO issuance reaches supply-based thresholds.

For readers, issuance is the supply measure, while halving mechanisms names the rules that change
emission rates when those thresholds are reached.

References: [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Alpha halving applies at subnet scope. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, halving still names supply-based emission reductions for TAO or subnet alpha tokens. A
subnet may run more than one incentive mechanism, but halving rules remain tied to subnet and
network supply thresholds rather than to a single mechanism label.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Relationship to Subtensor

Halving mechanisms and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary.
Subtensor names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while halving mechanisms name
supply-based rules that reduce TAO and subnet alpha emission rates enforced within that chain layer.
The [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes
the blockchain layer, and the [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)
documentation explains TAO and alpha halving rules.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while halving mechanisms name the official
emission-rate rules applied on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Block

Halving mechanisms and a block are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. A
[block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block) names one unit of chain
progression on Subtensor, while halving mechanisms include TAO halving rules that reduce the TAO
block reward by 50% after total issuance reaches supply-based thresholds. The
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) documentation describes that
block-reward reduction as part of network-level TAO halving.

For readers, a block marks ordinary chain progression, while halving mechanisms name supply-based
rules that can change how much TAO is emitted through block rewards after an issuance threshold is
reached.

References: [Glossary: Block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Netuid

Halving mechanisms and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A netuid
identifies a subnet on the network, while halving mechanisms include alpha halving rules that reduce
a subnet alpha token's emission rate after that subnet's total alpha issuance reaches a threshold.
The [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at
the subnet level, and the [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)
documentation describes alpha halving at subnet scope.

For readers, a netuid selects which subnet is in scope, while halving mechanisms name supply-based
emission rules that can apply to that subnet's alpha token separately from network-level TAO
halving.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Tempo

Halving mechanisms and tempo are related but different timing concepts in Bittensor vocabulary.
[Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo) names the number of blocks between
subnet reward calculation cycles, while halving mechanisms name supply-based rules that reduce TAO
or subnet alpha emission rates after issuance thresholds are reached. The
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) documentation explains TAO
and alpha halving separately from subnet reward cadence.

For readers, tempo belongs to how often a subnet's reward cycle advances in blocks, while halving
mechanisms belong to when emission rates can drop under supply-based tokenomics rules.

References: [Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving)

## Relationship to Coinbase

Halving mechanisms and coinbase address related but different parts of Bittensor emission
vocabulary. Halving mechanisms are the supply-based rules that reduce TAO or subnet alpha emission
rates after their respective issuance totals reach defined thresholds — the rules that govern when
and by how much the rate drops — while coinbase is the per-block protocol operation that injects
liquidity into subnet pools, accumulates pending emissions each block, and checks epoch boundaries
to trigger Yuma Consensus rounds. The
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) documentation describes the
threshold-based emission rate rules for both TAO and subnet alpha, and the
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes the per-block accumulation process.

Coinbase's per-block emission accumulation advances the running issuance totals that halving
thresholds are measured against. Each block, coinbase adds to both the cumulative TAO supply and the
per-subnet alpha issuance — the two quantities halving mechanisms monitor. Halving mechanisms set
the rules for when and how emission rates change; coinbase runs the per-block cycle whose outputs
build the cumulative supply that those rules apply to.

References: [Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving),
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
