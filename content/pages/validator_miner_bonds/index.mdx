# Validator-Miner Bonds

Validator-miner bonds are smoothed relationships between validators and miners inside Bittensor's
Yuma Consensus process. They connect validator evaluation history to validator-side emission
outcomes, rather than naming a social endorsement or a general quality score
([Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

The term separates validator weights from the smoothed bond state used later in consensus. Validator
weights are evaluation inputs; validator-miner bonds are relationship values that help shape
dividends
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## Consensus Bonding

Yuma Consensus places bonding after validator weights are compared with consensus. Weights are fresh
evaluation signals, while bonds carry a smoothed validator-miner relationship across consensus
rounds ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)).

That makes bonds part of the validator-side reward path. Miner incentives come from aggregate miner
ranking outcomes, while validator dividends depend partly on how validator-miner bond relationships
develop through consensus
([Glossary: Incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

This distinction keeps incentives and dividends separate. Incentives describe miner-side emission
context, while dividends describe validator-side outcomes that depend on bonds and other consensus
state.

## EMA Smoothing

Validator-miner bonds are smoothed over time with exponential moving average behavior. EMA smoothing
keeps bond values from being only a one-round signal and gives the relationship a running history
inside the consensus process
([Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema),
[Glossary: Exponential Moving Average (EMA)](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#exponential-moving-average-ema)).

Smoothing is the main reason bonds should not be read as a simple copy of the latest validator
weight. A validator weight is an evaluation signal; a bond is a smoothed relationship that evolves
as consensus rounds continue
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector)).

Liquid alpha can vary the EMA smoothing factor for validator-miner bonds according to consensus
alignment. That makes liquid alpha a bond-smoothing modifier, while the bond itself remains the
smoothed validator-miner relationship
([Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights),
[Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For
validator-miner bonds on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should
interpret bond fields in metagraph readings and consensus examples.

In localnet, validator-miner bond examples can be exercised in an isolated environment. Local bond
values reflect local chain state rather than production subnet relationships.

On testnet, validator-miner bonds can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet bond
context on a selected netuid is separate from mainnet metagraph state
([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

On mainnet, validator-miner bonds are live relationship fields used in production subnet consensus
context on the connected Bittensor network
([Glossary: Validator Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A bond example from one environment should not be read as
representing production validator-miner relationships on another network.

## Distinction from Weight Setting

Weight setting is the validator action that assigns miner scores each round. Validator-miner bonds
are the smoothed relationships built from those repeated submissions
([Glossary: Weight Vector](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-vector),
[Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)).

- **Weight setting** — validator submission of miner scores each round.
- **Validator-miner bonds** — smoothed relationships accumulated from those submissions.

Action vocabulary names each round; bond vocabulary names the accumulated relationship
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Bonds Moving Average

Validator-miner bonds name the smoothed relationship values Yuma Consensus maintains between
validators and miners. Bonds moving average names the subnet hyperparameter that configures how
those bond values are averaged over time
([Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Exponential Moving Averages](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/ema),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Relationship vocabulary and smoothing-configuration vocabulary answer different questions. A bond is
the accumulated validator-miner relationship; bonds moving average is the hyperparameter that sets
how that relationship is smoothed across rounds.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Validator-miner bonds are the smoothed relationship values Yuma maintains between validators and
miners across successive passes. They name accumulated relationship state inside settlement, not the
full mechanism that clips weights and credits emission shares
([Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that reads the weight matrix, applies clipping against the
consensus score benchmark, updates bonds, and then credits miner incentives and validator dividends
at the epoch boundary
([Yuma Consensus: Clipping](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#clipping),
[Glossary: Weight Matrix](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-matrix),
[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The weight-setting section above covers per-round score submissions. Bond vocabulary names the
smoothed relationships those submissions build across rounds; Yuma vocabulary names the full
settlement pass that uses them
([Glossary: Weight Setting](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#weight-setting)).

Readers should not read bond values as dividend amounts or miner rank on their own. Bonds inform
dividend allocation inside Yuma; credited outcomes still depend on the pass on the relevant netuid
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Validator-miner bonds** — smoothed validator-miner relationships updated across Yuma passes.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns submitted weights into emission shares.

## Distinction from Dividends

Validator-miner bonds record the smoothed relationship Yuma uses during consensus. Dividends name
the validator-side emission outcome that follows bonded miner evaluation
([Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

- **Validator-miner bonds** — smoothed validator-miner relationship state.
- **Dividends** — validator-side payout that follows bonded evaluation.

Relationship vocabulary stays upstream of payout vocabulary
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Distinction from Evaluation

Subnets define work, miners produce outputs, and validators evaluate those outputs. Validator-miner
bonds sit after that evaluation step, connecting validator judgments to the consensus process that
allocates incentives and dividends
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

This keeps the bond term narrower than the whole incentive mechanism. The incentive mechanism
describes task and evaluation design, validator weights carry evaluation signals, and bonds describe
the smoothed validator-miner relationship inside Yuma Consensus
([Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)).

That order keeps the language precise. The task defines what is evaluated, weights express validator
judgment, consensus aggregates those signals, and bonds preserve a smoothed relationship over time.

## Distinction from Mechanism

Yuma Consensus 3 belongs near validator-miner bonds because YC3 refines bond scaling. The base bond
term names the smoothed validator-miner relationship, while YC3 names a refinement that makes bond
movement more responsive to useful independent evaluation
([How Yuma Consensus 3 Makes Bittensor More Fair](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yc3-blog)).

Some subnets can also run multiple incentive mechanisms. In that case, bond context can be
mechanism-specific, because separate mechanisms can maintain separate bond pools for Yuma Consensus
calculations
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)).

Mechanism-specific bonds should therefore be read with the mechanism label attached. A bond pool
from one mechanism does not automatically describe every task or evaluation path inside the subnet.

## Distinction from Timing and Emission

Coinbase is the per-block mechanism that advances emission processing and triggers epoch-boundary
activity. Validator-miner bonds are read inside the Yuma Consensus round that coinbase helps
schedule, so the two terms sit at different points in the emission path
([Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Emission is the broader tokenomics process around those outcomes. Bonds influence validator-side
dividend calculation, while emission names the value-creation and allocation flow in which those
outcomes appear ([Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## Reader Boundary

Validator-miner bonds are consensus-reference vocabulary. They are not a staking guide, validator
strategy, miner ranking table, or payout forecast
([Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Use bond language when the focus is the smoothed validator-miner relationship. Use validator-weight,
rank, incentive, dividend, or liquid-alpha language when those specific parts of the consensus path
are the focus.

That vocabulary split is practical: validator weights are evaluation signals, ranks and incentives
describe miner-side outcomes, dividends describe validator-side outcomes, and bonds describe the
smoothed relationship connecting validators to miners
([Glossary: Validator-Miner Bonds](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-miner-bonds)).
