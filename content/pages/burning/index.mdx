# Burning

Burning is the removal of tokens from spendable circulation. In Bittensor tokenomics, the term helps
readers distinguish tokens that are destroyed or otherwise removed from normal circulation from
tokens that are distributed, staked, transferred, or recycled through another mechanism.

References: [Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Tokenomics Context

Burning is a supply-side concept. It describes what happens to tokens, not who earned them or
whether a subnet is valuable. That distinction matters because Bittensor articles may discuss
registration costs, emissions, staking, and recycling in the same tokenomics area, but each term
describes a different movement or accounting treatment of TAO.

References: [Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Recycling

Burning and recycling are related but not identical. Burning emphasizes removal from spendable
circulation. Recycling emphasizes reuse or redistribution through a protocol process. Keeping the
terms separate helps readers avoid treating every tokenomics sink as the same event.

Reference:
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Effect on Issuance

The [Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning) explains
that burned tokens remain counted in the record of token issuance even though they no longer
effectively circulate. Because they stay in that record, they are not re-emitted.

## Circulation Removal Context

The
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
describes removal from circulation as tokens that exist in neither wallet nor liquidity pool and
cannot be transacted. Burning names one form of that removal rather than recycling tokens back into
the record of issuance for future emission.

References: [Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Issuance

Burning and issuance address related but different parts of Bittensor supply accounting. The
[Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) describes TAO
circulating in the network, while the
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
explains that burned tokens remain in the issuance record even though they no longer effectively
circulate.

For readers, issuance names the circulating supply record, while burning describes removal from
transactable circulation without making that quantity available for future emission.

References: [Glossary: Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Burning removes tokens from spendable circulation. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, burning still names a network tokenomics sink. Subnet incentive mechanisms distribute
emissions among participants, but burning describes supply removal rather than per-mechanism miner
evaluation.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the term. It does not report live burn amounts, predict token price, or provide
investment advice. Readers should use current official sources when they need exact network values.

## Relationship to Subtensor

Burning and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor names
Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while burning names removal of tokens from
spendable circulation recorded through chain-layer tokenomics accounting. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning) describes removal
from spendable circulation.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while burning names a supply-side removal concept
processed on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning)

## Relationship to Extrinsics

Burning and extrinsics are related but different parts of Bittensor chain action vocabulary.
Extrinsics name signed transactions and calls submitted to Subtensor, while burning names tokens
removed from spendable circulation when certain state-changing actions are processed on chain. The
[Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics) describes
signed transactions submitted to the chain, and the
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning) describes removal
from spendable circulation.

For readers, extrinsics name submitted chain actions, while burning names the supply removal outcome
that some of those actions can produce.

References: [Glossary: Extrinsics](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#extrinsics),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning)

## Relationship to Netuid

Burning and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A netuid identifies a
subnet on the network, while burning can include TAO removed during subnet-entry actions such as
registration burns tied to a selected subnet. The
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level, and the
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register) describes subnet
entry as purchasing a UID slot through a dynamic registration burn in TAO.

For readers, a netuid selects which subnet context is in scope, while burning names TAO removal that
can occur as part of subnet-entry actions in that context.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)

## Relationship to Halving

Burning and halving are related but different parts of Bittensor tokenomics vocabulary. The
[Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving) describes a
reduction in token emission rate, while the
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning) describes removal
from spendable circulation. The
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
also explains that burned tokens remain in the issuance record even though they no longer
effectively circulate.

For readers, halving names an emission-rate schedule change, while burning names supply removal
without returning that quantity to future emission.

References: [Glossary: Halving](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#halving),
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning),
[Glossary: Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)

## Relationship to Coinbase

Burning and coinbase are related but different parts of Bittensor tokenomics vocabulary. Burning is
the removal of tokens from spendable circulation — the supply-removal concept that describes tokens
permanently taken out of any wallet or liquidity pool and not re-emitted. Coinbase is the per-block
protocol mechanism that drives TAO emission, injects TAO into subnet pool reserves each block,
accumulates pending emissions, and checks epoch boundaries to trigger Yuma Consensus rounds. The
[Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning) describes removal
from spendable circulation, and the
[Coinbase Implementation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/navigating-subtensor/emissions-coinbase)
documentation describes the per-block emission operation.

The two concepts sit on opposite sides of the supply ledger. Coinbase adds to subnet pool balances
and accumulates pending emissions each block, building the TAO and alpha quantities that flow to
validators, miners, and stakers when a subnet's Yuma Consensus epoch runs. Burning permanently
removes tokens from that flow — tokens that enter a burn are no longer available in any wallet or
pool and are not re-emitted through the coinbase pipeline. Coinbase names what enters the emission
system each block; burning names what exits spendable circulation without return.
