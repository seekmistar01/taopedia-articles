# Burning

Burning is the removal of tokens from spendable circulation
([Glossary: Burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burning)). In Bittensor
tokenomics, the term helps separate supply removal from emission, recycling, and ordinary staking
vocabulary.

## Supply-Side Concept

Burning is a supply-side term. It describes what happens to tokens, not who earned them or whether a
subnet is valuable. The
[Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
glossary entry places burning in token-removal vocabulary.

That makes burning different from reward allocation. Emission describes token creation and
distribution; burning describes removal from spendable circulation.

## Burning and Recycling

Burning and recycling are related but not interchangeable. The
[Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
entry describes recycling as subtracting tokens from the issuance record so the same quantity can be
emitted again later. Burning removes tokens from spendable circulation without making them available
for future emission in the same way.

This boundary matters because both terms can appear near token sinks. Recycling is a reuse path;
burning is a removal path.

## Issuance Record

Burning also has issuance context. The
[Recycling and burning](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning)
glossary entry explains that burned tokens remain counted in the record of token issuance even
though they no longer effectively circulate. The
[Issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) entry describes circulating
TAO supply.

This means burning changes spendable circulation without erasing the issuance record language used
in tokenomics references.

## Emission Boundary

Burning and emission sit on different sides of tokenomics vocabulary. The
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation describes token creation
and allocation. Burning describes removal from spendable circulation.

The distinction is simple: emission describes token flow into reward and subnet contexts, while
burning describes token removal from ordinary circulation.

## Halving Boundary

Burning and halving are also distinct. The
[Halving Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/halving) documentation describes
scheduled reductions in emission rate, while burning describes supply removal. Both can affect how
readers think about token supply, but they are different mechanisms.

Burning is therefore not an emission-rate schedule. It is a token-removal concept.

## Reading Boundaries

Burning is the right term when the focus is removal from spendable circulation. If the focus is
reuse for future emission,
[recycling](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#recycling-and-burning) is more
precise. If the focus is circulating TAO supply,
[issuance](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#issuance) is more precise. If the
focus is token creation and allocation, [emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)
is more precise.

These boundaries keep burning focused on supply removal, not market claims, subnet rankings, or a
full explanation of every tokenomics flow.
