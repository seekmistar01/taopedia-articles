# Triumvirate

The Triumvirate is the Bittensor governance body responsible for originating proposals. It belongs
to the proposal-creation side of Bittensor governance, while the Senate belongs to the review and
voting side
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

## Composition

The glossary identifies the Triumvirate as a three-person group associated with the Opentensor
Foundation
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

That composition is separate from the Senate. The Senate is the governance body that reviews and
votes on proposals after they have been put forward
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

## Governance Role

Bittensor governance separates proposal origin from proposal review. The Triumvirate creates
proposals, and the Senate approves or rejects them
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

This gives the term a narrow scope: Triumvirate names where a proposal begins in the governance
process. It does not name the voting body, the proposal itself, or the later status of that
proposal.

The governance overview also frames this split as part of Bittensor's replacement for direct
sudo-style privileged control. In the bicameral model, privileged changes move through proposal
origin and Senate review instead of depending on one administrative key
([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance),
[Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo)).

Bicameral legislature is the wider structure that contains both roles. Triumvirate vocabulary names
only the origin side of that structure, while bicameral legislature names the two-body arrangement
that pairs proposal creation with Senate review
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature)).
That keeps Triumvirate narrower than a full governance-system summary and wider than a single
proposal item.

## Proposal Relationship

A proposal is the governance item put forward by the Triumvirate for Senate voting
([Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)).

The Triumvirate is therefore not the proposal itself. It is the body associated with proposal
origin, while the proposal is the item that moves into review. This distinction keeps governance
body language separate from proposal-status language.

## Senate Relationship

The Senate reviews Triumvirate proposals and casts approval or disapproval votes
([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)). That relationship keeps governance
authority divided between creation and review.

Triumvirate language should point to proposal creation. Senate language should point to proposal
review and voting. Approval, rejection, and later application belong to the proposal path, not to
the Triumvirate term alone.

## Relationship to Sudo

Triumvirate and sudo are related but different governance terms. Triumvirate names the
proposal-origin body that puts privileged changes forward for Senate review, while sudo names the
legacy single-key privileged authority that the bicameral governance model replaced; in that model,
privileged changes move through proposal origin and Senate review instead of depending on one
administrative key
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Glossary: Sudo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#sudo),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

Readers should not treat the Triumvirate as a sudo-style key holder, or read sudo as an active step
in the proposal path. Triumvirate names proposal origin in the governance model, while sudo names
the privileged authority that model replaced.

## Subtensor Context

Subtensor is Bittensor's layer-one blockchain and system of record
([Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor)). Triumvirate
proposals matter to Subtensor because approved privileged changes can affect that chain layer
through governance ([Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

That does not make the Triumvirate a chain layer. It is a governance body whose proposals can lead
to privileged Subtensor changes after review. Subtensor names the system affected by governance,
while Triumvirate names one body in the governance path.

## Network Context

Bittensor documentation separates localnet, testnet, and mainnet environments
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Triumvirate examples should keep that environment boundary attached. A localnet or testnet example
can illustrate governance mechanics, but it is not evidence of a mainnet governance outcome.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For Triumvirate,
this sequence gives readers a boundary for interpreting governance examples and role-boundary notes.

Localnet examples are isolated and reflect local chain state, so they are useful for controlled
experiments rather than evidence of live Bittensor behavior. Testnet examples add shared
non-production conditions, which can reveal integration behavior without touching mainnet state.

On mainnet, Triumvirate examples should be read as governance behavior on the production Bittensor
network.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet, so outcomes from one environment should not be treated as
proof of behavior in another.

## Senate Approval and Triumvirate Close Both Execute Proposals

The [Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) states that proposals execute
only after Senate majority approval and a Triumvirate member close. Triumvirate vocabulary therefore
covers proposal origin and the close step, while Senate vocabulary covers the approval gate between
them ([Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

Neither body alone completes the path described there. Triumvirate reading should not imply that
creating a proposal applies privileged calldata without the Senate review and close stages that
governance documentation requires.

## Foundation Employees Hold the Origin Seats

[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance) material describes Triumvirate
members as Opentensor Foundation employees, while the Senate is formed from top delegate hotkeys.
That split keeps proposal creation and Senate membership on different selection paths
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate)).

For readers, Triumvirate names the staffed origin body that puts proposals forward. Senate names the
delegate-hotkey approval body that reviews them before close and execution.

## Close Applies Calldata in the Same Block

When a proposal is executed, governance documentation states that the calldata passed during
creation is included in the same block as the close extrinsic. Triumvirate close vocabulary
therefore marks the execution moment rather than the earlier broadcast or Senate vote stages alone
([Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)).

Readers should treat close as the governance step that ties an approved proposal to on-chain
inclusion, separate from proposal creation or senator approval by itself.

## Distinction from Bicameral Legislature

A triumvirate and a bicameral legislature name one body and the two-body structure that contains it.
The Triumvirate is the proposal-origin body that puts privileged changes forward, while a bicameral
legislature is the wider two-chamber arrangement that pairs that proposal-origin side with Senate
review and voting, so the Triumvirate is one half of the structure rather than the structure itself
([Glossary: Bicameral Legislature](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#bicameral-legislature),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

- **Triumvirate** — the proposal-origin body within governance.
- **Bicameral legislature** — the two-body structure pairing proposal origin with Senate review.

## Distinction from Senate

The Triumvirate and the Senate name the origin body and the review body. The Triumvirate originates
proposals and closes them for execution, while the Senate is the elected body that votes to approve
or reject those proposals between origin and close
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Senate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance/senate)).

- **Triumvirate** — the body that originates and closes proposals.
- **Senate** — the elected body that votes to approve or reject them.

## Distinction from Proposal

The Triumvirate and a proposal name a body and the item it puts forward. The Triumvirate is the
proposal-origin body, while a proposal is the governance item it creates for Senate review
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Glossary: Proposal](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#proposal)).

- **Triumvirate** — the body that originates the item.
- **Proposal** — the governance item it puts forward for review.

## Reader Boundary

The Triumvirate is not the Senate, a proposal, a proposal hash, or Subtensor itself. It names the
proposal-origin body inside Bittensor governance
([Glossary: Triumvirate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#triumvirate),
[Governance Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/governance)).

When the focus is the item being reviewed, proposal is more precise. When the focus is the voting
body, Senate is more precise. When the focus is the chain layer affected by governance, Subtensor is
more precise.

That precision is the useful boundary for the term. Triumvirate explains proposal origin; it does
not by itself prove approval, rejection, application, or the details of a privileged chain change.
