# Root Claims

Root claims handle alpha dividends from stake placed through the Root Subnet. They determine whether
earned alpha remains as subnet alpha or is converted into TAO and restaked on root:
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets).

The mechanism is narrow. It is about dividend treatment after rewards accrue, not about moving the
original root stake and not about a general staking workflow.

## Root Subnet Context

The Root Subnet is the subnet-agnostic staking environment in Bittensor. Root claims exist because
stake placed there can receive alpha dividends from other subnets, even though the root stake itself
remains tied to the Root Subnet:
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims).

That separation gives root claims their role. The underlying stake path stays on root, while the
earned alpha needs a handling rule once it is claimable.

## Keep and Swap Outcomes

Root claims have two outcomes. Keep leaves earned alpha as the alpha of the subnet that produced it.
Swap converts the earned alpha into TAO and restakes that dividend output on the Root Subnet:
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions).

Both outcomes apply to dividends, not to the original root stake. This distinction keeps root claims
separate from unstaking, moving stake, or changing delegation.

## Claim Type Context

The claim type is the preference that decides which outcome a root claim uses. Keep and Swap are
therefore best read as routing choices for dividend output:
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets).

The claim event and the claim type are related but different. A claim processes accumulated
dividends; the claim type determines whether the processed dividend remains as alpha or becomes TAO
restaked on root.

## Automatic and Manual Processing

Root claims may be processed automatically or triggered directly, but the same claim type governs
the resulting dividend treatment in either case:
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets).

This means the trigger path is not the core concept. The important article-level idea is the
dividend outcome: Keep preserves alpha exposure from the producing subnet, while Swap routes the
dividend into TAO on root.

## Flow-Based Emission Boundary

Root-claim swaps have a specific boundary in Bittensor emission accounting. They handle dividend
output, while flow-based emissions track subnet inflows and outflows for emission distribution:
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions).

This boundary prevents a misleading reading of every alpha-to-TAO conversion as the same kind of
subnet flow event. Root claims are a dividend-handling mechanism layered after rewards are earned.

## Reader Boundary

This article defines root claims as a staking and emissions concept. It does not provide wallet
instructions, claim timing advice, or a recommendation between Keep and Swap:
[Root Claims](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/root-claims),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets).

Readers who need exact procedure or settings should use the official task documentation for that
specific action. The stable purpose here is to explain what root claims are and why they differ from
the underlying root stake.
