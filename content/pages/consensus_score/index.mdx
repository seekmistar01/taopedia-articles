# Consensus Score

A consensus score is the agreement threshold Yuma Consensus uses when validator weights are combined
into final rank calculations. The Bittensor glossary frames it as a stake-weighted median of
validator-assigned weights, which makes it a filter around shared validator agreement rather than an
individual validator opinion.

References: [Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Why the Threshold Matters

Validator weights can differ because validators evaluate miner work independently. Yuma Consensus
needs a way to keep a single outlying weight from carrying more influence than the broader
stake-weighted agreement signal. The consensus score provides that threshold so weights far from
agreement can be filtered before final rank calculations.

References: [Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Relationship to Validator Weights

Validator weights are the inputs being compared. The consensus score is not another validator weight
and is not a separate evaluation rule for miners. It is the stake-weighted agreement point used
after validators have submitted weights, helping Yuma Consensus separate broad agreement from
outlier assignments.

References: [Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)

## Outlier Filtering

The glossary connects consensus score to filtering: weights near the median agreement signal remain
usable for final rank calculations, while outlier weights can be clipped away from the result. For
readers, the important idea is that consensus score protects the aggregation step from isolated
weight choices without requiring every validator to submit identical weights.

Reference: [Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score)

## Relationship to Consensus-Based Weights

Consensus-based weights, also called liquid alpha, use consensus alignment in the bond path between
validators and miners. A consensus score is narrower: it is the threshold-like agreement signal
around submitted validator weights. The concepts are related because both depend on whether
validator behavior is close to consensus, but they are not the same article topic.

References:
[Consensus-based Weights](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/consensus-based-weights),
[Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score)

## Relationship to Trust

Consensus score and trust describe different stages of the same clipping process. Consensus score is
the threshold-like agreement signal used during clipping, while trust is the outcome metric that
reflects how much of an evaluated miner's support survived that threshold. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score) frames consensus
score around the agreement threshold, and
[Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust) around the post-filtering result.

For readers, consensus score names the benchmark used to filter weights, and trust names how much
support remained after that filtering. They are related but measure different things, so they are
best read as two views of the clipping step rather than the same value.

References: [Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust)

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

Consensus score is usually discussed for one incentive mechanism at a time, but subnets can also run
multiple mechanisms in parallel. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms) notes
that each mechanism operates with its own bond pool for Yuma Consensus calculations.

For readers, a consensus score still names the agreement threshold used when validator weights are
filtered, but the mechanism context matters. Consensus filtering for one mechanism should be read
separately when a subnet uses more than one evaluation system.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Multiple Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#multiple-incentive-mechanisms)

## Careful Interpretation

A consensus score is not a public quality rating for a subnet, a promise about miner rewards, or a
manual setting for users. It is a consensus mechanism term used inside weight aggregation. Exact
math, implementation details, and operational behavior belong in the Bittensor references; the
Taopedia-level meaning is the role of a stake-weighted agreement threshold.

References: [Consensus Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#consensus-score),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)
