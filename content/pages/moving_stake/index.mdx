# Moving Stake

Moving stake is the operation of moving a staked position between hotkeys and/or between subnets in
a single atomic transaction, without first withdrawing to the coldkey. The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
lists it alongside transferring stake and direct unstaking as one of the participant-facing ways to
rearrange an existing staked position.

In Taopedia terms, moving stake names a delegation-change operation that can move a staked position
between hotkeys and/or between subnets. It belongs alongside staking (adding stake), unstaking
(withdrawing stake), and transferring stake (reassigning coldkey ownership) as one of the core
stake-management operations.

In plain language, moving stake answers what changes when a participant redirects an existing staked
position: the hotkey, the subnet, or both, without a separate withdrawal step. The operation keeps
the staked exposure active while re-pointing it to a new hotkey or subnet target
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

## Distinction from Staking

Staking adds new TAO to a validator hotkey on a subnet. Moving stake rearranges an existing
delegated position without withdrawing to the coldkey first
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — adds new TAO to a validator hotkey on a subnet.
- **Moving stake** — rearranges an existing delegated position without withdrawing first.

## Distinction from Unstaking

Unstaking withdraws delegated stake back to the coldkey. Moving stake changes which hotkey or subnet
holds the position while delegation stays active
([Glossary: Unstaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#unstaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Unstaking** — withdraws delegated stake back to the coldkey.
- **Moving stake** — changes hotkey or subnet while delegation stays active.

## Distinction from Transferring Stake

Transferring stake changes which coldkey owns a staked position. Moving stake changes which hotkey
and/or subnet holds that position
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

- **Transferring stake** — changes which coldkey owns a staked position.
- **Moving stake** — changes which hotkey and/or subnet holds that position.

## Distinction from Toggle Transfer

Moving stake relocates a staked position between hotkeys or subnets, while toggle transfer governs
coldkey-to-coldkey transfer permission on a subnet. The toggle is about who may hold the stake, not
which hotkey or subnet it backs
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Relocation vocabulary and coldkey-transfer permission vocabulary answer different stake questions.

## Distinction from Hotkey

The hotkey is one of the dimensions that moving stake can change. The
[Glossary: Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey) describes a hotkey as
the operational key that runs a validator and holds its delegated stake, while moving stake can
reassign a staked position from one hotkey to another in a single atomic transaction
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Hotkey vocabulary names the operational delegation target; moving stake vocabulary names the
rearrangement operation that can change that target.

- **Hotkey** — the operational key that holds delegated stake for a validator role.
- **Moving stake** — the operation that can reassign stake from one hotkey to another.

## Distinction from Subnet

The subnet is the other dimension that moving stake can change. The
[Glossary: Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet) describes a subnet as
the incentive-based competition market that holds a staked position, while moving stake can reassign
a staked position from one subnet to another without withdrawing to the coldkey first
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Subnet vocabulary names the market context; moving stake vocabulary names the cross-subnet
rearrangement operation.

- **Subnet** — the incentive market whose pool holds the delegated position.
- **Moving stake** — the operation that can reassign stake from one subnet to another.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Moving stake and autostaking address related but different parts of Bittensor staking position
management. Moving stake atomically transfers an existing delegated stake position from one hotkey
or subnet to another without first withdrawing to the coldkey. Autostaking automatically routes a
miner's emission proceeds into a staking position on a chosen validator, accumulating delegated
stake over time as emissions arrive
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Autostaking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

Autostaking creates and grows a position; moving stake repositions an already-accumulated position
without withdrawing from it.

- **Autostaking** — automatic routing of emission proceeds into delegated stake over time.
- **Moving stake** — atomic repositioning of an existing delegated position.

## Distinction from Coldkey Swap

Moving stake and coldkey swap are related but distinct stake-management operations. Moving stake
rearranges an existing delegated position from one hotkey or subnet to another while keeping the
coldkey and wallet identity unchanged. Coldkey swap is the staged migration of the entire wallet's
on-chain identity — TAO balances, subnet ownership, and registrations — to a new coldkey
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Rotate or Swap your Coldkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/coldkey-swap)).

Delegation relocation and identity migration answer different recovery and rebalancing questions.

- **Moving stake** — re-points delegation while the controlling coldkey stays the same.
- **Coldkey swap** — migrates the full wallet identity to a new coldkey pair.

## Distinction from Conviction and Locked Stake

Moving stake and conviction both act on an existing staked position, but they describe different
things. Moving stake relocates a staked position between hotkeys and/or subnets in a single atomic
transaction. Conviction and locked stake describe locking subnet alpha so it builds a time-based
commitment score and the staked balance cannot be silently reduced below the locked amount
([Staking and delegation overview: Moving stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#moving-stake),
[Conviction and locked stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/conviction-staking)).

Moving stake answers how a position is relocated; conviction answers whether part of a position has
been locked for commitment. A conviction lock can constrain how much of a position moving stake
could take out of that subnet.

- **Moving stake** — relocation of delegated stake between hotkeys or subnets.
- **Conviction and locked stake** — time-based commitment lock that resists balance reduction.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Moving stake rearranges an existing delegated position between hotkeys and/or subnets without
withdrawing first. Yuma Consensus settles validator weight submissions into emission shares each
tempo
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Moving stake** — atomic reassignment of delegated stake between hotkeys or subnets.
- **Yuma Consensus** — settlement turning validator weights into emission shares.

Delegation-rearrangement vocabulary and settlement vocabulary answer different stake layers
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

## Reader Boundary

This article defines moving stake as a concept. It does not describe movement steps, recommend when
to move stake, or state current fee, slippage, or fee-minimization behavior. Readers should use the
official staking documentation for operational detail
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Transfer

Moving stake and a transfer both relocate value but act on different forms of it. Moving stake
relocates an existing staked position between hotkeys or subnets without returning it to free
balance, while a transfer sends free, unstaked TAO from one coldkey account to another
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Transfer](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#transfer),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Moving stake** — relocating a staked position between hotkeys or subnets.
- **Transfer** — sending free, unstaked TAO between coldkey accounts.
