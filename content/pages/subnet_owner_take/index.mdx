# Subnet Owner Take

Subnet owner take, which the documentation also calls the owner cut, is the share of a subnet's
alpha emissions that goes to the subnet's owner. Documentation states that the ratio of all subnet
alpha emissions given to the subnet owner as stake is 18 percent, applied globally across subnets
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## What It Is

Each subnet continuously emits alpha to its participants. The owner take is the slice of that
emission reserved for whoever owns the subnet, set aside before the remainder flows to validators,
miners, and stakers through consensus. It is received as stake rather than as a liquid payout, so it
accrues to the owner's position on the subnet
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## The Documented Share

The documentation gives the owner take as 18 percent and describes it as a global, fixed value
rather than a per-subnet setting an owner chooses. Because it is expressed as a share of emissions,
the actual amount an owner receives still depends on how much that subnet emits, which varies with
subnet performance over time ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Distinct from Validator Take

Owner take is not the same as validator take. Validator take, often set near 18 percent by default,
is a separate and configurable cut that a validator or delegate keeps from the rewards of those who
stake to it. Owner take is the subnet creator's emission share and is not a per-validator setting.
The two can look similar in size but describe different roles
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

## Owner Take Is Allocated at Tempo End

[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation describes subnet alpha as
being distributed at the end of each
[tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo), the subnet's recurring reward
interval. On a subnet such as netuid 1, the documented 18 percent owner share is part of that
tempo-end distribution rather than a separate payout on every block.

That timing matters when reading owner take alongside daily emission activity. Alpha may accumulate
over the tempo, and the owner cut is allocated when distribution runs according to the documented
split proportions
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

## Yuma Consensus Subdivides Miners, Not the Owner Share

[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) documentation explains that
the miner-side allocation on a subnet such as netuid 1 is divided among miners by
[rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank) after validator weights are
aggregated. The documented 18 percent owner take is a separate bucket from that miner subdivision —
it goes to the subnet owner rather than being split again through miner ranking.

That separation keeps owner take in subnet-ownership vocabulary. Miner and validator shares flow
through consensus evaluation; the owner cut is allocated as the documented third share when
distribution runs at tempo end ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Injection Adds Alpha Before the Owner Share Is Divided

[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation separates subnet rewards
into injection and distribution. Injection adds
[alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha) to each subnet's pools
every [block](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#block), while the documented
41/41/18 split — including the 18 percent owner take on a subnet such as netuid 1 — applies when
distribution runs at the tempo boundary.

Owner take reading should keep those stages separate. Injection describes alpha entering the subnet
over time; owner take describes how much of the distributed alpha the owner receives as stake when
the tempo ends ([Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## Relationship to Crowdloans

When ownership of a subnet is shared rather than held by a single party, the owner take is divided
among the holders. The documentation describes this in the context of crowdloans, where the owner
cut is distributed to contributors in proportion to their share of the subnet, so the same 18
percent is split rather than paid to one owner
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans)).

## Subnet Context

Owner take applies per subnet, for example netuid 1, since each subnet emits its own alpha and has
its own owner. The share itself is the documented protocol value, while the resulting owner
emissions on any subnet are live chain state
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For subnet owner
take on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret documented
18-41-41 distribution examples and tempo-end owner stake credits.

In localnet, owner-take examples can be exercised in an isolated environment. Local tempo
distribution and owner-stake outcomes reflect local chain configuration rather than production
subnet emissions.

On testnet, owner-take behavior can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet alpha
distribution and owner stake context are separate from mainnet subnet state
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

On mainnet, subnet owner take is the live documented owner share of subnet alpha distributed at
tempo end on the production Bittensor network
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. An owner-take example from one environment should not be
read as representing production owner emissions on another network.

## Documented Split Is 18-41-41 at Tempo Distribution

[Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution) states that
accumulated alpha at the end of each
[tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo) is divided with 18 percent to the
subnet owner, 41 percent to miners, and 41 percent to validators and their stakers on a subnet such
as netuid 1. Owner take therefore names the owner bucket in that documented three-way split.

The percentages describe allocation at distribution time rather than every intermediate block step.
Injection can add alpha earlier, but the documented owner share applies when tempo distribution
runs.

## Miner and Validator Buckets Use Yuma After the Owner Share

The same distribution section assigns the 41 percent miner bucket through
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) miner-emission rules and the
41 percent validator bucket through validator-emission rules, including
[validator take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-) and staker
proportions. Owner take is allocated before those role-specific subdivisions rather than inside
them.

That ordering keeps subnet ownership separate from miner ranking and validator commission
vocabulary. Owner cut names the documented owner stake share; miner
[incentives](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentives) and validator
[dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission) follow their own paths.

## Alpha Outstanding Accumulates Before the Owner Cut Is Paid

Injection material explains that each block sets aside alpha to be emitted to participants,
including the subnet creator, while distribution applies the documented split when the tempo ends.
On netuid 1, owner take reading should therefore distinguish alpha accumulating over the tempo from
the owner stake share credited at distribution
([Glossary: Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

Flow-based injection can change how much alpha enters a subnet over time, but the owner-percent
language still describes the documented distribution bucket when rewards are allocated at tempo end.

## Owner Take Is a Protocol Allocation, Not a Fee on Users

A useful way to read owner take is as a protocol-level allocation of emissions rather than a charge
collected from miners or validators. Documentation places the owner share inside the emission split
itself, so it is carved out of what the subnet emits rather than deducted from another participant's
balance ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

For readers, this distinguishes owner take from validator take. Validator take is a commission
applied to delegated-stake emissions during distribution, while owner take is a fixed bucket in the
same split that funds the subnet creator's role
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

That framing also explains why owner take is allocated before the miner and validator buckets are
subdivided. Documentation applies the owner share first and then routes the remaining buckets
through Yuma Consensus, so owner take does not compete inside miner ranking or validator commission
math ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level. It does not report the owner take actually accruing
on any particular subnet or name a specific owner. Those are live chain state and should be checked
for the relevant netuid. The 18 percent figure is the documented value and is the protocol's stated
owner share at the time of writing ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).
