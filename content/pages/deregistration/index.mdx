# Deregistration

Deregistration is the removal of a Bittensor subnet miner or subnet validator from a subnet because
of poor performance. The glossary defines the term at the subnet role level rather than as a wallet
or account label
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

## What It Represents

When a subnet is full and a new entrant registers, the lowest-emission eligible incumbent can be
replaced under documented rules. Immunity period protects new entrants from ordinary removal for a
block-measured grace window after registration
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

The term applies to both subnet miners and subnet validators. It names entrant-level removal inside
a continuing subnet, not removal of the entire subnet
([Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration),
[Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration)).

Examples tied to netuid 1 illustrate one subnet's replacement path. They should not be read as
representing production removal behavior on another network
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Subnet Deregistration

Participant deregistration removes one miner or validator from a continuing subnet. Subnet
deregistration removes an entire subnet from the network
([Subnet Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-deregistration)).

- **Deregistration** — entrant-level removal when capacity and eligibility rules apply.
- **Subnet deregistration** — removal of an entire subnet from the network.

## Distinction from UID Trimming

UID trimming is a subnet-owner bulk resize that removes many low emitters down to a chosen maximum.
Deregistration recycles one slot when a full subnet accepts a new registration
([UID Trimming](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/uid-trimming)).

- **Deregistration** — one incumbent removed when a full subnet accepts a new registration.
- **UID trimming** — owner-initiated bulk capacity reduction.

## Distinction from Register

Register is subnet entry that purchases a UID slot. Deregistration removes an eligible participant
when capacity and replacement rules require it
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register)).

- **Register** — subnet entry that assigns a UID slot.
- **Deregistration** — removal that frees a slot under documented rules.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

Immunity period is the temporary protection window after successful registration. Deregistration is
the removal outcome once eligibility rules allow replacement
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period)).

- **Immunity period** — grace window that delays ordinary removal rules.
- **Deregistration** — displacement event once those rules apply.

## Distinction from Pruning Score

Pruning score ranks which UID is most expendable when a full subnet accepts a new registration.
Deregistration is the removal that frees the selected slot
([Glossary: Pruning Score](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#pruning-score)).

- **Pruning score** — ranking input for displacement candidates.
- **Deregistration** — removal action that follows when replacement is required.

## Distinction from UID Slot

A UID slot is the subnet position a participant occupies after registration. Deregistration removes
the neuron from that position when replacement rules apply
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

- **Deregistration** — entrant-level removal when capacity rules apply.
- **UID slot** — participant position that removal can vacate.

## Distinction from Metagraph

The metagraph is a snapshot of registered neurons and their state at each UID. Deregistration
removes a neuron from that roster when replacement rules apply
([Glossary: Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#metagraph)).

- **Deregistration** — removal of a neuron from its subnet UID slot.
- **Metagraph** — subnet participant state snapshot at one block height.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Deregistration is the removal of a subnet neuron from its UID slot when capacity and eligibility
rules require displacement. It names roster exit from the metagraph, not the recurring settlement
step that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary on each subnet. It reads
the weight matrix from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result
into miner incentives and validator dividend shares
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate deregistration from UID slots and from metagraph snapshots. Displacement
vacates a participant position; Yuma settles evaluation signals from eligible validators who remain
active on that netuid—not the removal event itself
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot)).

Readers should treat deregistration as roster-exit vocabulary on one netuid, and Yuma as the
epoch-boundary mechanism that allocates subnet rewards from consensus while validators remain
active.

- **Deregistration** — removal of a subnet neuron when eligibility rules apply.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This article defines deregistration at a high level. It does not list current subnet entrants, rank
participants, or describe registration commands. Live replacement eligibility belongs in mining and
validator references
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

## Distinction from Neuron

Deregistration and a neuron relate as an event and the entity it acts on. A neuron is the registered
participant occupying a UID slot in a subnet, while deregistration is the process that removes a
neuron from its slot — freeing the UID for a new registration when the neuron underperforms or its
immunity lapses ([Glossary: Neuron](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#neuron),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration),
[The Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

- **Neuron** — the registered participant occupying a UID slot.
- **Deregistration** — the process that removes a neuron from its slot.
