# Delegation

Delegation is the process of staking TAO to a validator. It names the support relationship between a
TAO holder and the validator receiving that stake
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term should be read separately from the validator role and from stake as an amount. Delegation
is relationship vocabulary inside the staking model.

## Validator Support

Delegation attaches staking support to a validator. A validator's total stake can include stake
delegated by others, so delegated TAO contributes to the validator's broader staking context
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

This makes delegation different from performing validation work. The delegator supplies support,
while the validator performs the validator role.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For delegation on
a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret validator sets, pool
state, and staking relationships
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

Localnet delegation examples can test support wiring in isolation. Testnet examples add shared
non-production staking state. On mainnet, delegation on subnets such as netuid 1 concerns production
validator relationships, pool reserves, and delegated positions on the active network.

Delegation examples from one environment should not be treated as evidence for another.

## Validator Take Precedes Delegator Credits

The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
explains that after a validator or delegate extracts their
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
the remaining emissions are credited back to stakers and delegators in proportion to their stake
with that validator.

That ordering sits between validator-side emissions and delegator outcomes. Delegation names the
support relationship, while validator take names the validator's kept share from delegated-stake
emissions before the rest moves toward those who delegated.

The proportional credit applies within the staking relationship around one validator. It does not
turn delegation into a fixed yield quote, but it does explain why delegator-facing
[Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission) follow after the
validator-side take rather than mirroring the full validator emission total. Validator take is part
of the distribution stage after validator emissions are calculated
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

## Distinction from Delegate

Delegation names staking TAO to support a validator hotkey, while delegate names the receiving
validator role that nominators support
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

For readers comparing figures behind one hotkey, delegation tracks nominated support actions and
delegate identifies who receives that support. Delegate stake reports validator-supplied TAO only,
effective stake counts delegate-owned plus nominated TAO, and delegation totals can differ from both
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

Use delegation when the article covers how support is attached and delegate when the receiving
validator role itself is the focus
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation** — the support relationship created by staking TAO behind a validator hotkey.
- **Delegate** — the validator role that receives that nominated support.

## Delegation Is an Ongoing Position, Not a One-Time Payment

A useful framing is that delegation creates a standing support position rather than a single
transaction that completes. Documentation describes delegated stake as remaining attached to a
validator and earning a proportional share over time, so the relationship persists until the holder
moves or removes it
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

For readers, this is why delegation is paired with management actions like moving and unstaking. The
position keeps participating across tempos, and the holder can repoint or withdraw it, which only
makes sense if delegation is an ongoing state rather than a finished payment
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

That standing nature also explains why returns are described proportionally rather than as a fixed
amount. Because the position remains active while emissions and the validator's take change over
time, the same delegated stake can correspond to different credited shares across periods
([Glossary: Validator Take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

## Distinction from Delegation Rewards

Delegation is TAO support staked behind a validator hotkey on a subnet. Delegation rewards are the
share credited back to nominators after the validator keeps its configured take from delegated-stake
emissions
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

- **Delegation** — support attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Delegation rewards** — the credited share after validator take.

The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#moving-stake)
describes moving stake as a way to transfer a delegated position between validators or between
subnets in one atomic step, without first withdrawing TAO to the coldkey.

That operation changes where
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation) points while
the position stays delegated. It differs from unstaking, which ends the support relationship and
returns value toward coldkey control, and from adding new stake, which increases support without
rearranging an existing position.

Moving stake therefore fits delegation management rather than the initial act of attaching support.
A holder can shift support from one validator hotkey to another, or from one subnet market to
another, while keeping delegated exposure active across one submitted rearrangement instead of an
unstake-and-restake sequence through the coldkey
([Moving stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#moving-stake),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is the TAO holder who delegates. Delegation is the stake relationship to a validator
hotkey on a subnet, not the holder's wallet role
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

For netuid 1 examples, name the validator hotkey and subnet when describing delegation. The same
nominator can hold separate delegated positions on different validators or subnets
([Glossary: Hotkey](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#hotkey),
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid)).

## Distinction from Root Subnet Staking

On an alpha subnet such as netuid 1, delegated TAO enters that subnet's pool and is tracked in alpha
behind the validator hotkey. On the root subnet, the same delegation concept keeps stake
TAO-denominated instead
([Staking into Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets),
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero),
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)).

Name the subnet before comparing delegation figures across markets
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Validator Permit

Delegation attaches TAO support to a validator hotkey. Validator permit is the separate on-chain
flag that allows a hotkey to validate and set weights on a subnet
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

A holder can delegate to a validator without performing validation work. Permit vocabulary belongs
when the article covers whether a hotkey may validate on netuid 1, not when it covers who supplied
staked support behind that hotkey
([Glossary: Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

Support-relationship vocabulary and validation-permission vocabulary answer different questions.
Delegation names who supplied staked support behind a hotkey; validator permit names whether that
hotkey may validate and set weights on a subnet.

- **Delegation** — nominated TAO support attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Validator permit** — the on-chain flag that allows a hotkey to validate and set weights.

## Distinction from Delegate Stake

Delegate stake is the TAO a validator stakes from their own balance. Delegation is nominated TAO
that other holders attach to that validator hotkey on a subnet
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

Both amounts can sit behind one hotkey on netuid 1, but delegate stake names validator-supplied TAO
and delegation names support from nominators. Effective stake counts both together when readers need
the full backing total
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Delegation and effective stake describe a delegate's support as relationship and total. Delegation
is the process by which nominated TAO is attached to a validator hotkey, while effective stake is
the resulting total TAO associated with that delegate after its own stake and nominator-delegated
stake are counted
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegation** — the process of attaching nominated TAO to a validator hotkey.
- **Effective stake** — the total TAO associated with a delegate after self-stake and nominations.

## Distinction from Nominate

Delegation names staking TAO to support a validator hotkey. Nominate is the extrinsic action that
attaches that support to the chosen validator on a subnet
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Readers following wallet flows meet nomination before broader delegation language. Nominate
describes the on-chain step that assigns stake support, while delegation names the ongoing support
relationship that results
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

Action vocabulary and relationship vocabulary answer different reader questions on the same staking
path. Use nominate when the article covers the staking action that selects a validator; use
delegation when it covers the support relationship that remains after that action
([Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

- **Delegation** — the ongoing support relationship after TAO is staked to a validator.
- **Nominate** — the on-chain action that attaches that support to a validator hotkey.

## Distinction from Autostaking

Delegation and autostaking both end in stake behind a validator but describe different steps.
Delegation is the act of attaching TAO support to a chosen validator to share in its rewards, while
autostaking is the automation that routes a miner's newly earned emissions into such a staking
position without a manual step. One names the staking relationship; the other names the automatic
routing that can establish it
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking)).

- **Delegation** — attaching TAO support to a validator to share its rewards.
- **Autostaking** — automatically routing earned emissions into a staking position.

## Distinction from Coldkey

Delegation describes where stake support is assigned, not a transfer of wallet signing authority.
The staking overview describes delegating TAO to validators, while wallet documentation keeps
coldkey and hotkey authority in the wallet-key model
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Wallets, Coldkeys and Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/keys/wallets)).

Support-relationship vocabulary and custody-key vocabulary answer different questions. A delegation
can attach stake to a validator without making the validator the owner of the delegator's wallet
keys.

- **Delegation** — the support relationship created by staking TAO behind a validator hotkey.
- **Coldkey** — the custody anchor that controls balances and authorizes sensitive wallet actions.

## Distinction from Stake

Stake names the amount associated with a validator position after support exists. Delegation is one
path by which that support becomes attached
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

Relationship vocabulary and amount vocabulary answer different staking questions. Delegation names
how support is attached; stake names the resulting amount associated with the validator position.

- **Delegation** — the process of attaching nominated TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Stake** — the amount associated with a validator position after support exists.

## Distinction from Dividends

Delegation establishes the support position behind a validator hotkey. Dividends describe an
emission share that can follow from delegated-stake relationships after validator-side distribution
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Support-relationship vocabulary and emission-outcome vocabulary answer different reader questions.
Delegation names the ongoing support position; dividends name the credited share that can follow
from that position.

- **Delegation** — the support relationship created by staking TAO behind a validator hotkey.
- **Dividends** — the emission share credited to stakers after validator-side distribution.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Delegation is the support relationship created when a TAO holder stakes toward a validator hotkey.
It names who backs a validator with stake, not the on-chain step that turns validator weight
submissions into emission shares
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain process that converts submitted validator weights into emission
shares after evaluation completes. It settles scoring outcomes on a subnet; it does not by itself
create the delegation relationship
([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Readers should keep the support relationship separate from the settlement step. Delegation answers
who stakes toward a validator; Yuma Consensus answers how validator weights become emission shares
in that subnet
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Delegation** — support relationship from staking TAO toward a validator.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain weight aggregation that settles emissions.

## Distinction from Multiple Mechanisms

When a subnet uses multiple incentive mechanisms, delegation remains the same staking-support
concept. The nominator-delegate relationship holds across mechanism contexts: nominators supply
delegated stake, and delegates perform validation in the relevant mechanism. The mechanism context
explains what the validator evaluates; delegation describes the staking-support side of that
relationship
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Child hotkeys also appear near delegation context. A child hotkey is a hotkey that can participate
in subnet validation under a parent hotkey. The child hotkey documentation describes how child
hotkeys relate to validation, while delegation describes how TAO support attaches to a validator
hotkey ([Child Hotkeys](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators/child-hotkeys),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

For readers, delegation stays focused on the staking-support relationship between a TAO holder and a
validator. Multiple mechanisms and child hotkeys add context around where and how validation can
occur, but delegation names the staking-support relationship rather than the validation context
itself ([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## Distinction from Dynamic TAO

The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation#staking-into-subnets)
states that staking is always local to a subnet. When a TAO holder delegates on an alpha subnet such
as netuid 1, the TAO first enters that subnet's automated market maker pool and is converted into
the subnet's
[Glossary: Alpha Tokens](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha) before
the delegated position settles behind the validator hotkey.

That conversion is part of the delegation path itself, not a separate reward step. The support
relationship is the same staking concept, but the amount held behind the validator is expressed in
subnet alpha units rather than as TAO sitting directly on the hotkey.

The
[Glossary: Root Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#root-subnetsubnet-zero)
is the contrasting path. Root Subnet delegation keeps stake expressed in TAO, while alpha-subnet
delegation produces subnet-specific alpha exposure through each subnet's paired pool reserves.

Because staking to a validator on different subnets is independent, a delegation statement should
name the subnet whose pool and alpha currency apply, not only the validator hotkey. The same
validator identity can carry separate delegated positions across separate subnet markets.

## Reader Boundary

Delegation is not a validator recommendation, balance report, fee explanation, or staking guide. It
names staking TAO to a validator as support inside Bittensor's staking model
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Delegation does not rank validators or calculate dividends.
