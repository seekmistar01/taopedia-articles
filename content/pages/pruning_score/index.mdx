# Pruning Score

A pruning score expresses how a subnet decides which participant makes way when it is full and a
newcomer wants in. It captures a simple principle of Bittensor: a place in a subnet is held by
continuing to earn, and the participant that has earned the least is the one asked to leave. On a
subnet such as netuid 1, that is the participant with the lowest pruning score
([Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For pruning score
on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret subnet slot
competition examples.

In localnet, pruning mechanics can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet pruning scores do
not represent production subnet participation.

On testnet, slot competition can be exercised in a shared non-production network. Testnet pruning
outcomes are separate from mainnet subnet state.

On mainnet, pruning score describes live production subnet slot competition. Observed rankings
depend on the selected subnet's current emission and participation context.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A pruning-score example from one environment should not be
read as representing production slot competition in another environment.

## Distinction from Activity Cutoff

Pruning score reflects emission standing when a full subnet must displace one participant. Activity
cutoff is the early-epoch block window within which a validator must submit weights to count in the
current Yuma Consensus pass
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#activitycutoff),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

Activity cutoff concerns validator participation timing within an epoch, not displacement ranking.

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing for single-slot displacement on a full subnet.
- **Activity cutoff** — epoch block window for weight submission to count.

## Distinction from Rank

Pruning score reflects emission standing when a full subnet must displace one participant. Rank is
the consensus standing that divides miner-side rewards after validator weights are aggregated
([Glossary: Rank](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#rank),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing for slot displacement on a full subnet.
- **Rank** — consensus standing that drives miner-side emission shares.

## Distinction from Trust

Pruning score reflects emission standing when a full subnet must displace one participant. Trust is
the share of validators that assign a non-zero weight to a miner, measuring how broadly that miner
is supported before consensus
([Glossary: Trust](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#trust),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing for slot displacement on a full subnet.
- **Trust** — the share of validators assigning non-zero weight to a miner.

## Distinction from Immunity Period

Pruning score reflects how much a participant has been earning on a subnet such as netuid 1.
Immunity period is the post-registration window that can protect a newcomer from early displacement
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing among participants on a full subnet.
- **Immunity period** — post-registration window that can block early displacement.

## Distinction from UID Slot

Pruning score ranks which incumbent is weakest when a full subnet must free one position. A UID slot
is the indexed participant position that displacement can vacate
([Glossary: UID Slot](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#uid-slot),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings standing used to rank displacement candidates.
- **UID slot** — the indexed participant position on a netuid.

## Distinction from Active UID

An active UID records whether a neuron currently meets activity conditions on the metagraph. Pruning
score instead names earned emission standing when slot displacement is driven by registration
pressure on a full subnet
([Glossary: Active UID](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#active-uid),
[Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)).

Active UID answers whether a slot currently satisfies activity rules. Pruning score answers which
incumbent looks weakest when documented deregistration ranking is needed to free one slot
([Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

## Distinction from UID Trimming

Pruning score and UID trimming both use emission standing, but they trigger different removal paths.
Pruning score ranks which incumbent gives way when a full subnet admits one new registration, while
UID trimming is an owner-initiated bulk resize that lowers the subnet toward a chosen maximum
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[UID Trimming](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/uid-trimming)).

- **Pruning score** — earnings standing for single-slot displacement at registration time.
- **UID trimming** — owner-initiated bulk reduction of low emitters.

## Distinction from Validator Deregistration

Pruning score and validator deregistration act on the same removal as selector and action. The
pruning score is the per-neuron value that ranks which UID is most expendable when a full subnet
must displace one, while validator deregistration is the validator-specific removal that frees the
slot
([Validating: Validator deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/validators#validator-deregistration)).

The two also differ in scope. Pruning score is role-neutral: the neuron with the lowest score, miner
or validator, risks replacement when all UID slots are occupied, so validator deregistration is only
the validator-side case of a ranking that applies to any neuron
([Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

## Distinction from Subnet Validator

Pruning score and the subnet validator meet at deregistration eligibility. Pruning score is the
per-neuron value that ranks which UID is removed first when the subnet is full, while a subnet
validator is a neuron that evaluates miners and submits weights in a subnet
([Glossary: Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-validator)).

A subnet validator is not exempt from that ranking. A low-scoring validator outside its immunity
window can be displaced like any other low scorer, so pruning score applies to validators as well as
miners
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

Pruning score ranks which neuron is most expendable. Validator deregistration is the validator-side
removal that can free the slot.

- **Pruning score** — earnings standing that ranks displacement candidates.
- **Validator deregistration** — validator-side removal that frees a slot.

## Distinction from Deregistration

Pruning score reflects how much a subnet participant has been earning when slots are contested.
Deregistration is the removal of a participant when capacity and replacement rules apply
([Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration),
[Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration)).

Pruning score names the standing metric. Deregistration names the removal event.

- **Pruning score** — earnings-based standing that can put a participant closest to displacement.
- **Deregistration** — the removal step when a slot must be freed.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Pruning score names the metric used to decide which neuron may be displaced when a subnet's UID
slots are full. Yuma Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts validator weight submissions
into emission shares each tempo
([Mining: Miner deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners#miner-deregistration),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Pruning score** — displacement metric when subnet UID slots are full.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page describes the concept at a high level. Which participant actually stands lowest on a given
subnet at a given time is live state that shifts as earnings change, and is best read from current
chain data at the time of observation. The durable point is the principle: a subnet place is earned,
the least-earning participant makes way, and a grace period protects newcomers first
([Mining in Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners)).
