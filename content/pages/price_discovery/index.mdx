# Price Discovery

Price discovery is how a market arrives at an asset's price through trading, instead of the price
being handed down. In Bittensor a subnet's alpha price is found this way: it emerges from the buying
and selling that pass through the subnet's pool, so the number reflects what participants are
actually willing to trade at rather than a figure set by anyone in particular
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## How the Market Sets the Price

A subnet's alpha price comes from the ratio of TAO to alpha held in its pool. Every buy and sell
shifts that ratio, so the price moves continuously: buying alpha removes it from the pool and lifts
the price, selling does the reverse. The price at any instant is simply the market's current answer,
arrived at through the trades that have already happened
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

## Discovered, Not Assigned

The defining feature is that no one dictates the price. It is an outcome of many independent
decisions to trade, updating as those decisions arrive. That is what separates a discovered,
market-set price from one chosen administratively: the market, not an authority, settles on the
figure, and it keeps re-settling as conditions change
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Why It Matters

A discovered price carries information. Because it aggregates what many participants are willing to
pay or accept, it acts as a signal of how the market currently values a subnet's token rather than a
static label. That is also why liquidity and arbitrage matter alongside it: deeper liquidity and
active arbitrage keep the discovered price responsive and consistent rather than easily knocked
around ([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

Price discovery belongs to pool trading vocabulary. Subnet
[emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) and
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) settle incentive rewards from
validator evaluation; they do not assign the alpha price readers see in the pool.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For price
discovery, that sequence changes how readers should interpret pool-price and trading-activity
examples ([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

In localnet, pool trading can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet discovered prices do
not represent production market behavior.

On testnet, subnet pool activity can be exercised in a shared non-production network. Testnet price
signals are separate from mainnet subnet state.

On mainnet, price discovery concerns live production subnet pools. Observed prices depend on the
selected subnet's reserves, liquidity, and trading activity
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

A price-discovery example from one environment should not be read as representing production pool
behavior in another environment.

## Distinction from Emission

Price discovery and emission both attach numbers to a subnet's alpha, but from different systems.
Price discovery is the market price that emerges from the subnet pool's TAO-to-alpha reserve ratio
as trading and conversions move it, while emission is the protocol-driven distribution of reward
alpha that Yuma Consensus settles from validator evaluation, so emission sets how much reward alpha
is created and to whom rather than the pool price a reader sees
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Price discovery** — the market price emerging from the subnet pool's reserve ratio.
- **Emission** — the protocol distribution of reward alpha from validator evaluation.

## Distinction from Slippage

Price discovery and slippage describe the resulting price and the per-trade impact on it. Price
discovery is the emergent price from the pool's TAO-to-alpha ratio, while slippage is how far a
given stake, unstake, or swap moves that price, which is larger in a thinner pool
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Price discovery** — the emergent price from the pool's reserve ratio.
- **Slippage** — how far a single trade moves that price.

The same conversion size has a larger price impact through smaller reserve balances, so a thin pool
shifts its TAO-to-alpha relationship more on each trade and the discovered price can move further
from the pre-trade level than it would in a deeper pool
([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage),
[Glossary: Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#slippage)).

## Distinction from Alpha Price

Price discovery and alpha price name a process and its result. Price discovery is how trading and
conversions settle a subnet's price through the pool, while the alpha price is the resulting
TAO-denominated quote that process produces at a given moment
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Price discovery** — the process that settles the price through trading.
- **Alpha price** — the resulting TAO-denominated quote it produces.

[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage) documentation describes
staking and unstaking as conversions that pass through the same subnet pool reserves as swaps.
Staking moves TAO into the pool and receives subnet
[alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha); unstaking reverses that
flow. Each action changes the TAO-to-alpha ratio the pool holds.

Price discovery therefore includes delegation activity, not only discretionary swaps. The alpha
price updates when participants stake, unstake, or trade because all three move the paired reserves
that define the current ratio
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
states that each subnet functions as its own automated market maker, with one liquidity reserve
holding TAO and a second holding that subnet's own dynamic currency. The discovered alpha price
reflects that subnet's pool ratio, not a network-wide figure shared across every netuid
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That per-subnet structure keeps price discovery local to the subnet being read. A high or low alpha
price on one netuid describes trading and pool activity inside that subnet's pool rather than a
single global label applied to all subnets.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Price discovery names how a subnet's alpha price emerges from trading in that subnet's pool. Buys,
sells, stakes, and unstakes shift the TAO-to-alpha reserve ratio, and the observed price reflects
what participants are willing to trade at
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

- **Price discovery** — how pools and trading reveal alpha prices.
- **Yuma Consensus** — on-chain settlement that turns validator weights into emission shares. Price
  discovery names pool-side alpha pricing; Yuma processes validator weights into rewards
  ([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

## Distinction from Alpha Reserve

Price discovery is how subnet pool trading reveals alpha pricing through activity. Alpha reserve is
the pool-held portion of a subnet's alpha context used in emission and liquidity design
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: Alpha reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection)).

- **Price discovery** — how trading activity reveals subnet alpha pricing.
- **Alpha reserve** — pool-held alpha in the subnet's reserve context.

Discovery vocabulary names the pricing process; reserve vocabulary names the pool-held alpha side
([Glossary: Alpha Price](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#alpha-price)).

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level and is not financial advice. The discovered price for
any subnet changes continuously with trading, liquidity, and demand, all of which move over time.
The durable point is the mechanism: the price is an emergent result of market activity, not a fixed
value read off a list.
