# Validator Permit

A validator permit is a Bittensor flag for validation rights inside a subnet. The term is scoped to
the subnet where the permit is being discussed and is not a universal validator label across every
subnet
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit)).

## What It Represents

A validator permit answers whether a UID holds the right to submit validator weights in this subnet.
It does not answer whether the validator is active, profitable, or highly ranked. The permit is a
binary rights flag, not a performance measure
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Stake weight explains how much consensus influence the validator's stake produces; a validator
permit records only whether the weight-submission right exists in that subnet. On netuid 1, permit
examples illustrate one subnet metagraph; they should not be read as representing production permit
assignments on another network
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

## Distinction from Max Validators

A validator permit is the rights flag granted to higher-stake neurons. Max validators is the
hyperparameter that caps how many permits can exist on a subnet at once
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Permit vocabulary names who may set weights; max validators vocabulary names the ceiling the permit
system fills.

## Distinction from Activity Cutoff

A validator permit records whether a UID may submit counting weights. Activity cutoff is the
early-epoch block window within which those weights must arrive to count in the current consensus
pass
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Permission and timing are separate gates on the same weight-submission path.

## Distinction from Deregistration

A validator permit is reassigned among registered neurons by stake standing. Deregistration removes
a neuron from its UID slot and ends any permit attached to that slot
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: Deregistration](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#deregistration)).

Standing-based authorization and slot removal answer different lifecycle questions.

## Distinction from Register

Register places a hotkey on a subnet UID slot. The validator permit is earned separately by top
stake-weight standing and records whether that registered UID may submit counting weights
([Glossary: Register](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#register),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Entry alone does not grant the permit.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Stake weight is the computed influence measure behind validator standing. The validator permit is
the binary rights flag earned by top stake-weight neurons on a subnet
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Influence vocabulary and permission vocabulary answer different reader questions on the same
validation path.

## Distinction from Validator Take

A validator permit decides whether a UID may validate and submit counting weights. Validator take
decides what commission the validator keeps from delegated-stake dividends once those rewards exist
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

Authorization precedes payout splitting on the validator dividend path.

## Distinction from VPermit

Validator permit is the per-subnet boolean flag on a neuron row. VPermit aggregates permit status
across subnets for a hotkey in wallet-facing views
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit)).

- **Validator permit** — per-subnet permission to set weights on one netuid.
- **VPermit** — aggregated permit view across subnets for one hotkey.

## Distinction from Min Allowed Weights

Min allowed weights sets how many targets a weight submission must include to be valid. The
validator permit decides whether that submission may count in consensus at all
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

Structural validity and authorization are separate gates on the same submission path.

## Distinction from Weights Version Key

The weights version key and a validator permit can both block a weights update at submission time,
but for different reasons. The version key check rejects a `set_weights` call whose declared version
is below the subnet's required minimum, while the validator permit authorizes a neuron to set
validator-style weights on other neurons at all
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

Version vocabulary gates the call's compatibility; permit vocabulary gates the neuron's authority to
submit counting weights at all.

## Distinction from Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator

The blockchain-versus-subnet-validator distinction separates a network-layer blockchain validator
that produces blocks from a subnet validator that evaluates miner work. A validator permit is the
per-epoch right that records whether a subnet UID may submit counting weights
([Glossary: Blockchain Validator vs Subnet Validator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#blockchain-validator-vs-subnet-validator),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

The role distinction names which kind of validator is meant; the permit names whether that subnet
validator's weights count this epoch.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Validator permit is the per-subnet flag that records whether a neuron may submit counting weights
this epoch. It names authorization on the metagraph row, not the settlement step that turns weights
into emissions
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

Yuma Consensus is the on-chain mechanism that runs at the epoch boundary. It reads the weight matrix
from eligible validators, applies clipping and bonding, and converts the result into incentive and
dividend shares ([Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Earlier sections separate permit from VPermit, min allowed weights, and the weights version key.
Those gates decide whether a call is authorized or structurally valid before Yuma ever aggregates
submissions.

Readers should treat validator permit as an eligibility flag on one netuid, and Yuma as the
mechanism that settles eligible weights into payouts. Holding permit does not by itself prove a
validator's dividend share
([Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions)).

- **Validator permit** — per-epoch permission to submit counting weights on one subnet.
- **Yuma Consensus** — epoch-boundary settlement from eligible validator weights to emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Validator permit names validation rights inside one subnet. Stake weight names stake-derived
validator influence, and VPermit names broader authorization context
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Glossary: VPermit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#vpermit)).

## Distinction from Kappa

A validator permit and kappa operate at different scopes in consensus. The validator permit is a
per-neuron flag, earned by ranking among the top stake holders, that grants the right to set
weights, while kappa is a subnet-wide hyperparameter that sets the stake-weighted majority threshold
used to clip weights during Yuma Consensus
([Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit),
[Subnet Hyperparameters: Kappa](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters#kappa),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus)).

- **Validator permit** — the per-neuron flag granting the right to set weights.
- **Kappa** — the subnet-wide majority threshold used when clipping weights.
