# Subnet Creator

A subnet creator is the role that defines what a Bittensor subnet is meant to produce and how that
work is evaluated
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The term belongs to subnet design vocabulary. It identifies the role that gives a subnet its work
target before miners and validators participate in that subnet.

## Design Role

The creator role sits upstream of miner work and validator evaluation. It gives the subnet a work
target, an interaction context, and an evaluation frame so subnet roles are not working against a
generic network-wide task
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

For readers, the creator shapes the context. Miners produce work inside that context, and validators
evaluate work inside that context.

Creator context often sits near subnet task, interaction protocol, and scoring method, but those
pieces answer different questions
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).
The creator names the role that brings those design choices together for a subnet.

## Task Context

A subnet creator defines the digital task miners are asked to perform. The subnet task names the
work target, while the creator role names who establishes that target for the subnet
([Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

The role and the task are not the same thing. The creator is the party responsible for the subnet's
design direction; the task is the work target miners try to satisfy
([Glossary: Subnet Task](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-task)).

## Protocol and Scoring Context

Incentive-mechanism documentation separates task, protocol, and scoring model as related parts of
subnet design. A creator's design context can include each of those pieces without making them the
same term
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

The subnet protocol describes how miners and validators interact. The scoring model describes how
returned work is evaluated. The creator role sits around those design choices rather than replacing
them.

## Operating Roles

Subnet creators are not the same role as miners or validators. Miners produce work for the subnet,
and validators evaluate that work against subnet standards
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

This distinction matters because creator vocabulary is about the source of the subnet design. Miner
and validator vocabulary is about producing and evaluating work after that design exists.

## Mechanism Scope

A subnet creator can shape more than one incentive mechanism inside a subnet. Multiple-mechanism
documentation describes separate mechanism contexts that can operate in parallel inside one subnet
([Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

For readers, one mechanism's task, protocol, or scoring model is mechanism-level context. A subnet
can contain more than one such context when multiple mechanisms are present.

That is why the creator term stays broader than one mechanism description. A mechanism explains a
particular evaluation path; the creator term names the role responsible for shaping the subnet that
contains those paths.

## Network Context

Subnet-creator examples belong to a network environment. The Bittensor Networks reference separates
mainnet, testnet, and localnet as different environments
([Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks)).

The creator concept is stable, but observed subnet behavior belongs to the environment where it was
observed. A localnet design test, a testnet trial, and a mainnet subnet do not provide the same kind
of evidence.

## Burn Cost Is the Entry Price for the Design Role

Establishing the design role described above requires paying burn cost, the dynamic TAO amount the
protocol recycles when a new subnet is registered
([Glossary: Burn Cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#burn-cost),
[Create a Subnet: Burn cost](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet#burn-cost)).

That cost attaches to subnet registration rather than to ongoing creator duties. Burn cost is paid
once to bring a subnet into existence; it does not by itself describe the work-target and
evaluation-method decisions the creator role makes afterward
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

## Hyperparameters Extend the Creator's Design Authority

Beyond the original task, protocol, and scoring design, a subnet creator's authority covers the
protocol settings that shape ongoing subnet behavior. Hyperparameter documentation describes these
as protocol state variables governing timing, validator-weight flow, consensus behavior, and
emission handling for a subnet
([Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

That authority is narrower than the design role itself. Hyperparameters adjust the protocol envelope
around an existing incentive mechanism; they do not redefine the subnet task or replace the scoring
model the creator originally set.

## Registration Follows the Design Context

Understanding subnets separates the design context a subnet creator establishes from the miner and
validator roles that later register and participate inside that subnet
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator)).

Creator vocabulary therefore belongs upstream of registration examples. It names who shaped the work
target and evaluation frame, not live UID status for a particular participant.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For the subnet
creator role on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret design
examples and mechanism outcomes.

In localnet, creators can design and validate work targets and evaluation rules in an isolated
environment. Local mechanism tests do not represent production subnet performance.

On testnet, creators can exercise those designs in a shared non-production network. Testnet subnet
behavior is separate from mainnet state on subnets such as netuid 1.

On mainnet, the subnet creator role applies to live incentive markets with real emissions on the
production Bittensor network
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. Creator design examples or mechanism outcomes from one
environment are separate from production subnet performance in another environment.

## Reader Boundary

Subnet creator should not be read as a subnet launch guide, an operations checklist, a funding
explainer, or a quality rating for a specific subnet. It names the design role behind a subnet's
work target and evaluation method
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

## Relationship to Subnet Lease

Subnet creator and subnet lease describe subnet ownership as originator and arrangement. The subnet
creator is the party that registers and owns the subnet, holding its owner emission share, while a
subnet lease is the crowdloan-backed arrangement that distributes that owner share among the
contributors who funded the lease
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Crowdloans](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/crowdloans)).

A lease does not change who originated the subnet; it splits the creator's owner emission share
among lease contributors, so subnet creator names the owning party and subnet lease names how that
owner share is distributed ([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

## Relationship to Immunity Period

A subnet creator and the immunity period meet at configuration. A subnet creator is the role that
defines a subnet's task and evaluation and sets the subnet's hyperparameters, while the immunity
period is one such owner-settable hyperparameter that fixes how long a new UID is protected from
deregistration, so the creator chooses the immunity window its subnet enforces
([Glossary: Immunity Period](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#immunity-period),
[Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Subnet Hyperparameters](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/subnet-hyperparameters)).

- **Subnet creator** — the owner role that sets the subnet's hyperparameters.
- **Immunity period** — the owner-settable window protecting new UIDs from deregistration.

## Relationship to Start Call

A subnet creator and a start call meet at activation. A subnet creator is the party that designs,
registers, and owns a subnet, while the start call is the activation step the creator issues to
begin the subnet's first emission period, so a created subnet does not emit until its creator makes
that call
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Create a Subnet](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/create-a-subnet)).

The two name different points in ownership. Subnet creator spans the design and operation of the
subnet, while start call marks the single step that turns emissions on, so registration establishes
the subnet and the creator's start call is what makes it economically live
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

- **Subnet creator** — the role that designs, registers, and owns a subnet.
- **Start call** — the activation step the creator issues to begin the subnet's first emission
  period.

## Relationship to Digital Commodities

A subnet creator and a digital commodity describe the role that defines a subnet and the output it
produces. A subnet creator is the role that designs the subnet's task and incentive mechanism, while
a digital commodity is the useful work the subnet asks miners to produce and validators to evaluate,
so the creator sets which commodity the subnet produces
([Glossary: Subnet Creator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subnet-creator),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

The two answer different questions. Subnet creator names the role whose incentive design decides
what counts as the product and how it is scored, while digital commodity names that evaluated
product, so the creator is the author of the rules and the commodity is the output those rules
define ([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).
