# Delegate Stake

Delegate stake is the TAO a Bittensor delegate stakes themselves. It is the self-staked portion of
the delegate's staking context, not the full amount of TAO that may be associated with that delegate
after nomination
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)).

The term is useful because delegate, nominator, stake, and effective stake all sit close together in
Bittensor staking vocabulary. Delegate stake keeps the focus on who supplied the TAO.

## Delegate and Nominator Boundary

A [delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate) is a validator that can
receive stake from nominators. A
[nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator) stakes TAO to a validator
rather than becoming that validator
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Delegate stake belongs to the delegate side of that relationship. Nominated TAO can add to the
delegate's broader staking context, but it is not delegate stake because it did not come from the
delegate themselves.

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For delegate stake
on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret self-stake totals,
nomination, and stake-weight context.

In localnet, delegate-stake examples can test staking wiring in an isolated environment. Local
self-stake totals reflect local chain configuration rather than production validator relationships
on the selected netuid.

On testnet, delegate stake can be observed in a shared, non-production network. Testnet self-stake
and nomination on a selected netuid are separate from mainnet staking state
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

On mainnet, delegate stake concerns production validator self-stake on the active Bittensor network
for the connected subnet context.

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A delegate-stake example from one environment should not
be read as representing production staking behavior on another network.

## Distinction from Staking

Staking names the process of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey. Delegate stake names only
the TAO a delegate stakes themselves rather than nominated support from others
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey.
- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO only.

## Distinction from Stake Weight

Delegate stake is the TAO a validator stakes from their own balance. Stake weight is the computed
influence measure used inside subnet consensus after self-stake and nominated support are combined
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).

- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO only.
- **Stake weight** — consensus influence derived from broader stake context.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Delegate stake names an amount behind a hotkey. Validator take names the share a validator keeps
from delegated-stake emissions before the remainder credits back to nominators
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO behind the hotkey.
- **Validator take** — commission on delegated-stake returns at distribution.

## Distinction from Delegation

Delegation names nominated TAO attached to a validator hotkey. Delegate stake names only the TAO the
validator supplied from their own balance on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

- **Delegation** — nominated TAO attached to a validator hotkey.
- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO only.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator supplies delegated support to a validator. Delegate stake is the validator's own
committed TAO rather than support from nominators
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake)).

- **Nominator** — wallet role that supplies delegated support.
- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO on the receiving hotkey.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Effective stake counts delegate-owned plus nominated TAO behind one hotkey. Delegate stake counts
only the self-supplied portion
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Subnet Metagraph](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/metagraph)).

- **Effective stake** — combined self-stake plus nominated support behind the hotkey.
- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO only.

## Distinction from Stake

Stake names the validator-associated amount after support exists. Delegate stake names only the
validator-supplied portion of that amount
([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Stake** — combined validator-associated amount behind the position.
- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO only.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Delegate stake names TAO the validator stakes from their own balance. Yuma Consensus is the separate
mechanism that converts validator weight submissions into emission shares each tempo
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Delegate stake** — validator-supplied TAO behind the hotkey.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

Delegate stake names the TAO a delegate stakes themselves, not nominated support, effective stake,
stake weight, or validator-selection advice
([Glossary: Delegate Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate-stake),
[Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake)).

Effective stake combines self-stake and nomination; stake weight is the subnet consensus influence
measure derived from stake context
([Glossary: Stake Weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake-weight),
[Understanding Subnets: Validator stake weight](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets#validator-stake-weight)).
