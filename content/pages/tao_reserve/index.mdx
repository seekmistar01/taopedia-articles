# TAO Reserve

A TAO reserve is the pool-held TAO side of a subnet's liquidity context in Bittensor. The official
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet as an automated market maker with two reserves: one containing TAO and one
containing the subnet's alpha token.

## Pool Context

The TAO reserve belongs to the subnet pool rather than to a participant balance. The same official
subnet overview describes TAO reserves as the amount of TAO staked into the subnet, paired with an
alpha reserve that contains alpha available for purchase. Together, those reserves form the pool
side of a subnet's Dynamic TAO economy.

This makes TAO reserve a tokenomics location concept. It does not redefine TAO itself; it describes
where TAO sits when it is part of a subnet's reserve pair.

## Price Context

The official subnet overview explains that a subnet pool's reserve ratio determines the price of the
subnet's alpha token. TAO reserve is one side of that ratio, while alpha reserve is the other. For
readers, the important point is that reserve language describes the pool relationship between TAO
and subnet alpha, not a standalone price quote.

Exact reserve amounts and prices are current state values. The concept article only identifies the
TAO reserve as the pool-held TAO side used in that relationship.

## Emission Context

The [Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection) documentation
describes TAO reserve injection as adding TAO to subnet reserves according to emission allocation.
Under flow-based emissions, subnet-level TAO reserve injection is determined from net TAO flow
rather than only from token price.

This puts TAO reserve in the injection stage of emissions. Later participant distribution still
depends on the subnet's reward and consensus processes, so TAO reserve injection should not be read
as a direct statement about any individual miner, validator, staker, or subnet owner payout.

## Relationship to Alpha Reserve

TAO reserve and alpha reserve are paired pool-side concepts. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) source
describes the two reserves together, and the
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#alpha-reserve-injection) documentation
explains alpha reserve injection as proportional to TAO injection so the pool can grow without
changing the alpha token price from the injection itself.

## Relationship to Multiple Mechanisms

TAO reserve names the TAO side of a subnet liquidity pool. The
[Glossary](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism) notes that each
subnet has one or more incentive mechanisms.

For readers, TAO reserve still describes pool-held TAO in one subnet market.

References:
[Multiple Incentive Mechanisms Within Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-multiple-mech-subnets),
[Glossary: Incentive Mechanism](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#incentive-mechanism)

## Relationship to Liquidity Positions

TAO reserve and liquidity positions both describe the TAO and alpha pool context of a Bittensor
subnet, but they name different layers of that context. The TAO reserve is the protocol-managed TAO
side of the subnet's AMM pool, used for staking conversions and emission injection, while liquidity
positions are participant-supplied concentrated liquidity ranges that add depth to the same trading
pool. The [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)
documentation describes the TAO reserve as one side of the pool, and the
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions) documentation
describes how participants add concentrated liquidity within that pool context.

For readers, TAO reserve names the core protocol pool state; liquidity positions name optional
participant contributions on top of that state. Both belong to the same subnet pool, but they
represent different origins: one is managed by the protocol, the other is supplied by participants.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)

## Reader Boundary

TAO reserve is not a staking tutorial, a live subnet ranking, or a yield estimate. It is a
tokenomics term for the pool-held TAO side of a subnet's liquidity context. Live reserve values,
prices, and trading outcomes should be checked in current official tools or chain data.

## Relationship to Reserve Ratio

TAO reserve and reserve ratio are related but different scopes in Bittensor pool vocabulary. TAO
reserve names the pool-held TAO side of a subnet liquidity pool, while reserve ratio names the
relationship between that TAO reserve and the paired alpha reserve. The
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes each subnet pool as holding a TAO reserve and a subnet-specific alpha reserve, and
explains that the reserve ratio between those two sides determines alpha token price context.

For readers, TAO reserve names one pool side, while reserve ratio names how that TAO side relates to
the alpha side inside the same subnet pool.

References: [Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)

## Relationship to Netuid

TAO reserve and a netuid are related but different parts of Bittensor vocabulary. A netuid
identifies a subnet on the network, while TAO reserve names the pool-held TAO side inside that
subnet's liquidity pool. The
[Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid) places netuid at the
subnet level, and the
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes TAO reserves as the amount of TAO staked into a subnet's pool.

For readers, a netuid identifies which subnet pool is in scope, while TAO reserve names the TAO side
held inside that pool.

References: [Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Subtensor

TAO reserve and Subtensor are related but different parts of Bittensor chain vocabulary. Subtensor
names Bittensor's layer 1 blockchain and system of record, while TAO reserve names the pool-held TAO
side recorded through subnet liquidity state on that chain layer. The
[Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor) describes the
blockchain layer, and the
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes subnet pools and their TAO reserves as part of subnet tokenomics on that layer.

For readers, Subtensor names the chain layer, while TAO reserve names where pool-held TAO sits in
subnet liquidity state on that layer.

References: [Glossary: Subtensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#subtensor),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to TAO

TAO reserve and TAO are related but different scopes in Bittensor token vocabulary. TAO is the
network incentive token, while TAO reserve names where TAO sits when it is part of a subnet's
reserve pair rather than naming the token itself. The
[Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao) describes TAO as the token
used to incentivize subnet activity, and the
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets) documentation
describes TAO reserves as the pool-held TAO side of a subnet liquidity pool.

For readers, TAO names the network token, while TAO reserve names the pool location concept for that
token inside a subnet market.

References: [Glossary: TAO](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tao),
[Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)

## Relationship to Protocol Alpha

TAO reserve and protocol alpha are connected through the reserve injection process. TAO reserve is
the pool-held TAO side of a subnet's automated market maker, while protocol alpha is alpha the
Bittensor protocol accumulates as a result of chain-side TAO buys during reserve injection. The
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha)
describes protocol alpha as alpha the protocol gathers from chain-side TAO buys during reserve
injection, cached per subnet rather than immediately recycled. The
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)
documentation describes how TAO is added to subnet reserves according to emission allocation.

TAO reserve names where injected TAO lands in the pool; protocol alpha names the alpha the protocol
accumulates from the corresponding chain-side activity during that same injection. Neither term
refers to participant balances: TAO reserve is the protocol-managed TAO side of the pool, while
protocol alpha is the protocol's own accumulated stake from the injection process. At subnet
dissolution, protocol alpha participates in pro-rata TAO settlement rather than remaining in the
pool alongside the TAO reserve.

References:
[Glossary: Protocol Alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha),
[Emission: TAO reserve injection](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#tao-reserve-injection)
