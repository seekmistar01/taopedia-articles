# Delegation

Delegation is the process of staking TAO to a validator. The official glossary describes delegation
as staking TAO to a validator, increasing that validator's stake and helping secure validator rights
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The term names a support relationship. It should be read separately from the validator role and from
the amount term stake.

## Validator Support

Delegation attaches staking support to a validator. The staking and delegation overview explains
that validator total stake can include stake delegated by others
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake)).

This makes delegation different from performing validation work. A delegator supplies support; the
validator uses its validator role to evaluate and submit weights.

## Stake Context

Delegated TAO contributes to the validator's stake context. The stake glossary defines stake as the
currency-token amount delegated to a validator position, including both validator-supplied and
delegated stake ([Glossary: Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

For readers, delegation is part of the stake picture. It changes the support attached to a validator
position rather than naming a separate subnet task.

## Consensus and Emissions Context

Delegation matters because validator stake affects subnet consensus and emissions. The staking and
delegation overview states that a validator's total stake helps determine consensus power and share
of emissions
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Permit](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-permit)).

This article keeps delegation at the reference level. It does not calculate dividends, rank
validators, or describe staking operations.

## Relationship to Nominate

Delegation and nominate are related but different scopes. Delegation names the support relationship,
while nominate names the staking action that attaches TAO to a validator
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

For readers, delegation is relationship vocabulary and nominate is action vocabulary. Both sit in
the same staking and validator-support context.

## Relationship to Dividends

Delegation and dividends are related but different parts of staking vocabulary. Delegation names how
TAO support is attached to a validator, while dividends describe an emission share returned through
delegated-stake relationships
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#dividends),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Delegation establishes the support position. Dividends describe one outcome that can follow from
emission distribution.

## Reader Boundary

Delegation should be read as a concept article term. It is not a validator recommendation, balance
report, fee explanation, or operational staking guide
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The stable article-level point is that delegation names staking TAO to a validator as staking
support.
