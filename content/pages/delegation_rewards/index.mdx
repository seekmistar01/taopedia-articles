# Delegation Rewards

Delegation rewards are what a delegator earns for staking TAO to a validator instead of validating
itself. By backing a validator with stake, a delegator shares in the rewards that validator
produces, in proportion to how much stake it contributed. The documentation frames delegation as
exactly this arrangement: stake placed with a delegate to share in the returns
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## A Share of the Validator's Earnings

A validator generates rewards from its role, and those rewards are split among the validator and the
stake delegated to it. A delegator's reward is its proportional slice of that flow, scaled by how
much stake it added relative to everything else delegated to the same validator. More stake behind a
validator means a larger share of the same pool, not a separate reward created from nothing
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## The Validator's Take Comes First

Before delegators are paid, the validator keeps a take, a commission on the rewards. A delegator
earns the remainder after that take is removed, so the take a validator sets feeds directly into
delegator returns: a higher take leaves a smaller share for those who delegated. Comparing takes is
therefore part of comparing what different validators offer a delegator
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

## Why Delegate at All

Delegation lets a TAO holder earn from validation without operating a validator. The holder lends
the weight of its stake to a validator it trusts and shares in the upside, treating the validator's
take as the cost of that convenience. The reward is the return on that choice, set against the
effort and risk the delegator avoids by not validating directly
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

## The Other Side of Dividends

Delegation rewards and validator dividends describe one flow from two angles. Dividends name the
validator-side result, while delegation rewards are the delegator's share of that result after the
take. Reading them together explains where a delegator's earnings actually come from rather than
treating them as a payment that appears on its own
([Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)).

## Development Stage Context

The
[Introduction to Bittensor](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/introduction#subnet-development)
describes subnet development as moving from localnet to testnet and then mainnet. For delegation
rewards on a subnet such as netuid 1, that sequence changes how readers should interpret validator
take, stake placement, and reward-share examples.

In localnet, delegation reward flows can be tested in an isolated environment. Localnet tempo
boundaries and delegator share examples do not represent production staking returns.

On testnet, delegation and reward distribution can be exercised in a shared non-production network.
Testnet validator performance and delegator shares are separate from mainnet subnet state.

On mainnet, delegation rewards are live outcomes tied to production validators and delegators on
subnets such as netuid 1. Observed returns depend on validator performance, take settings, and the
stake distribution on the selected subnet
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

The [Bittensor Networks](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/concepts/bittensor-networks) reference
separates mainnet, testnet, and localnet. A delegation-reward example from one environment should
not be read as representing production returns in another environment.

## Delegator Shares Are Credited at Tempo End

[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions) documentation describes subnet alpha as
being distributed at the end of each
[tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo), after
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus) aggregates validator
evaluations. A delegator's reward is a proportional slice of the validator-side flow that results
from that distribution stage, not a separate payout label on every block.

That timing keeps delegation rewards tied to finalized consensus outcomes. Validators and their
delegators participate across the tempo, but the documented reward split — including the delegator
share after [validator take](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-) —
applies when distribution runs at the tempo boundary on a subnet such as netuid 1
([Understanding Incentive Mechanisms](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/anatomy-of-incentive-mechanism)).

## Delegation Rewards Are Subnet-Local

[Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
treats staking as local to a subnet. TAO delegated to a validator on a subnet such as netuid 1
supports that validator inside that subnet's emission context, separate from delegation sent to the
same validator's hotkeys on other netuids
([Glossary: Netuid](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#netuid)).

Delegation rewards follow the same locality. A delegator's return depends on the validator
relationship on the subnet where the stake was placed, rather than on a single network-wide reward
pool copied across every subnet. Reading rewards subnet by subnet matches how delegated support is
recorded in staking material
([Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation)).

## Alpha Subnets Record Delegated Stake in Alpha Units

Staking documentation states that, except on the root subnet, stake held behind a validator hotkey
is recorded in that subnet's
[alpha](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#protocol-alpha) units after TAO enters
the subnet pool. On a subnet such as netuid 1, a delegator therefore commits TAO through the pool
and holds subnet-denominated stake while earning from that validator relationship
([Understanding Subnets](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/subnets/understanding-subnets)).

That recording rule sits beside reward vocabulary rather than replacing it. Delegation rewards still
name the delegator's share of validator-side returns, while alpha-subnet staking explains the unit
in which the supporting stake is tracked after the TAO-to-alpha conversion step
([Understanding Slippage](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/slippage)).

## Take Is Not the Only Thing That Shapes a Delegator's Return

Comparing validator take is useful, but documentation frames the delegator's reward as a share of
what a validator actually produces, which means take alone does not determine the outcome
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission)). A low take on a validator
that earns little can leave a delegator with less than a higher take on a validator that performs
well.

For readers, this reframes the comparison from a single number to two factors. The validator-side
return depends on consensus performance, and the take then decides how that return is split, so a
delegator is effectively choosing both a performer and a fee
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

That is also why the reward is described as proportional rather than fixed. Because both the
underlying earnings and the stake distribution behind a validator move over time, the same delegated
amount can correspond to different returns across tempos
([Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo)).

## Distinction from Autostaking

Delegation rewards are the proportional return delegated stake earns after distribution and take.
Autostaking automatically routes miner emissions into a validator-staking position
([Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Outcome vocabulary names what delegated support earns; autostaking vocabulary names one routing path
that can grow the delegated position over time.

## Distinction from Validator Take

Validator take is the commission a validator keeps from delegated-emission returns before nominators
receive the remainder. Delegation rewards are what remains for delegated stake after that split
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Take shapes nominator returns inside the same validator relationship that delegation rewards
describe.

## Distinction from Nominator

A nominator is the participant role that delegates support to a validator. Delegation rewards are
the proportional returns that role earns on delegated stake after distribution
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Role vocabulary names who delegates; delegation-reward vocabulary names what that support earns.

## Distinction from Nominate

Nominate is the action of attaching TAO support to a validator hotkey. Delegation rewards are the
returns that attached support can earn after consensus distribution and take
([Glossary: Nominate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominate),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Action vocabulary names support being added; reward vocabulary names the outcome once distribution
runs.

## Distinction from Dividends

On a subnet such as netuid 1, delegation rewards and dividends name different layers of the same
validator-side flow. Dividends are the validator-side emission outcomes Yuma Consensus produces from
bonds and miner incentives, while delegation rewards are the proportional share nominators receive
after validator take is applied to the delegated portion
([Glossary: Dividends](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#emission),
[Yuma Consensus: Validator emissions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus#validator-emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

For readers comparing returns on netuid 1, dividends name the validator-side payout category before
the nominator split, and delegation rewards name what remains for delegated stake after take. A
dividend figure therefore does not by itself equal a nominator return
([Emission: Distribution](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions#distribution)).

- **Dividends** — validator-side emission outcomes from consensus.
- **Delegation rewards** — nominator share after validator take on delegated emissions.

## Distinction from Effective Stake

Delegation rewards and effective stake answer different questions behind the same validator hotkey
on a subnet such as netuid 1. Effective stake is the combined total of delegate-owned and
nominator-delegated TAO behind that hotkey, while delegation rewards are the proportional returns
delegated stake earns after distribution and take
([Glossary: Effective Stake](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#effective-stake),
[Glossary: Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegation),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

For readers, effective stake is amount vocabulary that explains how much support sits behind a
delegate, and delegation rewards are outcome vocabulary that explains what that support earns after
consensus and take on netuid 1. Higher effective stake can change a nominator's proportional slice
without replacing performance or take vocabulary
([Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

## Distinction from Liquidity Provider Rewards

Delegation rewards and liquidity provider rewards both pay a participant for committing capital, but
from different sources. Delegation rewards are a share of a validator's emissions earned by
delegating stake to that validator, while liquidity provider rewards are fee income from trades
crossing a provided liquidity range. One is paid out of validator emissions through consensus; the
other out of pool trading activity
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[User Liquidity Positions](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/liquidity-positions)).

- **Delegation rewards** — a share of a validator's emissions for delegated stake.
- **Liquidity provider rewards** — fee income from trades crossing a liquidity range.

## Validator Take Splits Emissions Before Nominator Share

Delegation documentation walks through validator-side returns as a two-step split. A validator first
keeps the share of emissions matching their self-stake fraction of total stake, and validator take
then applies to the emissions earned on nominator support before the remainder is credited back to
those nominators
([Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Validator Take %](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#validator-take-)).

That ordering is why delegation rewards are described as net of take rather than as a flat fraction
of all validator earnings. The nominator share is what remains after take is removed from the
delegated portion on the selected subnet
([Glossary: Nominator](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#nominator)).

## Distinction from Staking

Staking and delegation rewards are an action and its payout. Staking is the process of attaching TAO
support to a validator, while delegation rewards are the share a delegator earns from that
validator's emissions for providing the support
([Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

- **Staking** — the process of attaching TAO support to a validator.
- **Delegation rewards** — the share a delegator earns from that validator's emissions.

## Distinction from Emission

Emission names the broader subnet reward and injection flow. Delegation rewards name the nominator's
proportional share of validator-side returns after distribution and take on delegated stake
([Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).

Network-wide reward vocabulary is wider than the delegator outcome vocabulary inside one validator
relationship.

## Distinction from Tempo

Tempo names the subnet epoch boundary when finalized rewards are distributed. Delegation rewards are
credited as part of that distribution step rather than as a continuous wallet fee
([Glossary: Tempo](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#tempo),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

Timing vocabulary and return-shape vocabulary answer different reader questions on the same payout
path.

## Distinction from Yuma Consensus

Delegation rewards name the returns that flow to delegators who stake TAO to subnet validators. Yuma
Consensus is the separate mechanism that converts validator weight submissions into the miner and
validator emission shares that underpin those returns each tempo
([Delegation](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Yuma Consensus](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/yuma-consensus),
[Emission](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/learn/emissions)).

- **Delegation rewards** — returns to delegators who stake to validators.
- **Yuma Consensus** — mechanism that turns validator weights into emission shares.

## Reader Boundary

This page defines the concept at a high level and is not financial advice. What a delegator actually
earns depends on the validator's performance, the take it sets, and the live distribution of stake,
all of which change over time. The durable point is the shape of the reward: a proportional share of
a validator's rewards, net of its take
([Staking and Delegation Overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)).
