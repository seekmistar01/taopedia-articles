# Autostaking

Autostaking is a Bittensor feature that automatically stakes a miner's earnings to a validator the
miner chooses. The official
[Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking) documentation
describes it as a way for miners to automatically stake mining income to a validator of their
choice, streamlining compound staking without manual intervention.

## Why Autostaking Exists

Mining produces a stream of emissions over time, and converting those earnings into stake normally
takes repeated manual action. The autostaking documentation frames the feature as removing that
friction, so a miner's rewards are continuously reinvested into the network instead of sitting idle
between manual stake operations.

For readers, autostaking is best understood as a standing instruction rather than a one-time action.
Once set, it keeps directing new mining emissions into stake without further steps.

References: [Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)

## How Autostaking Behaves

The autostaking documentation explains that a miner sets a destination validator, and as the miner
earns emissions from subnet participation, those emissions are automatically staked to that
validator. This lets a miner grow stake as it is earned, without repetitive manual stake movements.

The destination is configured per subnet for a given wallet, so the choice of validator can follow
each subnet the miner participates in. For readers, the key idea is continuity: autostaking turns
ongoing earnings into ongoing stake under the miner's chosen delegation target.

References: [Auto Staking for Miners](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/miners/autostaking),
[Glossary: Staking](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#staking)

## How It Relates to Delegation

Autostaking routes earnings into the same staking-and-delegation system that other participants use.
The
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation)
describes how stake is delegated to validators, and autostaking simply automates the miner side of
that flow by sending fresh emissions to a chosen delegate.

For readers, autostaking does not introduce a new reward type. It is a convenience layer that feeds
a miner's emissions into ordinary delegation rather than requiring manual staking each time.

References:
[Staking and delegation overview](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/staking-and-delegation/delegation),
[Glossary: Delegate](https://docs.learnbittensor.org/resources/glossary#delegate)

## Reader Boundary

Autostaking should not be read as a yield promise, a validator recommendation, or a step-by-step
setup guide. Which validator to choose and whether to automate staking are decisions for each miner,
and current behavior belongs in official references and live chain state. This article describes the
concept of automatically staking mining emissions to a chosen validator.
